    Cultural Revolution Timeline: The Early Years

    • 1966, Feb – Beijing mayor Peng Zhen's “Outline Report on Current Academic Discussions” from the Cultural Revolution Group
    • 1966, May – Nie Yuanzi big‑character poster accusing Beijing University vice‑chancellor of undermining the Cultural Revolution
    • 1966 – “May Sixteenth Circular” condemning Peng Zhen's outline report and purging Peng
    • 1966, May – Maoist Cultural Revolution Group becomes the Central Cultural Revolution Group headed by Chen Boda
    • 1966, May – First Red Guards at Tsinghua University secondary school
    • 1966, Jun – People's Daily editorial “Sweep Away All Monsters and Demons”, reproduces Nie Yuanzi's poster
    • 1966, Jul – Mao swims in the Yangzi near Wuhan
    • 1966, Jul – Mao calls enlarged meeting for the Cultural Revolution Group
    • 1966, Aug – Mao's “Bombard the Headquarters” poster
    • 1966, Aug – Central Committee issues the “Sixteen Points” of the Cultural Revolution
    • 1966, Aug – Campaign to “Destroy the Four Olds and cultivate the Four News” launched
    • 1966, Sep – Red Guards from Beijing (including Nie Yuanzi) attempt to occupy CCP headquarters in Shanghai, repelled by locals
    • 1966, Nov – Shanghai Workers' Revolutionary Rebel Headquarters created
    • 1967 – “January Power Seizure” wave
    • 1967, Jan – First Revolutionary Committee established in Heilongjiang
    • 1967, Feb – Attempt to form a Shanghai Commune
    • 1967 – “February adverse current” guidelines issued by Mao
    • 1967 – “Counter‑revolutionary May 16 Conspiracy”
    • 1967, Jul – Wuhan Incident
    • 1967, Aug – Seizure of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and burning of the British Embassy
    • 1967, Oct – Classes ordered to resume
    • 1967, Oct – Mao calls for revolutionary organisations to forge “great alliances”
    • 1967–69 – Campaign to Purify Class Ranks
    • 1968, Mar – Joint editorial “Revolutionary Committees Are Good” widely published
    • 1968, Jul – Mao convenes meeting with leading Red Guard figures in Beijing for self‑critique
    • 1968, Oct – Liu Shaoqi expelled from the CCP
    • 1968 – PLA re‑establishes order, often through violent crackdowns that contribute heavily to the Cultural Revolution death toll
