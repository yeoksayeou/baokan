楚溪春在週會講述游剿控制面戰術 4

〔中央社北平廿六日電〕冀主席楚溪春，廿五日在週會時宣述「游剿控制面」戰術。楚氏謂：根據最近冀東平漢北段戰役經驗，證明守點控制面不如「游剿控制面」打擊大。匪配合大部隊作戰，要不死守，一城一地，不犧牲人，不損失槍及物資，最近戰役，淶水，定興，新城，香河，武淸等縣，因戰略關係，都曾一度放棄，均先後收復，以新城的空室淸野做得最好，今後如永保有此等做法，則匪槍枝無從搶，物資亦無處掠刦。氏繼謂：實行游剿戰有三個基本條件：（一）消息　通，以判斷匪情，決定撤或守。（二）空室淸野，使不但士兵撤退，老百姓亦撤退，大量物資運走，小部份食糧用具埋藏，大軍拆卸，分散廠埋，水井掩美國，使匪入城無吃無喝，找不到物資，找不到運具，亦找不到人民。（三）封鎖匪軍，清野之後，立即斷絕行人，不使匪放出一個偵探。楚氏結論謂；這是戰略的轉變，希望人民瞭解，並擁護這種戰法。

農村復興與農業改良 20

最近政府當局對於農業問題，有了深切的注意。地政部積極政力於全般土地改革的研究與計劃，農林部注意於農業推廣的實施，幷計劃以浙江廿五縣爲示範區，辦理現代化的農業推廣輔導。各方論壇近來對於農業科學化問題的注意，已超越了士地改革的技術問題。這是一種進步的現象，不約而同的，認爲農業問題的關鍵，不完全在土地改革和土地分配的問題，它的歸趨仍在農業的改良，效率的提高，生產的增加，使農民完全免於饑饉匱乏，而完全取得合理化的生活水準。這種合理化標準化的農業經濟，我們可稱爲黎民不饑不寒的農業經濟，也是民生主義首先所追求的小康經濟。

我國是農業經濟社會，國家的「安」與「亂」，幾乎全視農業生產是否獲得充分發展，農民生活是否能獲得安居樂業而定。至於要使農業生產發展，不僅要求土地分配平均，並且要求效率提高，生產增加，以補土地所有分配的不足；要使農民生活的安居樂業，不僅要求天災水旱的消滅，兵糧徵發的避免，並且要求永遠有安定的和平的環境。有人以爲解決了士地分配問題，便算解決了農民的問題，這種理論是不健全的，而且也不是健康的農業經濟，這是見山不見林的皮毛之見，從現代經濟觀點來說，這種理論已經迂腐了。

現在，且看我國士地問題的現狀，即可知農業問題的眞正關鍵在那裏？根據確實的估計，我國雖號稱地大物博，但已墾土地面積，仍然是很小的，僅約十三億市畝，佔士地總面積百分之十左右；若以此已墾的土地面積，分攤於三億二千餘萬的農民，每人所得不過三·七五市畝（約合〇·六英畝），與美國伊斯特敎授估計每人維持最低生活所需的二·五英畝比較，相去很遠了。益以年來天災人禍頻仍，連已墾的土地又有不少荒蕪，黃河泛區耕地的僵化即其一例。如就土地與人口的比例來說，顯然是人多地少，統計數字很明白地指出這種「不均」的現象。據一九二七年的統計：三十畝以下的貧農中農，佔總人數的百分之七十九，而其所佔總耕地面積的比例僅百分之十九；三十畝至五十畝的中晨，佔總人數的百分之十六，其所佔鐵耕地面積的比例爲百分之十七，五十畝以上的地主，佔總人數僅百分之十五，而其所佔總耕地面積的比例多至百分之六十二。又據一九三七年中央農業實騐所調查所得：我國自耕農爲百分之四大，半自耕農爲百分之二四，佃農爲百分之三十。經通八年抗戰的結果，自耕優有不少淪落爲半自耕農或佃農，使土地的所有權更形集中，自然這種「不均」的現象，必需要解決分配問題。關於士地分配，民生主義早定了「平均地權」的解決方法，以達「耕者有其田」的目的。可惜政府過去對於士地改革工作未能推行，或是執行得不澈底，甚少效果。例如：過去確認土地丈爲改革土地問題的開端，但是土地淸丈祇在極少縣內舉行；又如推行「二五减租」作爲扶植自耕農的階梯，但「二五减租」直到今日仍未普遍實行，最近湖南農民要求「二五减租」，竟反遭受殺身之禍。足見土地分配問題的解決，今天的阻礙仍然很多，政府在當前旣感共產黨問題的嚴重，應該痛下決心，迅速實行民生主義的土地改革政策，另方面再配合農業經濟的改良與進步，從土地利用問題上，以求我國農業生產之發展和農村經濟的繁榮。

土地的利用，亦即農業生產經營的問題。現代農業生產的發展，舉世已多實行機械化與科學化，在生產技術上，採行集體耕種，在農業經營上，採行大農塲制。我國土地呈現零星與離數的現象，在經營上，可漸進走上大農塲制。本報七月廿三日刋戲蚌埠通信，詳述皖東北（包括五河瓖遠實城三縣）試行農墾機械化，農業科學化，不僅從技術上使用曳引機耕田，成績斐然，爲各地農民所稱羨，並且在經營上，獲得意外的成功，生產增加了，農民團結了，農家小康生活的獲得，完全寄託在農集科學經營的輔導工作上，農民與政府的關係是相依爲命的。農林部最近擬與聯合國和浙江省政府合作，於杭嘉湖寗紹金等廿五縣，辦理現代化的農業推廣，這是推行集體耕種，試行大農塲制的絕好地區。以皖東北試行農耕機械化的經驗來說，農林部如在浙江廿五孫試行這個制度，浙江人民自必報以熱烈的歡迎。希望農林部早日將這巨大計劃，付諸實現！

總之，農業問題是今日我國社會經濟問題中，最感迫切而急待決的問題。也唯有農業改良，效率提高，生產增加，擴大土地利用的效用，纔能够使三億二千餘萬的農民大衆，免於饑饉匱乏，而完全取得合現化的生活　　。我們贊成農業經濟的改良與進步，我國與國際的合作，使農業生產得到充分發展。我們遙矚皖東北農業機械化的日新進步，格外盼望浙江大農場的集體經營之能早日實現！

Chu Xichun Describes Guerrilla Control Surface Tactics at Weekly Meeting 4

[Central News Agency, Beiping, 26th] Chairman of Hebei Province, Chu Xichun, announced "Guerrilla Control Surface" tactics at the weekly meeting on the 25th. Chu stated that based on the recent experience in the battle of the northern section of the Hebei-Beijing Railway in eastern Hebei, it proves that defending points to control the surface is not as effective as "Guerrilla Control Surface" in striking the enemy. When bandits coordinate with large units for combat, they must not defend to the death, sacrificing a city or a piece of land, without sacrificing people or losing guns and supplies. In the recent battles, Lai Shui, Ding Xing, Xin Cheng, Xiang He, Wu Qing, and other counties were abandoned once due to strategic reasons, but they were all recovered later. Xin Cheng's practice of clearing the fields and houses was the best. If this method can be maintained in the future, the bandits will have no way to seize weapons or loot supplies. Chu continued, stating that there are three basic conditions for implementing guerrilla warfare: (1) Information is unobstructed to judge the enemy's situation and decide whether to retreat or defend. (2) Clearing the fields and houses, so that not only the soldiers retreat, but also the people retreat, a large amount of materials are transported away, a small amount of food and utensils are buried, and the large armies are dismantled and buried separately. The wells are covered to prevent the enemy from having food and drink when entering the city, and from finding supplies, vehicles, or people. (3) Blockading the bandit army, immediately cutting off pedestrians after clearing the fields, preventing the bandits from sending out a scout. Chu concluded that this is a strategic shift, and he hopes that the people will understand and support this tactic.

Rural Revival and Agricultural Improvement 20

Recently, the government authorities have paid close attention to the agricultural issue. The Ministry of Land Affairs is actively focusing its efforts on the research and planning of comprehensive land reform, and the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry is paying attention to the implementation of agricultural extension, and plans to take 25 counties in Zhejiang as demonstration areas to carry out modernized agricultural extension guidance. In recent forums, attention to the issue of agricultural science has surpassed the technical issues of land reform. This is a progressive phenomenon, and it is unanimously believed that the key to the agricultural issue does not lie entirely in land reform and land distribution, but rather in agricultural improvement, increased efficiency, and increased production, so that farmers are completely free from hunger and poverty, and completely obtain a reasonable standard of living. This kind of rationalized and standardized agricultural economy, we can call it an agricultural economy where the people are not hungry or cold, which is also the Xiaokang economy first pursued by the Principle of People's Livelihood.

China is an agricultural economic society, and the "peace" and "chaos" of the country almost entirely depend on whether agricultural production can be fully developed and whether farmers' lives can be peaceful and prosperous. As for making agricultural production develop, it not only requires equal land distribution, but also requires increased efficiency and increased production to make up for the insufficiency of land ownership distribution; to make farmers' lives peaceful and prosperous, it not only requires the elimination of natural disasters such as floods and droughts and the avoidance of conscription of soldiers and grain, but also requires a permanently stable and peaceful environment. [This newspaper]: A broadcast from London on the 26th stated that the ability of both Britain and the United States to cope with the Berlin situation has become stronger today. The occupation military governments of both sides, on the pretext of "technical difficulties," suddenly ordered the blockade of all railway transport between the Western Zone and the Soviet occupation zone, but mail and telegrams remained unobstructed. The so-called "technical difficulties" are actually a retaliatory action against the Soviet Union's blockade of Berlin. When the Soviet Union ordered the cut-off of all land and water transportation between the western zone of Berlin and the outside world, it also used "technical difficulties" as an excuse. It is also learned that after the British and American military governments in Frankfurt ordered the blockade of all railway transportation between the Western Zone and East Germany today, more than 70,000 tons of supplies will not be able to start shipping. Most of these supplies come from Italy and were originally scheduled to be delivered to the East German occupation zone in early August.

[United Press, London, 26th] Senior British, American, and French representatives held talks for more than an hour in the office of the British Deputy Foreign Secretary Strang today to discuss new ways of negotiating with Moscow. Since the Foreign Ministers' meeting broke up unhappily last December, this three-power meeting is probably the most important. The London meeting came after a gathering of US military and diplomatic representatives in Berlin on the 25th. The series of meetings is understood to be related to the West's new policy on the Berlin crisis and the entire Soviet relationship. After the meeting, French Ambassador Massigli was the first to walk out of the venue, rush into the car, and rush to an appointment. Five minutes later, the American delegation appeared at the door, smiling for photographs, but refusing to speak. The members of the delegation included US Ambassador to the United Kingdom Douglas, US Ambassador to the Soviet Union Smith, and State Department expert on Soviet issues Bohlen. In addition to Strong, the British representatives also included Dean, the Director of the Political Department of the Foreign Office's German Division. Although each representative refused to speak, this meeting clearly discussed new strategies for dealing with the Soviet Union, hoping to resolve the Berlin crisis and the entire German situation. A Foreign Office spokesman said that the final version of the new note to the Soviet Union has not yet been approved. "Several drafts will be sent to the three powers for study." British and American representatives met again on the afternoon of the 26th, and Foreign Secretary Bevin and British Ambassador to the Soviet Union Peterson, who had asked for leave to return home, also attended. British Commander-in-Chief in Germany Robertson will fly back to London on the 27th to report on recent developments to Bevin before the House of Commons debate on diplomacy on the 28th.

(5) Develop cooperation with the Soviet Union and people's democratic countries in all matters and objects in terms of economic culture.
