首都大專學生遊行請願　與軍官總隊衝突　雙方傷者不下百六十人五人垂危　政府當局嚴防事態擴大 3

〔本報南京一日電〕京中大專十一單位學生數千人，於今日和平代表團離京赴平開始進行和談聲中，因舉行大規模遊行示威運動，致與軍官發生衝突，引起互毆，雙方受傷者不下一百六十餘人，五人重傷，生命垂危。事態嚴重，首都衛戌總部為防止擴大，業經採取緊急措施，於當晚十時起提早特別戒嚴，並下令自二日起如有任何收容總隊隊員主動前往任何學校，與學生發生任何衝突者，一律就地槍決。行政院長何應欽對於此一不幸事件，除表示無限遺憾外，將予調查，追究責任。據悉學生之欲舉行示威遊行，治安當局事先已接獲報告，故曾設法予以勸告，但未收效。迨至一日晨十時，十一單位大專學生三千人，在中大集合後，先到總統府請願，派代表十人，請謁李代總統，提出數項要求。包括要求全面公費，實行七項諾言，爭取真和平，反對假和平等，由秘長翁文灝代見，表示同情學生之要求，允予轉達，並將設法改善全體員生工友待遇，學生乃離總統府，遊行全市，以卡車數輛為宣傳車，沿途高呼口號「反飢餓，反迫害」，「反對美援」，「反對發行大鈔」，「反對徴兵徴糧」，「反對假和平」，並扮秧歌向民眾宣傳，至下午二時廿分仍返中大解散，各校學生分別結隊返校。當學生遊行時，曾在大中橋附近，因所喊口號過於刺激，並張貼標語，向軍官宣傳，與駐該地附近之軍官總隊（均為徐蚌會戰撤退歸來之軍官）數人發生糾紛，軍官撕去學生所貼標語，及至學生解散後，劇專學生約一百數十人結隊返校，行抵大中橋，與軍官士兵約三百餘人，發生衝突互毆，學生被抓去者達六十餘人，受傷者十餘人，一部學生逃至政大求援，政大學生集合數百人，前往救援，又與軍官隊相遇，復有十餘人受傷，其中二人重傷，（一名丁雍平，一為女生田文慧），中大學生據報，乃整隊赴總統府請願，（時已六時）要求懲兇賠償醫藥費等。在大中橋附近之軍官隊，亦聞訊當卽強佔劇專遊行之卡車數輛，駛往總統府，與中大學生，再度發生衝突。治安當局防繼續出事，提早於十時戒嚴，學生無法行動，故死傷人數，迄未查明，惟知傷者多已送醫院，聞有學生十人，尚無下落，本日參加遊行之學生，有政大·中大·金大·金女大·劇專·建國·法商學院·東方語專等十一個學校。約計學生五千餘人。

此次因衝突受傷之學生由鼓樓醫院收容者計政大廿九日人，中大廿一人，建國十六人，聯勤總部一人，其中丁雍平·吳德義·方秉祥·左士傑·程履澤等五學生受重傷，生命垂危。至雙方死亡數字正調查中。

衛總發言人談衝突經過 5

〔本報南京一日電〕首都衛戌總部發言人政工處長羅春波，一日晚九時在該部招待首都中外記者卅餘人，首由政院發言人鮑靜安報告，謂何院長對本日京市大專學生因遊行與軍官總隊第三大隊官兵衝突事，除表示甚為遺憾並予以重視外，已令有關當局查明責任作公平處理。旋由羅處長報告衝突經過，詳情如下：（一）羅首謂此次學生遊行，衛總昨日下午卽接得情報，曾勸阻本京所有十一個專科以上學校，不必舉行，嗣經請示總統府及政院，奉指示應予勸導，如不聽，則聽其遊行，但須沿途保護，並注意維持其秩序；（二）本部接奉指示後，卽令飭各警憲加緊維持治安，計自今晨九時起，由各校集合於四牌樓之大專學生，漸增至四五千人，十一時起，卽由劇專為前導，整隊出發　，首至總統府請願，繼卽循國府路，沿主要大路，至白下路，沿途散發傳單，張貼標語，高呼口號，並由各校組成之宣傳隊，向道旁羣眾舉行個別宣傳，但截至二時，該遊行行列解散止，秩序井然，迄未發生任何意外。（三）至二時許，初未料衛總突接南區警局報告，謂遊行中一部學生於解散後，至大光路大中橋，意欲向軍官總隊第三大隊官兵（按該隊皆係於作戰時被共軍俘虜後歸來官兵）進行宣傳，該隊拒不之納，學生乃於該隊門首，遍貼標語，同時並散發傳單，軍官隊隊員乃動手撕其傳單標語，因此遂由口角而致動武互毆，衛總得南區警局報告後，經派員彈壓調解，並將雙方受傷者各數十人送醫院療治後，事態本已平息，詎學生方面於毆鬥發生後，有一部分別回校求援，重新集合者達二千餘人，軍官總隊方面為不願示弱，亦有增援，於是彼此報復，互毆又復開始，情形更烈。本部爰又派員前往調解。此時學生方面因衝突擴大後，遂有主張更赴總統府請願者，而軍官總隊亦卽隨後尾追，致至國府路時，乃又發生毆鬥，當時情形紊亂，衛總隨往彈壓，官佐亦有二人受傷。至此，總司令始不得不令調特務團參加鎮壓，並嚴令軍官總隊隊員如再不聽，就地格殺不論，當時由衛總逮捕之軍官隊隊員共凡八人。至八時始將軍官隊隊員集合歸隊，傷者送院，事態至此始告平息。（四）受傷人數據衛總統計，在大光路大光新村軍官總隊部第一次受重傷者計學生六，輕傷者六十六，共七十二人，第二次受重傷者五，輕傷者廿四，共廿九人。軍官總隊方面第一次受輕傷者四十八人，重傷及第二次受傷五。以上總計學生受重傷者十一，輕者一〇一，共一一二人，軍官總隊四十人，雙方總數為一六〇人。（五）事發後，衛總已將受傷者分送鼓樓中央兩醫院，輕傷者於代為包紮換藥後，分別護送回校，故自始自終，除軍官隊隊員七人迄尚在本部拘押中外，（另一人嫌疑犯格桑卻培（藏籍）已開釋）衛總從未逮捕任何學生。（六）總之，此次學生與收容總隊隊員發生衝突，全係一偶發事件，事先軍官收容總隊旣未知學生欲來宣傳，學生亦未事先通知該隊欲往宣傳，致遂因雙方對事情看法不同，一時因感情激動而發生衝突，至本案將來如何處理，當予多方詳加調查後，請求上峯辦理之。羅處長於報告終了後，最後並由該部拘押之七名軍官隊員章成方李培根等報告衝突經過亦甚詳盡。

滿臉笑容心思沉重　和談代表離京光景　送行親友多托帶美鈔袁頭 9

和談代表團一行，終於在這個西諺的扯謊日子（四月一日）裏，不扯謊的走了，趕到飛機塲送行的，除官塲人物而外，以民意代表，立法委員居多。

代表們到得最早也最受人注目的是老牌和平老人邵力子，他走到那裏，大家就包圍到那裏，一片笑聲，一陣擁擠，于右老為了找他，也走來走去。邵夫人要他不要走，他說：「有什麽關係，他們散步散步」，大家都鬨然大笑。

新聞記者包圍邵老，他是盡量的不停的走路，請他坐下來，他不肯，於是這個包圍圈就成了游擊隊，游來游去。有人說：「這是後輩包圍前輩」，因為邵老是老記者。

代表團出發人員的名單，總是秘而不宣，新聞記者到處採訪不得，只好求之於過鎊的名單，結果未卜先知，他們只用號碼代替，因此記者只好在體重上猜，如五十四公斤的，可能是章士釗，六十五公斤的可能是張治中等。

張羣一到機塲就向張治中問：「邵先生呢？邵先生呢」？等找到時，二張一邵連說：「我們有私話，有私話」，逃出記者的包圍圈，去開「三人小組會議」去了。

和談開始進行了 11

政府和談代表團一行六人，由「和平將軍」張治中氏率領，已於昨晨春光明媚之中，自京飛平，與中共代表團開始談判了。這次和談的進行，不僅是國共兩方的釋嫌修好，而且有關整個的國脈民命，尤其對於歷經戰禍的全國人民，不啻是黑暗中的一綫光明，售後全國人民能否脫離水火，而實現其幸福與愉快的生活，將視這次和談的結果如何而定。所以，全國人民，不論南北，不分男女，不問老幼，昨天莫不以迫切的心情，目送政府和談代表團的北飛，并殷懃祝望代表北上「只許成功，不許失敗」。我們站在人民的立場，乘此和談剛剛開始之際，提出幾點意見，以供國共雙方和談代表的參考。

第一，國共兩方的和談代表，必須站在人民的立場。我們對於「主權在民」或「人民世紀」之類的理論，雖不過分迷信，但相信人民的力量是偉大的，民意的歸趨是不可違抗的。任何政府如若脫離人民，不論其所擁有的武力如何強大，終有垮台的一天；任何政黨，不問其聲勢如何浩大，但若沒有民眾為基礎，一味迷信武力，戰爭到底，也是必歸於失敗的。這種歷史的教訓，史不絕書，不勝枚舉。此必為國共兩方的和談代表所諗知。記得民國十五六年國民黨北伐時，稱自己的軍隊為國民革命軍，今日中共也稱其軍隊為「人民解放軍」，「國民」與「人民」在字面上雖有差別，但其含義則一，如譯為英文，同為People。所以今日國共雙方進行和談，必須站在人民的立場。否則就要失去和談的意義。

第二，唯有在「人民的利益高於一切」的原則之下進行和談，和談始有光明的前途。國共兩方之間的悲歡離合，已有三十年的歷史，彼此之間的新仇舊恨擢髮難數，今日和談如仍不忘報復主義，堅持血的債要用血來償的話，則我們可以武斷地說，和談絕無前途可言。反之，雙方代表如能放棄小我的成見，而能從大處着眼，以人民的利益與國家的幸福為前提，則天下決沒有不可解除的仇恨，尤其是政黨與政黨之間的政爭，成功不能引以為榮，失敗也不必認為奇恥大辱。「成則為王，敗則為寇」的陳腐觀念，決不能容許它遺留於今日政黨政治的時代。我們特別要提醒雙方和談代表，國共雙方動輒以「為國為民」相標榜，人民早已聽夠了，誰真誰假，這次和談倒是一個絕好的試金石。今日全國人民在戰亂的煎熬之下，再也活不下去了，國家民族也已到了存亡絕續的開頭，局勢的嚴重甚於抗戰初期國共合作的時候，雙方如若還不能抱頭痛哭，釋嫌修好，團結一致，建設一個獨立·富強·康樂·自由的新中國，還說什麼「為國為民」！不論是那一方面必為人民所共棄。

乒乓拍的選擇　乒乓拍是拍乒乓的頂要緊的用具，也是唯一的用具。所以乒乓拍的選擇是應該十分注意的。

我們小時候用的乒乓拍，在拍的中部，有着黃荳大小的圓洞，多的有二三十個，少的也有十幾個。排列的形式，是很不一律的。這種拍多半是用杉木做的，質輕而缺乏彈性。現在因為拍中的洞旣沒有意義，又不美觀，已在被淘汰之列。

過去一般人用的乒乓拍，大多是用夾板做成，因夾板是用幾塊薄板，依板的紋路交叉着膠成的，所以很少受氣候的影響。再好一點的乒乓拍是用桐樹或是柳桉做的。質較軟而彈性亦强。有的更用軟木做柄，拍時十分舒適。硬球用乒乓拍，用的一面，上有有刺的橡皮。拍的式樣很不一致，以前最盛行的拍是橢圓形，拍柄作扁平式的；現在比較用熱水袋式圓形柄的居多了。拍的大小，形式，及所用材料，都可各隨自由，乒乓規則上并不限的。

那一種比較便利，須看各人的喜歡，不過依我的意思，拍的質地須輕，彈性須强，過分大是沒有好處的。

我國人慣說「力大如牛」，法國人愛說「力大如驢」，英美人則喜歡說「力大如熊」。事實上，熊的氣力確比牛，驢都大到十幾倍。據說，熊若舉掌一擊，牛驢的頭顱可被擊成稀爛。

Ping Pong Knowledge 101

— Written by Wang Xin Jing, Edited by Chen Linsheng (4)

Choosing a Ping Pong Paddle: The ping pong paddle is the most important tool for playing ping pong, and the only tool. Therefore, the selection of a ping pong paddle should be given careful consideration.

The ping pong paddles we used as children had small, round holes about the size of yellow beans in the middle, ranging from as many as twenty or thirty to as few as a dozen. The arrangement of these holes was very inconsistent. These paddles were mostly made of cedar wood, which is lightweight but lacks elasticity. Nowadays, because the holes in the paddle are both meaningless and unsightly, they are being phased out.

In the past, most ping pong paddles were made of plywood because plywood is made by gluing together several thin layers of wood with the grain running in different directions, so it is less affected by the weather. Better quality ping pong paddles are made of paulownia or Philippine mahogany. These woods are softer and more elastic. Some even use cork for the handle, making the paddle very comfortable to hold. Ping pong paddles for hard balls have a rubber surface with studs on one side. The styles of paddles vary greatly. The most popular paddle in the past was oval-shaped with a flat handle; nowadays, round handles resembling hot water bottles are more common. The size, shape, and materials used for the paddle are all up to personal preference and are not restricted by ping pong rules.

Which type is more convenient depends on personal preference, but in my opinion, the paddle should be lightweight and elastic, and being excessively large is not beneficial.

Linsheng's note: Since the introduction of hard ball competitions in this city from the 35th year of the Republic, most players have switched to using rubber paddles. Wooden paddles have also been largely phased out. However, due to economic reasons, wooden paddles are still used by many primary and secondary school students.

(2) The History of Ping Pong in China

The success of any endeavor depends on continuously identifying shortcomings, gradually improving, and ultimately achieving a more perfect state.

Ping pong is no exception.

Ping pong was invented based on the principles of tennis. At the time, there were no fixed standards for the size, weight, color, or hardness of the ball. These were gradually improved until a standard was established. However, due to individual preferences, ping pong balls were broadly divided into soft balls and hard balls. Ten years ago, soft balls were popular in both China and Japan. Because international ping pong competitions now use hard balls, the Chinese ping pong community has been making great efforts to promote hard balls in the past five or six years. Unfortunately, there are too few outstanding talents. If we want to occupy a place on the world ping pong stage in the future, we must pay special attention to hard ball techniques.

In the past, China relied entirely on foreign countries for its ping pong balls. At that time, ping pong was not yet well-developed, and the sales of balls were limited, resulting in little loss. After China and Japan signed an agreement for international competitions in the 12th year of the Republic, ping pong became increasingly popular, and the sales of ping pong balls gradually increased. At the same time, the outflow of money also increased day by day. We are undoubtedly very pleased with the development of ping pong, but we must strive to reverse the outflow of money.

In the 15th year of the Republic, the China Ping Pong Company, with the aim of recovering rights and promoting ping pong, diligently researched and developed the Lianhuan brand of standard ping pong balls. Black Bears of North America 105

— Fang Zhou

We Chinese often say "as strong as an ox," the French like to say "as strong as a donkey," while the British and Americans prefer to say "as strong as a bear." In fact, the strength of a bear is indeed ten times greater than that of oxen and donkeys. It is said that if a bear strikes with its paw, the heads of oxen and donkeys can be smashed to pieces.

The forests of Alaska, Allison La, and Florida in North America are active areas for bears. Their fur is mostly black, with a few being cinnamon, coffee, or light brown. Regardless of the color, North American residents call them "black bears."

Black bears can weigh up to four hundred pounds and stand three feet tall. They can stand upright like humans, at which time their height is six feet. In short distances, they can travel thirty miles per hour. Speaking of their strength, they can be said to be unmatched among animals except for elephants. They can pull three hundred pounds of wood and uproot twenty-foot-tall oak trees. Once, a black bear in Florida grabbed a one hundred and fifty-pound pig with its forefoot and ran nearly a mile before abandoning its prey when farmers chased after it in groups.

Black bears eat everything: fruits, roots, insects, fish, and any animal they can catch. They especially like to eat honey and ants. Telephone lines are often damaged by them because they mistake the "buzzing" sound of the telephone lines for bees, so they often climb up telephone poles and tear down the wires. Speaking of the black bear's ability to climb trees, it is a skill they have trained since childhood. A cub less than three months old likes to climb low trees, and only jumps to the ground when the branch droops under its weight. Black bears are also quite clever at catching ants. They dig out ant nests, extend their forefoot, and let angry ants crawl all over it before licking it clean.

Every year from November to April, is the "hibernation" period for black bears. During this time, they hibernate in rock caves or large tree holes, without eating or moving, and their heart rate and breathing become particularly slow. Female bears give birth during this period, producing two or three cubs per litter, each weighing less than one pound, blind and hairless, and only able to leave the cave to play after a month of hibernation. "Hibernation" is a resting period for black bears, but it is also a period of misfortune for them, because hunters often use this time to catch them, and they have no choice but to submit.

Many black bears are kept in American national parks, and they are very tame. It is said that they love to have their picture taken. When tourists raise their cameras, they will pose in the most beautiful and majestic poses, allowing tourists to capture them in the lens.

Epic Poetry of the Late Ming Dynasty 106

— Fan Shouwu

In this chaotic world, it is easiest to evoke people's thoughts of historical relics. On a cold spring night, under the flickering light of a lamp, I happened to think of Huang Chunyao, a heroic warrior of the late Ming Dynasty, and the three poems he wrote on the theme of "Wild Man."

Chunyao, styled Yunsheng, was from Jiading in the Ming Dynasty. He was a Jinshi graduate in the Guiwei year of Chongzhen (Guiwei is the sixteenth year of Chongzhen, when Li Zicheng captured Beijing and the emperor hanged himself). At that time, the bandits were rampant, the politics of the court were corrupt, there was famine in the world, and the people were filled with resentment. Chunyao was heartbroken by what he saw, resigned and returned to his hometown, organized a local militia, and called on the people of his hometown to prepare to protect the country and their villages. Unfortunately, the general trend was gone, and it was impossible to prop up the crumbling building with one hand. Therefore, he could only express his sorrow for the times and his feelings in poetry. The "Wild Man" poems, which have been passed down for thousands of years, are the product of this kind of emotion. The poems say:

The wild man sighs at the hardship of the royal army, the Qin bandits and Chu bandits are like hedgehog hairs, they attack cities and plunder the countryside, officials die, and the people north of the Yangtze River wail. Yesterday I heard that dead thieves robbed wealth and distributed it to the officers as bribes, chopped off the heads of the people and hung them on tall trees, and when dawn came, the thieves were gone.

The wild man sighs at the bad year, digging wells in the ponds, and the bottom of the wells are dry, locusts lead their young to cover the sky, and it is useless to squander one's family's wealth on farming. The court adds levies from time to time, and complaining to the officials only earns a shake of the head, meeting the clerks on a narrow road, soft fur coats, fast horses, demanding money.

The wild man sighs that there is no one in the court, and the Ming Party in the court is like fish scales, ten officials are summoned, and nine remain silent, crawling and barely accommodating themselves. Who in the court manages yin and yang, frequent solar eclipses in recent years have devastated the four seas, I want to write a letter to ask the courtiers, but I am afraid that people will rebuke me as a foolish man.

The original poems can be found in the "Ming Poetry Collection," selected by Shen Deqian, who commented after the poems: "The general situation of the court and the countryside is such that even if one wants not to perish, how can it be possible?" History is a ruthless mirror, and the words in the poems are still worthy of our vigilance three hundred years later.

On Cannibalism 107

— Qian Dacheng

In ancient times, it was customary for people to eat people. The Spring and Autumn Annals of Zuo even mentioned the phrase "eat flesh and sleep on skin," which may have been an exaggeration by Zuo Qiuming? However, there are indeed many historical records of cannibalism. Even children know about Ma Shumou eating people. After a group of loyal ministers and filial sons personally killed their enemies, they would routinely eat their flesh raw. Not only would people not say they were barbaric, but they would praise their loyalty and filial piety? It is said that even now, some savage tribes eat their parents as a sign of filial piety. In short, whether eating enemies or eating parents, eating people is always considered a form of filial piety, no wonder Lu Xun said that the entire history is just two big words: "cannibalism," but this literary advocate of hatred only knew that cannibalism was malicious, but I know that cannibalism also has good intentions. Cannibalism is detestable, but sometimes it can also be commendable.

There are many ways to eat people, but the most wonderful is the following record in the "Chicken Rib Compilation":

"In the early Tang Dynasty, the bandit Zhu Can used people as food, saying that eating drunk people was like eating pickled pork. ... And since the year of Bingwu in the Jingkang era (of the Song Dynasty), when the Jin barbarians invaded China, ... bandits, soldiers, and even residents ate each other, and the price of meat was cheaper than dogs and pigs ... old and thin men were called "burning fire," young and beautiful women were called "enviable sheep," and children were called "rotting with bones," and they were all commonly called "two-legged sheep." ..."

Please don't say they are cruel, they are based on scripture. Didn't the Zuo Zhuan say? "It's like birds and beasts, the ministers eat their flesh and sleep on their skin!" They are not eating people, they are eating birds and beasts!

I have never eaten human flesh, because I am not a loyal minister or filial son, nor am I a savage. But a retired military officer once told me that he had eaten human heart and liver. It is said that the taste is similar to pig liver; at first, he didn't know he was eating human flesh, so he ate it with relish, but after he found out, he vomited, and people scolded him for being too timid, saying that he was not worthy to eat human flesh. When I met him, he was in dire straits, and perhaps his lack of success in life was because he couldn't get used to eating human flesh?

Since ancient times, filial sons and daughters have cut flesh from their thighs to cure their parents' illnesses, which is an evolution of the way of eating human flesh and can be considered a national essence.

Cannibalism! Cannibalism! There are many patterns, and the motives are different. Some people eat people, and some people are willing to be eaten. The knights in "Han Feizi" even treat guests with their own flesh, which is even more of a heroic act.

Someday, when I have time, I will write a detailed article on the topic of "cannibalism," but for now, let's leave it at that.
