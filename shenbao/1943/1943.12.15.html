從開羅到德黑蘭—國際問題答客問

問：從美英渝的開羅會議到美蘇英的德黑蘭會議分別發表其宣言以後，頗引起一般人士的議論，這兩個會議，究竟有何意義？

答：從本質上看，這兩個會議，是完全一致的，不過從世界實際形勢看，却叉各不相同。所謂一致，是說在目前世界戰爭新階段，由於軸心國各方面的新發展，以及歐亞戰局之推移，使反軸心各國對於作戰方略以及彼此間政治問題，都有取得協調的必要，這是一致的要求。所謂不同，即是美蘇英渝間具有不同的企圖與地位，甚且相互間尙有許多不易解決的矛盾，重慶無法參加美蘇英會議，而蘇聯亦有不得不避免參加美英渝會議的苦衷。這裏最主要的一點，是日蘇關係之微妙的存在，和蘇渝之間的矛盾。誰都知道，斯太林是今日著名的現實主義者，他決不肯遷就正在鬧着渝共磨擦的重慶政權，他更不能不顧到違反日蘇中立條約的嚴重的後果。

問：然則，從開羅與德黑蘭會議之分別舉行一事，巳可看出反軸心國間最大的矛盾尙未消除了·但是，羅邱蔣會議的結果，巳經發表宜言，請問開羅會議的結果與趨向如何？

答：開　會議歷時五日，一共討論三個問題：一是軍事問題，二是戰爭目的問題，三是侵奪日本殖民地問題。然而　個會議又有羅　將會議與羅　伊（土耳其總統伊諾奴）會議之別。後者討論的中心在前者討論之中心在太平洋問題，完全爲英美防止渝將「動搖」而設法拉攏的一種手　。實際上渝蔣所得的還只是空頭支票，他不能不承認反軸心的世界戰略「繼續以對　爲第一」的事實。所以，渝蔣此行，帶回了些什麽。確是一大疑問。

問：那麼，開羅會議實際上不過美英兩國神經術的新發動而已，看不見　　意義。

答：誠然。但看宣言中關於南洋諸國的問題，竟保證馬來，婆羅洲，緬甸等仍為「英屬」，束印度仍為「荷屬」，菲律賓仍為「美屬」，這些巳在日本協助下解放獨立的南方新國，羅邱蔣會議竟企圖使他們重新陷入帝國主義舊秩序統治之下，更可看出開羅會議的眞面目了。

問：在東亞民族門爭的崗位上，我們對於開羅會議宣言自然只有憎惡的成分了。可是，德黑蘭會議又如何？

答：羅邱史會議，喧傳已久，上次莫斯科三國外長會議，各方面似乎頗加重視，我們就巳指出：這不過是為羅邱史會議的準備而巳，可是，結果給人的印象，却只是「虎頭蛇尾」四字。從這次會議的任務說·一方面是繼續莫斯科會議的原則，進而確定共同對德的戰略方針，另一方面是由三巨頭親自商定戰後的國際對策方針。現在除了宜言「從北，東，南三方向德國合攻」之外，只不過發表了一篇關於伊朗問題宣言，無怪美國與論，對德黑蘭會議，幾乎一致表示失望，而蘇聯方面，也認為有繼續舉行二次三次會議之『絕對必要」了。

問：如此看來，開羅會議和德黑蘭會議只是反軸心國內部矛盾發展與國際局勢演變中的兩齣挿劇，根本不値得重視了？

答：那也並不盡然·從開羅到德黑蘭會議的舉行及其結果，多少已經顯示反軸心詣國對於目前戰局之重視，相互間某種程度的了解，和對中立國進行新的誘脅的企圖，因此，不問為歐洲或為東亞，都應認淸這一事實，團結一致，强化自巳的戰力，以保衛民族解放及新秩序的推進。

From Cairo to Tehran—International Questions and Answers

Question: Since the Cairo Conference of the United States, Britain, and Chongqing and the Tehran Conference of the United States, the Soviet Union, and Britain each issued their declarations, they have aroused considerable discussion among the general public. What is the significance of these two conferences?

Answer: In essence, these two conferences are entirely consistent. However, from the perspective of the actual world situation, they are quite different. The consistency lies in the fact that in the new stage of the current world war, due to the new developments on all fronts of the Axis powers, and the evolution of the war situation in Europe and Asia, it is necessary for the anti-Axis countries to achieve coordination in their war strategies and political issues between each other. This is a consistent requirement. The differences lie in the fact that the United States, the Soviet Union, Britain, and Chongqing have different intentions and positions, and even have many difficult-to-resolve contradictions between them. Chongqing could not participate in the US-Soviet-British conference, and the Soviet Union had the dilemma of having to avoid participating in the US-British-Chongqing conference. The most important point here is the subtle existence of Japanese-Soviet relations and the contradictions between the Soviet Union and Chongqing. Everyone knows that Stalin is a famous realist today. He will never accommodate the Chongqing regime that is currently embroiled in friction with the Communists, and he cannot ignore the serious consequences of violating the Japanese-Soviet Neutrality Pact.

Question: In that case, the fact that the Cairo and Tehran conferences were held separately indicates that the greatest contradiction among the anti-Axis countries has not yet been eliminated. However, the results of the Roosevelt-Churchill-Chiang conference have already been announced. What are the results and trends of the Cairo Conference?

Answer: The Cairo Conference lasted five days and discussed three issues: first, military issues; second, the purpose of the war; and third, the issue of seizing Japanese colonies. However, the Cairo Conference also had the distinction between the Roosevelt-Chiang conference and the Roosevelt-Inonu (Turkish President Inonu) conference. The latter's discussion focused on the Pacific issue, while the former's discussion was entirely a method for Britain and the United States to prevent Chiang from "wavering" and to win him over. In fact, what Chiang Kai-shek got was only empty promises. He could not but admit the fact that the anti-Axis world strategy "continues to prioritize dealing with Germany". Therefore, what did Chiang Kai-shek bring back from this trip? This is indeed a big question.

Question: So, the Cairo Conference was actually just a new development of the United States and Britain's psychological warfare, and it doesn't seem to have any significance.

Answer: Indeed. But looking at the issues concerning the countries of Southeast Asia in the declaration, it guarantees that Malaya, Borneo, Burma, etc. will remain "British", the Dutch East Indies will remain "Dutch", and the Philippines will remain "American". These new countries in the South that have been liberated and made independent with the help of Japan, the Roosevelt-Churchill-Chiang conference actually attempted to make them fall back under the rule of the old imperialist order, which shows the true face of the Cairo Conference even more clearly.

Question: Standing on the position of the East Asian nations' struggle, we naturally only have hatred for the Cairo Conference declaration. But what about the Tehran Conference?

Answer: The Roosevelt-Churchill-Stalin Conference had been publicized for a long time. The previous Moscow Conference of the Three Foreign Ministers seemed to be taken quite seriously by all parties. We had already pointed out that this was just a preparation for the Roosevelt-Churchill-Stalin Conference. However, the result gave people the impression of just being a "big fanfare for nothing". From the perspective of the tasks of this conference, on the one hand, it was to continue the principles of the Moscow Conference and further determine the common strategic policy against Germany. On the other hand, it was for the three giants to personally discuss the postwar international countermeasures policy. Now, apart from declaring to "attack Germany jointly from the north, east, and south," only a declaration on the Iranian issue was issued. No wonder American public opinion almost unanimously expressed disappointment with the Tehran Conference, and the Soviet Union also believed that it was "absolutely necessary" to hold a second or third conference.

Question: In this case, the Cairo Conference and the Tehran Conference are just two interludes in the development of internal contradictions and the evolution of the international situation within the anti-Axis camp, and they are not worth paying attention to at all?

Answer: That's not entirely true. The holding of the Cairo to Tehran Conferences and their results have more or less shown the anti-Axis countries' emphasis on the current war situation, a certain degree of mutual understanding, and attempts to carry out new inducements and threats against neutral countries. Therefore, whether it is for Europe or for East Asia, we should recognize this fact, unite as one, strengthen our own combat power, to defend national liberation and the promotion of the new order.
