近郊通訊

七寳鎭槪况

— 陸愼廷

七寶為上海西南之一市鎭，一度亦為上海食米之一大來源地，該築距徐家滙二十四里，題吳淞約七十餘里。東與漕河涇為界，南與舊上海縣為界，西南為松江縣界，西北為　　縣界，北與蒲松縣為界。境內有河流「蒲滙塘」横貫其中，恰將鎭市劃分為南北兩部。塘上有大石橋，名日「塘橋」，為鎭上塘南塘北之鏈鎖；因居全鎭中心，故為最繁榮之區域。鎭上居民約萬人。

交通

鎖上無重要交通可言，僅南大　，北大街，東街，西街，典當街等石塊路數條而已。鎭外之交通，則有陸路水路可分：陸路可通卡車，小汽車，人大車，三輪軍，自由車等。東有公路通程家橋漕河涇，可達上海。南有公路通莘莊，可搭滬杭鐵路之火車，西北公路有長途汽車，可達靑浦縣城。水路則可航各種民船，沿蒲滙塘而西，可逹泗涇，繞道又通靑浦松江。沿蒲匯港而東，可達上海，但因年久失修，幫於徐家滙處淤塞，　成死水，臭不可聞。惟蘇州河及南黄浦璽道尚通。

政治

塘南屬松江縣政府統轄，塘北屬靑浦縣政府統轄，各有區公所鎭公所等機關。區公所內有區長一人，係縣政府委派；助理員兩名，所員十條人，均由區長聘請，共同治理區政。鎭公所內有鎭長一人，係當地人民公推才高德望者任之，為人民排解糾紛。此外有土地局，錢糧局，煙酒稅局等收機關，約七八處，亦有警察局維持治安。

Suburban Correspondence

General Conditions of Qibao Town

— Lu Shenting

Qibao is a town located southwest of Shanghai, and it was once a major source of rice for Shanghai. It is situated 24 li from Xujiahui and approximately 70+ li from Wusong. It is bordered by Caohejing to the east, the old Shanghai County to the south, Songjiang County to the southwest, 　　County to the northwest, and Pu Song County to the north. The river "Puhuitang" runs horizontally through the territory, dividing the town into northern and southern parts. There is a large stone bridge on the river, named "Tang Bridge," which serves as the link between the northern and southern parts of the town. As it is located in the center of the town, it is the most prosperous area. The town has a population of approximately 10,000 people.

Transportation

There is no significant transportation within the town, only several stone roads such as South Street, North Street, East Street, West Street, and Pawnshop Street. Outside the town, transportation is divided into land and water routes: Land routes are accessible by trucks, cars, rickshaws, tricycles, and bicycles. To the east, a highway leads to Chenjiaqiao and Caohejing, providing access to Shanghai. To the south, a highway leads to Xinzhuang, where one can take the Shanghai-Hangzhou Railway. To the northwest, a highway provides long-distance bus service to Qingpu County. Water routes allow various types of civilian boats to navigate west along the Puhuitang River, reaching Sijing and, via a detour, Qingpu and Songjiang. Navigation eastward along the Puhuitang River leads to Shanghai, but due to years of disrepair, the river is silted up near Xujiahui, resulting in stagnant and foul-smelling water. Only the Suzhou River and the South Huangpu waterways remain navigable.

Politics

The southern part of Tang Bridge is under the jurisdiction of the Songjiang County government, while the northern part is under the jurisdiction of the Qingpu County government. Each has district and town offices. The district office is headed by a district chief, appointed by the county government, and assisted by two assistant staff members and ten office workers, all hired by the district chief, who jointly administer district affairs. The town office is headed by a town chief, who is selected by the local people for their talent and integrity, and is responsible for resolving disputes among the people. In addition, there are about seven or eight collecting agencies, such as the Land Bureau, Grain Bureau, and Tobacco and Alcohol Tax Bureau, as well as a police station to maintain law and order.

Products

The products of Qibao Town and its vicinity are mainly agricultural crops, with rice, cotton, and soybeans being the most important. Other crops, such as wheat, broad beans, corn, and vegetables, are also produced in considerable quantities. Fruits include watermelons, cantaloupes, and honey peaches. In addition, residents also raise a considerable number of pigs, sheep, geese, chickens, and ducks. Aquatic products include crucian carp, carp, shrimp, crabs, and eels. Special products include spiced dried broad beans, milk ice, and clams.

Industry and Commerce

Commerce is centered around Tang Bridge, with various vegetable stalls, snack shops, and general merchandise stalls on the bridge. Tea houses, hotels, restaurants, flour mills, rice shops, butcher shops, tofu shops, clothing stores, department stores, cigarette and paper shops, candy stores, and barbershops are located on the north and south sides of the bridge. Due to the lack of electricity, it is not possible to install motors, so only light industries such as old-fashioned straw paper mills, iron shops, coppersmith shops, incense making workshops, wineries, cotton ginning factories, oil presses, and woodworking shops exist. The market is doing fairly well, but is not extremely prosperous.

Education

Next to the big temple on the north side of Tang Bridge is the county-run central elementary school, with seven classrooms and over 300 students. Principal Lu Kangchang works hard to teach, with considerable success. However, the county government is severely short of funds, so teachers' salaries are not only low, but also often delayed. To the west of Pawnshop Street in Tangnan is the San Shan Tang Elementary School, whose principal is the local celebrity Xu Yonglin. This school is a charitable organization with its own property, so teachers' salaries are very good. Unfortunately, the number of students is small, only about 100. In addition, there is also a Catholic Elementary School, with more than 100 students, divided into male and female schools, completely separate, and presided over by Father Ma. The teachers in this school are all "fathers" and "mothers," and the teaching and management are quite strict, with student achievement exceeding that of ordinary schools in Shanghai. The school buildings are spacious, and the location is quiet, with a very good environment.

Combining the above three schools, there are only five to six hundred elementary school students, which is approximately one-twentieth to one-thirtieth of the total population. The opportunity to receive education is scarce, which can be seen in general, as parents have historically not valued sending their children to school.

Folk Customs

Most of the local people are farmers, with a small number going out to do business, so they are mostly honest and simple in their ways of thinking and frugal in their lives. Men have a habit of visiting tea houses and eating mutton and drinking liquor every morning before work, such as carrying rice and loading goods, or even crawling through barbed wire.

The people's lives are relatively stable, hence the saying: "Live in a thatched cottage without worry, eating aged rice." In recent years, influenced by Shanghai's "evil slaves' civilization," drugs are prevalent and gambling is rampant. A large number of people smoke opium and heroin, and mahjong and pai gow are everywhere. It is heard that the authorities are strictly cracking down.

Eighty to ninety percent of the people believe in Buddhism. They do not know the true meaning of Buddhism, but only the form of worship. There are "Guandi Temple," "Land God Temple," "Jiuli Temple," "South Temple," etc., and there are also frequent gatherings of the "Guanyin Society" to eat vegetarian food and pray. In addition, there are also the "Qibao Catholic Church" and the "Gu Jia Catholic Church," both built by the French before the war, but there are few believers.

Every year on the fifteenth day of the third month, there is a "market" at the big temple square in Tangbei. Merchants from all directions gather here to sell bamboo utensils, ironware, and all kinds of useful items. People from all over come from thousands of miles away, and there are also Beijing opera, animal shows, magic shows, and martial arts performances for entertainment. Once this time has passed, all kinds of entertainment cease, and at most there are storytellers and blind singers.

If Shanghai citizens develop this place, work hard to build it, and promote education and reform politics, then its future prosperity and development can be expected in the near future.
