### Prospects for the Pacific War

Due to the enemy-American invasion of Manila and Iwo Jima, the recent Pacific war situation has become particularly tense. Some people who do not fully understand military common sense and the overall situation cannot help but rashly judge the outcome of the war, but in reality, it is not as simple as imagined.

If we observe the enemy-American war results already obtained, although they have occupied Manila, the Japanese troops on Luzon Island still maintain considerable strength. The purpose of war is to eliminate the enemy's fighting power, not the gains and losses of a single city or place. Therefore, the main battle on Luzon is still in its preliminary stages. If we observe the enemy-American purpose of advancing on Luzon and Iwo Jima, one is to prevent Japan from obtaining resources from the South Seas, and the other is to use the "island-hopping" strategy to advance to the next target: the Chinese mainland and the Japanese mainland. In terms of the former, the aim of cutting off Japan's access to resources cannot be effectively achieved until the enemy-Americans have occupied all the islands in the South Seas. In terms of the latter, due to the recent smooth progress of the Japanese army's offensive on the mainland and the continued stability of Taiwan, even if the battles on Luzon and Iwo Jima come to an end, the future battles will not allow the enemy-Americans to be optimistic.

Why do we say this? The further the enemy-Americans advance, the wider the scope of operations, and the more difficult and complex the war becomes. Recently, U.S. Major General Howard William said in the American newspaper *Daily Telegram*: "So far, the U.S. military's island-hopping strategy of concentrating its forces to attack Japan's weaknesses has been smooth, but this type of operation becomes less effective the closer it gets to the Japanese mainland. When the radius of the circle of internal operations shrinks, the power of counterattack and counter-offensive will be greatly enhanced. Japan will use this point to make the U.S. military struggle to cope in various places in the future, and will no longer be able to concentrate its forces."

Secondly, the U.S. military's transportation capacity will also encounter difficulties in the future. The result of the offensive is to expand the scope of the war, which means increasing the distance of transportation. In the past, if the enemy-Americans wanted to maintain the same strength in the Pacific as in the European theater, the ships required in the Pacific were three times those in Europe. No matter how high the estimated production capacity of American ships is, it will never be able to keep up with the needs of military consumption. Today, when considering both the Eastern and Western theaters, the enemy-Americans will either lose one or both. The enemy-Americans currently have only 30 million tons of usable ships, and more than half have already been used in the Pacific, which has resulted in a stalemate on the Western Front in Europe. According to American newspapers, this has caused serious protests from the British government and the Tagore regime, forcing the enemy-Americans to conclude an agreement with Britain, when British Deputy Foreign Secretary Richard Law visited Washington, to supply the ships needed to maintain the European offensive in the future. From this point of view, it is virtually impossible for the U.S. military to maintain its offensive in the Pacific.

Furthermore, the steps of the anti-Axis powers in the Pacific theater are inconsistent, even full of flaws. Although enemy-Britain has tried its best to meet the needs of enemy-America in the Pacific, more than half of its Pacific fleet consists of aircraft carriers, used to make up for the excessive consumption of the U.S. Navy, but in reality, it is very difficult. As British Pacific Fleet Commander Fraser said on January 14th of this year: "The American military in European waters follows British methods, and we also want to match American methods in the Pacific, but there are various difficulties in the joint operations of the British and American navies." To avoid responsibility for inconsistent steps, British naval representative to the U.S. Somerville also declared on January 20th: "The British Pacific Fleet is subordinate to U.S. Chief of Naval Operations King, not Nimitz. Therefore, we have no direct relationship with Nimitz's attacks on Japan." On the other hand, the contradictions between Chongqing and the U.S. in the China theater have already been reflected in the previous Stilwell incident and need not be elaborated on.

Not only are the contradictions between the anti-Axis powers profound, but even within the enemy-America, opinions are divided regarding the invasion of Japan. The Nimitz method of landing in China and the Macworth method of avoiding a prolonged struggle with Japan on the mainland and wanting to make full use of the superior anti-Axis naval power are still being debated in the enemy-American newspapers. We can observe from the enemy-Americans' prudence in their offensive methods that they are not confident about future operations. We can also deduce the future of the Pacific War from this inconsistent pace of the anti-Axis powers and the debates within the enemy-America.

In short, the Pacific war situation is indeed extremely serious, but from the overall situation, it is not that it is not becoming more and more favorable to us day by day. When we are in difficulty, the enemy's difficulties are even greater. How to endure this temporary difficulty, fully contribute our war effort, and firmly believe in victory is the common responsibility of the East Asian peoples, and it is also a necessary condition for winning victory.

### An Exercise in Futility on Paper by England, America and the Soviet Union: Full Text of the Crimea Conference Communiqué

Since February 4th, the leaders of England, America, and the Soviet Union have been holding continuous talks in Yalta, Crimea. The conference officially concluded on February 12th. Subsequently, Churchill, Roosevelt, and Stalin jointly issued a communiqué. According to Reuters, the full text is as follows:

(1) Elimination of Germany

Our joint military plans will only be made public when they are put into practice. However, we believe that the close military cooperation achieved at this conference between the general staffs of the three countries will contribute to an early end to the war. Separate meetings of the general staffs of the three countries will continue as necessary.

(2) Division and Administration

After the armed resistance of Germany is thoroughly crushed, we will order the unconditional surrender of Nazi Germany. We have agreed on the common policies and plans required to enforce unconditional surrender. These provisions will not be published until Germany has been completely defeated. According to the agreed plan, the armed forces of the three countries will occupy specific regions of Germany. We have discussed the issue of losses suffered by the United Nations due to this war caused by Germany, and we believe that the only reasonable solution is to compel Germany to compensate for these losses to the greatest extent possible.

A commission for the compensation of losses will be established and entrusted with reviewing the scope and capacity of German reparations to the United Nations. The commission will be located in Moscow.

We have decided to convene a worldwide international organization with our allies in the near future to maintain peace and security. We believe that this organization is extremely important because it may prevent aggression and eradicate the political, economic, and social causes of war through close and continuous cooperation of peace-loving people around the world.

The Dumbarton Oaks Conference laid the foundation. However, the most important issue, such as voting procedures, was not decided at that time. This conference was able to resolve this difficulty. We have drafted and signed the Declaration on Liberated Europe. This declaration stipulates the political agreements of the three countries, as well as actions to jointly resolve the political and economic problems of liberated Europe based on democratic principles. The original text of the declaration is as follows:

To foster the conditions in which the liberated peoples may exercise these rights, the three governments will jointly assist the people in any European liberated state, or in any former European Axis satellite state where in their judgment conditions require, to establish (a) conditions of internal peace; (b) to carry out emergency measures for the relief of distressed peoples; (c) to establish interim governmental authorities broadly representative of all democratic elements in the population, and pledged to the earliest possible establishment through free elections of governments responsive to the will of the people; and (d) where necessary, to facilitate elections.

### Property Transactions Quiet, Prices Decline

The Shanghai property market experienced very active trading in the second half of last year due to an abundance of idle capital and rampant speculation in various industries. By the end of December last year, prices had increased to about nine or ten times compared to the same month the previous year. However, since the market reopened this spring, business for various companies has suddenly become quiet, and land prices in various districts have clearly declined. It is understood that the main reasons are: (1) There are many unfounded rumors in the market, and both buyers and sellers are mostly taking a wait-and-see attitude, with very few transactions, so prices have tended to fall back. (2) Property transactions are mostly priced in gold, and this spring, gold prices have soared, increasing by about more than double compared to last year. Sellers are holding onto their properties and waiting for an opportunity to sell. It is said that if gold prices do not fall back in the future, there will be no possibility of particularly booming property transactions.

### Commemorating the "March 3rd" Friends' Day - By Huang Jingwan

In the 23rd year of the Republic (1934), on March 3rd, I, along with six good friends from various provinces in Shanghai, initiated Friends' Day at the Morning Geng Village Work-Study Group in West Shanghai. Since then, every year on this day, I have invited good friends to talk, discuss, and celebrate in remembrance. This year is the eleventh Friends' Day. At this time, when things are more difficult and people's lives are even more distressed, I am unable to hold a banquet. I will just write a folksong to bring joy to all of you gentlemen. The lyrics are as follows:

Friends' Day Song: I propose Friends' Day, set for March 3rd. People are the spirit of all things, friendship is one of the five relationships. The world is in a great war, facing an unprecedented catastrophe. A scene of wailing, hearing it makes my heart sad. Thinking of life and death, it is easy to get along in this world. Sincerity and love are important, and goodness should be widely spread. Humanity values mutual assistance, good friends are trustworthy. Faithfulness and righteousness are long-lasting, never forgetting promises made in life. Every year on this day, plan a heated gathering. I only wish that my compatriots will always be good friends and never change.
