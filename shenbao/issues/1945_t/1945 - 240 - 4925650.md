### "Distant Friendship, Close Kinship" Policy Achieves Preliminary Success; President Sun Discusses the Sino-Soviet Treaty

Central News Agency, Chongqing, 27th - After the Sino-Soviet Treaty of Friendship and Alliance was approved by the Legislative Yuan, a Central News Agency reporter requested an interview with President Sun Ke, but due to time constraints, a thorough discussion could not be held. The reporter then visited him again on the 26th. Sun first stated: "The signing of the Sino-Soviet Treaty of Friendship and Alliance can be called an epoch-making innovation in Sino-Soviet diplomacy." Sun then described the diplomatic relations between China and the Soviet Union over the past twenty-two years and said,

"Far Eastern Peace is Guaranteed."

"The greatest significance of this treaty is to guarantee peace in the Far East and prevent Japan from ever launching aggression again within thirty years. This kind of regional security system is a double guarantee for peace in the Far East under the auspices of the United Nations. It is necessary to use the peace period to lay the foundation for national construction and create a truly strong country so that peace in the Far East can last forever." The reporter inquired about the impact of the establishment of the treaty on domestic unity. Sun replied with a pleasant tone, "This treaty has a great relationship to promoting our country's unification."

"Promote Unification and Establish a Buffer Zone. I believe that domestic unity problems can make a sharp turn for the better from now on. The treaty clearly stipulates that the Soviet Union's aid to us, both morally and materially, is related to the National Government that is recognized by all of our allies, meaning that it is not related to local governments and other regimes not recognized by the central government, and will never interfere in our domestic affairs. As for the Outer Mongolian issue, our country recognizes its independence, which is both necessary in fact and in the spirit of Sun Yat-sen's Three Principles of the People. The Outer Mongolian independence has reached 25 years of history, people's lives are improving everyday, our country should recognize it, and establish normal diplomatic relations. This is a buffer zone that benefits both China and the Soviet Union. As for the provisions regarding the Chinese Eastern Railway and Lushun, they are also based on the alliance relationship, which ensures that the Soviet Union and China jointly watch over Japan, preventing it from rising again and ensuring the permanent establishment of peace in the Far East. Indeed, it has this need. In short, the establishment of the Sino-Soviet treaty is most severely affected by Japan. In the future, peace in the Far East can be guaranteed, the foundation for national construction can be laid, and it can be called a preliminary success for our 'distant friendship, close kinship' foreign policy."
