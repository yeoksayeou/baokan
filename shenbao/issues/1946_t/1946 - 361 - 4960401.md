### A Major Post-War Issue: Repatriation of Japanese Soldiers and Civilians

The three million people from the Chinese mainland have already been repatriated. In addition, there are still one million people who were originally in the Northeast. Due to the complexity of the national situation, the fate of these Japanese is still uncertain.

Repatriating millions of Japanese prisoners of war and expatriates from China to Japan is indeed a huge and difficult task, but with the cooperation of China and the United States, it can be said that it has been "successfully completed."

On the day the Japanese Emperor announced the unconditional surrender edict, the day the Japanese army laid down their weapons, was also the day the World War and the Sino-Japanese War officially ended. There were four million Japanese soldiers and expatriates in China, but after a year of efforts, three million of them have returned to their homeland. The remaining one million were originally in Manchuria (the Nine Northeastern Provinces). Due to the unfathomable Soviet policy, the repatriation of these one million people cannot be resolved for the time being, and the United States also feels powerless.

Larns, a special correspondent for "World Report Weekly," recently issued a report from Shanghai, explaining the details of the repatriation of Japanese soldiers and civilians very clearly. The main points are translated below.

The repatriation of Japanese soldiers and civilians began in December of last year with Sino-US cooperation. The method was to systematically disarm them first and gradually concentrate them. However, because China is vast and the Japanese soldiers and civilians are widely distributed, it had to be carried out in different regions. At that time, it was decided to deal with South China, Taiwan, and Vietnam first. In more than three months, a total of more than 600,000 Japanese were repatriated. The next was Central China, with a total of more than 800,000 Japanese repatriated. When the repatriation work was underway, a noteworthy conclusion was reached regarding the extent to which Japanese expatriates had penetrated into China: the ratio of Japanese soldiers to expatriates was three to two. In other words, for every three Japanese soldiers repatriated, two civilians were repatriated. The progress of the repatriation of Japanese soldiers had a dual impact on Japan itself: on the one hand, there was a general sense of comfort, feeling that nationals scattered in various places had finally returned; on the other hand, it caused various economic worries.

On the positive side, many families were able to reunite, surplus manpower could be applied to production work, and the government would not be at a loss when drafting reconstruction plans. This raised the prestige of the United States a lot, because the United States, unlike the Soviet Union, was able to abide by the responsibilities stipulated in the Potsdam Declaration.

But on the negative side, the cost of repatriating Japanese soldiers and civilians had to be largely borne by the Japanese government, and this expense was considerable. At the same time, it was really a tricky thing to accommodate so many returning soldiers and civilians in Japan's fragmented economic structure after the war. What's more, the food and housing problems became even more serious. But no matter how bad the negative aspects were, the Japanese still felt that the rapid progress of the repatriation work was worthwhile. Therefore, no matter how huge the expenditure was, they still hoped to quickly send the one million Japanese soldiers and civilians in Manchuria back to their country. But this matter is more complicated because the Soviet Union is involved.

The rapid repatriation of Japanese soldiers and civilians from the US-controlled areas has made the Japanese feel dissatisfied with the Soviet measures. This can be seen from the recent demonstrations held by the Japanese in front of the Soviet Embassy in Tokyo, demanding the acceleration of the repatriation work. The Soviet Union has always paid close attention to Japan and has widely publicized the glory of communism and the Soviet Union's concern for the Japanese people, but the twists and turns of the repatriation work have obviously been a major blow to the work of the Japanese Communist Party and the Soviet delegation.

After many compromises, the Soviet Union finally decided to repatriate 10,000 to 15,000 Japanese people per month from a port in eastern Siberia (recent reports said that MacArthur and the Soviet authorities had established an agreement to repatriate 50,000 people per month—translator), but the Japanese still could not feel very satisfied, because the United States repatriated Japanese from mainland China, the peak of the work had reached as many as 12,000 people per day.

Another thing that made the Japanese dissatisfied was that the Soviet Union was preparing to send Japanese civilians first and delay the repatriation of Japanese prisoners of war as much as possible. According to various estimates, there are at least 800,000 Japanese soldiers in Soviet hands. The United States believes that 700,000 of them have been transported from Manchuria to Siberia to work in labor camps. The Japanese believe that this violates the Potsdam Declaration. At the same time, if the current method is followed, it will take at least several years to repatriate one million people.

Of course, no one can predict how future developments will unfold, because the situation may change at any time. But one thing is very noteworthy, that is, the repatriation of Japanese soldiers and civilians from mainland China has been completed. The war that Tokyo has always called the "Sino-Japanese Incident" has finally come to a formal end after a long nine years. (Translated by Ya Nong from "World Report Weekly")

### A Record of Galloping Horses at Xin Suo Guan

— Special Dispatch from Xi'an, 8th Day of the Month; Correspondent Shao Shenzhi

I arrived in Yao County late at night. Although there were small shops outside the city, it was impossible to find lodging, and it was necessary to knock on the gate to enter the city. At this time, the sky was as black as ink, and you couldn't see your fingers. The desolate wilderness was silent at night, and only the barking of dogs echoed in the cold.

Yao County is built along the Qi River, with very high walls. To enter the city, one must cross the Qi River, and there is a gate on this side of the river. To knock on the gate, one must first knock on the gate of the outer defense. The so-called "blockhouse line" is only a little over ten li from the city. In Sanyuan in the morning, someone in the army told me: "When you get to Yao County, you may still hear gunfire. Just now there was a phone call saying that the Communists are attacking the line." Now that I have arrived at this "important place," it is already late at night, and I expect that the security will be extremely tight. Therefore, I devised a plan to "earn" my way through the gate. I draped a blanket over my shoulder and used a flashlight to explore the road ahead.

Earning Entry into Yao Gate at Night: The Gate Opens Under Red-Tasseled Spears

After walking for a while, I heard a loud challenge for the password in the darkness. I pointed my flashlight and saw a red-tasseled spear. It was a member of the local militia dressed in black. He stood across the gate, blocking my way. I explained where I was coming from and that I needed to enter the city immediately. The red-tasseled spear replied: "This gate is locked. Go look to the north." So I walked further north and encountered another red-tasseled spear. I thought that if this gate was also locked, I would have to find a way to get him to unlock it. Unexpectedly, the gate was ajar. I exchanged a few brief words with the red-tasseled spear. When he heard that I came from Sanyuan, he opened the gate without hesitation.

I crossed the Qi River on a flat stone bridge and went up a very steep, long slope. At the top of the long slope was the tightly closed city gate. After I knocked on the gate, a head appeared from the crack in the door and spoke to me. If I told him that I was a reporter from Shanghai, he would not understand and would not open the door. So I told him that I was from the local commissioner's office, returning to the city from Sanyuan with urgent official business. It seemed that the security was not as strict as I had imagined. While I was negotiating, two soldiers came up behind me. They took my place and spoke to the head in a commanding tone, ordering him to open the gate. The door gradually opened, and I saw that the head belonged to a soldier with a gun and ammunition.

We quickly entered the gate. There were no lights in the city, and it was as silent as death. Along the way, at street corners and alleys, we encountered many more red-tasseled spears. With the help of a soldier, I managed to get the door of a bathhouse-cum-hotel opened. The innkeeper was sleepy-eyed and made clicking noises between his teeth as he made my bed and brewed tea. Looking at the state of the inn, I understood the poverty of social life in Yao County, the so-called "gateway to northern Shaanxi." The water was salty, the bedding was black, and the brick bed was cold. Coupled with the snowy sky, the desolation was even more pronounced.

Generals Han Qi and Fan Zhongyan of the Song Dynasty once established their headquarters in Yan'an to defend against the three borders. The so-called "One Hundred and Twenty Qin Passes," of which Xin Suo, on the Cheng Yu road, was one of the most important passes. Defending the border outside the One Hundred and Twenty Qin Passes was a life of distant garrison service. When the situation became unfavorable, Fan Zhongyan established his headquarters in Yao County, which was then called Yao Prefecture. "Old Fan's belly is full of tens of thousands of soldiers," and the people of Yao County greatly admired Fan Zhongyan. The current county government is located where he had his headquarters. The place where this famous general of the western frontier held court and inspected his troops still exists, now with a plaque that reads "Jing Fan Tang," and serves as the county government's courthouse. The courtyard is narrow, with an old cypress tree. The light is dim, and it faces the drum tower in the center of the city, just a hundred paces away.

It gives people a sense of the border city's military atmosphere of the past, and one can almost hear the sounds of metal weapons and galloping horses.

However, Yao County is in Guanzhong, not the border city of the Han and Tang dynasties. But in the Eastern Han dynasty, there were also Western Qiang, and the entire Guanzhong region was a border area. From hinterland to border city, times have changed, and thousands of years have passed unnoticed. Outside the city, the Qi and Ju rivers flow gently, telling of the vicissitudes of life. It has always been "far" from Chang'an. Although it is now connected by railway, transportation is still difficult. The people of Yao County still look back to the past, believing in myths and legends.

### City of Myths

Yao County also has a stele forest, a private collection of over sixty stone tablets, housed in a solitary, dark, and lightless building that feels like a cave. Most of them are Northern Wei votive inscriptions, with strange and unrestrained brushstrokes. Unlike the Longmen Grottoes votive inscriptions, there are few elegant and graceful pieces. However, there are two extremely complete Thousand Buddha steles. This cannot be compared with the scale of Yungang or Dunhuang, but the carvings are neat and detailed. Thousands of identical Buddha statues in relief equally illustrate the depth and surging tide of Buddhist culture. In addition, there are several Sixth Dynasty stone Buddhas with broken legs and feet. There are also a dozen or so tattered Republic of China army infantrymen who look like beggars. Some of them, like the Sixth Dynasty stone Buddhas, sit under the wall in front of the solitary building, watching the red-tasseled spears drilling on the large parade ground, chanting "one, two, three, four." Others pile up some dead branches and yellow leaves next to the Wei steles, burning them for warmth, the smoke stinging the eyes, nose, and throat. I squeezed into this small house that had become a smoke-filled cave, and with tears in my eyes, I finished looking at the strangely styled steles and stone Buddhas. The group of living stone Buddhas stood up and said to me:

"What should we do? Officer." I didn't know what to do either. After talking with them, they all began to chatter incessantly. They said that they were originally from the 22nd Army, stationed in Toutoulin, but were captured because "the Eighth Route Army attacked us." "The Eighth Route Army had so many men! How could we not be defeated?" I said that Xi'an had sent troops to rescue them, right? "That was after we were defeated that they came to rescue us!" They said, "The Eighth Route Army said to us: What are you fighting for? You fight, and then they come to rescue you. If you want to join the Eighth Route Army, you can stay here. If you don't want to, we'll send you back. We don't want to join. They fed us well on the way here, but when we got here, the county government only gave us two *cang* a day per person. How can we get enough to eat? They don't even believe us when we sigh. It's cold!"

So that's how it was. No wonder they were asking this "officer" what to do. They were wearing suspicion like clothing and given two *cang*, unsure whether it was relief or punishment. I asked around for them in this small city, but couldn't find a solution. The general trend made me understand that the solution had already been decided, which was to do nothing and let nature take its course.

The people of Yao County believe that they live in the middle of a boat. The Northwest lacks water, and they imagine that the place where they live is a boat. They have reason to say so: Look, there is a Ju River and a Qi River, and the county seat is like a boat floating in the middle. They don't know in what year, but a well was dug in the county government office, and the boat started leaking. Yao County was flooded. Later, they quickly filled it in, and cast a half iron bull to stabilize the boat, so that we could live peacefully. I did see a half iron bull. This has more practical significance than Noah's Ark in the Old Testament. They also boast of the Tang Dynasty calligrapher Liu Gongquan and the Sui Dynasty scholar Sun Simiao, the "King of Medicine" whom the people of northern Shaanxi piously worship. The Liu family north of the city was originally the ancestral home of Liu Gongquan, so this place name has been preserved from the Tang Dynasty to the present.

North of Sanyuan, the terrain rises continuously. From a distance, it looks like a mountain, but when you climb to the top, it is a plain. When you look at the outer mountain from a distance, you see a plain again when you climb up. It has a bold and imposing aura. This is the characteristic of the Qin-Long Loess Plateau. The place names in this area are all "something-yuan." The widespread use of the character "yuan" originated in Qinzhong. South of Chang'an is Leyouyuan, which in ancient Chinese literature represents beautiful scenery. But the "yuan" here is a village, farmland, the so-called edge of the Guanzhong Plain area. The Jinghui Canal area is uniquely blessed, and cotton can be harvested at a world record of 120 catties per mu, while here it is only twenty or thirty catties, a stark contrast, but still enough for a general standard. The soil is thin, and the water conservancy is insufficient, relying on the heavens for harvest. There is a saying in Qinzhong: "Eighty-three months of rain," meaning that there is hope for a harvest if it rains in the third, eighth, and thirteenth months of each year. This saying is especially applicable to the arid areas north of Yao County.

These days the blockhouse line is tense. When I arrived, I didn't hear any gunfire, but the commissioner and county magistrate shouted that it was terrible. Gu Zhutong, the commander-in-chief of the army, told them clearly when he came to Xi'an last time: "If you lose the county government, defend the office; if you lose the office, defend the desk." This is the responsibility of the county magistrate. The inspection at the city gate is particularly strict.

A broken county town, the streets are also covered with horse and mule droppings, to allow it to dry easily, because severe winter is about to descend. There is a mountain east of the city named "Mount Wutai" to adorn the faith of the people of Yao County. Even more beautifully decorated is the Medicine King Temple, with its gold and jade splendor. A large cart road leads directly to the county seat, and the incense burns year-round. Middle school students in the county seat kneel before the mud statues, shaking medicine containers, to seek prescriptions for someone in their family. Sun Simiao was undoubtedly a good doctor in the Tang Dynasty. According to the inscribed record in Lingjing, Emperor Taizong of Tang once visited Sun and asked him to serve as an official, but Sun still lived in seclusion here. He praised him, saying, "If he does not become one of the three dukes, he will surely become a famous doctor." He conferred upon him the title of "True Man." Sun wrote the "Essential Formulas Worth a Thousand Gold," and Guo Si, a scholar of the Song Dynasty Huiyou Pavilion, had it engraved in Huazhou to spread it widely. Later, another set was engraved in the Taoist temple. Now these stone steles are as complete as ever, because there are not many rubbings, and the inscriptions are clear, all of which are simple prescriptions. The "Overseas Formula" stele contains Sun's notes on his pillow and health preservation inscriptions, which are mostly in line with scientific principles. The main idea is quiet health preservation, revealing traces of the confluence of Confucian and Taoist thought. The backwardness of culture shows the strength of the power of gods and ghosts. The people of Yao County bring wine to the mountains to worship such temple gods, and their images shine. Myths circulate, while life is poor. If anyone were to try to close it down, they would undoubtedly be strongly opposed. Some people believe that it is better to be immersed in superstition than to be awakened. Moreover, they do not know when they will be saved again.

### Wheat Fields on the Loess Plateau: Public and Private Wastelands for Anyone to Cultivate

The Xian-Tong branch line ends at Yao County. There are coal trucks from Yao County to the coal mine at Wulipu, five miles south of Tongguan, which also sell passenger tickets. From Wulipu to Tongguan Jin Suo Guan, you must hire a horse-drawn carriage.

On the loess plateau in this area, galloping on horseback, pulling on the reins, and looking at the boundless world, the rise of the northwest has been liberated from the labels of closed and backward since the development of the interior from the coast.

On the road north from Yao County to Jin Suo Guan, the wheat is green, but there is still so much wasteland. The government encourages land reclamation, but after a while, only the slogan remains: "Public and private wastelands, for anyone to cultivate." It is not very effective, because land reclamation requires capital and labor, and promoting a new movement requires organization. Over the years, the main theme of the county governments has been handling military service, maintaining the old methods of production. Tongguan County alone has built two hundred blockhouses, while the county's population is only 56,000. Yao County has only 50,000 people, Sanyuan has less than 100,000, and Yijun has less than 40,000, all of whom have a frightening burden.

A highway roughly follows the Qi River northward, with mountains on both sides. Where the loess has eroded, the Permian limestone layer is exposed, as well as conglomerate and shale. The wheat fields I saw were on both sides of the Qi River. Horses and mules run in the fields and eat the wheat, and no one thinks it strange, but people from the south would find it strange. At this time, farmers do not want it to rain or get warmer, otherwise the wheat will grow taller, and it will be difficult to have a good harvest next year, because the soil is loose and they are afraid that it will not be strong. They even need to flatten it, so these wheat plants are like piles of wild vegetables, flat and spread out in the field. At this time, they do not mind the trampling and grazing of sudden horses, as long as it grows firmly. There is a farmer's saying here: "Pigs in the first month, cats in the second month, women running all over the ground in the third month." In the first month, the crops have not yet sprouted, so pigs are allowed to run wild. In the second month, cats are in heat and do not stay home. In the third month, farm women pick wild vegetables everywhere. This is the case throughout the northern wheat belt.

All along the way, there were farmers driving mules. Some were transporting coal from the Jin Suo Guan area to Yao County, and some were carrying corn to the city to sell. But there were no mule carts, the only tool for travel, which shows that there was no travel. The ancient road was desolate. The horse I was riding was borrowed from the army, but it was not thin. Walking with these farmers, I felt that the men were thin and the warhorse was fat. They drove their mules, shouting loudly, or cursing intimately, with the whip cracking, making the ancient road a little less lonely. Their shouts were only short commands, mostly with one sound, occasionally with a retroflex sound. They said that there were more than a hundred commands for driving mules, which could not be recorded in writing or phonetic symbols. They could use commands to control the speed and direction, but when I learned their commands, I couldn't control the mules. They said, "The mules recognize the sound." The mules are all raised very fat. They say, "Four fish mixed with feed are better than bait." This saying means that as long as the grass is mixed evenly, the livestock will eat it all, without special bait.

They rely on pitiful agriculture, and the agricultural knowledge and life accumulated by the gods and ancestors. There are often many moving farming songs buried in the Chinese countryside, and this place is no exception. They sing of the weeds, saying, "Artemisia capillaris in the first month, artemisia annua in the second month, cut down and burned as firewood in the third month." They say that the tender grass in the first month is as fragrant as artemisia capillaris, and praise artemisia capillaris (a medicinal herb that can be soaked in wine). The grass is green in the second month, and the grass is lush after the third month, ready to be used as fuel. This is also the season when the ground is frozen and the grass is yellow. As I listened, I actually wrote down a large pile of proverbs. I felt that due to the difference in latitude, water conservancy, geology, and climate, their expectations for rain and early harvests, or their predictions of the weather, were significantly different from those in the south.

They have their own names for animals and plants. Male pigs and dogs are called "tooth pig" and "tooth dog," while females are called "grass pig" and "grass dog." Cats are called "lang cat" and "female cat." However, male horses are called "son horse," and female horses are called "mule horse." Male mules are called "son mule," and females are called "grass mule." Middle-aged pigs have a special name, they call them "Kalangzi." Crickets are called "vinegar spiders," and ants are called "butt floating horse." These are all local dialects. Artemisia capillaris is called wild flat bean, and Bupleurum chinense is called chicken tongue. They hate cuckoos, calling them "murdering robbers," because their calls are unpleasant and they have no good feelings for them. They do not know the elegant names given to this bird by scholars and poets.

Flocks of wild pigeons and wild chickens gather in the wheat fields, flying slowly and in large numbers. If there were hunting guns, there would be a harvest.

### Tongguan Coal Mine

Last year on Double Tenth Day, Tongguan County was renamed Tongchuan County. When people in Shaanxi talk about Tongguan, they specifically call it Northern Tongguan, because the pronunciation is the same as Tongguan. The telegraph bureau's Latin alphabet spelling encountered difficulties and often sent the telegrams of the two counties to the wrong place. But Tongguan Coal Mine is still Tongguan Coal Mine.

There is a severe coal shortage in Xi'an, but tens of thousands of tons of coal have accumulated in Yao County and Tongchuan, with mixed coal selling for four or five yuan per ton. The price in Xi'an is three times higher. Some employees on the Cheng-Tong branch line have given up their jobs waving red and green flags to traffic in coal, making a profit of three times their salary. I asked why the Longhai Railway could not transport the coal to Xi'an to alleviate the coal shortage this winter. The answer was that there were not enough cars. ### A Brief Discussion on the Uses of Plasma

— Yi Jianlong

Significant progress has been made in therapeutic drugs during the Second World War. This remarkable progress has provided a deeper level of protection for human health and lives. In terms of the order of progress, the first can be said to be the synthesis and evolution of mineral preparations; the second is the preservation and unlimited use of plasma; and the third is the invention and mass production of penicillin. People in the country clearly have a considerable understanding of the therapeutic value of the first and third, while the uses and methods of the second seem to be only partially understood, and even abandoned altogether due to improper use. In the early years of the War of Resistance, the author studied blood transfusion therapy with experts in the United States and Canada, and later presided over a blood bank in Kunming for many years. Based on research and experience, I will give a brief introduction.

(1) Composition—To know the uses of plasma, one must first understand its effective components. Plasma is made from unclotted blood and is a mixture of colloidal and crystalline substances. Its effective components can be described as follows:

(A) Essential Nutrients—All nutrients that have been digested by the gastrointestinal tract must be absorbed and transported through the plasma before they can reach the tissues of various parts of the human body. Furthermore, the remaining or stored nutrients in the tissues must also be absorbed and transported through the plasma before they can enter nutrient-poor tissues. From this perspective, plasma is nothing less than a general hub for nutrients. All nutrients needed by the human body, such as proteins, amino acids, fats, fatty acids, glucose, vitamins, and inorganic salts, are readily available.

(B) Substances Maintaining Osmotic Pressure—The circulating blood volume in a healthy human body is constant, accounting for 9% of body mass. Its ability to remain constant depends largely on the osmotic pressure within the plasma. This osmotic pressure can prevent the exudation of fluid from blood vessels, and can also return what has already exuded back into the blood vessels. There are two substances in the blood that maintain osmotic pressure: proteins and salts.

(C) Endocrine Hormones—Endocrine hormones are the mechanisms that constantly regulate changes in various parts of the human body. For example, thyroid and growth hormones control development, insulin controls carbohydrate metabolism, and ovarian hormones control fertility. These endocrine hormones originate in endocrine glands and are transported to various organs through the blood. Therefore, endocrine hormones are also present in plasma.

(D) Various Enzymes—Metabolism and detoxification in the human body are mostly mediated by enzymes. The plasma contains a variety of enzymes, the most important of which is thrombin.

(E) Various Antibodies—When the human body is invaded by bacteria, it produces antibodies that circulate in the plasma to fight the bacteria. The types and amounts of antibodies in the blood vary from person to person. In most mixed plasma, the types and amounts of antibodies are generally higher.

(F) Other Life-sustaining Factors of Unknown Origin.

(2) Uses and Methods—Knowing the effective components of plasma allows us to infer its uses. The uses and methods of plasma can be described as follows:

(A) Traumatic and Surgical Shock

The etiology of shock is still under debate, but its significant pathological change, the escape of plasma from blood vessels, is generally recognized. Due to the action of certain malignant factors, the blood vessels dilate and permeability increases, causing plasma to escape into the surrounding tissues. As a result, the circulating blood volume is greatly reduced, and the supply of oxygen and other nutrients to the tissues is insufficient. If not treated immediately, the patient will likely die within nine to twelve hours. The effective method for treating shock is the rapid infusion of plasma. American shock expert Dr. Scudder said, "If blood is not transfused when it should be, death is imminent; insufficient blood transfusion is a waste of effort." Blood specific gravity, hematocrit, and blood pressure are the three standards for determining the timing and quantity of blood transfusion. If the blood specific gravity and hematocrit are higher than normal, while the blood pressure drops to below 70%, it is clear evidence that the blood is too concentrated and the heart's pumping volume is greatly reduced, and a large amount of plasma should be infused immediately until all three return to normal. The expressions of shock patients in our country are often not as obvious as those of Caucasians. Even when facing life-threatening situations, they remain calm, so laboratory tests and diagnosis are very important. If the patient can still eat and has no symptoms of dehydration, concentrated plasma or concentrated serum albumin can be injected intravenously to increase the osmotic pressure within the blood vessels, so that the plasma that has permeated into the surrounding tissues returns to the blood vessels. To prevent the occurrence of surgical shock, it is best to infuse 300 to 500 cc of plasma before and after major surgery. This procedure has become an indispensable routine in operating rooms of major hospitals in the United States.

(B) Hemorrhagic Shock

Bleeding from internal and external injuries can cause shock, but massive hemoptysis, gastrointestinal bleeding, ectopic pregnancy bleeding, and metrorrhagia can also cause severe shock. The difference between this type of shock and the shock mentioned above is that blood specific gravity, hematocrit, and blood pressure all decrease simultaneously. In this situation, plasma should be infused rapidly through multiple veins simultaneously to help the patient survive the critical period, followed by the infusion of whole blood or red blood cells. If the circulating blood volume is lost by more than one-third, it can be life-threatening, while the loss of red blood cells, if it does not exceed 60%, can still maintain its minimum level of gas exchange.

(C) Burns or Scalds

Severe burns or scalds can cause shock. Therefore, in addition to local treatment, plasma should be infused to prevent shock. Even after the shock has passed, plasma continues to leak from the affected area. According to Dr. Scudder's estimate, if a third-degree burn covers one-third of the body's surface area, the amount of plasma lost from the affected area in 24 hours is more than 4000 cc. To maintain the patient's normal blood volume, plasma infusion is essential. If the patient can still drink, concentrated plasma should be used.

(D) Secondary Anemia

The treatment for this type of anemia is to eliminate its source and promote the production of red blood cells. To promote the production of red blood cells, it is necessary to supply as much of the raw materials for red blood cell production as possible, including proteins and iron. Plasma contains sufficient protein and other nutrients, which can supply some of the raw materials for red blood cell production, so it can be an adjunct to iron therapy.

(E) Malnutrition

In severe malnutrition, the protein in the blood is lower than normal. As a result, water continues to permeate from the blood into the surrounding tissues, forming systemic edema. For this type of patient, only by measuring the protein in the blood and continuously injecting concentrated plasma can the patient recover quickly.

(F) Protein Deficiency Syndrome

Protein deficiency syndrome is caused by fatty changes in the renal tubules. In these patients, blood protein is continuously excreted from the kidneys. As a result, the protein in the blood is significantly reduced, and systemic edema can also form. These patients should also be treated with plasma. If the daily excretion of protein is more than 25 grams, and the patient cannot consume more protein, then 400 cc of plasma should be infused daily, since the protein content in plasma is usually around 7%.

(G) Febrile Diseases

In patients with diseases such as typhoid fever, typhus, and tuberculosis, gastrointestinal digestion and absorption are abnormal, and the body's consumption is high, resulting in a negative nitrogen balance. This pathological change not only reduces the patient's resistance but can also cause adverse complications. If plasma is continuously injected in addition to drinking more fluids, it can increase the patient's nutrition and resistance.

(H) Gastrointestinal Diseases

Whether or not these patients have undergone surgical treatment, if they cannot consume more nutrients, their condition will inevitably deteriorate. The remedy is to continuously inject plasma to increase nutrition and promote the healing of the affected area. An immobile adult needs at least 50 grams of protein per day, which requires 700 cc of plasma. Healthy plasma contains 7 grams of protein per 100 cc.

(I) Acute Infectious Diseases

Mixed human plasma contains a variety of antibodies, so it is effective against measles, pertussis, chickenpox, scarlet fever, erysipelas, and adenitis. American pediatrician Dr. Bavenberg's experiments have proven that among 4,733 children who received measles prevention injections (20 to 40 cc of plasma injected intramuscularly per person), only six cases of primary measles were found, while among 11,280 children who did not receive measles prevention injections, 94 developed primary measles.

(J) Premature Infants

With the advancement of modern medicine, infants born less than six months after conception can also be kept alive. In addition to regulating the temperature of the incubator, providing oxygen, and special nutrition, small amounts of plasma can also be continuously injected.

(K) Increased Intracranial Pressure and Breast Lumps

For these patients, continuous injection of concentrated plasma or concentrated serum albumin while reducing fluid intake is much more effective than treatment with magnesium sulfate laxatives.

Although plasma has many uses, there are also contraindications. In cases of organ dysfunction, excessive blood pressure, congestive heart failure, or acute pulmonary edema, plasma infusion should be used with caution, and large amounts are especially not recommended.

(3) Methods for Distinguishing the Quality of Plasma—After the victory of the War of Resistance, surplus dry plasma from the US military also flowed into our country's market along with other surplus materials. The modern pharmacopeia does not yet have methods for distinguishing between good and bad products. However, it is inevitable that the quality of various products will vary. To facilitate identification, I will briefly describe the dry plasma identification method for users' reference:

(A) Color—Expired dry plasma is a light, amorphous powder that is always straw yellow. It should not be used if it is clotted or has a light green tint.

(B) Unexpired dry plasma should dissolve completely within one minute after adding distilled water.

(C) Solution Turbidity—Turbidity is a common physical property of liquid plasma, but under an photoelectric colorimeter, its turbidity should not exceed 700 degrees.

(D) Residual Moisture—The residual moisture in dry plasma should not exceed 1.0%.

(E) No Pyrogens—Pyrogens are the corpses or waste products of bacteria. If the equipment used to prepare the plasma is not washed with special chemicals, or the soda water used to dissolve the dry plasma is not disinfected with a special filter, then a fever reaction is unavoidable.

### Latest Vitamins

— Shao

Although folic acid has been discovered for many years, its chemical structure was only elucidated and its artificial synthesis successfully achieved most recently.

Chemically speaking, folic acid is composed of the following three different chemical groups: pterin, p-aminobenzoic acid, and glutamic acid. Pterin is a type of yellow pigment found in butterfly wings; folic acid is also a yellow crystal.

Folic acid was originally a component of the vitamin B complex, initially called the "Lactobacillus casei factor" because it is an essential nutrient for the growth of *Lactobacillus casei*. Recent reports indicate that folic acid is also an essential element for human hematopoiesis and has specific effects on various megaloblastic anemias, similar to liver extracts. However, liver extracts are difficult to purify, and their determination requires clinical cases of pernicious anemia, resulting in high prices and inconvenient application. Folic acid is an artificial synthetic product that does not require biological determination. With mass production in the future, the price will inevitably be low, and the day when it completely replaces liver extracts is not far off.

As early as 1935, Day et al. reported that natural folic acid (initially called Vitamin M) had the effect of preventing a nutritional anemia in white mice. However, the therapeutic effect of this artificial synthetic product on human megaloblastic anemia was not discovered until 1945 by the American vitamin expert Spies et al. Spies et al. tested it on several cases of nutritional megaloblastic anemia common in the southern United States and obtained the same therapeutic effect as liver extracts, namely a rapid increase in the number of reticulocytes in the blood, followed by a steady rise in the number of red blood cells and hemoglobin levels. This year, it was also tested on Sprue, megaloblastic anemia during pregnancy, and true pernicious anemia, all with similar success. These results have been fully confirmed by Darby, Moore et al., and Zuclzer & Cgden et al. in the same country. Recently, Wilkinson et al. in the United Kingdom used folic acid in five cases of pernicious anemia and also obtained equally satisfactory results.

However, folic acid is not the active ingredient (antianemic factor) in liver extracts. Folic acid is a bright orange-yellow crystal, while the most refined liver extracts are colorless. Willmson believed that folic acid might stimulate the production of blood factors in the liver. However, according to the experimental report published in the October issue of the Journal of Pathology and Bacteriology last year by Jacobson & William in the United Kingdom, the antianemic factor in the liver and folic acid may belong to the same class of compounds, and their main chemical group is pterin. According to the research of these two scholars, synthetic xanthopterin and leucopterin, as well as liver extracts, can all produce the same reticulocyte response in splenectomized rabbits, while other substances such as iron and various vitamins (unfortunately, folic acid was not tested) do not have this effect. Therefore, they believe that the liver antianemic factor may be a pterin derivative.

I think about it again and again, and I can't come up with a good term to give to this year. I might as well give it a "nickname" and call this year the "Year of Many Winds".

Although the weather has been cold recently, the winds outside have not been that strong. Why call it the "Year of Many Winds"? Am I stirring up trouble? Indeed, the meaning of "wind" is very broad; the winds I am talking about are neither the southeast wind nor the northwest wind, but rather the "rising winds", "hitting winds", "seizing winds", and "overturning winds" of this year.

First, the "rising winds". Whenever a rising wind sweeps over, people are as if in a daze, at a loss. When the rising wind has passed, they wake up and take a breath. But good times don't last long. The second rising wind intensifies and comes one after another, putting people back into a state of confusion. The small people live their lives in these endlessly cycling rising winds, and they really don't know when prices will stop rising.

Next, the "hitting winds". Whether you are in a theater or on a sports field, you can see this kind of wind, constantly being directed by someone. I don't know why human nature has become so irritable. People start fighting at the slightest disagreement, beating each other to a bloody pulp, all "as if nothing happened," happy to be watched by others.

Third is the "seizing winds". This year, it is difficult to solve the problem of livelihood, and there are not a few people who take risks, and the seizing winds are becoming more and more intense. But there are different kinds of seizing methods. "Robbers and bandits" rob openly, while corrupt officials and treacherous merchants rob secretly. Those who rob openly say that they are robbing because it is difficult to solve the problem of livelihood, but what are those who rob secretly doing it for? The more you rob, the more you spend, causing each other, and the robbing is endless!

Finally, let's talk about the "overturning winds". This seems to be the "grand finale". As the end of the year approaches, overturning winds are prevalent.

### Amidst the Flames of War, There is a Paradise

—

The housing shortage that came with the victory still cannot be resolved even now, a serious problem. The authorities seem to have put some thought into it, and plans have been drawn up one after another, residential areas, people's villages, these are forever just talking on paper.

Recently, I was like "Columbus" discovering a new continent, and I saw a genuine "people's village". Where is it? The location is near "West Station" and "New Longhua". The residents here seem to be like those in "Peach Blossom Spring", living without contention with the world, working at sunrise and resting at sunset.

This place was originally wasteland, but now, shop owners complain of hardship, and factory owners sigh of poverty, saying that the end of the year is difficult to pass, and there is another hurdle after one hurdle. These days, they have a feeling of "every day is like a year", and every day is like "New Year's Eve". I think that if we don't fundamentally think about settling down and unifying, no matter what wise methods you come up with, it will not be able to withstand this overturning wind.

I don't know what kind of wind will come after the overturning wind. In short, this year is the "Year of Many Winds". To be honest, we small people do not miss this kind of year; our only hope is that next year will be "good weather and abundant harvest, the country will be peaceful and the people will be safe", and that we small people will no longer suffer!

It has long become a new "people's village". The newly built houses are not three-dimensional red tile bricks, but they are very neat and orderly, and each household is independent, living very freely and comfortably, with bedrooms, kitchens, living rooms, and some beans, wheat, vegetables, and green onions planted on the open space outside the door. Although there is no tap water, it is not a problem to dig a pond for drinking, and each household raises some chickens, ducks, pigs, and sheep. Therefore, the New Year's dishes may be richer than those of the salaried Shanghainese.

According to friends, the houses here do not need to be "topped" with gold bars. They are all built by fellow villagers from northern Jiangsu themselves, and the building materials needed are moved from rubble yards elsewhere. The old traders said that they lived so comfortably without spending a penny, and they didn't need the dangerous mouths of the second landlords, and they didn't need to pay rent. "Donations and taxes" were like a horse and a cow to them. Although the landlords had negotiated with them, these fellow villagers from northern Jiangsu really had nowhere to live. As the saying goes, there is only a house to keep people living in, not to demolish the house and drive people out, and everyone has a heart of compassion!

In this new "people's village", there are also several village shops, cigarette paper shops, small teahouses, barber shops, and pancake stalls, which are small but complete.

From here, I saw the self-reliance of the people of northern Jiangsu. Each fellow villager from northern Jiangsu can endure hardship and is hardworking, has no dependency, and can "not ask for help" in every place to live. The children already know how to "scavenge" and sell tea without relying on their parents for food and drink, so I think the people of northern Jiangsu are very worthy of emulation.

### The Organization of Beggars 20

— Huang Jiamo

Performing Arts and Begging (Continued)

(6) "The True and False Dumbs" — Dumbness is a type of disability, and the beggar gangs also use it as a magic weapon for begging for sympathy. However, there are true and false ones, and the profession is divided into four types. They are called "Hiding from Heaven," "Crying Mouth," "Blocking the Road," and "Acting Real." "Hiding from Heaven" are genuinely mute beggars, while "Crying Mouth" are those who have learned the trick. Pretending to be mute is originally the easiest trick, and their begging is divided into two factions: "Blocking the Road" and "Street Walking." They go their separate ways and do not interfere with each other. "Blocking the Road" involves kneeling by the roadside, knocking their heads on the ground, chanting something, pointing to heaven and earth, then pointing to their own mouths and hearts, patting their chests to beg. "Street Walking" involves begging from house to house. False dumbs gather when begging, and are not allowed to talk as a rule, only being allowed to speak after returning to the "Imperial Incense Hall" (beggar factory).

(7) "Nose Guanyin" — This group must wear straw shoes and tie straw rings on their heads. "Double Dragons Entering the Sea" involves using bamboo to carve two long objects, suspending tassels on the ends. When begging, they insert the bamboo into their nostrils, exposing the tassels outside. "True Dragon Entering the Mouth" is about putting a snake through the nose. Both factions "breathe through one nostril."

(8) "Sitting and Cursing" — With crippled limbs, they sit by the roadside cursing wildly. Their curse goes: "Good feet, good hands, the road to heaven; bad feet, bad hands, the gate to hell. Out in the open, secretly coming, not a penny wasted on empty ground." The singing is full of ups and downs, making people feel pity.

(9) "Selling Ear Picks" — Selling bamboo ear picks along the street is another branch of the beggar gang. The gang calls themselves "Holding a Gauze Hat," not knowing where the meaning comes from. But those who work this profession are unwilling to admit that they are beggars. Probably because the income is not bad, they have already joined the ranks of small vendors. It is said that one must be able to hold a gauze hat with the right hand and be able to independently use a knife to manufacture with the left hand to be considered a family skill.

(10) "Bitter Flesh Knife" — This gang is divided into two types: "Waist Blocking" and "Enclosure." When a "Waist Blocking" beggar goes to the street, they must first wear the upper body and carry a two-foot-long disciplinary knife. When not given money, they would strike their waist hard, leaving new red marks. Making the shopkeepers both surprised and pitying, giving money to end the matter. "Enclosure" is similar to street performances, setting up a venue on the roadside wasteland, asking people for donations. However, these people do not have the good skills of the Jianghu gangs, just randomly bang and knock for a while, deceiving idle people.

(11) "Dancing Single Stick" — Similar to the above, they belong to the sub-category of Jianghu street performances, but are despised by the Jianghu gangs. One type is called "Neat Attributes," which can dance the wooden stick in a variety of ways. Another type is called "Beating Single Stick," which uses three short sticks, one thick and two thin, dancing and begging along the street.

(12) "The True and False Blinds" — If there are true and false dumbs, there must be true and false blinds, and the false blinds are more than the true blinds. The fake goods are called "Cold Look" in the gang, which is also a wonderful metaphor. Holding a bamboo pole, the eyelids turned outwards, hiding the black pupils, it is admirable that they still understand makeup skills. The street call has a saying: "Raise your head and not see the road to heaven, lift your feet and not see the height of the road." Such things cannot be fully remembered. "Rotten Beauty" sitting and begging also belongs to this gang. On the surface, it is covered with sores, displayed on the roadside for exhibition. In fact, most of them use the makeup skills passed down from the beggar gang. In addition, there are those who kneel by the roadside knocking their heads, knocking their foreheads into giant blocks like walnuts. Also belongs to this gang, to be described in detail later.
