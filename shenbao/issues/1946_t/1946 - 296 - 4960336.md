### Wishing the United Nations General Assembly Success

The United Nations General Assembly opens in New York today. Politicians and diplomats from fifty-one countries around the world are gathering together to begin the arduous task of discussing how to create world peace. The eyes of all humanity are focused on New York, closely watching the development of this international conference. Although most observers still hold a pessimistic view of the future of the United Nations General Assembly, the desire of people in all countries for world peace is so urgent today that as long as there is a glimmer of hope, they ultimately feel that there is a possibility of a breakthrough and will never give up hope. People in all countries still have unlimited expectations for the success of the United Nations General Assembly, so U.S. Undersecretary of State Acheson, on the eve of the United Nations General Assembly, addressed the delegates of various countries and said very frankly: "The United States has consistently supported the United Nations as its foreign policy; and will continue to boldly move forward and continue to adopt this policy without discouragement. The United States government firmly believes that the United Nations will make full progress on the difficult long road and complete world peace." Although these are diplomatic pleasantries from American diplomats, they are enough to increase the confidence of delegates from various countries that the United Nations General Assembly will be successful.

Today, the center of gravity of the world political situation is undoubtedly completely on the relationship between the United States and the Soviet Union. The key to the success or failure of the United Nations General Assembly also lies here. U.S. Secretary of State Byrnes, who had just returned from Paris, declared when reporting to the people of the country on the proceedings of the Twenty-One Nation Peace Conference: "The most worrying aspect of the current situation is that the tension between the United States and the Soviet Union is increasing rather than decreasing, and the United States has reached the end of its concessions to the Soviet Union." However, Soviet Foreign Minister Molotov, after arriving in New York, issued a statement to the American press saying: "I believe that sincerity and goodwill will certainly lead to mutual understanding; therefore, I also believe that the work facing this United Nations General Assembly will be completed, and any difficulties can be solved easily. The Soviet delegation will be able to contribute to this United Nations General Assembly and the Foreign Ministers' Meeting to be held in early November, so that it can complete its work to promote peace and the well-being of people of all countries, large and small, around the world." Although these are diplomatic pleasantries, they are enough to make the world increasingly hopeful that the relationship between the United States and the Soviet Union will improve. Therefore, on the eve of the opening of the United Nations General Assembly, we saw New York filled with an atmosphere of peace and brightness, which is really gratifying, and we hope that the world situation will be brighter from now on and that the world's yearning for peace will not be in vain.

Since we are in this kind of international atmosphere, we should naturally welcome the opening of the United Nations General Assembly with a happy mood and sincerely wish the United Nations General Assembly success. However, looking back on the past and looking forward to the future, after making a comprehensive analysis of the current international situation, we ultimately feel that the future of the United Nations General Assembly is not to be taken too optimistically and that great wise and benevolent politicians from various countries still need to work hard to overcome all difficulties and pave a broad road for world peace. Based on what we have seen, we would like to offer a word to politicians and diplomats from various countries.

First, when we recall the international situation over the past year and a half, we have only seen one disturbance after another, and several basic issues have gone in a big circle and returned to their original starting points without any resolution. This is really the most discouraging phenomenon after the war. In Europe, it has been a year and a half since Nazi Germany unconditionally surrendered and the war ended. In the Far East, it has been more than a year since Japan surrendered. During this period, many politicians and diplomats have tried their best to work for peace, but true peace has not yet come to the world. Internationally, there have been meetings of the five foreign ministers, meetings of the three foreign ministers, the United Nations General Assembly, the meetings of the four foreign ministers, and then from the meetings of the four foreign ministers to the Paris Peace Conference, and now it will be from the United Nations General Assembly to the meetings of the four foreign ministers. The number of international conferences has created an unprecedented record in human history, but what are the results of these international conferences? Let's make a total settlement. The only achievement is the five peace proposals passed by the Paris Peace Conference. Other important issues could not be resolved by the Foreign Ministers' Meeting, so they were transferred to the Paris Peace Conference for handling, but the Paris Peace Conference still failed to reach a resolution, and now they have been returned to the Foreign Ministers' Meeting for discussion. This cruel fact gives us a valuable lesson, that is, creating peace is not easy and requires the cooperation of all countries to have hope of success.

Second, when we study the reasons for the slow progress of peace work, we cannot but attribute it to the fact that the great powers cannot treat each other with sincerity, which leads to mutual suspicion and the emergence of two opposing worlds. In fact, the Soviet Union needs peace and does not want to see war again; the United States also needs peace and hates war; since the goals of both sides are the same, why can't they cooperate in international conferences? This is obviously due to mutual suspicion between the two sides. There is a saying in Chinese: "Without sincerity, there is nothing." Since the United States and the Soviet Union cannot treat each other with sincerity, no problem can be solved. On the contrary, if both sides can be open and honest, nothing is unspeakable, and any difficulty can be easily solved; as the saying goes, "Where sincerity reaches, metal and stone are opened." Foreign Minister Molotov also said this in his speech in New York, which makes us believe that delegates from all countries, especially the United States and the Soviet Union, will be able to treat each other with sincerity in this United Nations General Assembly, abandon all differences in opinions, and reach a completely unanimous agreement.

Third, because we hope that the United Nations General Assembly can be successful, we must point out the defects in the United Nations organization, that is, it lacks democratic spirit in its organization. The post-war world has already entered a new era of peace and democracy, and the present can be regarded as a democratic century. People in all countries of the world not only demand the democratization of domestic politics, but also demand the democratization of international politics. The politics of power has become a historical relic and should not be allowed to revive and reappear on the international stage of the democratic century. After carefully studying the United Nations Charter, we can know that the organization of the United Nations General Assembly that opens today has very undemocratic aspects. In its theory, all countries of the United Nations, regardless of size or strength, are equal in law and completely equal. However, in the Security Council, there is a distinction between permanent members and non-permanent members, and permanent members have the privilege of exercising the veto power over the resolutions passed by the Security Council. Any resolution that is approved by all countries cannot be established as long as one major power (permanent member) expresses dissent. What an undemocratic system this is! Therefore, if we want to hope that the United Nations General Assembly can be successful and that it can become the only effective tool for maintaining world peace, then the veto power must be abolished. There has long been such a movement in the United States, and it has been advocated to all countries in the world, and all countries have expressed their approval. The only country that has expressed opposition is the Soviet Union alone. Before the relationship between the United States and the Soviet Union has improved, the Soviet Union alone holds a dissenting opinion. From the standpoint of the Soviet Union, it naturally has its reasons, but when all countries treat each other with sincerity and no longer have fears, the Soviet Union naturally has no reason to insist that the United Nations organization still retain its fatal wound.

In short, the issues discussed at this United Nations General Assembly, in addition to current international disputes, will also discuss various issues that are beneficial to human welfare. It has a very close relationship to the future of all mankind. Therefore, for the benefit of all countries, and also for the future of the world, this conference is only allowed to succeed and not allowed to fail. Let us wish the United Nations General Assembly success here.

### The Inside Story of Shanghai Pickpocket Gangs (3)

— Huang Jiamo

The Hat Is Stolen, Gone Like a Wild Goose in the Sky

"Catching turtles" means snatching hats, especially expensive Western-style felt hats. Shanghainese commonly refer to it as "throwing the top palace," which has almost become a well-known term, but those involved have stopped using it. Usually, the "hard pickpocket" brothers who are particularly skilled at snatching hats are always in groups of two or three, lingering at tram and bus stops. They first spot the hat worn by a passenger near the window. Once the vehicle starts moving, they snatch it from the window and run away. The loser is not able to get off the vehicle immediately, and the vehicle is often packed like sardines. By the time the loser gets off the vehicle, the thief is long gone. When rickshaws and pedicabs are speeding, the felt hats of the passengers on the vehicle are also good targets. The pickpocket gang only needs to lightly stretch out their arms to grab the hat. Then they run madly in the opposite direction and hand it over to an accomplice. Even if the police pursue them, they often fail to catch them. This kind of "pickpocket gang" also "catches turtles" on the train platforms of the two railway bureaus. In recent years, the security at the railway platforms has been stricter, and the traces of this kind of people have not been often found on the platform.

The activity groups of the "hard pickpocket gang" are all controlled by the "uncle gang." The time and location are determined in advance, and "foreigners" who are not part of the gang cannot intrude and "steal their business."

The time for them to take action is set in the early morning, noon, and dusk. Because in the early morning, citizens are crowded at the train stations, and at noon and evening, pedestrians are crowded on the roads, both of which are conducive to escaping after the theft. Their activity locations are chosen in places with many side streets, or where the alleys are branched, and one of the skilled members of the group carries out the snatching and runs immediately. At this time, the loser will inevitably shout and chase after them. Then the accomplices pretend to be unrelated passersby and stop the loser to falsely inquire, in order to delay the loser's time and prevent pursuers who see the injustice. According to this strategy, they adapt to the situation and let the accomplice who snatched the item escape from danger through the side streets. These accomplices are called "wind blockers."

Sneaky and with Many Tricks

"Literary errand" is the second type of "eating bad rice." Unlike the forceful robbery of the "hard pickpocket" gang, they are sneaky "rats and dogs thieves." Because some of their behavior is relatively civilized, it is called "literary errand." The categories of their activities and performance methods are as follows:

(1) "Flipping over the head" - This gang always uses thick ropes or wooden ladders to climb over other people's walls or roofs to enter the house and then steal.

(2) "Prying open the fan" - The theft involves prying open the door with a small iron crowbar.

(3) "Running early morning" - Most of the people's houses are opened early in the morning to pour out the toilet or to clean. The petty thieves use this time to sneak in and grab something quickly.

(4) "Running to the lamplight" - Also known as "crow's head." In the evening dusk, most residents leave the back door ajar, and the petty thieves use the opportunity to sneak in and be active.

(5) "Going to heaven's watch" - People dry clothes on the drying racks on the sun terrace or in the courtyard every day, and most of the time, no one is watching. The petty thieves use long bamboo poles to pick down the clothes, regardless of whether they are dry or wet, and sweep them all away.

(6) "Cutting the crack" - If a residence locks the door, it shows that no one is inside. If the petty thief knows in advance that the family is rich, they will take advantage of the lack of preparation to break the lock and enter the house to steal calmly.

(7) "Running to the kiln" - This gang of thieves does not set a specific time, and their activity method is also very free. They steal anything they can get and leave immediately. (1) Forged Lost Receipt

A reader named Yi-ming asks for clarification on several legal questions: In the 32nd year of the Republic of China [1943], Mr. Jia mortgaged two shares of land to my late father through Mr. Yi as an intermediary. After our hometown was occupied in the 29th year [1940], Mr. Yi took advantage of his power to buy the land from Mr. Jia. I was not notified at the time (Mr. Jia has now passed away). After the victory, I entrusted someone to negotiate with Mr. Yi, who colluded with my stepmother to forge a lost receipt, stating that when Mr. Jia previously mortgaged the land to my late father, the loan receipt was lost and the debt had been repaid. The lost receipt was signed with my late father's seal. I have several questions and would appreciate your answers: (1) The original intermediary and the buyer were both arranged by Mr. Yi, and I was not notified. Can I deny this transaction? (2) Mr. Jia has passed away. Can his property be considered mine and can I apply for land registration? (3) Can I sue for forging my late father's lost receipt? (4) Can publishing a notice to invalidate my late father's seal be used to invalidate the lost receipt at the same time?

[Answer from Lawyer Cao Ya-xia] (1) No. Because after the property owner sets a mortgage, they can transfer the property to others, and there is no regulation that the intermediary for the mortgage cannot also be the buyer. (2) No. It is not permitted by law for the ownership of the mortgaged property to be directly transferred to the mortgagee. (3) If it is indeed forged, you can sue for the crime of forging documents. (4) Publishing a notice of invalidation cannot invalidate a receipt that was previously established.

(2) Redemption of Mortgaged Land

Ren Cheng-fu asks: In the third year of the Republic of China [1914], my great-grandfather mortgaged a piece of land with a 'living deed' for sixty silver dollars, and built a house on it. The contract clearly stated a five-year term for redemption. After five years, thirty-five silver dollars were added, and another contract was written, stating that when redeeming, the original sum should be returned together, with no shortage. In the thirteenth year [1924], ten silver dollars were borrowed with a monthly interest rate of three percent. The house was burned down by the enemy during the war. When the enemy surrendered, I wanted to rebuild the house on the land, but the mortgagee intends to redeem it. I can barely afford to build the house, but I worry about future disputes. Legally speaking, can the land be redeemed by the mortgagee? Please answer in your column.

[Answer from Lawyer Cao Ya-xia] According to Judicial Yuan Interpretation No. 2558, item 7, "For pawn rights established before the implementation of the Regulations for Clearing Real Estate Pawns, if a term is specified, redemption is allowed within two years after thirty years from the date of the pawn, regardless of whether the term has been extended by contract." The Regulations for Clearing Real Estate Pawns were implemented in the fourth year of the Republic of China [1915]. This pawn was established in the third year of the Republic of China [1914], and can be handled according to the above interpretation. The legal period for redeeming pawned items can be extended by two years after the implementation of the Supplementary Regulations for Handling Civil Litigation After Recovery, as stated in Article 7 of the same regulations, if the legal period for redemption could not be observed due to the war. Therefore, legally speaking, this pawned item can still be redeemed.

### Banks Should Pay Even if the Draft Stub Has Not Arrived

According to the usual practice of Chinese banks, if a remitter remits money from a bank in Location A to Location B, holding the demand draft issued by the bank in Location A and sends it to the payee. When the payee collects the money from the bank in Location B, the bank in Location B usually only allows payment after verifying that the draft and its stub or counter-stub are consistent. If it is a clean payment on delivery, the stub can be ignored. However, the Negotiable Instruments Law does not stipulate that payment should be made only after the stub is verified. It stipulates that the holder of the instrument should present it for payment on or within two days after the due date. If it is overdue by three days, the holder of the instrument can legally seek recourse from the bank. The legal provisions and customs are not entirely consistent. The Central Bank recently received a notice from the Joint Treasury forwarded from the Ministry of Finance, informing that banks should fulfill their payment obligations within the legal time frame in order to protect credit and facilitate customers. Moreover, the Negotiable Instruments Law stipulates that payment can be postponed for three days with the consent of the holder of the instrument, leaving room for maneuver. In addition to verifying the stub, there are other methods that can be used to identify the authenticity of the draft. Therefore, it will be much more convenient for payees to collect money from banks in the future.

### The Danube River Has Many Troubles The Struggle Between the United States and the Soviet Union Over Navigation Rights Affects European Peace

The United States has recently been fighting for an issue vital to European peace in two international conferences: the unrestricted use of the 1,750-mile-long Danube River for international trade. At the Paris Peace Conference, over Soviet opposition and after the United States withdrew its special proposals, a vague proposal to convene a special conference to internationalize the Danube River was passed. At the same time, at Lake Success, the United Nations Economic and Social Committee passed a similar U.S. proposal. But countries under Soviet control have vowed to refuse to attend any international conference that opposes their sole management of the Danube.

Therefore, this issue cannot help but arise again, making it difficult for the United Nations to deal with. The following is a dispatch from Vienna by Howard, the Berlin correspondent of U.S. Newsweek, detailing the various important factors behind this complex issue.

The Viennese have a witty satire based on Strauss's waltz, called "The United States Occupies the Vienna Woods, the Soviet Union Occupies the Blue Danube." The reason is that American officials and their families live in the stately houses in the "Vienna Woods" in the American occupation zone of Vienna, while the stretch of the Danube River and its tributaries from Enns in Upper Austria to the Black Sea is under Soviet control.

### American Control of One-Fifth

— Translated by Zuo Xi

In reality, the United States controls one-fifth of the Danube River's total length, including a narrow upstream section from Ulm in Baden-Württemberg to Linz within the American occupation zone of Austria. From there, it extends along the southern bank to Enns, where the Soviets have placed underwater cables. In the river section above Linz, the Americans possess one-third of the Danube River fleet. This fleet comprises 971 vessels from Austria, Czechoslovakia, Yugoslavia, Hungary, France, Bulgaria, Romania, and other countries. These ships were used to transport retreating German expatriates and supplies during the final months of the war, or they fled upstream after the war to avoid capture by Soviet forces. Among them are 34 passenger ships, 105 tugboats, 55 motor barges and tankers. The rest are ordinary barges, oil barges, crane ships and other assorted vessels.

American engineers have cleared the river channel in Germany from Linz to Regensburg and have established three Danube shipping companies to manage navigation on a 296-mile stretch of the river within the American occupation zone. These three companies are the Bavarian Danube Shipping Company (DDSG), the Bavarian Lloyd Shipping Company, and the Comos Shipping Company, which is owned by Bavaria but chartered by the Netherlands. Of the 971 vessels, 390 are assets of the Danube Shipping Company. All the ships and other equipment of this company within the Soviet occupation zone were confiscated last winter by the Soviets as "German assets" under the Potsdam Agreement. Therefore, the company has requested that the Americans not allow the ships to sail until the issue of the Danube Shipping Company's sovereignty is resolved. The company demands that the Soviet side return the confiscated dock equipment and 220 vessels. Although the Austrian Parliament has nationalized the company, making it an asset of the Austrian government, the Soviet Union continues to occupy and operate these vessels and equipment under the name of the Danube Shipping Company.

The Blue Disappears

There is a Viennese saying that the Danube always appears blue in the eyes of a lover. But in fact, this putty-colored river that winds around Vienna like a dead snake shows no trace of blue. Seen from the sky, it resembles a flypaper, covered with dilapidated docks and warehouses, scattered with collapsed bridges, and the protruding hulls of various sunken ships. Viennese people like to gather along the riverbank, resting and reading under the trees, and even brave swimmers venture into this river. However, the stretch from Linz to below Vienna is entirely stagnant, foul-smelling water, with no ships sailing on it. The port of Vienna, with its ten-mile long docks and wharves, used to handle 1.7 million tons of shipping annually before the war, but now it is silted up and desolate.

According to the Soviets, the choke point of the Danube is the Tulln bridge, about fifteen miles from Vienna, which was destroyed by Hitler's guards. All five spans of the bridge are broken and submerged in the river. Recovering the Tulln bridge from the riverbed must be carried out in dangerous rapids and requires specially trained engineers, workers, and underwater equipment, all of which the Soviets lack. Austrian engineers estimate that, under normal conditions, with appropriate tools and labor, the bridge could be completely recovered in 30 to 40 days. However, the Soviets, using only one crane and a few Austrian workers, have only recovered one span of the bridge in nine months.

State of Stagnation

In the second week of July of this year, the Soviet Danube Shipping Headquarters in Budapest, Hungary, sent a circular to all shipping companies under Soviet control, announcing that the section of the Danube River from Vienna to the Black Sea was open for navigation. Companies only needed to give 24 hours' notice to confirm weather and river conditions and could freely use that section of the river. Only the Czech shipping company has accepted this Soviet proposal, in order to transport 2,000 tons of wood pulp from Bulgaria to Bratislava, Czech Republic.

Below Bratislava, the extent of navigational hazards, tariff regulations, border inspections, various taxes, and loading and unloading fees are unclear, and there are many restrictions. Even the extent of Soviet control is unknown.

There is no regular commercial transport on the Danube. Only about 300 ships operate in Bulgaria, Romania, and Yugoslavia, and about 30 to 50 operate in Hungary. However, due to the danger of mines and obstacles, transport is limited to one-way transactions and is extremely risky. British and American officials are well aware that the Soviets currently control the northern estuary leading to the Black Sea, but they admit that they know nothing about the situation at the other two estuaries and the Sulina branch to the Black Sea.

It is a very obvious fact that before World War II, the Danube was the trade artery of seven Central European countries, with a total of about 3,000 ships traveling to and fro, handling 3.5 million to 4 million tons of cargo annually. Now, however, it is in a state of stagnation. While Europe is looking forward to a peaceful resolution, these seven countries could have rebuilt their collapsed economies through Danube trade, but instead they are eking out a living on UN relief supplies and a small amount of freight.

### Potsdam

— Translated by Zuo Qi

After Germany surrendered in May 1945, the three major powers, Britain (Churchill and Attlee), the United States (Truman), and the Soviet Union (Stalin), held a meeting in Potsdam in July to lay the foundation for lasting world peace and discuss plans to intensify the war against Japan. As a result, the name "Potsdam" became famous worldwide and became a news term in newspapers.

Potsdam is located about eighteen miles southwest of Berlin, Germany, on the Havel River. The area is beautifully scenic and has historically been the site of German imperial residences, with magnificent buildings. The Potsdam Conference was held in the old imperial palace.

Earlier, there were reports that the three major powers would meet again in Potsdam to discuss intractable international disputes, but this did not materialize.

Recently, the American Association for Chinese Policy accused the government of having made secret agreements concerning China at the Potsdam Conference and demanded that they be announced, but the US State Department denied this.

### Congratulatory Message on Shaw's Birthday

First, I doubt that there has ever been a greater intellectual and satirist in human history who never attacked or disliked private individuals. Like general second-rate satirists, or even great predecessors such as Voltaire and Aristophanes, he never thought of hurting others as they did. He attacked conservatism and excessive rhetoric, superstition and science, as long as he thought one of them was overbearing, he would attack it regardless of everything. He also has a rare asset: the courage of genius, or I might even call it the stubbornness of genius. I still remember when his first play was published, everyone thought it could not be put on stage. International Oddities and News

### King of Turtles

— Yu Qiang

Regardless of whether a profession is high or low, large or small, as long as it is managed properly, "every trade produces its master." There are two brothers in New York's Broadway—Al Gold and Stanton Gold—who have become rich by selling painted turtles, which can be said to be "masters" outside of the three hundred and sixty trades.

Eight years ago, the Gold brothers started this novel business. After several years of hard work, the business prospered, and they have become wholesalers of "hard-shelled products." Gift shops and dime stores all over the United States sell these novel little gadgets. In addition to the old store on Broadway, they have also added several branches in midtown Manhattan.

These "hard-shelled products" are turtles painted with various colors, about the size of a pocket watch, with an average price of forty-five cents each. Customers can choose a name or a few words to engrave on the turtle's back with indelible ink. Some people engrave the name of their girlfriend, some use it as a birthday or wedding gift, some use it for advertising or display, and some even use it for proposing marriage.

Hollywood star Bing Crosby ordered a batch of "hard-shelled products" for his four children. Shirley Temple, Deanna Durbin, and other Hollywood stars are all fond of this little gadget, and even the serious Charlie Chaplin could not "avoid the trend." Former British Prime Minister Chamberlain once ordered fifty turtles by mail and gave them to his grandchildren. The package for the turtles is a specially made box with ventilation holes, lined with damp moss.

There are many reasons to buy this slow-moving animal. Parents often buy two larger turtles to bring home and paint for their children. A week later, they buy a smaller one, and when the children are asleep at night, they put the small turtle with its "parents." The next morning, the children discover this "miracle" and shout with joy, saying that the little turtle was brought by "cranes."

Some people believe that turtles can bring good luck, so they buy one for "good luck." There are also some people who are too lazy to reply to letters and feel bad about it, so they send a turtle with the inscription: "My reply is as slow as this guy."

The Gold brothers' most sincere customer was a father with two children. He bought three turtles and made a fortune. He had the names of his two children engraved on two turtles respectively, and the name of his family's good friend Nana engraved on the other. Later, Nana visited his house and saw her "namesake." She was moved by the gesture of friendship and took out a sum of money to establish an education fund for the two children.

The Gold brothers have turtle farms in two towns in Louisiana. About 15,000 turtles are shipped to New York every week, all arranged in rows in boxes filled with moss, most of which come from Louisiana. After arriving in New York, the turtles are placed in large buckets, waiting to be "manufactured." The first step is to apply a layer of protective white paint on the turtle's shell. Then, the little things are picked up by the dozen with a yardstick, and a skilled female worker brushes them with a brush, and then puts them on a rack to dry. The next step is to apply a thick layer of enamel—red, yellow, and blue are the three most popular colors.

If the turtle is large, it is suitable for birthdays or weddings. After the turtle is selected, appropriate patterns are painted on its back with special pigments. The "finished" turtles are displayed in buckets partially filled with water—the water in the buckets is warm in winter to prevent hibernation, and cool in summer to "relieve the heat."

The Gold brothers sell a special turtle food containing dried ant eggs and dried flies, the former from Finland and the latter from Mexico.

The Gold brothers also sell another novel thing: small American alligators. However, the "King of Turtles" complained that alligators are not very docile, nor are they easy to obtain year-round, and they do not have much attractive magic. Therefore, from a business standpoint, the Gold brothers still prefer turtles.

### National Treasure, White-Haired Youth - The Humorous Mr. Wu Zhihui (Part 1)

— Jifeng

Everyone is familiar with this aloof and humorous Mr. Wu Zhihui. When senior government officials took office, he was a veteran supervisor, always giving a speech, with a few witty remarks interspersed in the solemnity. On the 15th of this month, Mr. Fang Zhi, the new chairman of the Shanghai Municipal Party Headquarters, was sworn in, and the officiating officer was none other than the 81-year-old Mr. Wu. Recently, Mr. Wu also presided over the Mandarin Promotion Committee in Shanghai. (See this newspaper on the 16th.)

Morality and Scholarship

Mr. Wu is a veteran of the Kuomintang, with morality and scholarship crowning his contemporaries. Chairman Chiang Kai-shek respects him as a "national treasure," Feng Yuxiang once called him a "white-haired youth," and Hu Shih praised him as "one of the great thinkers of the past three hundred years." He is extremely aloof, striving to restrain himself. Throughout his life, he has taken not being an official, not riding in rickshaws, and not using servants as his principles. When he was in Chongqing, he often wore old cloth clothes, carrying a cane, and walked to and fro on the road. The "anti-war house" where he lived had thin tiles and a bamboo frame. It was probably built with bamboo and mud. The furnishings were very simple, and the room could only accommodate a few people.

Name Changed to Jingheng

Mr. Wu was originally from Xueyanqiao, Wujin. When he was young, he went to Yanghu County (under Changzhou Prefecture) to take the children's examination. Because his accent sounded like a Wuxi person, the other candidates suspected that he was impersonating and beat him up. Therefore, Mr. Wu hated it extremely and identified himself as a Wuxi person for the rest of his life. In the seventeenth year of Guangxu, he passed the provincial examination. However, he was extremely opposed to feudal ideas. Once, when the Wu clan was compiling its genealogy, he did not approve of it and angrily overturned all the character plates, which caused great hatred from the clan leader and public relations. He had no choice but to escape from his hometown. In the twenty-fourth year of Guangxu, he served as the president of Nanyang Public School in Shanghai. Together with Yuan He Chen Xiaozhi, he agreed not to read thread-bound books again. He also believed that "lacking reverence and knowledge is not enough to bear the weight of the world," so he changed his name to Jingheng.

In the tenth year of the Republic of China, he served as the vice president of the Sino-French University in Lyon, leading a group of students abroad. One day, Mr. Wu found a pair of pants on the ship. He personally washed them clean and then issued a notice: "A pair of stinky pants was found on the ground and has been washed clean. Whose are they? Please claim them." Sometimes, when students threw melon seeds and nutshells everywhere, Mr. Wu also personally held a broom and swept them clean.

Vowed Not to Be an Official

In the thirteenth year of the Republic of China, he served as a central supervisory committee member and has been re-elected ever since. As a founding elder of the country, he is already the most veteran of the veterans. Also, because he vowed not to be an official, he does not hold any positions other than supervisory committee member, and he does not easily attend unless it is an important meeting. Therefore, he has also earned the title of "reclusive celebrity in the court." (To be continued)
