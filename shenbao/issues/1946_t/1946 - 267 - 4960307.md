### Four Foreign Ministers Gather in Paris

The Paris Peace Conference has been going on for a month, but no agreement has been reached. All parties are concerned and eager. The war has just ended, and the opinions of various countries on rebuilding the peaceful order in Europe are not entirely the same, and they do not want to act hastily. Later, we saw US Secretary of State Byrnes's speech in Germany and Soviet Foreign Minister Molotov's speech at the Peace Conference Subcommittee, each clarifying the foreign policies of the United States and the Soviet Union. At this time, US Secretary of Commerce Wallace gave a speech in the United States, advocating appeasement of the Soviet Union. As a result, public opinion was in an uproar and condemned him. Based on the developments of the past few weeks, we would like to make a forecast of international politics.

The world situation in the year since the armistice has obviously stopped all the coordination during the war due to the cessation of hostilities. Originally, the maintenance of this coordination lacked a considerable practical basis and was only a wartime compromise. In order to maintain this wartime compromise, even Roosevelt, who advocated international justice, did not hesitate to abandon his principles and his position, and not only built a high iron curtain with the Soviet Union, but also spread its "new democracy" of socialist countries outside the iron curtain. ### The Necessity of Suppressing High Interest Rates

High interest rates are one of the most troublesome problems currently faced by the industrial and commercial sectors.

On one hand, there is inflation, and on the other hand, there is a sense of excess capital with no way out. According to normal theory, interest rates on short-term loans should be low at this time, and high interest rates should not occur. However, theory is theory, and reality is reality. This kind of contradictory phenomenon is not the only one in the current economic sphere.

As a result, the Four Banks' tight monetary policy has attracted special attention from the general public. All kinds of loans are being applied for continuously by various industries and businesses in various places; the deposit and withdrawal amounts of commercial firms and banks are also being discussed; whether loans of more than 50 million must be approved by the Four Banks is also being criticized; and some people have even raised the fundamental question of whether the Four Banks themselves should continue to exist.

There are, of course, legitimate reasons for the Four Banks to tighten the money supply and refuse to lend. Judging from the intention, stabilizing prices and avoiding further stimulation are the main reasons. Recalling the beginning of the establishment of the grain loan, within two or three days, rice prices in various places experienced a great surge. Therefore, their cautious attitude can also be said to be well-founded.

However, the existence of high interest rates is a fact, the difficulty of turnover felt by the general industrial and commercial sectors is also a fact, and the closure of many factories and shops is even more of a fact. It is said that the current interest rate in Japan is only about two percent, and in Taiwan it is only about six percent. Then, whether the short-term interest rate of one and a half or even two percent in this port is too high for the industrial and commercial sectors that need to compete with others, and whether it should be improved, really does not need further discussion. Have those officials and financial giants who preside over the Four Banks never thought of this?

There are two main reasons for high prices: one is inflation, and the other is insufficient supply. These are the most obvious facts. Because of the result of tightening the money supply, many production industries have been unable to sustain themselves; the supply of materials will inevitably gradually decrease in the future. Is this situation indirectly raising prices? This issue should not be ignored!

Moreover, the rising trend of prices does not seem to be stopped or reversed by tightening the money supply. In recent days, major commodities such as gold, U.S. dollars, yarn, cloth, rice, wheat, flour, cooking oil, woolen cloth, and rayon have not been soaring again? On the one hand, the rising trend remains the same, and tightening the money supply does not have the slightest inhibitory effect. On the other hand, it has forced industrial and commercial sectors to collapse and indirectly promoted the rising trend. We believe that this approach has indeed reached the stage where it should "correct mistakes when they are known". Be careful not to stubbornly continue to make mistakes to the end, causing China's industrial and commercial sectors to be destroyed.

### Comparative Research on New and Old Income Taxes

— Xu Riqing

(3) Implementation of Comprehensive Income Tax

The previous classified income tax system in our country levied taxes based on income, such as profits from enterprises, interest from deposits, and rental income from houses. Therefore, it can be called an income tax. It failed to meet the principle of fair burden based on ability. Comprehensive income tax, however, taxes all income, so it is also known as general income tax. It uses the individual income of taxpayers as the calculation unit, so it is also called ad personam tax, that is, the total income of all kinds of income of each taxpayer is calculated, and then various expenses are deducted from the total income as the tax standard. For example, France implements comprehensive income tax, stipulating that all people in France with an annual income of more than 7,000 francs must pay this tax.

Article 3 of the new income tax law stipulates that in addition to the classified income tax levied in accordance with the preceding article, individuals whose total income exceeds 600,000 yuan shall be subject to a comprehensive income tax. The calculation method is to combine the individual's various incomes for the whole year into a total income, but the following items can be deducted: 1. 100,000 yuan per person for family members living together or relatives who must be supported; 2. 50,000 yuan per person for family members who are students in secondary schools or above; 3. Various income taxes and land taxes already paid (Article 19 of the same law). The method of declaration is that the obligated person shall report the type and amount of income of the previous year to the主管征收機關 (competent collection authority) before May 1 each year. The 主管征收機關 (competent collection authority) and the district and township offices or central primary schools establish a joint declaration association for review (Article 26 of the same law) to avoid the malpractice of false reports or non-reporting.

In the past, our country used the excuse of lax household registration and incomplete statistics to not implement the comprehensive income tax. This resolute decision to implement it this time is not without considerable reason. In the future, the collection of comprehensive income tax will be fraught with difficulties, which is inevitable. As the saying goes, "Human effort can overcome heaven," and it is expected that there will be a day to overcome these difficulties.

At the beginning of the establishment of the comprehensive income tax, people in the society and the public opinion circles feel that there is a suspicion of duplication. In fact, the classified income tax is a tax on income, and the comprehensive income tax is a tax on people, and there is no duplication, because the objects are different. The comprehensive income tax is most in line with the principle of ability to pay, and at the same time, the progressive tax system must adopt progressive tax rates. Our country's classified income tax is limited by the environment and insufficient conditions, and has failed to give full play to its effectiveness, so that those who have more pay more, and those who have less pay less. Taking the income from securities deposits as an example, both 10,000 yuan and 10,000,000 yuan of deposit income are subject to 10%, which is unreasonable. Therefore, the government adopts comprehensive income tax to make up for its deficiencies and prevent the concentration of wealth. The original intention of the legislation is perfect, and whether the implementation can be smooth remains to be seen the efforts of those in charge. Furthermore, considering the advanced countries, those who first levy classified income tax and then levy comprehensive income tax include France, the United Kingdom, and Italy. Moreover, the tax rates of comprehensive income tax in various countries are higher than those in our country. ### Travelogue in Northern Jiangsu (6) Blood Shadows on the Battlefield East of Taixian County

— Special Correspondent Hang Zhou

Taixian Dispatch, 10th of This Month

There are two long embankments in northern Jiangsu that are well known: on the left is the Grand Canal embankment, and on the right is the 范公堤 (Fan Gong Dyke). Between the embankments is a large area of lakes and marshes. In the past, people feared every year that floods would collapse the Grand Canal embankment, turning this area into a swamp. Since the Communist Party's army came to northern Jiangsu, people's fear of floods has been transferred. —— There are too many examples to prove that wherever the Communist army goes, it becomes a "sea of blood." —— "Sea of blood" is a more terrifying term than "flood"!

The topography of northern Jiangsu limits military activities, so the battles are fought either on the Grand Canal embankment or on the Fan Gong Dyke. When the Shaobo battle in late August was in full swing, the Communist army launched a sudden attack on the right flank, and battles broke out once again in places like Rugao, Haian, Qutang, and Huangqiao. The people who had just returned to their hometowns once again embarked on the road to destruction.

As a result, the focus of the war quickly shifted to the right. The Communist army's offensive was obviously not only echoing the left flank, but also attempting to further seize Liangtai and threaten Jiangnan. More than a month ago, the Communist army had made such an attempt and suffered a crushing defeat. Now it is reorganizing its forces, and the national army must of course crush it again.

Second Phase of the War Second Act

The situation on the right flank is dangerous, and the Communist army is very rampant. Taking Haian as an example, the Communist army used twelve regiments to surround the national army, which was only one-fifth of its size, but after fighting for more than a week, the Communist army could not move Haian even a little bit. Qutang and Daxiaobaimi have already fallen into the hands of the Communist army. The situation in Huangqiao was once unclear, and fighting is still going on nearby. In the bag-shaped area from Rugao to Baimi in the northwest and Huangqiao in the southwest, there are even more mobile Communist troops. Large and small battles are constantly taking place on the periphery of Taixian and Taixing counties.

The right flank is "lively," while the left flank appears silent. On September 6, the reporter boarded a "wild chicken" bus running on the Yangtai Road in Yangzhou to see the second act of the second phase of the war in northern Jiangsu.

The Yangtai Road is a major artery in northern Jiangsu at present. The national army has put in a lot of effort to open it up and maintain it. There are many fortresses on both sides of the road. These fortresses have all been stained with blood in front of them. Although they can no longer be seen now, I have seen one by one the smiles of the farmers who are harvesting the abundant rice crop in exchange for those bloodstains. This year is a great harvest year in northern Jiangsu. The Chinese farmers who "depend on the sky for food" should have thanked God, but in Gaoyou, Baoying... in those so-called "liberated areas," how many farmers are still shedding tears over their harvest!

Universal Vigor and Vitality Doing What One Says

After passing through checkpoints one after another, I arrived in Taixian. This is the reporter's second trip to Tai. Yangzhou is like a middle-aged woman, but Taixian is much younger. What I feel is universal vigor and vitality. County Magistrate Ding Zuobin is a graduate of the 16th class of the military academy and has served as a guerrilla commander. He is a complete soldier. The district chiefs, township chiefs, militia captains, and even the secretaries and section chiefs in the county government all have relationships with the military academy and guerrilla warfare. They have shared hardships and are now facing hardships. They usually joke around, but when they get down to business, they are really energetic and quick. Among the counties controlled by the national army in northern Jiangsu, Taixian can be said to be the one that cooperates with the military the most. Repairing roads and building fortifications are done as soon as they are said. Ding Zuobin cannot sit idly in the county government office for three days. He likes to run around and see everything. Some things require four or five official documents to be transferred and put on hold for ten days and eight days. He goes to the countryside himself and solves them with a single sentence.

The soldiers now are not all roughnecks, and some have quite good political cultivation. Ding Zuobin has the straightforward and enthusiastic personality of a northerner. The people of Taixian are proud to have such a county magistrate. He said that he doesn't know how to be an official, he only knows how to do things. He scolds his subordinates "not to spoil his affairs."

Jiangnan is a land of fish and rice, while Jiangbei is a land of salt and rice. They are both equally rich. If Jiangnan and Jiangbei have different sentiments, the most obvious may be in terms of the people's character: Jiangnan is weaker, while Jiangbei is quite strong. Strong people with organization are a very good local armed force. The general feeling in northern Jiangsu is that the local armed forces are not enough, but Taixian has silently made quite a bit of progress. Every township has a militia, with more than 2,000 people and more than 1,000 guns, helping the army a lot.

In the past two days, Dasizhuang, which is more than 20 miles southeast of Taixian and 18 miles away from the Kou-Tai Highway, was attacked by the Communist army. There were no national troops stationed there, and the militia resisted on its own, not allowing the Communist army to take advantage of it. Ding Zuobin received the report and personally led four or five hundred militiamen to reinforce it, driving the Communist army away in one fell swoop.

Jiangyan Approaching the Front Line Listening to Machine Gun Fire

On September 7, the reporter, accompanied by Wu Xiaode, Chief of Staff of the 108th Brigade, and Staff Officer Gao Hongsheng, inspected the eastern battlefield of Taixian, and Ding Zuobin also went along.

Starting early in the morning, we first went to Jiangyan. On the Tai-Jiang Road, commercial vehicles were nowhere to be seen, and military vehicles rushed around, quickly covering 36 miles. Jiangyan is a large town and a distribution center for grain. In previous years, the grain transport ships that stopped here were connected for three or four miles. It is only ten miles away from Magou, the front line here, and the sound of machine guns can be heard clearly every night. Having gotten used to it, no one is disturbed by it. A bathhouse is still painting its facade and choosing a lucky day to open. We didn't stay long and rushed to Magou.

In the sense of defending Taixian, Magou is a very important stronghold. Since the Communist army launched its offensive, it has cut off the Tai-Hai Road. On the evening of August 29, it gathered more than two regiments and took advantage of the fact that the national army had just changed its defenses, and rushed straight to Magou, hoping to take it in one fell swoop, coerce Jiangyan, and threaten Taixian. The garrison in Magou only had one company, but this company was not flustered at all. "Fighting doesn't depend on having a lot of people," they calmly guarded the fortifications, killing one when it came, and killing two together. The Communist army surrounded Magou and charged again and again, and those who came up could not go back. Company Commander Zheng Yadong listened to the Communist army's charge horn in the trench. It blew a total of 20 times, indicating that the 19 charges had all failed, and the 20th charge could not avoid its final fate, and they also fell down. The dark night passed away in the battle, and the sound of gunfire on the other side died down. The brothers jumped out of the trenches to greet the rays of the sun and took a look: "Ah! So many corpses!" They were so excited that they forgot their fatigue and counted them. There were three or four hundred bodies.

As soon as I arrived in Magou, the brothers of Zheng Yadong's company scrambled to tell me about this wonderful battle. I deliberately spoiled their fun and asked:

"How many casualties did you have?"

"One dead and four wounded," a soldier replied triumphantly.

One to four hundred, or five to two thousand (the total number of Communist army casualties), this record must make the Communist army gnash their teeth.

Sure enough, the impetuous Communist army came to "avenge" every night after that, but every night they brought corpses as gifts.

We once stood on a fortress to observe the terrain. I asked Battalion Commander Zhao Haiyun:

"How far is this from the Communist army's outpost?"

"Half a mile at most."

"Isn't it dangerous?" I asked in a very bookish tone.

"They dare not - the Communist army comes out at night."

Parachutes Ejected Supplying the Garrison

Back in Jiangyan, it was 3 p.m. After resting for a while, when we were preparing to return to Taixian, a bomber flew over the sky, slowly circling, seemingly looking for a target. Having seen too many planes, we paid no attention to it, when suddenly cries of alarm erupted from all around: "Dropping bombs!" People were shouting and running. I was also taken aback. Looking up, I saw several parachutes floating in the sky, swaying as they descended. "Not like bombs," I thought intuitively: "Bombs shouldn't be dropped here." But if it's not a bomb, it shouldn't be dropped here either. I thought again: "The plane must have mistaken the place."

The things that fell from the parachutes did not explode. "The plane probably mistook Jiangyan for Haian. The besieged garrison in Haian needs supplies." I thought further.

However, the plane did not realize its mistake, and it continued to eject parachutes one by one like a lotus flower. The villagers had never seen this thing before and rushed to the fields to search for it. Wu Zhifen, the commander of the 324th Regiment, ordered all the dropped items to be concentrated at the regimental headquarters for disposal.

At dusk, we returned to Taixian. The brigade contacted the air force and confirmed that the batch of things was to supply the garrison in Haian.

Although the siege of Haian has not been completely lifted, and the Communist troops in other places are still harassing everywhere, the dangerous period for northern Jiangsu has passed after the national army's redeployment. The army and people of northern Jiangsu have faith. "Where did the New Fourth Army come from? Beat it back where it came from!" A wild child adapted a song and sang. The Mid-Autumn Festival is coming! "The round moon shines on Kyushu, some families are happy and some are sad?" The food shops in Taixian started preparing two days ago to celebrate this poetic and picturesque festival, but the refugees who have no home to return to, sadly facing the bright moon, don't know how much new sorrow has been added!

### Washing and Preserving Wool Products

— Pei

The Special Qualities of Wool: Before discussing how to wash wool products, we should first know the special qualities of wool. The main component of wool, the wool fiber, has what is called an outer scale, which must not be rubbed vigorously. If rubbed too vigorously, the fluff on the wool yarn will form clumps, losing its original beauty. Wool yarns have a very weak alkaline reaction, so you must use high-quality soap when washing them, otherwise the wool yarn will easily become stiff. If you are washing colored wool yarn, and there is a possibility of fading when washing, you must first use a color-fixing method before washing. The color-fixing method is to mix one teaspoon of tannic acid and one teaspoon of acetic acid in one liter of clean water, put the item to be washed in it and soak for five or six minutes, and then wash it.

— Yuan

The method of supervision should have common sense. You will naturally be able to achieve the subtlety of commanding at will!

As for lazy and dissolute people, who steal time and enjoy leisure, or are stubborn and difficult to train, even if the wages are low, the gains and losses are not enough to offset them. Therefore, the best servants are those who are diligent and thrifty, and who are gentle in nature and have no heart of arrogance or rudeness. This is considered qualified. Conversely, it is not easy to manage them. Even if they have special skills, they should not be hired, lest they cause trouble later.

For people living at home, clean clothes and utensils are the most important thing, and so are servants. Gorgeous clothes are not appropriate, and damaged and dirty clothes are also unsightly. The reason why clothes are dirty and tattered is not because of poverty, but mostly because of laziness and laziness in washing and mending. As the saying goes, "The effort of one stitch, when sewing is timely, is enough to save the labor of nine stitches." If a servant does not know how to clean themselves, it is impossible to expect them to be diligent in other things. It is like climbing a tree to catch fish, which is impossible. As for servants flaunting their clothes and dressing up like their mistresses, it is also not appropriate. The master should patiently guide them, teach them to save, and explain the benefits and harms of extravagance. She should tell them that the money earned from labor is very little and should not be wasted at will. She should also tell them that people who earn a living through physical labor sometimes have to stop working, or they may suddenly suffer from old age or illness, and they will need money for support. If they do not save regularly, when something happens, they will not know where to get it in a hurry. In general, all hired workers should be diligent and thrifty and not be extravagant. If clothes are torn, they should be mended, not thrown away. They should not indulge in eating and drinking. As for shopping and socializing, they should also be restrained at all times to save money. As the ancient saying goes, "Saving one penny is the same as earning one penny." When they reach the end of the road, it will be too late to regret their mistakes!

All of these are their duties. However, cooking and washing each have specific duties, which cannot be explained in a simple way. In wealthy families, there are many servants, and each has their own job, such as cooks and wet nurses. Even if this is not the case, the master must assign their duties and cannot entrust everything to one person. However, sweeping and cleaning the house and cleaning the utensils are the usual duties of servants. Now let me talk about them as follows:

Get up at dawn, open the windows and doors, and roll up the curtains to let fresh air into the room. If it is winter, and there is a stove in the room, you must first remove the ashes and leftover charcoal, and then sweep the floor. When removing the ashes, the technique should be gentle, and it is best to cover the gold and porcelain utensils, chairs, and wall clothes in the room with cloth to prevent dust from collecting. When sweeping the floor, first sprinkle water on it, which can also reduce the spread of dust. After sweeping, wipe the windows. All glass cabinets, fences, and the edges of mirrors and picture frames must be wiped. If there are copper or steel utensils, they should be rubbed with soft leather to prevent them from rusting. If rust is seen, it must be sanded with sandpaper or coated with lime, and it must be smooth before stopping. Other items, such as tables, chairs, and frames, must be wiped so that there are no stains left. All of these things should be done before the master gets up. Once the master gets up, the bedroom must be cleaned, the bedding must be spread, and it must be swept and wiped as described above.

If there are vases or water-filled basins in the room, they must be emptied and refilled with clean water every day.

Every few days, the quilts and pillows should be exposed to the sun. Other things, such as sweeping the stairs or beating the carpets, must be done on sunny days and when you have free time. Do not annoy the master. In the evening, close the windows and lock the doors. Do not be lazy or forgetful. These are also the duties of servants.

The above is only a brief discussion, but you must provide guidance in a proper way so that you can enjoy the benefits. If there are any mistakes, you must gently persuade them and show them kindness so that the servants will return with gratitude and be sincerely convinced. Otherwise, even if you spend a lot of money, you will not be able to buy their loyalty, which is not the best policy.
