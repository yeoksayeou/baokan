### Avoiding Unnecessary Suffering

Yesterday, we discussed how Confucius was a great statesman. His political philosophy lay in [winning the hearts of the people]. He first sought social stability, and then he could achieve the harmonious submission of the populace. He started with the two points of "[not worrying about scarcity but worrying about inequality, not worrying about poverty but worrying about instability]" to seek [equality] and [stability] in society. He also used the positive spirit of "[working so hard that he forgets to eat, enjoying himself so much that he forgets his worries, and not realizing that old age is approaching]" to seek the realization of his propositions.

In fact, the way of governance lies in winning the hearts of the people. This has been a principle of governance in history, both ancient and modern, Chinese and foreign. It can also be said that those who win the hearts of the people will have a prosperous country, and those who lose the hearts of the people will have a declining country. This has been a law of history in any country. In Confucius's time, it was a monarchical state, with the ruler as the center, but it still emphasized the hearts of the people, because "[the people are the foundation of the state]." If the people are not valued, then a country without people cannot stand in the world, which is undeniable.

Indeed, as Mr. Li, the chairman, said in his address at the inaugural meeting of the China News Association the other day: "[China is a 'Republic,' the Chinese government is the 'National Government,' and some even say that 'officials are public servants of the people.' Therefore, whether objectively or subjectively, the interests of the people must be given top priority, especially the suffering of the people, which should be specifically pointed out in detail, and relevant parties should be urged to pay attention to improvement jointly]." China has always been based on the people as the main body, so the interests of the people should naturally be given more importance. Therefore, the object of politics must be the interests of the people. The government is originally a rigid machine, but it is the rulers in the government who are flexible. Therefore, the rulers should go deep among the people. Although they cannot always personally experience the lives of the people, they should at least always pay attention to the sufferings of the people and always listen to the opinions of the people. With the feeling of drowning when others drown, and being hungry when others are hungry, they should plan ways to relieve the suffering of the people. This is what it means to win the hearts of the people.

However, the present time is wartime. The people's lives in wartime cannot be compared with those in peacetime. The people's lives in wartime are destined to be hard and to increase the burden. Indeed, this is a matter of course. However, we must also understand that there should be a limit to suffering and increasing the burden. In order to benefit the war and strive for victory, we should grit our teeth and endure hardship under any circumstances. However, taking advantage of the name of wartime to increase unnecessary burdens and suffer unnecessary hardships will not only not benefit the war, but also hinder the war, because unnecessary suffering is very likely to increase popular resentment and invisibly reduce the potential for victory. Therefore, the rulers should do their best to avoid unnecessary suffering for the people. For example, black market rice costing 12,000 yuan per picul is an unnecessary suffering. If the rulers manage the rice policy well, the black market price of rice would not be so high. Another example is the black market for train and ship tickets, where the value is several times higher than the ticket price, even though there is a nominal price, it is impossible to buy a ticket. These are all unnecessary sufferings. There are countless others of this nature.

Secondly, talking about war, modern war is a war of the whole people, a war of total strength, and also a war of production. In other words, every person must hold fast to their post and exert all their strength to contribute to the war. Therefore, production is necessary for war, and production is necessary for cooperation in the war. However, the current situation is the opposite of increasing production, hindering production at any time and any place, and even the people's necessities of life cannot maintain the minimum needs. Then, cooperation in the war will inevitably be just empty talk.

### Lack of Skilled Workers

Although companies are doing their best to improve the welfare of workers, in many factories, while the number of aircraft workers has not decreased, women, unqualified conscripts, and the elderly account for about 63% of the workers, and the number of people with low work efficiency is constantly increasing. The biggest problem in the United States today is finding skilled workers for [direct] labor; that is, people who can directly manufacture aircraft, not white-collar clerks engaged in [indirect] labor. From January 1941 to August 1942, the Selective Service System conscripted 66,000 skilled workers from factories on the West Coast. Now, another 58,000 have been conscripted. This is about one-fifth of the workforce. These 58,000 workers normally produce about 800 bombers and fighters per month, so the actual loss in production is enormous. Most of those conscripted are skilled workers, and their work will inevitably be handed over to others. The replacements cannot be women, people with disabilities, and the elderly. As a result, these people can never be as fast and efficient as their predecessors. One of the largest aircraft companies in the United States, Douglas Aircraft Company, stated: [The Selective Service System's conscription has limited the number of aircraft that Douglas Aircraft Company can build. The second replacement plan has further reduced production.] Finally, it is proposed to further mechanize the current facilities to compensate for the reduction in personnel. However, the productivity of machinery is also approaching its limit. During the first six months of last year, the labor time required to build an aircraft was reduced by 33%.
