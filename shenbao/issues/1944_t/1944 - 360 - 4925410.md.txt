### 歲暮反省

The year 1944 is coming to an end today.

Another year is about to pass. What have we actually done during this year?

In his New Year's Day address this year, the late Chairman Wang instructed us that this year should be directed towards three major tasks: "rectifying thought," "ensuring security," and "increasing production," for further efforts. How have we actually worked towards these goals? What achievements have we made? Or have we regressed?

In his letter to the citizens of Shanghai on New Year's Day, acting Chairman Chen pointed out that there was an anomaly and a contradiction in Shanghai. The anomaly was that the normal income of the general public was not enough to make a living, leading to a precarious situation for the law and order and social unrest. The contradiction was that production should increase, but it was reduced due to a lack of fuel, and hoarding raw materials for profit made people unwilling to produce, so that the front lines could not receive sufficient supplies and the rear could not enjoy a proper life. These situations are not unique to Shanghai; many places are similar. Have we overcome this anomaly and contradiction this year? How much have we overcome? Or has the situation worsened?

The newspaper's editorial on New Year's Day also put forward three hopes for this year: first, "hope for a clean and enlightened government"; second, "hope to alleviate the suffering of the people"; and third, "hope to fulfill the mission of participating in the war." How much of our hopes have been realized during the year? Or has the disappointment become even greater?

The late Chairman Wang's instruction to "rectify thought" and our hope for "a clean and enlightened government" are inseparable. Only by "rectifying thought" can we achieve "a clean and enlightened government," and only with a clean and enlightened government can we rectify thought. In "rectifying thought," the late Chairman Wang also instructed us: "All things that hinder the uplift of the will to fight a decisive battle and discourage the rejuvenation of the national spirit must be completely eliminated and eradicated," such as "corruption and selling of positions, which are the bacteria that erode politics; hoarding and profiteering, which are the pests that poison the livelihood of the people; extortion and blackmail, which are the roots of disaster that disrupt society; and decadence and debauchery, which are the toxins that weaken the race." Since corruption and the selling of positions are the bacteria that erode politics, it is deeply distressing that corruption and selling of positions have repeatedly emerged this year, from the major corruption case of Hou Hu Gu Zhou, the major fraud case of special taxes in Shanghai, and the case of malpractice by officials of the Rice Control Association. How can politics be clean and enlightened? Hoarding and profiteering and extortion and blackmail, in fact, this year, large-scale hoarding and profiteering, manipulating materials, and stimulating price increases have become more rampant than last year, and many have special forces as their background; extortion and blackmail incidents that cannot be carried out without political power or the backing of guns are rampant everywhere this year. Not to mention anything else, the most common thing is that once you go out, whether at the pier, at the station, in vehicles, or even on the road, you will inevitably experience or witness such incidents. If you have anything with you, you are more likely to be extorted. In such a chaotic atmosphere, the decadent and dissolute life, which is enough to weaken the race, will inevitably become more and more unseemly.

Since thought cannot be rectified in this way, and political discipline and social morality have deteriorated so badly, and are even getting worse, how can we complete "ensuring security" and "increasing production"? How can we realize our hopes of "alleviating the suffering of the people" and "fulfilling the mission of participating in the war"?

Not only have bandits not been eliminated in various places, but incidents of extortion and blackmail with political backgrounds and the backing of guns are also heard everywhere, making it even more difficult to ensure security and making it impossible for the people to live and work in peace. Moreover, we are a country participating in the war, and even the rear security of many places still needs to be maintained by Allied forces, which is truly shameful.

Due to the fact that political discipline is not yet sound and security has not been ensured, various economic policies and control measures have not been properly implemented, and material purchase countermeasures and supporting measures have not been made reasonable, the impact on increasing production is naturally significant. The decline of various production industries this year has reached a very serious point, to put it mildly.

Regarding the people's livelihood, the vast majority of our people are constantly affected by poor security, extortion and blackmail, and the ravages of high prices, and their lives have become so difficult that they cannot sustain themselves. The anomalous phenomenon of "the normal income of the general public not being enough to make a living" mentioned by acting Chairman Chen has now become the most common thing. "The people are in dire straits," and perhaps there has never been such a tragedy as it is today. With the people on their last breath, how can we still talk about fulfilling the mission of participating in the war?

We must make a thorough reflection on the above today at the end of the year, and from tomorrow onwards, both the government and the people must work hard to change for the better. If we continue to make empty promises and muddle along, then the future will be unimaginable.

### 抑平物價與執行法規

— Meng Zhou

To suppress and stabilize prices, it is necessary to manage materials, which is a common sentiment among informed people. However, in order to suppress and stabilize prices and manage materials, it is necessary to first formulate complete, appropriate, practical, and easy-to-implement management methods, and then implement them conscientiously according to the methods. For example, land transportation must first open up roads, and then follow the roads forward, so as not to go astray. At present, various laws and regulations have also been formulated regarding the management of prices and materials, but from our perspective, they are still crude and incomplete. For example, at the same time as inventory registration, detailed methods should be clearly defined on how to store, how to distribute, and how to price, so that the competent authorities can have a basis for implementation. If detailed methods are not clearly defined first, and then methods are devised to deal with problems when they arise, it will inevitably lead to arbitrary measures and various malpractices. Therefore, incomplete methods are like having no roads. In places where there are no roads, driving vehicles will inevitably lead to overturning. Also, even if detailed methods have been set for prices and materials, if the methods are not appropriate and difficult to implement, then these methods are like rugged, uneven, and blocked roads, and vehicles cannot drive on them, and the result will inevitably be abandonment. As a result of this situation, the methods set will become mere formalities. Therefore, in order to achieve the purpose of managing materials and suppressing and stabilizing prices, the first step should be to formulate complete, appropriate, practical, and easy-to-implement management methods.

If there are complete, appropriate, practical, and easy-to-implement management methods, but there is no centralized and unified competent authority to implement them, then these methods will not be effective. The organization is like a vehicle. If the vehicle is withered and unsuitable for practical use, then even if the road is flat and can reach straight, it will not be able to drive. At present, regarding the management of prices and materials, there is often competition and mutual evasion between various public organizations, government offices, and military camps in various localities. This is due to the fact that the powers and limits of the various organizations have not been clearly defined. If it can be clearly stipulated that a certain organization is responsible for the management of prices, that a certain organization is responsible for the implementation of allocation, and that a certain organization is responsible for cracking down on hoarding, then the powers and responsibilities will be clear between each other, and the malpractice of competition and evasion can naturally be eliminated. Also, in name, military and police organizations do not have the power to manage prices and materials, but in reality, because the military and police have the strength and indeed have the need for military supplies and security, they inevitably provide excessive assistance on matters related to prices and materials, so incidents of inconsistency between name and reality often occur. If the government cannot enable the competent authorities to have the strength to implement them, and it is difficult to limit the participation of the military and police, then it may be better to consider the facts and clearly stipulate that the military and police will be responsible for managing a certain matter within a certain area and for a certain period of time, so that powers and responsibilities are clearly defined, names and reality are unified, and merits and demerits will not be unaccountable. Therefore, the second step in suppressing and stabilizing prices and managing materials should be to establish a centralized and unified competent authority.

The management methods for prices and materials must be complete, appropriate, practical, and easy to implement, and the organization and powers of the competent authorities must be centralized and unified. These are all matters that laws and regulations should stipulate. Therefore, formulating good laws and regulations is most important for suppressing and stabilizing prices and managing materials. However, with good laws and regulations, there must also be dedicated and law-abiding personnel to be responsible for implementation, so that the duties of the laws and regulations can achieve their purpose. It is like the same situation that after the road is completed and the vehicle is equipped, there must be someone to drive it. However, as long as the personnel responsible for implementing the duties of the laws and regulations can be dedicated and law-abiding, they can be competent, and it is not necessary for them to be geniuses or extraordinary talents. People who claim to be geniuses or extraordinary talents often do not want to strictly adhere to the scope and often destroy laws and regulations. In fact, if a certain law or regulation is difficult to implement, you can request modification, but before it is modified, it must be strictly observed and not allowed to be despised. If a certain law or regulation is not yet complete, you can also explain the law or regulation and formulate a method, submit it to a higher level for approval, and use it as a precedent as a basis, and you cannot arbitrarily advocate and hastily change the example. In short, destroying laws and regulations and arbitrarily changing examples are all convenient for malpractice and become a source of corruption. Therefore, the third step in suppressing and stabilizing prices and managing materials should be to select dedicated and law-abiding personnel to serve in the duties of implementation.

The above three steps are equally important. Chinese people have always advocated rule by people and not rule by law. They do not pay attention to the formulation of laws and regulations, and after the laws and regulations are formulated, they regard them as their text and forget them over time. In this situation, it is truly going in the opposite direction to bring politics into a normal track, so strictly enforcing laws and regulations is a prerequisite for suppressing and stabilizing prices and cannot but be paid attention to. As for the personnel responsible for performing their duties, it is not easy to make them all dedicated and law-abiding. If you are not frugal and hardworking, you cannot be dedicated. If you do not read laws and regulations carefully, you do not know how to abide by the law. Therefore, government offices and public organizations must select diligent, thrifty, and studious people to serve in their duties in order to eliminate malpractices and be incorruptible. However, when it comes to employing people, the authorities tend to regard frivolous and active people as talents, and do not favor diligent, thrifty, and studious people. As the trend goes, being dedicated and law-abiding is considered out of date and becomes a rare phenomenon.

### Forty Days of Drifting at Sea

During the height of the Mariana Islands battle, four brave men, Petty Officer First Class Sesaki, Seaman Tatsuo Kanakawa, Seaman Masao Tani, and Kunio Sakoda, escaped from Omiya Island and drifted at sea for forty days, navigating over a thousand miles, finally returning safely. These four men belonged to the 00 Meteorological Team and were crew members of the 00 ship Asahi Maru (240 tons). They had no connection with the Omiya Island garrison but happened to pass by the island and were caught up in the enemy's attack. Later, under orders from their superiors, they escaped the island, broke through the encirclement of enemy warships and aircraft, and miraculously survived after experiencing many hardships. The following is a summary of what Sesaki and Kanakawa recounted.

Discovery of Enemy Ships

Petty Officer First Class Hazaki and others were ordered to sail the second Asahi Maru back to the mainland via Omiya Island for hull repairs. This happened in early June. On the 11th of the same month, while sailing near Omiya Island, they saw twenty friendly aircraft hurrying towards Saipan Island, sensing something was wrong. That night, they saw the enemy fleet's flares, and the situation at sea was chaotic, confirming the enemy's mobile force was attacking.

The next day (12th), before dawn, they anchored as planned two nautical miles from Showa Street on Omiya Island. At this time, enemy carrier-based aircraft were attacking. The dim sky was filled with enemy planes, and ships in the port and ground troops fiercely fought against them. Soon, the Asahi Maru was also strafed by three Grumman fighters. They immediately fought back, but a fire broke out, and everyone had to go ashore. After landing, everyone rushed to the 00 Team headquarters to report the battle situation and actions. To prepare for the enemy's landing on Omiya Island, a landing force was immediately formed. Petty Officer First Class Sesaki and others were assigned to a certain platoon commanded by a certain lieutenant, with Petty Officer First Class Sesaki serving as the platoon leader.

From June 16th, the enemy's bombing became more intense, with over two hundred aircraft attacking each time, accumulating to over a thousand aircraft daily. Moreover, from that day on, enemy warships also approached the island and began naval bombardment. At that time, among the new American warships sailing proudly before their eyes, the third one (40,000-ton class) suddenly exploded, with a large column of fire erupting, and then it sank. This was the result of a surprise attack by their submarine. They saw this and shouted "Banzai!" From that day until July 7th, the enemy's air and sea attacks were continuous. The coastal positions of our army were completely destroyed by concentrated naval bombardment. The naval forces had no choice but to deploy along the Mengga Mountain area, and the 00 platoon of Petty Officer First Class Sesaki and others retreated to the Hakoye River.

Rising Up to Fight the Enemy

By July 19th, the enemy's attempts to land became more urgent. Aircraft carriers and battleships approached the coast and began a fierce attack. Through binoculars, they saw American artillerymen rolling up their sleeves, smoking cigarettes, and calmly firing their cannons, which made them hate them so much!

Soon, the enemy used fifty amphibious tanks as a vanguard, landing about 50,000 soldiers in the Showa Street area. Our army and naval garrison immediately rose up to repel the attack, inflicting 15,000 casualties on them, but our losses were also significant, and they had to retreat to the new defense line of Orida Village. At this time, the enemy sent more follow-up troops to invade Hokuto Cape.

From then until July 22nd, in short, the enemy's firepower was superior. Even though our garrison was brave and good at fighting, it was difficult to reverse the situation, so they gradually retreated. Finally, the time for the final general attack matured. By August 7th, our army was isolated by enemy tank groups, but the morale of the entire army was even more elevated, vowing to fight to the end.

Sesaki's platoon, in order to carry out its mission, broke through the enemy's encirclement and turned to Shirahama Beach. The platoons were dispersed, carrying transmitters, receivers, etc., and infiltrated the dense forest. From this time on, the islanders' espionage activities were rampant, and our actions were often reported to the enemy. Enemy aircraft often shut off their engines and glided over the dense forest, communicating with ships at sea and continuously carrying out concentrated shelling and bombing. Enemy tanks roamed freely, using flamethrowers to set fire to the dense forest, and laying dense electrical wires on the roads. Touching it carelessly would result in a concentrated attack. Petty Officer First Class Sesaki and others crossed these obstacles and continued forward. At dawn on August 9th, they found an enemy tank on the road, crept up to it quietly, and the American soldiers were still asleep in the tank. They immediately used several hand grenades to destroy it, and then continued towards Shirahama Village. Unexpectedly, the enemy had already reached the village, so they had to slip into a cave on the coast to wait for an opportunity. They gathered to count the number of people, a total of twenty-four from the platoon leader down. The 00 platoon leader had already been killed in battle.

On August 15th, the platoon leader learned that the fighting on the island was very serious and decided to recruit a suicide squad to send liaisons to the mainland. Petty Officer First Class Sesaki and the other three immediately volunteered, not fearing death to complete this mission. After searching, they finally found two Army Type 95 folding river-crossing boats in the jungle. They immediately repaired one, built masts, and loaded it with one斗 three升 of unpolished rice, one hundred coconuts, a tank of rainwater, and some dry bread. The boat was made of five-millimeter-thick plywood, fifteen feet long and five feet wide.

On the night of August 20th, Sesaki and others received the following order:

"Petty Officer First Class Hazaki and others are hereby ordered to leave Omiya Island by river-crossing boat from Shirahama Beach on August 20th. May you all have good fortune in battle. Commander of the 000 Platoon Landing Force, Navy Lieutenant So-and-so."

Escape in the Dark

Earlier, on the night of the 19th, the enemy's mobile force near Rokutai seemed to be moving, and the enemy's surveillance at sea was very strict. Therefore, Sesaki's departure had to be delayed for a day. It was not until 7:00 PM on the 21st that they had the opportunity to leave the island. Under the strict surveillance of the enemy's sea and land forces, the four men quietly left the island, but were immediately discovered by the enemy's patrol boat and fired upon. Fortunately, they skillfully used the darkness to escape, but Tani and Sakoda were finally wounded in the shoulders and abdomen. Due to the enemy's strict surveillance, the boat wandered between Rokutai and Omiya Island for three days. On the fourth day, the wounded recovered a little, and they finally sneaked past the警戒line, using a wristwatch compass, a sea chart torn from a naval diary, and the stars in the sky, sailing at sea for thirteen days. During this period, enemy aircraft often flew by, and the silhouette of Omiya Island was always visible in the distance. At night, they remotely saw the electric lights flashing at what seemed to be the Akashi Street airfield. Thinking that the enemy was rushing to repair the airfield, the anger in their hearts was indescribable.

On September 6th, they finally could no longer see Omiya Island. After that, the fresh water gradually became insufficient. On September 21st, they finally saw the silhouette of an island, guessing that they had reached Taiwan or the Ryukyu Islands. Unexpectedly, on the 23rd, a storm suddenly arose, and the boat was carried away by a wind of thirty meters per second, drifting uncontrollably for three days and three nights, until the weather cleared on the morning of the fourth day. At this time, a considerable amount of fresh water had accumulated in the tank, and their spirits had recovered. They also knew that the boat had caught the Kuroshio Current they had been hoping for. On the morning of the fifth day, they raised the sail again, heading northwest. The boat sped along with the wind, white foam rising from the stern, and schools of sardines chasing after the boat. Just as they were worried about running out of food, they saw these fish. They looked at each other with great joy, immediately made a net with gauze, caught forty or fifty of them, and swallowed them raw.

Friendly Aircraft to the Rescue

After sailing at sea for another week, they saw seven friendly aircraft. Having only seen enemy aircraft in the past, everyone was very excited. Seaman Kanakawa was a signalman. He immediately sent out a distress signal. However, the friendly aircraft all had important missions and did not rescue them, but one dropped a bag of food before hurrying away. However, having encountered friendly aircraft, they felt a surge of courage. They also saw seaweed and pieces of wood floating by, and everyone was excited that they were close to land.

On the morning of September 30th, they saw three islands. At noon, they reached an island and were rescued by Chinese fishermen. It turned out to be an island near the Zhoushan Islands near Shanghai. Before reaching the island, they had been without food for two weeks, drinking seawater for three meals a day. After resting on the island for three days, they contacted the naval garrison. On October 9th, they arrived in Shanghai, returned to the local unit, and reported everything. (Report by Kawano News Team) (Translated from the December 1st Mainichi Shimbun)

### Tower

— Bai Wen

The tower is the traitor's sword,

Its sharp point piercing straight into the heart of heaven.

Its entire body is covered with delicate holes,

In which once flashed

The lovely image of an ancient royal concubine.

One day, suddenly with a magical story,

The tower became feared and respected by people;

Now it is covered in vines and moss;

No longer do adventurous youths risk climbing it.

It was a long ago dynasty's emperor,

To praise his own dignity and achievements,

Who conscripted thousands upon thousands of laborers,

To build the tower's foundation with their blood and flesh.

Or perhaps it was a devout monk,

In memory of the greatness of Buddha.

So, in the name of heaven,

He solicited donations from devout men and women everywhere:

The history of the past gradually became forgotten by people,

The tower thus became a mystery.

Now it stands isolated atop the mountain peak,

Merely for later generations to reminisce upon,

People, aside from repeatedly praising its magnificence,

Also dream of the legendary female demon within the tower.

Time passes year after year,

Green vines wither and then turn verdant once more,

Sometimes, just when it is a sunny and peaceful vernal equinox,

The tower's surroundings are filled with sightseers;

Sometimes, when a wildfire starts at the foot of the mountain,

The tower is plunged into incomparable loneliness,

Although it harbors a sadness that is not understood,

It remains silent,

Harboring a wait.

One evening, an old man came,

(I have heard the tower's elders tell of this)

He is a villager who had been wandering far away for many years,

Now he has returned wearily,

Bringing with him a body full of fatigue,

He gropes his way closer to the tower.

Wanting to use his dimming old eyes,

To search for the scars of his youth;

He murmurs his lament,

And the silent tower also groans.

Thus, in a deep

Deep midnight,

The tower roared in unison,

Ending its life together with him.

### Essentials of Basketball Theory (8)

— Liang Zhaoan

(7) Free Throw Shooting Method

In recent years, schools all over the United States, under the guidance of good coaches, have been using weeks to conduct short-term free throw shooting training. The amazing results can be said to have rapidly improved the level of basketball. Therefore, the spirit of earning points by shooting free throws is also worthy of attention. In many competitions, the win or loss often depends on the accuracy of the free throw. It is said that there was a game in which both sides did not score, but one of the teams won the game because they made a free throw at the end. This may be just a rumor, but it shows that free throws are valued by people.

Although shooting free throws is a relatively easy task, the tense and exciting emotions in the game will inevitably affect the accuracy of players who lack practice. Even players who practice hard are rarely seen to be able to make more than seven out of ten shots in a game. In the basketball practice class at Springfield University in the United States, those who fail to make seven out of ten free throws are not allowed to pass. This is a standard that our Shanghai players must strive to achieve.

The simplest free throw method is the two-handed low throw method. This method uses both hands to hold the sides of the ball with the fingers spread as much as possible (the palms do not touch the ball). The grip should be stable and light. The feet are separated and stand flat. If you use the chest push throw method, then the feet are separated front and back. When shooting the ball, the body squats down, bends the knees, lowers the elbows to the sides of the body, and the center of gravity of the body should be upright, not too forward or too backward. When the ball is sent out, the body rises with it, but it should not jump up or rush forward diagonally. Both eyes should focus on the iron ring of the basket. The path of the ball should be from top to bottom. It is best not to touch the backboard, but sometimes touching the backboard is easier than not touching it. You can do whatever is convenient. Do not change any free throw method that you are already accustomed to and have a good grasp of, otherwise the use of a new method will be even more inaccurate. The points that should be changed are only the errors that often cause the ball to be thrown out and miss.
