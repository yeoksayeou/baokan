英軍進攻里比亞

北非英軍，自十八日起，已開始對里比亞作猛烈之進攻，並獲迅速之進展，猶憶本月六日，蘇聯國防委員會主席斯達林氏曾在莫斯科十月革命紀念會中，發表演說，謂「紅軍之所以暫時遭遇逆境，係由於歐洲方面缺乏對希特勒之第二戰綫；苟歐陸能建成第二戰綫，則紅軍地位必可轉佳而對德不利，吾人深信此點在最近將來必可成為事實。」同時英國議會及一般民意，亦多贊成建立新的戰塲，一面援助蘇聯，一面打擊德國；英政府對此，因事涉軍機，雖屬避免作公開之答覆，然以戰略家著聞之英相邱吉爾氏，當然充分認識此點之重要，而積極布置，以促其實現。昨報載路透社倫敦電稱，英當局正大舉刷新陸軍陣容，其高級統帥，將有重要之更動，卽現任內國軍總司令白魯克將軍，將繼狄爾將軍任英帝國軍參謀主任：東南指揮部總指揮巴蓋特中將，將繼任內國軍總司令：而狄爾將軍則將出任印度孟買省長。據路透社軍事訪員觀察，以為此次變更，為英國將以更積極形式從事陸地戰爭之先聲，然則北非攻勢之展開，殆正英軍增闢第二戰綫，大舉發動陸上反攻之序幕。

我人試一檢討英國之戰鬥力，自以海軍為其骨幹，然近年來整軍經武之結果，英人陸空軍力，亦大有可觀，而空軍尤質量並進，屢揚威於歐陸。英國茍增闢對德陸地戰塲，固必以陸軍配合空軍為交戰之主力，然為充分發揮本身優點，自當出動海軍，以海陸之協同，作攻勢之進擊。適合此一條件，可供英軍登陸作戰之戰塲，其在西歐，為挪威，為法國西岸；其在南歐，為義大利，為巴爾幹；其在北非，則為里比亞。而挪威僻處西北，不足為戰局重心；法國淪陷區及義大利巴爾幹諸地，雖饑寒交迫，民怨沸騰，究因交通便利之故，與德國本部為混然之連合體，德國援師，可以朝發而夕達，英軍渡海來攻，於勢有所不利。而里比亞則不然。里比亞為義大利屬地，今由德義聯防，其實際作戰指揮權，自墨索里尼數度「肅軍」後，已全入德人之手。然德人以客籍之將校，握發號施令之大權，終未能齊一軍心，戰鬥精神，因而大减。且德義之於里比亞，有地中海阻隔其間，而地中海之制海權，至今操於英國。軸心方面之兵員與戰具，雖非不能偷渡以抵北非，其間究冒甚大之危險，隨時可被英巡邏艦擊沉或捕獲。加之埃及為英之軍事同盟國，其作戰根據地與英共之，而自阿比西尼亞義軍總司令奧斯泰公爵於今年五月二十日向英國乞降後，整個東非及紅海區域，已全歸英軍控制。故西歐大陸上之戰略形勢，軸心國家以逸待勞，而北非戰略形勢，則以英國為其主體。然則英軍於此發動反攻，實居一種有利地位。去冬及今春，當華維爾將軍駐節開羅之日，亦曾對里比亞作試探進攻。其時德義對蘇猶未揭開戰幕。得以較多之力量，應付英軍之攻勢。至於今日，蘇聯方以全力與德軍精銳鏖戰於東歐，德國決無充分之餘力可以他顧，故北非英軍此度配合地中海英艦隊，大舉西攻，當可得更多之收穫，建立永久之戰果。

適在此時，而外電傳來，法國駐非洲總督魏剛將軍，突被維希政府免職。魏剛將軍自去年法國敗戰後，卽出駐非洲，統率一部分之武力，對維希為有力之支持；貝當對德國，有時猶能相當强硬應付，魏剛與有力焉，其突被免職，與此當不無關係，而德國之覬覦非洲法國屬地，不斷對法增加壓力，亦可於此徵之。蓋法屬北非洲在軍事上及經濟上均具重要性，其對於西地中海猶埃及之於東地中海；而法屬西非洲則一面為向南美洲發展勢力之跳板，一面足以控制南大西洋。德人之欲得此而甘心，自在意中。同時德國對土耳其及西班牙，最近亦頗有所企圖。凡此諸端，益增北非英軍此度發動攻勢之重要性。蓋目前里比亞為軸心國家在非洲唯一之中心根據地，苟能一舉加以肅淸，則不特可以鞏固英國對非洲之控制權，並消滅德國在非洲擴展勢力之企圖，卽聯繫英蘇之地中海交通綫，亦將因此而更確保其安全，以為將來作更進一步的軍事發展之基礎也。

船巳經受到急流的阻力「拍拍」在悲鳴，舵工右手把舵，左手拿着竹篙蹻起屁股向前撑。岸上的兩位縴手負担更重了，四肢着地，胸部跟地平行，嘴裹不停的「啊呵，啊呵，」可是半步也前進不得。「客伙，助點力吧！」求援聲代替了「啊呵」。五員生力軍一齊奔上去拉着竹蔑編成的縴纜。居然流水敵不過這八人的合力，船兒緩緩前進了。

學着老縴手的步伐，在紅沙灘上安閒的走，忽然縴纜將我們帶到鵝卵石灘上。太陽光下鵝卵石的灼熱，觸到每個新縴手柔嫩的脚板上，發出一陣呼痛聲。手裏的重量阻止我們逃避這種痛苦，水邊的潮濕又使我們不忍穿套乾潔的布鞋。於是「草鞋，草鞋，到犍為辦草鞋！」老縴手的提出，變成新縴手的決定。可是老縴們並沒有一個着草鞋的。

終乎被縴纜拉進水裹，「啊，好安逸」！水被太陽蒸晒得溫暖的，脚踏在平滑的鵝卵石上，爽適而有凉意。沉重的脚步攪亂了水裏的淸靜，驚散了魚囝的聚會！這樣的水邊給予我們帶雅氣的新縴手極大的引誘。有些竟在船過急流卸責之後，大要捉魚囝的遊戲。

船在打彎的石灘上擱淺了。兩位穿着長衫的老縴手走下河，慢慢的將船送進深水裏去。在水中工作的時候，他們將長衫捲到腰部，看出沒有穿褲子。上岸之後，搖身一變，又恢復斯文的樣子。這時躺在草地上等待船行的我，對此頗感興趣。可是縱目遠看，河的上流站着一位重載本船的水裹工作者，水已達到胸部，他索性將長衫晚下，當為頭巾，圍在頭上。有人說：「川人眞斯文，連勞動者的身上都常常披着一襲長衫」。不過船夫們為了禮貌，為了工作便利，不得不如此。如果　到荒凉險峭的地段，便可以看到一批批裸體縴手拉着無女客的貨船。恍若置身於十年前宣傳極盛一時的德國健康營裏！

過了犍為，常常越過山坡和港汶。走上一座丘陵，船夫便要求派一人「斷後」，照顧縴纜，以免牽掛在樹枝或岩石上妨碍前進。這是一種很苦的工作；有時要越過不必經過的尖頂，有時要爬上突出江岸的大岩石，有時落得很後，有時前進很快。所以常常換人輪値，甚或兩人同時工作，倘若遇到最繁重最危險的關頭，便得船夫親自出馬了。

到達汶河或是不適宜揹牽的地方，便得一齊上船飄過河去。在一個懸崖絕壁聚夾着兩岸的地段，我們這隻吃水頗淺的小木船，沿崖脚划行，同時用竹篙　在平直的山崖上一個個圓錐形　裏撐過——這些硐是千百年來若干船夫辛勞的結晶。「人工偉大」，我們不費力的循得前人的遺跡前進，不期然的這樣讚嘆着。

這時一隻重載船在中流上駛，站在船梢高架的木板上的舵工，一面掌舵，一面敲着小鼓，和着十二把木漿聲跟十二位船夫合唱的「啊呵」聲。船到急流，划手全體轉身向艙出死力的反扳漿。鼓聲急了，漿聲急了，「啊呵」聲也急了，同時還傳出一陣陣的脚踏甲板聲。穿過夾谷，一切聲響停止，划手們陸續跳下水去，走上縴路背縴。

螢光燈之裝置及（續）　電力公司管理章程

— 汪經鎔

根據上述舉例，交易中之最為公道者，當為購買之數量等於送達之數量，即購買之數量與送達之數量，應為一百與一百之比，若果此比例較低，即屬不正常，不公允者，此項比例，在商業上名之日，「商品比例」或稱「商品因數」，而在電學上，則電器用具實在所消耗之電量，與發電機為之而輸供之電量之比例，稱為「電力因數」。

通常任何商品尠有以低「商品因數」出售者，換言之，即不以「多送少購」之方式出售，吾人從未今日購炭兩籮，而明日退回其一籮，他日再購數籮，而又退回其一部份，至於購買汽油，或煤氣時，亦不出以此種方式，通常吾人對於電流之購用，亦何獨不然，惟有多種電氣用具，如螢光燈者，具有一種天然之特性，當其接於五十週波之交流電時，無論吾人覺察與否，一對電綫，在每百分之一秒時間內輸送「一包」電力，而此種電具，僅用其一部份，而退還其餘不必之一部份，此種電具可稱為電力之「試用主顧」，因此電力公司之發電機構與饋電機構，因電流退回之頻繁，而增加非必要之工作，正與地毯店因地毯之退回，而支付無謂之車資與人工無異，更有進者，地毯之式類繁多，大小不一，「多送少購」之制度，有時尙為事理所容許，惟電力公司所輸供之電力，品質既無軒輊，式樣亦無分別，故其計算電價時，並未將此項無用之服務費加入計算之事實，亦無將此項無用之服務費加入計算之理由，假如螢光燈之用戶，竟得享受此「多送少購」（即低電力因數）之權則結果將使電力公司之費用，普遍增加，於是影響於一般之電費價率，即不用螢光燈之用戶，亦將受其連累，此又為一不正常，不公尤之情事，當為公正之用戶，所不取者也，幸而此低電力因數之弊病，並非不可救藥之絕症，祗須採用一種名為儲電器，Capieltor或Condenser者，即可校正之，非特此也，儲電器之效用，不獨可以刪除無謂之消耗，且可促進用戶之經濟効率，其對於用戶之利益不祗一端，茲分條縷述於後，　用戶於改裝螢光燈時，設該燈之「電力因數」巳經由儲電器加以校正者，則其所加於電綫之負荷較少，不必更換原來電綫，即可裝置較多之燈盞，得較足之光量，若在新裝置中裝配同數之燈，可用較細之電綫，　高電力因數之裝置，能將用戶綫路上之電流量减低，因此改善燈盞之電壓，而增進燈盞之功能，　路綫上電流之减低可以减少路後之電耗損失，幷减省電費，　二十瓦及四十瓦之燈盞、裝用高雷力因數之「雙燈」輔助器者，較之裝置其他任何式樣之設備，每度電力，發出更多之光量。（完）

嬰孩和學步小孩　應該怎樣避免肺癆

— 滌淸

衣服除保護體溫外，宜盡可能地輕鬆。過於護暖的棉毛絨，法闌絨這一類，並不足貴的，因為這些衣服足以妨礙孩子的呼吸機能。

餵乳　在孩子初生的幾個月裹，由健康的母親授乳，是最重要而有益的。倘若母親患着肺病，便不適宜於授乳，為避免母親的咳嗽唾沫傳染起見，自然以雇用健康奶娘為適當

哺乳期的孩子，牛乳或者奶粉之類的食品，應常是每天的主要食料。漸漸並進而給以米糊食品或其他。　還須要準時欽以橘汁，每日少量的魚肝油，都能增强他抵抗病菌的力

小孩每每不喜歡吃蔬菜，這是很可惜的。因為新鮮蔬菜，蒿苣等等及紅蘿葡蕪　等裏面含着極有價值的保護原質。尤以佳美的水果與蔬菜混和的食品，最為有益。每日一品脫的牛乳，也是不可少的。不過必須經過肺病檢查或淸濾消毒過的，方能常飲。由淸潔牛乳中製成的白搭，乳酪，奶餅之類，也是極其有益的。

傳染病如痧子，百日咳之類發生時，必須予兒童以嚴格的體格檢查。有人時常說：「痧子見小孩所不能免的。最好在他入學以前出過就好了。」這是大大的錯誤。稍長年齡的兒童出着痧子，其嚴重性質實比嬰兒期為輕。所以吾們應當盡力保護小孩避免此種疾病的傳染。旣患着此種病症後，當盡力注意看護。因為在痧子漸漸痊愈的時期，最有肺癆開始的危險。

入學以後　現在的小孩大多從小便進幼稚園，甚至在不滿五足歲的孩子，便進幼稚班了。幼稚班常常是露天的，而且每天有着長時期的休息。

在這個時期，或者送他進福兒診療所，那醫生會得吿訴你這小孩營養上所當特別注意之點，飲食上穿衣上與任何特別困難之點。這種機會，可以充分明瞭，學步小孩怎樣會得到毛病，怎樣纔可以期其速愈而不枉費時日。

小孩肺癆的嚴重情形，較稍長年齡的兒童為低，可是病菌便在此時潛伏。最好是及早予以檢騐，由醫師的診斷而及早予以防範或療治。

若是有特別不良健康的癥象，當然要請醫生，或者到福兒所去檢騐。頸核增大，發熱，面色蒼白，或是食慾不振，都暗示着應該給予特別檢査，切勿大意輕忽。（完）

River Journey in Sichuan (II)

— Ji Sheng

The boat was already groaning under the resistance of the rapids, “clap, clap”. The helmsman held the rudder with his right hand, and with his left hand, he held a bamboo pole, raising his backside and pushing forward. The two trackers on the shore bore an even heavier burden, their limbs on the ground, their chests parallel to the ground, incessantly “ah-ho, ah-ho”, but unable to move forward even half a step. “Guys, lend a hand!” The call for help replaced “ah-ho”. Five new recruits rushed up together, grabbing the tow rope made of woven bamboo strips. Surprisingly, the rushing water could not withstand the combined strength of these eight people, and the boat slowly moved forward.

Learning the steps of the old trackers, walking leisurely on the red sand beach, suddenly the tow rope led us to a pebble beach. The heat of the pebbles under the sunlight touched the tender soles of every new tracker, eliciting a chorus of cries of pain. The weight in our hands prevented us from escaping this pain, and the dampness by the water made us reluctant to put on clean cloth shoes. So, “Straw sandals, straw sandals, go to Qianwei to get straw sandals!” The suggestion of the old trackers became the decision of the new trackers. But none of the old trackers were wearing straw sandals.

Finally, we were pulled into the water by the tow rope, “Ah, how comfortable!” The water was warmed by the sun, and our feet stepped on the smooth pebbles, feeling refreshing and cool. The heavy footsteps disturbed the quietness of the water and scattered the gatherings of the small fish! This kind of waterside gave the new trackers with an air of elegance a great temptation. Some even wanted to play the game of catching small fish after the boat had passed the rapids and they were relieved of their responsibility.

The boat ran aground on a bend in the pebble beach. Two old trackers wearing long gowns walked down to the river and slowly guided the boat into deeper water. When working in the water, they rolled up their long gowns to their waists, revealing that they were not wearing trousers. After going ashore, they transformed themselves again and resumed their cultured appearance. At this time, I, lying on the grass waiting for the boat to move, was quite interested in this. But looking into the distance, there was a worker standing in the upper reaches of the river carrying our boat, the water reaching his chest, he simply turned his long gown down and wore it as a turban on his head. Someone said, “The people of Sichuan are truly cultured, even the laborers often wear a long gown.” However, the boatmen had to do this for the sake of politeness and for the convenience of work. If you go to desolate and precipitous sections, you can see batches of naked trackers pulling cargo ships without female passengers. It’s as if you were in a German health camp, which was heavily promoted ten years ago!

After passing Qianwei, we often crossed hillsides and harbors. Walking up a hill, the boatmen would ask for someone to be assigned to “guard the rear”, to take care of the tow rope, so as not to get caught on tree branches or rocks and hinder progress. This was a very arduous task; sometimes we had to cross unnecessary sharp peaks, sometimes we had to climb onto large rocks jutting out from the river bank, sometimes we lagged far behind, sometimes we moved forward very quickly. So, we often took turns, or even two people worked at the same time. If we encountered the most arduous and dangerous moments, the boatmen had to take matters into their own hands.

When we reached the Wen River or places unsuitable for towing, we had to all get on the boat and drift across the river. In a section where cliffs and precipices gathered on both sides of the river, our small wooden boat with a shallow draft traveled along the foot of the cliff, while using bamboo poles to push against the conical cavities carved into the smooth cliff face – these cavities are the crystallization of the hard work of countless boatmen over the centuries. "Human ingenuity is great", we moved forward effortlessly following the trails of our predecessors, and could not help but exclaim in this way.

At this time, a heavily loaded boat was sailing in the middle of the river. The helmsman standing on the elevated wooden platform at the stern of the boat was steering the boat while beating a small drum, and the sound of the drum was combined with the sound of twelve oars and the sound of “ah-ho” sung by twelve boatmen. When the boat reached the rapids, all the rowers turned around and desperately pulled back on the oars. The sound of the drum became faster, the sound of the oars became faster, and the sound of “ah-ho” also became faster, and at the same time, a series of footsteps on the deck was heard. Passing through the valley, all the sounds stopped, and the rowers successively jumped into the water and walked on the tow path to pull the boat.

Installation of Fluorescent Lamps (Continued): Electricity Company Management Regulations

— Wang Jingrong

Based on the examples mentioned above, the fairest transaction is when the quantity purchased equals the quantity delivered, that is, the ratio of the quantity purchased to the quantity delivered should be one hundred to one hundred. If this ratio is lower, it is abnormal and unfair. This ratio is commercially called the "commodity ratio" or "commodity factor," while in electricity, the ratio of the actual electricity consumed by electrical appliances to the electricity supplied by the generator is called the "power factor."

Usually, few commodities are sold with a low "commodity factor," that is, they are not sold in a "giving more, buying less" manner. We never buy two baskets of charcoal today and return one basket tomorrow, or buy several baskets again later and return part of them. The same is true when buying gasoline or gas. Why should the purchase and use of electricity be any different? However, many electrical appliances, such as fluorescent lamps, have a natural characteristic that when connected to a 50-cycle alternating current, whether we realize it or not, a pair of wires transmits a "packet" of electricity every hundredth of a second, and these appliances only use part of it and return the rest that is not needed. These appliances can be called "trial customers" of electricity. Therefore, the generating and feeding mechanisms of the electricity company increase unnecessary work due to the frequent return of current, just like a carpet store paying unnecessary transportation and labor costs due to the return of carpets. Moreover, there are many styles and sizes of carpets, and the "giving more, buying less" system is sometimes tolerated by reason. However, the quality of the electricity supplied by the electricity company is uniform, and the styles are the same. Therefore, the calculation of electricity prices does not include the cost of this useless service, nor is there any reason to include the cost of this useless service. If users of fluorescent lamps are allowed to enjoy this "giving more, buying less" (i.e., low power factor) right, the result will generally increase the electricity company's costs, which will affect the general electricity rate, and users who do not use fluorescent lamps will also be affected. This is another abnormal and unfair situation, which is unacceptable to fair users. Fortunately, the drawback of this low power factor is not an incurable disease. It can be corrected by using a device called a capacitor or condenser. Not only that, the effect of a capacitor is not only to eliminate unnecessary consumption but also to promote the economic efficiency of users. Its benefits to users are not limited to one aspect. The details are listed below: When users retrofit fluorescent lamps, if the "power factor" of the lamp has been corrected by a capacitor, the load on the wires is less, and more lamps can be installed without replacing the original wires, resulting in more light. If the same number of lamps are installed in a new installation, thinner wires can be used. A high power factor installation can reduce the current flow in the user's circuit, thus improving the voltage of the lamps and increasing the function of the lamps. The reduction of current in the circuit can reduce the power consumption loss after the circuit and save electricity bills. Twenty-watt and forty-watt lamps, when equipped with high power factor "dual lamp" ballasts, emit more light per unit of electricity than any other type of equipment. (The End)

The Ancestor of Motion Pictures

— Zhi Yu

Undeniably, motion pictures are the most developed form of drama in the entertainment world of the 20th century. People probably all know that this was invented by foreigners! But who knows that shadow plays already existed in our country during the Northern Song Dynasty? (See Meng Yuanlao's "Tokyo Dream Record" and Wu Zimu's "Dream Liang Record" from the Southern Song Dynasty.)

Mr. Gu Jiegang once wrote an article on Australian shadow plays, and Mr. Jiang Danian also wanted to write a book on Changqiao shadow plays. They all wanted to promote and enhance China's inherent art and culture. I dare not have any such dreams, I just want to write about some of the interesting things about the shadow plays of our ancient times—which can also be said to be the ancestors of shadow plays—for people who like to study shadow plays to refer to.

Westerners say that shadow plays evolved from zoetropes, which is not without reason. Our ancient shadow plays were invented using the principle of the zoetrope.

The most important part of ancient shadow plays was a donkey's skin, which was ground very thin, almost transparent. People, horses, dogs... were painted on the skin, which was considered the negative. Behind the skin was a very bright oil lamp—although not as bright as the thousands of light electric lamps of today, it was similar to a hundred-light lamp. About ten feet away from the donkey skin, a white cloth screen was hung, which was the so-called silver screen. In this way, the paintings on the donkey skin would be reflected on the white screen, and the donkey skin could be moved, so the images on the screen would also move. This seems similar to modern cartoons; really, it's almost the same!

Moreover, this kind of shadow play also had sound. There was a singer who stood next to the screen and sang or spoke according to the characters in the play (male, female, painted face, clown).

If you think what I'm saying is too absurd, then I can tell you that the British Museum in London still displays the donkey skin and oil lamps used to make shadow plays in our country during the Northern Song Dynasty..., but it seems that Chinese museums do not have this kind of antique.
