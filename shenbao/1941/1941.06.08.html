軸心攻勢的再檢討

德義兩國巨頭於本月二日在勃倫　山隘長談五小時之久，究竟談了些什麼，決定了些什麼，言人人殊，始終沒有一個確切報告。彙集各方報道，似乎雙方會談的主要內容，仍不外（一）確定今後的軍事行動，（二）應付美國參戰的可能性，以及（三）商討如何建設「歐洲新秩序」，以應付未來可能的長期戰。關於第三點，間題側重於經濟的改組和資源的調整，關於第二點，一說德義將迫令美國在準備完成之前參戰，另一說則謂軸心國仍將慫恿日本行動，以牽掣美國，個中問題異常微妙，容當另文爲之剖析，茲請單就第一點加以檢討。

近日一般看法，大多認爲德義的次期攻勢，不出東進與西進兩途，或東西並進。所謂西進，即是猛撲直布羅陀，以切斷西地中海的咽喉；所謂東進，則爲趁着最近巴爾幹和克利特島的勝利，繼續在東地中海採取攻勢，一方利用傘兵進撲埃及，一方憑藉叙利亞以突擊巴勒斯坦和外的丹，然後再利用兩翼包抄的戰略，會攻蘇彝士運河這個重耍據點；不過，無論東進或西進，軸心國均需要先在政治軍事外交各方面，有所佈置。第一，西歐方面，必須先把對法關係，弄得服服貼貼，連日所傳魏剛將軍特自菲洲防地趕回本國，反對達爾朗內閣柔弱外交之說，如果屬實，則希特勒的「法德合作」政策至少已須受到相當的牽掣，更談不到如何脅迫維希政府，以從事對英作戰；他如西班牙的弗朗哥將軍也始終沒有百分之百地投入軸心懷抱，今後如何促其入彀，以代德義「在火中取栗」，至少還需要相當的努力。

其次，東地中海方面，克利特島雖已費盡九牛二虎之力，予以攻佔，但進攻埃及的軍事準備，似乎還嫌不夠充分；叙利亞的局勢既因法國內部尙有爭執而未能立即「明朗化」，同時土耳其乃至蘇聯的態度，也似乎還在模稜兩可之間，乖巧的希特勒當然知道投鼠忌器的明訓，吾則前此何至連已到口邊的伊拉克，都甘願「拱手讓人」？何况華維爾將軍過去之所以不以全力死守希臘，不以全力保衛克利特島者，其用意殆正在集中兵力，加强埃及和近東方面的防務，塞浦路斯島既非克利特可比，亞歷山大之久爲英國近東軍主力所在地，更爲不容吾認之事實，而德國他日唯一可以憑藉的叙利亞，又早已處於英軍三面包圍的不利形勢之下，蓋以美國的接濟，此際似乎正在經由紅海直達埃及，在此種種情勢之下，除非近東外交政治局勢發生劇變，好像還不容想像希特勒的閃電攻勢又將在那一方面迅速奏功。

綜上觀之，可見希特勒之必須繼續擴大攻勢，雖已毫無疑問，但是如何進攻較有把握，以及向何方進攻較易着手，似乎還在審愼討議之中，也說不定審議結果，感覺尙須在實行發動大規模軍事行動之前，多做一些準備工作，不過，時間正是他最大的敵人，因此，無論如何，他在不久的將來，必須再作一次——「閃電攻勢」即使這一攻勢將依然無關整個戰局的最後勝敗，但他爲保持威信，維繫人心，以及企圖打破長期戰的危機起見，仍將有不得不爾的苦衷。

可可與朱古律（續）

— 髙劍農

然而當着Cortez率領着一班粗擴無議的兵士把古阿茲忒克帝國征服以後，他們驅迫着士人給他們開礦掘取黃金：這多少年來是那麼被墨西哥人所珍惜愛護勤慎培養的可可樹，　時荒廢拋棄，全被作踐了。不過後來西班牙人也認清了這樹的價值，於是反把可可完全壟斷起來，嚴禁出口，為的可大利獨吞。最後他們也把它移植到南美，又於公元一六七四年和一六八〇年兩次遠輸至菲律賓羣島，從這裏普及到印度東部和南洋群島一帶：因為這些地方的氣候相宜，移植的工作非常成功。至於葡萄牙人呢，也把可可樹傳播到非洲幾內亞灣和剛果河流域一帶，現在這些地方反成了可可最主要的供給地：譬如黃金海岸的Cortez海港，每年輸出可可約十五萬噸，本地的出產量，則是二十五萬噸。

培植可可樹的工作是非常麻煩和艱苦的：它需要常年有攝氏二十七度的氣溫，極豐富的雨量，極肥沃的土地；它又怕烈日和暴風，所以必需有蔭影把它遮蔽起來。平常人是把它栽種在低濕的山谷裏；要是在毫無遮攔的平地上呢，就利用更髙大的香蕉樹等當它的護符。它長到三年後纔開始開花，不過人總是把它們斫去，一直等到第五年頭上：這時它開始結果子了，往後能供給多量的收獲到二十五年或三十年，可可樹的花是一年常開的，不過人採取可可，卻只在六月和十二月每年舉行兩次。

可可的服食法，譬如沖茶喝，溶化在牛奶裏吃等，已經是家喻戶曉，不必去多叙述它；可是對於它製造「朱古律」糖的演變和進化，卻不能默而不言的不稍為介紹一下。說起古墨西哥人對於可可的吃法，那不但不能動我們的食指，惹我們的饞涎，恐怕反要叫我們聞而喪胆，見而失魄的了！原來他們吃時是把可可焙炙了，研成細粉，混合在玉蜀黍麵裏，熬成漿糊的樣子，－－又一種「湖塗！」－－然後加上重量的……辣椒，大批的胡椒！這紅褐色的糊塗，就是古墨西哥人所最喜愛，最常吃的原始「朱古律！」啊這東西，我想可以按照古墨西哥人原來的字音Tchocolatl譯作「抽口辣，」或按照西班牙文或法文的chocolat譯作「收口辣：」無論怎樣吧，這傢伙的辣勁，一定是我們吃不消的。然而墨西哥人是那麼嗜痂成癖，是那麼狂熱的擁護它，以致有Chia　pa城的天主教信徒，寧可同天主教斷絕了關係，因為那城的天主教願欲禁止他們在參加宗教的儀式時吃他們那寶貴的「抽口辣！」

荘嚴一點的說吧：朱古律所以能成為現在人人愛吃的東西，實在是西班牙人的發明，因為這必需知道糖的利用啊。西班牙人不特在裏面加上蔗糖，且在裏面攙上香料，如同凡尼拉（Vanille）豆，一種叫Orjeval的花，甚至龍涎和麝香。這種仍因墨西哥人舊名的「朱古律」糖，首先傳入西班牙去，也不由的大出風頭起來。不過它的價值是非常的昂貴，多少年來只有王公貴族能夠偶一梁指罷了，平民是沒有口福能親炙這纔堪稱得起　漿玉液的「神糧」哩。公元一六五一年，朱古律初次傳入法國：那時候路易十四正在童年，由太後奧國亞納（Anne　d'Autrich）攝政，太後也立刻垂青寵愛了這甘美的食品，叫它名馳遐邇。一六五九年路易十四和西班牙公主瑪利亞德勒撤結婚：這熱騰香甜的朱古律融液，又在富麗堂皇的王宮華筵上同多數的貴族士女相見，也更進一步的深入社會層中。從一七一五年到一七二三年的法國攝政斐理伯，更是嗜之成癖，大量享用。然而這時它如同在西班牙一樣：因為身價過於高貴，不可響邇，還只是趨奉朱門，作富豪貴族的點綴品罷了。是在這一時期，醫生們在朱古律裏加進許多滋補的藥品，而且也由漿糊改成丸粒的樣子，謊稱它是什麼補陰壯陽的妙藥，以致天主公教貶稱朱古律為「魔食！」—－卻是可以和作對。一—於是普通的朱古律，為的同道醫藥上用的朱古律相別，就冠上「康健」字樣，稱為「康健朱古律。」總結一句：起初的朱古律是漿糊狀，是「湖塗，」它所以變成為現在的塊狀或丸狀，實是由醫生們的加以改良進步而來。

朱古律雖說沒有咖啡那種提神助興的特別效能，卻也是一種極好看食品，況說它那香甜的滋味，比起那苦澀的咖啡，當然更受多數人的歡迎。它的銷路既然日廣，於是就有特設專店以從事大批製造的：法國的第一座朱古律糖店創設於一七七六年，名叫Grand　d,Aussy。然而這座店大概不是為圖謀民眾幸福而設立的，因為它還帶著「王家」朱古律店的稱號呢！經後又經過多少年，因為可可的供給增多，蔗糖的大批生產，價值低廉，於是朱古律也一換其從前專門趨奉貴族的面目，而自貶身價藹然同我們這一般民眾日相親近起來。現在呢，只要我們願意花上不多的代價，就也可以一嘗這「神糧」的滋味了。

— 楊春綠

學期快要結束了！小朋友又在忙着應付這將到的學期考試了。

我們不論做什麼事，事前一定要有相當的準備，才能獲得圓滿的結果。應付考試，也是如此；在考試以前，必須有充分的準備。最好依照校裏規定的考試日程，和各科的性質程度，自己預訂一個溫習的時間表。但是須要有一個限度，就是不妨碍身體的健康爲原則，休息的時間，是萬萬不能缺少的。如考試的前夕，還開夜東，第二天你的精神便不好了，甚至會生起病　，那是冬麼不上算呀！在溫書的時候，要能抓住要領，自已找出問題來。或者集合幾個同學，相互討論，更能使你明瞭得透澈些。

到考試的時候，最怕的是慌張。如果臨考心慌，就是容易的題目，也要變做難題了。所以拿試卷之後，切勿急急動手，應該先略看一遍，把容易做的先做，較難的題目，仔細想過後再做。做不出的，寗可不做，不要偷看書本，或抄襲人家，那是最不道德的行爲。要是給敎師看到了，還要受到嚴厲的處罰咧！等到完全做好以後，如有多餘的時間，自己再複看遍，看看有錯誤沒有，切勿急於搶交卷。此外，還要注意考卷的整潔，字跡要寫得端正，這是同你的成績很有關係的。

考試　不是什麼難事，也不是什麼苦事，只要你能平日用心學習，臨考冉加複習整理，便不難應付裕如了。小朋友們！祝你們考試勝利！

Cocoa and Chocolate (Continued)

— Gao Jiannong

However, when Cortez led a group of rough and uncouth soldiers to conquer the ancient Aztec Empire, they forced the scholars to mine and extract gold for them. The cocoa tree, which had been so cherished, nurtured, and diligently cultivated by the Mexicans for so many years, was then neglected and abandoned, and completely trampled upon. However, later, the Spanish also recognized the value of this tree, and as a result, they completely monopolized cocoa, strictly prohibiting its export, in order to swallow all the profits. Finally, they also transplanted it to South America, and then, in 1674 and 1680, they shipped it to the Philippine Islands twice, from where it spread to eastern India and the South Seas Islands: because the climate in these places was suitable, the transplantation work was very successful. As for the Portuguese, they also spread the cocoa tree to the Gulf of Guinea and the Congo River basin in Africa, and now these places have become the main suppliers of cocoa: for example, the port of Cortez on the Gold Coast exports about 150,000 tons of cocoa every year, while the local output is 250,000 tons.

The work of cultivating cocoa trees is very troublesome and arduous: it requires a constant temperature of 27 degrees Celsius throughout the year, abundant rainfall, and extremely fertile soil; it is also afraid of the scorching sun and storms, so it must have shade to shield it. Ordinary people plant it in low-lying mountain valleys; if it is planted on open plains without any cover, they use taller banana trees as its protector. It does not begin to flower until it is three years old, but people always cut them off, and wait until the fifth year: at this time, it begins to bear fruit, and can provide a large amount of harvest for the next 25 or 30 years. The cocoa tree flowers all year round, but people only harvest cocoa twice a year, in June and December.

The ways of consuming cocoa, such as brewing it to drink like tea or dissolving it in milk to eat, are already well-known, so there is no need to describe them in detail; however, the evolution and development of its manufacture into "chocolate" candy cannot be ignored without a brief introduction. Speaking of the ancient Mexicans' way of eating cocoa, it would not only fail to move our taste buds or whet our appetite, but would probably terrify and horrify us! Originally, when they ate it, they roasted the cocoa, ground it into a fine powder, mixed it with cornmeal, and boiled it into a paste - another "muddle!" - then added a large amount of... chili peppers and black pepper! This reddish-brown muddle was the original "chocolate" that the ancient Mexicans loved and ate the most! Ah, this thing, I think can be translated as "Chou Kou La" according to the original ancient Mexican sound Tchocolatl, or as "Shou Kou La" according to the Spanish or French chocolat: no matter what, the spicy kick of this stuff must be unbearable for us. However, the Mexicans were so addicted to it, so enthusiastically supporting it, that the Catholic believers in the city of Chia pa would rather break off relations with Catholicism, because the Catholics in that city wanted to prohibit them from eating their precious "Chou Kou La" when participating in religious ceremonies!

To put it more solemnly: the reason why chocolate can become something that everyone loves to eat now is really the invention of the Spanish, because this requires knowing the use of sugar. The Spanish not only added cane sugar to it, but also mixed it with spices, such as vanilla beans, a flower called Orjeval, and even ambergris and musk. This "chocolate" candy, which still bore the old Mexican name, first spread to Spain, and became very popular. However, its value was very expensive, and only princes and nobles could occasionally taste it for many years. Common people did not have the good fortune to personally taste this "divine food" that could be called the essence of jade juice. In 1651, chocolate was first introduced to France: at that time, Louis XIV was in his childhood and was regented by Queen Mother Anne of Austria. The Queen Mother also immediately favored and doted on this sweet food, making it famous far and wide. In 1659, Louis XIV married the Spanish princess Maria Theresa: this hot and sweet chocolate melt met many nobles and ladies at the magnificent royal banquet, and further penetrated into the social strata. From 1715 to 1723, the French regent Philippe was even more addicted to it, enjoying it in large quantities. However, at this time, as in Spain: because of its excessively high value, it was out of reach and was only a decoration for the rich and noble. It was during this period that doctors added many nourishing medicines to chocolate and changed it from a paste to a pill, falsely claiming that it was a magical medicine for tonifying yin and strengthening yang, so that the Catholic Church derisively called chocolate "devil food!" - which is agreeable and contrary. - Therefore, ordinary chocolate, in order to distinguish it from the chocolate used in medicine, was crowned with the word "health" and called "health chocolate." To sum up: the original chocolate was a paste, a "muddle," and the reason why it became the current block or pill shape was due to the improvement and advancement of doctors.

Although chocolate does not have the special effect of coffee in refreshing and invigorating, it is also a very good-looking food, and its sweet taste is of course more popular than the bitter coffee. As its sales have become increasingly widespread, there are now special shops dedicated to large-scale manufacturing: France's first chocolate candy store was founded in 1776 and was called Grand d'Aussy. However, this store was probably not established to seek the happiness of the people, because it still carried the title of "Royal" chocolate store! After many years, because of the increased supply of cocoa and the large-scale production of cane sugar at low prices, chocolate also changed its former face of exclusively catering to the nobility, and modestly lowered its price to become closer to us ordinary people. Now, as long as we are willing to spend a small price, we can also taste the flavor of this "divine food."

Various Functions of Silkworms

— He Fu

Children! I know that there must be quite a few of you who raise silkworms, but if you randomly grab a child and ask, how do silkworms spin silk? Why do silkworms make cocoons? Why do they turn into silkworm moths after making cocoons? I'm afraid no child can answer. Children only know that silkworms seem to be doing "magic tricks" and think they are very magical. In fact, this is not magical at all. It is also one of the types of animals. A silkworm is a larva, with twelve ocelli distributed on both sides. It has eyes but no organs such as ears and nose. It has five pairs of legs and nine pairs of breathing spiracles. It also has a heart, blood, stomach, and intestines, but the blood does not contain red blood cells, so it is not red. It does not have a brain, but there are nerve nodes in the throat, chest, and abdomen. The hard layer on the outside is its skeleton. Finally, because it has a special organ—the silk gland—that other animals do not have, it can spin silk entirely because of its silk gland. The silk gland is a kind of colloidal liquid, which hardens into silk when it comes into contact with air.

When a silkworm molts from an egg, it has hair on its body. Ordinary silkworms need to molt four times before they can spin silk, make cocoons, and wrap themselves inside. Later, they become pupae. The pupae do not eat or move. After hibernation, they molt again and become six-legged silkworm moths (adults) with wings. The entire body of the silkworm moth is covered with very fine scales, white in color, and the wings often have yellowish-brown markings. Silkworm moths do not eat anything. After laying eggs, they die soon.

Silkworms—in the larval stage, mainly seek food and are nutritional bodies. Because the lifespan of larvae is very short, they need to reproduce, so they spin silk to make cocoons and become silkworm moths. In the adult stage, they do not eat anything and mainly lay eggs, so they are reproductive bodies. The reason why silkworms spin silk to make cocoons is for the sake of reproduction.

Water Purification Method

— Xi Dan

The water we drink is "primitive water" from when the earth was opened up hundreds of years ago. What is "primitive water"? Primitive water is what is formed by the combination of two parts of hydrogen and one part of oxygen. Pure water is a colorless, odorless, and transparent liquid. Water purity can be demonstrated using the following methods:

(1) Water Color Observation Method: Place a piece of white paper on the bottom of a glass cup, pour some water into the cup, and look at it from above. If the water presents a color or is turbid, it is not drinkable.
