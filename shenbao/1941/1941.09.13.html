統制外滙初步完成後

英美凍結資金·經一月來之安排，今始見有頭緒，然亦賴有英美兩政府勸告之力，不然，市場黑匯，彼此利用，安得有消滅之日。但今之消滅，能否不生波折，澈底付諸實行，尙待事實證明，目下期許之，猶爲過早。惟事亦不難，市上所謂外匯，實際上僅美滙一種而已：美滙之淸算，應操之於紐的市塲，苟無例外之可有，進出胥歸其控制，自不虞有游離之美滙什外，更無分化之作用可言，故上海外滙之出納只須英美銀行有維持推行之決心，雖欲作崇，不可多得。假如黑滙澈底消滅之後，外滙出納，統籌於平準會，而以爲法幣至此，失支其自由買賣外滙　連帶亦失去所有之價值者，是誠大謬：蓋法幣之兌換英美滙，並不以買賣自由與限制。增减其價値，自經統制之後，反可有確定之行市、不若黑滙市塲而瞬息變化。或謂僅一部分物品可以結滙則法幣亦僅有一部分得見其功用，是亦纏夾之談；蓋物品有一部分可以結滙，有不能結滙，爲物品之選擇問題，非法幣之效用問題。法幣並不指定某一部分可以買外滙，某一部分不能買外滙，只要爲法幣，無一不可用以購外滙，此即法幣眞價值所在，亦即張張法幣，有張張購買外滙之可能也。且因消滅黑滙市場之故。滙率釘住於相當水準、因之物價亦得維持相當之標準。此則與安定民生，大有裨益。

今此供給外滙之物品，寬大異常，非但顧及上海人之生活，亦且顧及上海人之享用：非但顧及工業之運用之材料，亦且顧及工業發動之機件。然而自奉則百般揮霍，奉公則分文不費，專圖享用不顧公益之人，猶以爲未足。套滙圖利之輩，感覺不便、時思破壞無論已；即投機操縱之徒，亦有爲黑滙市塲消滅，不便於其行動者，而陰懷嫉忿。此皆自外生成，不可以人情理性論。後此物價之趨勢，所受於幣値之影響者當微，而所受於人為操縱者猶不能絕。蓋如（一）不供給外滙之物品，勢必任意高漲，以爲囤樍居奇之計，（二）則明知有供給，而亦以不供給物品價格作榜樣。步步上抬。（三）則需求者孰知市上所售之物品何者應廉，何者爲高。則運銷者猶可逞其渾水摸魚之慣技。若是則所謂統制外滙，仍是利工商而殘民生，雖統制之目的並不爲物價，而統制之效用，固亦兼及於物價之調整，設事實果如是。不無令人惋惜耳！

故以為清單甲所列之物品若米若煤若石油產品，此三者爲人生日用所必需，一方旣須供給以外滙，一方亦應控制其物價。如米，自經工部局公賣以來，或因其成本關係，公定價有時高出於市價，致有以低劣之米，攙雜於公賣米中混售漁利之弊，似此尙爲購銷之技術問題，不足爲公賣米病；蓋自有工部局米應世，確有使米市操縱，不易奏效，米價調整，差有成就，功固不可湮沒。故今對於洋米供給之外滙，似應用以供給工部局之公賣米爲限，俾工部局有統制租界內糧食之眞實力量！蓋惟工部局辦米不以營利爲目的，志不在牟利。但爲徵信於公衆，工部局亦應將分批定米之數暈，淮價外費，以及出售情况之爲盈爲虧，每月或每一時期，公布報端，庶足以昭大信而間執奸商之口。納子煤與煤屑所製成之煤球，已成爲全市唯一之燃料。次之爲煤油，並有一部分供燃燈之用。此外爲工廠食糧之烟煤，及及熟水店之煤屑，皆直接間接，與民生日用有深切之關係。向來洋行煤商之出售方法，在整批而不注重分售，遂使用棧單，而將煤盡落於有財力之囤戶及煤炭商手中，眞正之消費者，有所需求，反不可得，得亦出價奇昂；而大利歸於商人，害則普及於貧民。今旣供給以外滙，理應平準其物價，改革其分配辦法，自惟有停止棧單買賣，以絕操縱囤積；亦惟有注重門莊現售，以杜商店於中居奇牟利，又惟有兼應用戶之需求。而後可實際入於消費者之手，減輕一般生活之負起。煤油地位，已不若煤之重要，然其爲民生日用品則一；且自煤貴以來，小家庭用火油烹庖者日多，電費貴，而有改點火油燈者亦不少。煤油之價。似乎得煤油公司之拘束。尙不若煤價之橫跳，然亦十餘倍於當年。故應力求以公允之方式，分配於消費者，不應以高厚之利潤，供囤戶之揮霍。烟葉徒爲消耗而已，而亦與米煤同列，是則專顧大衆之享用，及英美友邦商人之事業，惟於分配外滙之際，不應與米煤等量齊觀也。

至於法幣之地位，爲一般所深切注意，其實不焦慮於漏巵未塞之前，而焦慮於漏巵旣塞之後是爲多慮。此固不必談高深·但問一家開支·節的持家。少一文開支，必於其所持之財產，多保存一文；反之而揮霍無度。則其財產日朘月削，終至破產而後已！况自已揮霍不夠，且復供人使用，涸竭更可立待。及知揮霍之不當，而思量入以爲出，防他人之侵蝕，則惟有嚴密出納不爲功。出納旣嚴密矣，消耗有限制矣！而慮財產之價値不如前，得無有此事實乎？故法幣之價値，自經此次統制外滙成功，基礎愈形鞏固，價値不求自高，會看勝成之來臨，而知祀憂之無謂。之商船、除仰　息於此外國專制權力外、不復能自由經營任何和平之商業矣、大西洋曾爲日當永爲吾人自由友好之孔道者、於是將成美國商業、美國海濱、美國內地各城之致命的威脅矣、希特勒政府違反海洋法律與其他各國所享曾經公認之權利、搜自在紙上宣布海洋廣大區域、甚至連位於西半球之巨浸在內、己予封鎖、船隻除自冒被沉之危險外、不得因任何事故駛入該區域、若軍實際現已在廣　之區、而在擅自封鎖區域内與遠在其外者、任意不發警吿、擊沉船隻、此種攫取海洋控制權之納粹企圖、僅爲別在西半球全部實行之納粹陰謀之配合部分、一切皆趨向同一目的、蓋希特勒之前鋒團、不特爲其所承認之秘密員、目爲彼置吾人中之僉壬、已在新世界中爲彼準備立足與橋端、俾一旦攫得海洋控制權、即可需用之、

Large Numbers of American Warships Ordered to Deploy to Search for German and Italian Vessels; President Roosevelt Announces Major Steps, Close Cooperation with Allied Naval and Air Forces

United Press, New York, 12th: It is estimated that several hundred warships are now implementing President Roosevelt's orders. In effect, our forces will closely cooperate with the British and Allied navies and aircraft. This means our warships and aircraft will use British and Allied bases at any time and anywhere. The majority of our forces will naturally use battleships or enter the Mediterranean Sea and other sea areas. According to President Roosevelt's orders, our military commanders will independently and jointly with the British search for German and Italian ships. The large-scale maritime search we are currently engaged in will be largely limited to routes transporting Lend-Lease military supplies to Britain and South Africa, as well as routes in the Pacific Ocean to Vladivostok, Rangoon, and the Red Sea. Routes to Australia and from Australia to Singapore, India, and African ports may also require limited patrols.

Full Text of Roosevelt's Speech

Reuters, Washington, 12th: In his eagerly awaited broadcast last night, President Roosevelt warned Germany and Italy that their warships would face danger if they entered the American defense sea lanes. He said, "I have issued instructions to the commanders-in-chief of the U.S. Army and Navy to implement this policy immediately. The full responsibility rests with Germany. There will be no shooting unless Germany continues to provoke it." The President stated that a German submarine first fired at the American destroyer Greer, intending to sink it. This is a fact, and from a legal and moral standpoint, it constitutes an act of piracy. This is not an isolated incident but part of an overall plan. In July, a submarine in North American territorial waters followed an American warship, attempting to position itself for an attack. Its periscope was clearly visible. At that time, there were no British or American submarines within a hundred miles, so the nationality of this submarine was evident. Nazi submarines are the rattlesnakes of the Mediterranean, and now is the time for active defense." The President continued, "We have not sought a shooting war with Hitler, and we still do not want to seek one. But we also do not want to seek peace at the cost of allowing our naval vessels to be attacked by Hitler while performing their duties. The Nazis want to abolish freedom of the seas and seize absolute control of the oceans. This is an act of lawlessness. If the Nazis were to seize control of the oceans, their second step would be evident: to control the United States and the Western Hemisphere by force. I have no illusions about the seriousness of this step. It is unavoidable for the protection of the nation. The invincibility of the U.S. Navy is only true when the British Navy exists. The traditional policy of American maritime freedom still applies, not only in the Atlantic but also in the Pacific and other oceans. American defense vessels will again wait for the Axis powers to attack first."

The ship was carrying U.S. mail to Iceland, flying the American flag, and its identity as an American vessel was unmistakable. Despite the fabrications of Hitler's propaganda machine and the willingness of some anti-American groups to believe their words, I must inform you of the blunt facts: the German submarine fired on this American destroyer without warning, intending to sink it. We have established outposts in Iceland, Greenland, Labrador, and Newfoundland in the north. Policy of Attacking American Ships is an Act of Piracy

When the American destroyer was attacked, it was fulfilling its legitimate mission. If the submarine could see the destroyer when it fired the torpedoes, then the attack was a deliberate attempt by the Nazis to sink a clearly identifiable American warship. If, on the other hand, the submarine was submerged, unwilling to take the trouble to ascertain the nationality of the ship, and fired torpedoes based on sounds picked up by hydrophones, as claimed in the German communiqué, then the attack is even more brutal, as it indicates a policy of indiscriminate destruction against any ship at sea, whether belligerent or non-belligerent. This is an act of piracy. From a legal and moral standpoint, this is not the first or the last act of piracy committed by the Nazi government against the American flag in this war. Attacks have occurred one after another. A month ago, the American-flagged merchant ship Robin Moor, sailing in the mid-South Atlantic, was sunk by a Nazi submarine in a situation violating established international law and humanitarian principles. The ship's passengers and crew were forced into open boats hundreds of miles from land. This is a direct violation of international treaties signed by the German government. The Nazi government has not offered an apology, claimed it was a mistake, or offered compensation for the losses. In July 1941, an American warship sailing in North American waters was followed by a submarine, which for a long time tried to position itself for an attack. Its periscope was visible above the surface. At that time, there were no British or American submarines within hundreds of miles, so the nationality of this submarine was evident. Five days ago, we learned that the merchant ship Sessa, flying the flag of our sister republic, Panama, had been sunk. Only three survivors remain. On August 17th, the ship was attacked without warning, first by a torpedo and then bombarded near Greenland. It was carrying supplies for the civilian population of Iceland. It is now feared that the other sailors on board have drowned. The fact that German submarines were operating in that area has been confirmed, so there is no reasonable doubt about the nationality of the attacking submarine. Five days ago, another American merchant ship, the Steel Seafarer, while sailing to a port in Egypt, was sunk by a German aircraft in the Red Sea, 220 miles south of Suez. A total of four ships flying the American flag have been definitively confirmed as sunk or lost, two of which were U.S. Navy warships. The fifth ship sunk clearly flew the flag of Panama. We Americans are now standing firm in the face of this situation.
