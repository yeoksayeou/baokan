事有凑巧，一九〇八年間，摩洛哥內亂，土著排外情緒如火如荼。薩威耶族土著，曾在加薩勃朗加港，殺死法人五名，歐洲人三名。事態如此嚴重，法國就派砲艦轟擊該港，並派兵三千佔據該港及其北面的拉巴特。稍後，新蘇丹即位，統馭無方，不到三年，弄得全國皆叛，被叛軍所圍。蘇丹只得要求法國救駕，法國唯恐不盡命，派兵一萬人，打敗叛軍，乘勢佔據斐茲城，重要城鎭，至是盡入法人之手。

一九一二年十一月間，法國和西班牙簽訂最後一次協定。摩洛哥北部長的二百英里深的六十英里之區，劃歸西國。丹吉爾港及其附近四十英里地方劃爲國際中立地帶，其餘，屬於法國，天下三分，暫安一時。法國對於摩洛哥，採取和都尼西亞同樣的懷柔政策，以免壓力過重，引起土著的反抗，而無謂地流血。換言之，蘇丹接受法國保護，法國總監爲太上政府，法國文武官員助理當地行政。

可是即使這樣的懷柔政策，也未見收效於一時，不久摩洛哥土著叛變，殺死斐茲城法人八十名，一時全境騷然。西班牙轄境情形更糟，西國軍隊且被土著軍擊敗。土著領袖有名克里姆的，智勇雙全，號召山嶺地帶的土著，攻擊外國人，實行其摩洛哥人的摩洛哥主義。一九二五年，聲勢極盛，企圖進攻法國佔領區域，法國談虎色變，不得不和西班牙聯合對付，法國並派現任元首的貝當上將督戰。戰爭的壯烈，空前絕後，可惜法國沒有像吉百齡一般的大文豪，把殖民地戰爭寫得可歌可哭。是年終法國殖民地軍隊已陣亡一萬一千餘名。至一九二六年，克里姆始就擒，解往雷羽尼翁島。國境差吿敉平。

摩洛哥是一個摩登的古典的同時又是一個野蠻的國家。境內飛機和駱駝隊同時存在，而且存在的年代也是一般。他們已經浴了一千年的東方文化，他們又容納了西方各色人種和文化。全境面積四十三萬一千方公里，人口的六百萬左右。其重要城市和港口有首府拉巴特，斐茲，馬拉喀斯，加薩勃朗加，摩洛哥城，摩加杜爾，阿加的爾等。全境富於森林橡樹和牲畜。高原上有原始的柏樹，幹高矗天，有的幾和十四層大厦相等。樹的年齡有的達四百年。法國當局努力保護森林，但土著却隨便把它斫伐。斫伐的方法非常奇特，不用斧頭，不用鋸子，只在樹幹上放一把野火，把樹身燒斷，讓大樹自己躺下來。

摩洛哥的政治中心雖在法國總監駐節所在的拉巴特，但回敎土著酋長歡迎住在馬拉喀斯城，該城房屋都是染紅色的，號稱「赤城」，城內有棕櫚樹九百株，紅綠掩映，極盡美觀。各城市中，回教寺院林立，土著信奉回敎誠篤「上帝只有一個，穆罕默德是上帝的先知，」人人這樣禱吿着。拉巴特的蘇丹，每逢星期五拜神的時候，有偉大的行列，民衆夾道歡呼，懿歟盛哉。

摩洛哥內地有許多大大小小的酋長，他們各住着堡壘或營帳，自成一個小小天下，較大的酋長往往妻妾成羣，僕從如雲，擁有數萬匹的馬牛和羊。土著分居各有領域，山地以擺擺人居多，平原以阿刺伯人及摩爾人居多。摩洛哥民性强悍，好爭鬥，不怕死。他們對於自己的酋長和蘇丹，往往也崛强不服從，何况對於外國人。所以雖至今日之下，單身的外國人仍不敢深入腹地。不過編人法國殖民地軍隊的糜洛哥人，却是以勇敢善戰聞名。

也談談貓 31

— 史亭

很有人喜歡拿人跟動物來比較。於是，發出一種「人不如狗」，「人不如貓」之類的感嘆。其實，這種比較和這種感嘆都是不大必要的。

譬如，許席珍先生有感於在黑夜裏行路不方便，會撞到電綫木幹上去，於是，就希望這時候，自己是一只貓了。可是，待我們細細地想一想，似乎覺得有一對貓的眼睛，自然不妨，不過，澈底地成爲一隻貓，實在是要不得的

因爲，人之不習慣於黑暗，在某一方面講，自然不能不說是一種弱點。但是，正因爲人們是不習慣於黑暗，他們才渴求於光明；如果每個人都習慣了黑暗，還有誰再去向黑暗戰鬥，爭取光明呢？

是的，黑暗常常會遮掩了大地，我們須要一付像貓一樣尖銳的眼睛，在黑暗中能够看淸四周的東西，否則，我們不免要踏入汚水潭，跌進陷阱，不能防備暗中襲來的狼羣，更不能够和牠們鬥爭了。可是，我們不能成爲一隻貓，因爲貓是喜歡黑暗的，牠須要黑暗來掩飾，等待機會，獵取食物的人。如果做了黑暗中的貓，那就無異自己願意被黑暗征服了。

所以，我們須要貓的眼睛，可是不要成爲一隻黑暗中的貓。

Worldwide Attention on French North Africa (4) 17

As luck would have it, in 1908, Morocco experienced internal strife, and the anti-foreign sentiment among the natives was raging. Indigenous people of the Zawiya tribe killed five French citizens and three Europeans in the port of Casablanca. Due to the severity of the situation, France dispatched gunboats to bombard the port and sent 3,000 troops to occupy the port and Rabat to its north. Later, a new Sultan ascended to the throne, but his rule was ineffective. Within three years, the entire country was in rebellion, and the Sultan was besieged by rebel forces. The Sultan had no choice but to request help from France. Fearing failure, France sent 10,000 troops, defeated the rebels, and took the opportunity to occupy Fez. As a result, all important towns fell into French hands.

In November 1912, France and Spain signed their final agreement. The northern part of Morocco, a zone 200 miles long and 60 miles deep, was assigned to Spain. The port of Tangier and the surrounding 40 miles were designated as a neutral international zone. The rest belonged to France. The world was divided into three, bringing temporary peace. France adopted a policy of appeasement towards Morocco, similar to that in Tunisia, to avoid excessive pressure that could incite native resistance and unnecessary bloodshed. In other words, the Sultan accepted French protection, with the French Resident-General acting as the supreme government, and French civil and military officials assisting in local administration.

However, even this policy of appeasement did not yield immediate results. Before long, Moroccan natives revolted, killing eighty French citizens in Fez, causing unrest throughout the territory. The situation in the Spanish-controlled area was even worse, with Spanish troops being defeated by native forces. A native leader named Krim, known for his intelligence and bravery, called on the natives in the mountain region to attack foreigners and implement his "Morocco for Moroccans" policy. By 1925, his influence was at its peak, and he attempted to invade the French-occupied areas. France was terrified and had to join forces with Spain to deal with him. France even sent Marshal Pétain, the current head of state, to oversee the war. The war was unprecedented in its intensity, but unfortunately, France did not have a great writer like Rudyard Kipling to write about the colonial war in a way that was both moving and tragic. By the end of that year, the French colonial army had suffered over 11,000 casualties. In 1926, Krim was finally captured and sent to Réunion Island, and the national borders were largely pacified.

Morocco is a modern, classical, and barbaric country all at once. Airplanes and camel caravans exist side by side within its borders, and have done so for quite some time. They have been immersed in a thousand years of Eastern culture, while also embracing various Western peoples and cultures. The total area of the country is 431,000 square kilometers, with a population of about six million. Important cities and ports include the capital Rabat, Fez, Marrakesh, Casablanca, Meknes, Mogador, Agadir, and others. The country is rich in forests, oak trees, and livestock. The highlands are home to ancient cypress trees, with trunks towering into the sky, some reaching the height of a fourteen-story building. Some of these trees are four hundred years old. The French authorities are working hard to protect the forests, but the natives indiscriminately cut them down. Their logging method is very peculiar, using neither axes nor saws, but simply setting fire to the trunk, burning it through, and letting the tree fall on its own.

Although the political center of Morocco is in Rabat, where the French Resident-General resides, Muslim native chiefs prefer to live in Marrakesh. The city's buildings are all painted red, earning it the name "Red City." The city boasts nine hundred palm trees, with red and green intertwined, creating a beautiful sight. Throughout the cities, Muslim mosques stand tall, and the natives are devout in their Islamic faith, praying, "There is only one God, and Muhammad is His prophet." In Rabat, when the Sultan prays on Fridays, there is a grand procession, with people cheering along the way, a truly magnificent sight.

The interior of Morocco is home to many large and small chiefs, each living in fortresses or camps, forming their own little worlds. Larger chiefs often have many wives and concubines, as well as numerous servants, and own tens of thousands of horses, cattle, and sheep. The natives live separately in their respective territories, with Berbers predominating in the mountains and Arabs and Moors predominating in the plains. Moroccans are known for their strength, love of fighting, and fearlessness of death. They often rebel against their chiefs and Sultans, let alone foreigners. Therefore, even today, lone foreigners dare not venture deep into the interior. However, Moroccans who enlist in the French colonial army are renowned for their bravery and skill in battle.

Berber men are all thin and wiry, but their movements are exceptionally agile. The women reveal their beautiful dark eyes, full of passionate charm. They are more open-minded than their Muslim sisters elsewhere, not wearing veils and managing household affairs, being true virtuous wives and good mothers, and also possessing considerable property rights. If it weren't for the harsh African sun, their skin would be very fair, as they are of partly Caucasian descent.

Talking About Cats 31

– Shi Ting

Many people like to compare humans with animals. As a result, exclamations such as "Humans are inferior to dogs" and "Humans are inferior to cats" arise. In fact, this comparison and this exclamation are largely unnecessary.

For example, Mr. Xu Xizhen, feeling inconvenienced by walking in the dark and bumping into telephone poles, hopes that he could be a cat at such times. However, when we think about it carefully, it seems that having a pair of cat's eyes is naturally fine, but completely becoming a cat is really undesirable.

Because, in a certain sense, people's unaccustomed to darkness is indeed a weakness. However, precisely because people are not accustomed to darkness, they yearn for light. If everyone were accustomed to darkness, who would fight against darkness and strive for light?

Yes, darkness often obscures the earth. We need a pair of sharp eyes like a cat's to be able to see clearly the things around us in the dark. Otherwise, we will inevitably step into puddles of sewage, fall into traps, be unable to defend against the wolf packs attacking from the dark, and even less be able to fight with them. However, we cannot become a cat, because cats like darkness; they need darkness to conceal themselves, wait for opportunities, and hunt for food. If we become a cat in the dark, it is tantamount to willingly being conquered by darkness.

Therefore, we need the eyes of a cat, but we must not become a cat in the dark.
