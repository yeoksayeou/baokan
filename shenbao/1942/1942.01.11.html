The North Country is cold. Every winter, there are ice games. This game started in the early Qing Dynasty, called "ice games". In "shooting the ball," a decorated gate was set up in front of the imperial seat, with a ball suspended above called the "celestial ball," and a ball suspended below called the "terrestrial ball." The players were divided into teams to compete in shooting. Each team had one person leading with a small flag, and two people following with bows and arrows, one shooting the celestial ball and one shooting the terrestrial ball. Those who hit the target would be rewarded. Often, one or two hundred people would participate in the game, lining up in order, so they would snake around like a dragon. In addition, there were also "sliding ice mountains." First, water was poured to create an ice mountain, three or four zhang high, incredibly smooth. Brave and strong people would wear shoes made of pig skin, which made them slide even faster. They would slide straight down from the top, and those who did not fall would win.
