一從地理上說起

翻開世界地圖，西部給南北美洲佔據着大部份土地，隔開大西洋，北面是兵戈擾攘的歐洲，南部是廣大的阿非利加洲。自非洲再往東部轉過來，就可以見到最大的印度半島。半島的右面是孟加拉灣，左面是阿刺伯海，和紅海相接。自蘇伊士運河開　後，歐洲就獲得一最近的途徑，載運香料與絲回去，不必再繞道好望角，曠時日久地來到東方。錫蘭島是印度半島的前哨，雄踞在印度洋中。

印度邊境，有高山作爲屛障。西北部的蘇里曼山脈把印度和阿富汗隔開，北部是世界最髙山脈喜馬拉雅山，使印度和中國的西藏分爲二部，東部羣山成爲緬甸與印度的天然分界錢。地理學者通常把印度分成三部：第一是偉大的山脈，拱衛在印度的邊境；第二是廣澳的北部平原，或稱印度恒河流域平原，這是由印度三條主要的河流——印度河，恒河，布拉馬普特拉河——及其支流的流域所形成；第三是印度高原，就是南部的半島部份，由東西高止山脈的對峙，而成爲德在高原。此外，恒河和印度流域之間，有塔爾沙漠，雨量稀少。

印度的海岸極少深的海灣和港口。如其人民生長在北夏華，那哥不爾，或印度的心臟新德里等地方的，離海岸很遠，簡直不知道海是怎樣的遼闊。因爲海岸綫整齊，所以絕少可以利用作為港口。孟買算是優良的自然港口，印度東部的加爾各答，雖然也是港口，但位處恒河的三角洲上，並非臨海的。東部海岸沒有西部這揉多岩石，海水沿岸多沙洲，大輪船因爲水淺不能靠岸，而且暗礁衆多，小船駛行，亦很危險。郵船能直接駛抵碼頭的只有微紮加帕坦和麻德拉斯，二地都是利用人工建築深水碼頭，使船舶可以止碇。

印度北部共有三條主要河流，已如上述。三條河流的起源都在北部邊境的山谷中。山上終年積雪，氣候寒冷。溶雪都緩緩流入河道，還有山洪的冲瀉，所以河流湍急奔騰，一直流入北部平原，才是波平如鏡，時時因爲地勢平坦，河流放棄原有河床，另覓新道。在半島部份的河流和平原上的不同，向東流的主要河流如忠哈那地河，哥達維利河，向西流的如納巴達河，泰普梯河等，都發源於髙原，水量主要是由雨季時的陣雨所積聚而流下，不是雨季時河水極淺，只能駛行極小的小船。印度髙原的斜坡由西朝東，所以雨水即自西高止山脈東流，注入孟加拉灣

因爲地位處於熱帶，印度的氣溫很髙，極少變化。尤其是中部平原，炎熱異常，疫癘盛行。喜馬拉雅山地緯度較北，比較溫和，夏季多有到那裹去避暑的。德干高原因爲地形較髙，比北部平原反來得凉爽。印度河下流雨量極少，田地需要人工灌漑。印度北部平原如判遮普，合衆部，信德給是苦旱，就需要運河的水量，灌漑田畝。

農業是印度主耍的生產，印度全人口十分之七大都是農民。主耍產品有米棉，茶，黃麻，甘蔗等品。恒河下流因田地肥沃，產米極富。印度河下游和德干高原中部產棉，產量居世界第二位，英國棉織品的原料都在此取給。茶除錫蘭產量極豐外，阿薩密，孟加拉等地，均由中國移植茶種，傾銷歐美市塲。黃麻是印度主要輸出品，產地與茶同。其他雅片，橡皮，咖啡亦有出產。與農業相關的　牧方面，運輸笨重木材等，有龐大無比的象隊。耕種與拖拉車輛，牛爲第一。印度每二人可以享用一頭牛，產牛量居世界第一位。礦產方面多煤，石油，錳，金，寳石等。煤產於孟加拉西部，石油產於判遮普，阿薩密，錳產麻得拉斯和孟買，產量居世界第一位。金礦在買索爾和海得拉巴，寳石自古爲有名產地，近雲南邊界一帶更多。印度富源遍地，物產豐盛，英國的經濟資源，大部分都取給於此。所以對於今日的印度問題，英國必須有自知之明，局勢已到緊要關頭，必須互相瞭解，前途才有希望。

在政治上而言，印度共分二部，英屬印度由印度政府統治，印度政府設總督一人，主持全印政務；另外則是印度諸部，各由當地王公管理一切。首都原設加爾各答，一九一二年後，改設新德里。印度幅員遼闊，英屬印度政府劃分全印爲十一大省，六小省。印度諸部受王公統治，印度政府特任命政治顧問一人，幫同治理政務。有的部落區城極大，如海得拉巴，買索爾等，直接與政府發生聯繫，有的小至幾方里，即與境內的省公署保持聯絡。國內婆羅門敎，回敎，佛敎流行均盛。婆羅門敎耶印度敎，信徒衆多，分僧侶，士族，平民，勞働者，奴隸等五種階級，界限森嚴。回敎盛行於俾路支，佛敎起源印度，印人信奉的較少，緬甸和泰國奉行者多，是世界二大佛敎國。此外尙有信基督敎，錫克敎，猶太敎，拜火敎的，各立門戶，有時甚至發生衝突。

印度人口，大約有三萬四千萬人。（340，000，000）人口密度，並不平均，有的部份極少，有的城市人口密集。大致以住在平原或較高的山區，冲積土，或經過人力墾殖的土地上者居多。語言多至百十餘種，最通行的是西印度語和孟加拉語。

首都新德里，地勢衝要，居北部平原的心臟，爲全國第六最大城市，鐵路四通，工商極盛，四周土地因爲灌溉得宜，產棉豐富，即運到新德里的工廠中紡織。建築美麗，尤其是總督府的雄偉富麗，世界罕有。

加爾各答當印度平原的門戶，可稱印度的「商業首都」，大商船可以自由進出恒河流域豐富的農產物都在此出口。鐵路由此橫貫印度以抵孟買，或至麻得拉斯，南印度諸城，或恒河流域去。有宏大的大學，公園，植物園，博物館等，號稱印度的倫敦。

孟買是印度第二大城，人口在百萬以上，港灣深廣，停泊安全，是印度最優良的商港。孟買市建在對岸小島上，和新加坡形勢相仿。孟買後面的髙原上產棉極豐，原料採集後送到孟買市上的棉織廠中紡織，所以孟貿的紡織業是亞洲首屈。市街整潔，寺院建築宏偉，且有完善的船渠倉庫，略帶歐美景色。

麻得拉斯爲印度第三大城，人口約有五十萬。主要輸出物爲皮革，主要的工業就是硝皮。此地沿岸本來很平直，不能算作良港，自英人努力經營後，築防波堤，設棧橋，浚海底，才可停泊大輪，有今日這樣興盛。可見人羣的努力，足以改造自然的缺陷。

台詞擁堵的研究 76

— 陶順聖

在春假裏，旣不能像往日那樣遊山玩水，與其悶在家裏亂翻書，不如看一二齣話劇來得有意思。最近上海的話劇似乎比較有發展，新的劇本固然還不多見，可是新的演員却進來了不少，看完了幾齣春假中的名劇，我意想對於一般演員的念台詞，說幾句隨便的話了。當然，這些話對於著名的大演員們，是不値一讀的吧？

當演員在舞臺的時候，他（或她）若是耍把劇本的中心思想和情節傳達給觀衆，除了表演以外，就得藉他的台詞的背誦了。誦讀台詞應當是一步要緊的工作，可是有些演員却不能做得好。這或許是他們對於聲音和字音還缺少一點注意和訓練，以致有時字音之尖團不分，聲音長短，髙低，輕重，大小，分配得不很自然，破壊台詞的美麗，戲劇的演出。

聲音的不準確，可是有三種原因：

習慣——各地方有各地方的土音，習慣了以後，爲發出土音便利起見，聲音器官往往成了一種特殊的形式，以致阻礙準確的發聲。——這祇要把聲音器官的位置，設法練習糾正過來，就不難了。譬如硬說國語，在舞臺上是欠美的。

生理——牙齒漏風，倒還易於補救，舌短舌厚，聲帶不靈等現象，就比較困難了。

心理——演員上塲後，臨時發生意外，以致心慌意亂，發聲錯誤——這是可以原諒的，然而演員自己和舞裏監督最好能事前加以注意。

至於字音的分辨，除非演員天生生理上實在有缺陷，不然總是容易的。對詞時導演若能稍加指導，當有很大的效果

總之，演員平時得努力訓練自己的聲音聲調，研究字音的讀法，並虛心地接受人家的意見。經騐資質和知識程度情緒等，也都是決定讀音詞的條件。有了藝術化的聲音，然後才能把台詞靈活地背誦出來。

當然一個天才的演員，並不單靠他的聲音，單靠誦讀台詞，而是靠他各方面的成就，和不斷地努力。

Introduction

Since the cloud of war in the South China Sea extended to the Indian Ocean, the corner of India has become the focus of world attention. This is because the future of India is not only related to the gains and losses of the Pacific War but also to the movements of the global situation. Recent British-Indian negotiations have reached a deadlock. How they will develop in the future, whether they will continue to be delayed, or whether there will be other changes, all await the answer of future facts. However, the sentiments of the Indian people have rarely been noted by the outside world, and thus it is difficult to understand the current situation without being detached. Therefore, we have taken various aspects of India that the world is eager to understand, such as geography, race, history, political system, religion, and culture, and divided them into articles, exhausting the descriptions, and compiling them into a special issue. We will publish them daily, hoping that they will be of interest to those who care about world affairs.

1. Starting with Geography

Opening the world map, the western part is occupied by most of North and South America. Separated by the Atlantic Ocean, to the north is Europe, disturbed by war, and to the south is the vast continent of Africa. Turning east from Africa, you can see the largest Indian peninsula. To the right of the peninsula is the Bay of Bengal, and to the left is the Arabian Sea, which connects to the Red Sea. Since the opening of the Suez Canal, Europe has gained the shortest route to transport spices and silk back, without having to go around the Cape of Good Hope, spending a long time to reach the East. Ceylon Island is the outpost of the Indian peninsula, towering in the Indian Ocean.

The Indian border has high mountains as a barrier. The Sulaiman Mountains in the northwest separate India from Afghanistan, and the Himalayas in the north, the highest mountain range in the world, divide India and Tibet in China into two parts. The eastern mountains become the natural boundary between Burma and India. Geographers usually divide India into three parts: first, the great mountain range that guards the Indian border; second, the vast northern plains, also known as the Indo-Gangetic Plain, which is formed by the basins of India's three major rivers—the Indus, the Ganges, and the Brahmaputra—and their tributaries; and third, the Indian Plateau, which is the southern part of the peninsula, formed by the opposition of the Eastern and Western Ghats, becoming the Deccan Plateau. In addition, there is the Thar Desert between the Ganges and Indus basins, with scarce rainfall.

India's coastline has very few deep bays and ports. If its people live in places far from the coast, such as North-West Frontier Province, Nagpur, or New Delhi, the heart of India, they simply do not know how vast the sea is. Because the coastline is regular, there are very few places that can be used as ports. Bombay is considered an excellent natural port, while Calcutta in eastern India, although also a port, is located on the Ganges Delta and is not on the sea. The eastern coast does not have as many rocks as the western coast, and there are many sandbars along the coast. Large ships cannot dock because the water is shallow, and there are many reefs, making it dangerous for small boats to navigate. Only Visakhapatnam and Madras have mail ships that can directly reach the docks. Both places use artificial construction of deep-water docks to allow ships to anchor.

There are three major rivers in northern India, as mentioned above. The origins of the three rivers are all in the mountain valleys on the northern border. The mountains are covered with snow all year round, and the climate is cold. The melting snow slowly flows into the riverbeds, along with mountain torrents, so the rivers are rapid and turbulent, flowing into the northern plains, where the water is calm and like a mirror. Because the terrain is flat, the rivers often abandon their original riverbeds and seek new routes. The rivers in the peninsular part are different from those in the plains. The main rivers flowing eastward, such as the Mahanadi River and the Godavari River, and those flowing westward, such as the Narmada River and the Tapti River, all originate from the plateau. The water volume is mainly accumulated by the monsoon rains, and the river water is extremely shallow when it is not the rainy season, and only very small boats can navigate. The slope of the Indian Plateau is from west to east, so rainwater flows eastward from the Western Ghats and into the Bay of Bengal.

Because it is located in the tropics, India's temperature is very high with little variation. Especially in the central plains, it is extremely hot, and epidemics are prevalent. The Himalayan mountains are located at a higher latitude and are relatively mild. Many people go there to escape the summer heat. The Deccan Plateau is higher in elevation and cooler than the northern plains. There is very little rainfall in the lower reaches of the Indus River, and the fields require artificial irrigation. The northern plains of India, such as Punjab, United Provinces, and Sindh, suffer from drought and require water from canals to irrigate the fields.

Agriculture is India's main production, and seven-tenths of India's total population are farmers. The main products are rice, cotton, tea, jute, and sugarcane. The lower reaches of the Ganges are extremely rich in rice due to the fertile land. Cotton is produced in the lower reaches of the Indus River and the central Deccan Plateau, and the production ranks second in the world. The raw materials for British cotton textiles are supplied from here. In addition to the abundant production of tea in Ceylon, Assam, Bengal, and other places have transplanted tea seeds from China and sell them to the European and American markets. Jute is India's main export, and the place of origin is the same as tea. Other products such as opium, rubber, and coffee are also produced. In terms of animal husbandry related to agriculture, transportation of heavy timber, etc., there are huge elephant teams. Cattle are the first choice for cultivation and towing vehicles. India has one cow for every two people, and its cow production ranks first in the world. Mineral resources include coal, oil, manganese, gold, and gemstones. Coal is produced in western Bengal, oil is produced in Punjab and Assam, and manganese is produced in Madras and Bombay, with the production ranking first in the world. Gold mines are located in Mysore and Hyderabad, and gemstones have been famous production sites since ancient times, with even more near the Yunnan border. India is rich in resources everywhere, and the products are abundant. Most of Britain's economic resources are supplied from here. Therefore, Britain must have self-awareness regarding today's Indian issue. The situation has reached a critical juncture, and mutual understanding is necessary for there to be hope for the future.

In terms of politics, India is divided into two parts: British India, ruled by the Indian government, which has a governor-general who presides over all Indian political affairs; and the Indian princely states, each governed by its local princes. The capital was originally in Calcutta, but after 1912, it was moved to New Delhi. India has a vast territory, and the British Indian government divides India into eleven major provinces and six minor provinces. The Indian princely states are ruled by princes, and the Indian government appoints a political advisor to assist in governing political affairs. Some tribal areas are extremely large, such as Hyderabad and Mysore, and directly contact the government, while others are as small as a few square miles and maintain contact with the provincial government offices within their borders. Brahmanism, Islam, and Buddhism are all popular in the country. Brahmanism is Hinduism, and there are many followers, divided into five classes: monks, nobles, commoners, laborers, and slaves, with strict boundaries. Islam is prevalent in Balochistan. Buddhism originated in India, and fewer Indians believe in it. There are many followers in Burma and Thailand, which are the world's two major Buddhist countries. In addition, there are also those who believe in Christianity, Sikhism, Judaism, and Zoroastrianism, each establishing its own sect, and sometimes even conflicts occur.

India has a population of approximately 340 million people (340,000,000). The population density is not even. Some parts have very few people, while some cities have a dense population. Generally, most people live in the plains or higher mountainous areas, alluvial soils, or land that has been reclaimed by human labor. With rampant greed and unrestrained enjoyment of material pleasures, life in Shanghai is unstable. Abstaining from Greed

National Teacher Qingliang said, "If you compare your mind to the Buddha, you will be the same as the Buddha from beginning to end. The beginning and end are the same, only the middle is different." To understand why the middle is different, one should investigate the origin of human beings. Confucianism and Taoism say that humans inherit the harmony of Heaven and Earth, originating from the Wuji (Ultimateless), which gives rise to the Taiji (Supreme Ultimate), which in turn gives rise to the Two Forms, evolving into the three powers of Heaven, Earth, and Man. Buddhism, on the other hand, believes that humans and Heaven and Earth are all contained within the Alaya-consciousness. The Consciousness-Only theory states that everything is created by the mind, which shows that all things are ultimately empty. Humans originally possessed supreme and radiant light, born in the Heaven of Radiant Sound, and were called Devas (gods). The Dharma-body (spiritual body) permeated the Dharma-realm (universe). At that time, the earth was flat like a palm, and people could fly and soar, essentially the same as the Buddha. Only because of a single deluded thought, the three coarse and six subtle aspects arose: mountains, great earth, greed for play, and greed for earth-cakes. Disaster enters through the mouth. After greedily eating the earth-cakes, their spiritual nature diminished, drifting further from the Buddha, and they became earth-bound people. At this time, with radiant light, the one thought had not yet moved, the spiritual light was abundant, so it is said to be the same as the Buddha from the beginning. Once the one thought has moved, greedy desires and attachments arise, leading to suffering. The Awakening of Faith says, "Movement brings suffering." Cause and effect are inseparable. As greedy desires grow, unrestrained material pleasures are sought, and the human population increases daily. However, material resources are limited, and insatiable greed leads to a sense of insufficiency. Insufficiency leads to contention, contention leads to anger, fighting, and killing, plunging the entire world into chaos, harm, and terror. All of this is caused by greed and delusion obscuring the spiritual nature. My teacher explained that illuminating the mind and seeing one's nature means that the human mind is obscured by the three poisons, giving rise to eighty-four thousand afflictions. If these afflictions are peeled away layer by layer, one's true nature will appear, and the mind will become clear, then one can return to the Buddha-ground, meaning that one will be the same as the Buddha in the end. But how can one peel away these afflictions? Only by receiving and upholding the Five Precepts.

Abstaining from Killing

The first of the precepts is not to kill living beings. A sense of compassion is inherent in everyone. For example, when seeing pork, one chooses the fattest and leanest pieces to satisfy one's appetite. But if it were human flesh, everyone would be horrified and run away. In reality, pigs and humans are both flesh. It is only that eating pork is a habit, and one does not realize the cruelty involved. We should not kill, which means not killing animals and all sentient beings, nor should we neglect the invisible killing. If you want to know the suffering of war in the world, just listen to the sounds from the slaughterhouse in the middle of the night. The so-called "killing sounds" are not limited to the slaughterhouse, and the so-called "killing time" is not limited to midnight. When people catch and kill fleas, bedbugs, etc., that is also killing. This is visible killing. If people are blinded by the three poisons, leading to killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct, isn't that also a form of suicide? Their Dharma-body and life are obscured by delusion and attachments. If people can understand this, they should remove the three poisons, and the mind will naturally become clear. If evil is gone, then good is also absent. Good is merely the opposite of evil. If there is no root, where can evil exist? One must know that no-mind is clarity, while having a mind leads to obscurity, resulting in disharmony. For example, if a preacher is attached to what he is preaching, then he is slandering the Buddha because having a mind leads to obscurity. The Sixth Patriarch's verse says, "Originally there is nothing," which means no-mind. When talking about illuminating the mind and seeing one's nature, regardless of any religion, as long as one understands thoroughly, it can be achieved. Buddhism is different, it treats animals and all sentient beings with equal compassion and universal love. In this way, the mind will naturally become clear and the nature will naturally appear. Those who study Buddhism, especially those who study the precepts, should view everything equally, without distinction between closeness and distance. What I now offer starts from small things, accumulating day by day, naturally eliminating all evil karma, and finally returning to my true nature, illuminating the mind and seeing my nature, then I will ultimately be the same as the Buddha.

When an actor is on stage, if he (or she) wants to convey the central idea and plot of the script to the audience, in addition to acting, he must rely on the recitation of his dialogue. Reciting dialogue should be an important step, but some actors cannot do it well. Habit - Each place has its own local accent. Physiological - Leaky teeth are easy to fix, but short or thick tongues and inflexible vocal cords are more difficult to deal with.

Psychological - If an accident occurs temporarily after the actor takes the stage, causing panic and wrong pronunciation - this can be forgiven, but it is best for the actor himself and the stage director to pay attention beforehand.

The game of chess, some say it originated with Emperor Wu of the Zhou Dynasty (this Emperor Wu refers to Emperor Wu of the Northern Zhou Dynasty; recent people have mistakenly thought it was King Wu of the Zhou Dynasty), but that is not actually the case. The elephant game created by Emperor Wu of Zhou is completely different from the system of chess. According to the continued Buddhist scriptures and the records of successive generations of Zen Master Nianchang, under Emperor Wenzong's reign of Kaicheng (836 AD), there is an entry "Made Chess," with an original note saying: "In the past, Shennong used the sun, moon, stars, and zodiac signs as symbols; Prime Minister Niu Sengru of the Tang Dynasty used chariots, horses, generals, and soldiers, and added cannons to replace them as a mechanism." This "mechanism" should be a mistake for the word "chess." Therefore, the game of chess actually originated with Niu Sengru. Niu wrote a book called "Xuan Guai Lu," in which one section, "Cen Shun," records the movements of chess at that time, but does not mention the number of paths. There is no "elephant" in the chess, and the two armies are called Tianna and Jinxiang. It was not until the Northern Song Dynasty that it was known that the roads were eleven, the chess pieces were thirty-two, and the two armies were called Chu and Han. Cheng's poem about the elephant game says, "Heroes like Liu and Xiang also compete idly." However, there was still no "elephant" but "pian" and "bi." It was not until the Southern Song Dynasty that Liu Kezhuang wrote a poem about the elephant game, saying, "Kunyang rushed with elephants," and then there was an "elephant" in the chess, but no "pian" or "bi." It can be seen that the "elephant" actually came from the combination of "pian" and "bi," and the cannon was originally in the rear, but then it was placed in the front. Therefore, the eleven roads were also changed to nine roads, and the division in the middle was also called the Great River, all completely the same as the current system. Therefore, the game of chess actually originated in the Tang Dynasty and was completed in the Song Dynasty.
