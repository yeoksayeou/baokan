Today in Japan's Invasion of China

This page links to issues of Shenbao published on your local calendar date in the years 1937 (from July), and 1939 until 1945 (through August) if there are issues available for the relevant day. Dates are determined by your browser’s time‑zone. More years may be added later.

    Some Key Events in Japan's Invasion of China

    • 1928, May - Jinan incident with Japanese forces in Shandong
    • 1928, Jun - Zhang Zuolin assassinated by Japanese
    • 1931, Sep - The Japanese Invasion of Manchuria
    • 1932, Jan-Mar - Japanese attack and occupation of parts of Shanghai after staged attack on Japanese monks and civilians
    • 1932, Mar - Japanese sponsored Manchukuo declared in northeast China with Qing emperor Puyi as head of state
    • 1932, May - Shanghai Ceasefire Agreement signed by China and Japan
    • 1933, Jan - Japanese occupation of Rehe (Jehol)
    • 1933, Feb - Lytton report condemns Japan
    • 1933, Mar - Japan withdraws from League of Nations after Lytton report
    • 1933, May - Tanggu Truce creates demilitarized zone in northern Hebei
    • 1934, Mar - Puyi resumes imperial status, taking reign title Kangde
    • 1934 - Prince Te Inner Mongolian movement
    • 1934 - Second visit to China of German military advisor Hans von Seeckt, secret treaty signed
    • 1935 - He-Umezu agreement signed, "Hebei-Chahar Political Council" formed
    • 1935 - Qin-Doihara agreement
    • 1935 - December 9th Movement against Japanese imperialism
    • 1935 - "East Hebei Autonomous Council" formed with Japanese support
    • 1936, Dec - The Xi'an Incident
    • 1937, Jul - The Marco Polo Bridge Incident
    • 1937, Jul - Beiping and Tianjin occupied by Japanese forces
    • 1937, Aug - Battle of Shanghai begins, lasting until late November
    • 1937, Sep - CCP Red Army re-designated as the Eighth Route Army
    • 1937, Sep - Lin Biao forces defeat Japanese in Pingxingguan ambush
    • 1937, Oct - Shijiazhuang falls to Japanese
    • 1937, Oct - Battle of Xinkou, major battle opens road to Taiyuan
    • 1937, Nov - Taiyuan falls to Japanese
    • 1937, Dec - Chiang moves HQ to Wuhan
    • 1937, Dec - Chiang Kai-shek says he will not negotiate with Japanese until withdrawal
    • 1937, Dec - Panay Incident
    • 1937, Dec - Fall of Nanjing to Japan and the Nanjing Massacre
    • 1937, Dec - "Provisional Government of the Republic of China" formed by Japanese at Beiping
    • 1937, Dec - by this time Suzhou (Nov), Hangzhou, Jinan, Beiping fall to Japan
    • 1938, Jan - Japanese say they will not negotiate with Chiang
    • 1938, Jan - Chiang Kai-shek executes Han Fuju for failure to resist
    • 1938, Mar - "Reformed Government of the Republic of China" formed by the Japanese at Nanjing
    • 1938, Mar - Japanese defeat in Battle of Taierzhuang
    • 1938, May - Xuzhou falls to Japanese, Chinese eventually withdraw to Wuhan
    • 1938 - Li Zongren defeats Japanese at Taierzhuang
    • 1938, Oct - Wuhan and Guangzhou fall to Japanese, Chiang moves HQ to Chongqing
    • 1938, Nov - GMD raze Changsha
    • 1938, Dec - Wang Jingwei leaves Chongqing heads to Hanoi
    • 1938, Dec - Konoe Fumimaro "Three Principles" statement for settlement of the war
    • 1938 - The Burma Road is completed
    • 1939 - Soviet-Japanese clashes, Nomonhan Incident
    • 1939, Mar - Burma road opens to traffic
    • 1939 - Fierce battles for Changsha, which holds until 1944
    • 1939 - Mengjiang government in Inner Mongolia founded with Japanese support
    • 1939, Dec - Wang Jingwei secret agreement with Japanese
    • 1940, Mar - Wang Jingwei declares new "Reorganized" Nationalist government at Nanjing
    • 1940, Jul - Burma road closed for several months
    • 1940, Aug - Communist Hundred Regiments Offensive begins
    • 1940, Aug - Declaration of the Co-Prosperity Sphere under Konoe Fumimaro
    • 1940, Sep - Japan occupies northern Vietnam after fall of France
    • 1940, Oct - Battle of Huangqiao between Nationalist forces and New Fourth Army of the CCP
    • 1940, Dec - Chiang Kai-shek gives ultimatum demanding all Communist forces move north of Yangzi by end of year
    • 1941 - US lend-lease extended to Chinese government
    • 1941, Jan - New Fourth Army Incident
    • 1941, Apr - Fuzhou falls to Japanese
    • 1941, Oct - Zhengzhou falls to Japanese
    • 1941, Dec - Hong Kong falls to Japanese. Japan invasions in Southeast Asia and attack on Pearl Harbor
    • 1941, Dec - Notice the sudden shift in reporting in the Shenbao as Japanese forces take control of the international settlement in Shanghai
    • 1942-4 - Joseph Stilwell US commander of China Burma India Theater
    • 1942-3 - Hundreds of thousands die in Henan Famine
    • 1942 - Chinese expeditionary forces to Burma in failed aid of British defence
    • 1942, May - Wang's Nanjing regime launch Rural Pacification Movement
    • 1942, Jun - Japanese defeated at Midway
    • 1943 - Wang Jingwei regime permitted to take control of
    • 1943, Jan - Wang's regime reorganized, launch New Citizen Movement
    • 1943, Jan - Japanese and Thai attack Yunnan
    • 1943, Jan - Treaties abolish extraterritoriality
    • 1943 - Japanese "mopping up" campaigns in Jin-Cha-Ji and Ji-Lu-Yu Border Regions
    • 1943, Nov - Chiang Kai-shek attends the Cairo Conference
    • 1944, Mar - Wang Jingwei falls ill and dies
    • 1944-5 - Japanese "Ichigo" offensive
    • 1944, Jun - Changsha falls
    • 1944, Jul - Dixie Mission begins with US observers arriving in Yan'an
    • 1944, Nov - Guilin falls to Japanese
    • 1944, Dec - Chinese Expeditionary force take part in retaking of Burma
    • 1945, Apr - Battle of Hunan
    • 1945, May - Fuzhou and Nanning retaken by Nationalists, other cities follow in summer
    • 1945, Aug 8 - Soviet invasion of Manchuria
    • 1945, Aug 10 - News of a Japanese offer to surrender spreads throughout Chinese forces
    • 1945, Aug 15 - Japanese surrender and Japanese emperor's radio announcement
    • 1945, Sep 2 - Formal Japanese surrender
