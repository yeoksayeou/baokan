德國左右派之影片爭

世界各國之政黨。苟其主張相反者。則無論保事。皆可借攻訏之具無論何種細徵之事。皆可以報復之手段出之。其目的旣在攻訏。手段旣在報復。則必不暇問及其事之關於國家利害如何。影響於社會安危如何。故政爭一啓。遂無寗息之日。其可危爲何如也。如今日柏林電謂。左派各黨。在影戲院反對開映普王大腓特利克歷史影片。幾釀大暴動。經大隊察示威驅散。至探究左派各黨所以反對此影片之故。則因憤恨國家社會黨之阻撓開映西線無戰事影片之故。遂指此影片爲鼓吹帝制思想。反對和平主義。以報復之耳。夫國家社會黨之反對西線無戰事。本屬借題發揮今左派各黨之反對大腓特利克影片。又因國家社會黨之發揮而發揮之。此種極細微之事用心尙且如是。則凡較有關係之問題可藉口以相攻擊者。更不知其如何景象矣此政爭之所以可慮也。雖然。凡爲人類。無不有爭勝於人與不甘受屈於人之心。故世界無論何種社會。莫不以報復爲處世應有之手段。大之至於驚天動地之戰事。亦無不由此而起。豈僅政黨如是哉。

英國汽車工業進化史

近世汽車發達之國。除美洲新大陸首屈一指外當以大不列顚居次席。試一廻溯其三十年來之進步史與他國比較之。亦一饒有趣味之事也。按三十年前。英國所有之各種汽車尙不满三千輛。從事製造汽車者。不足二千人。蓋當二十世紀之始。汽車工業猶在萌芽時代。一般民衆對於汽車一物。胥視爲玩好奢侈之品。殊不注意。近今所謂汽車速度紀錄及耐久試騐等名詞。悉未創出。至若十二缸及十六缸汽車之設計。與夫造新紀錄如「金箭號」者之驚人成績。尤非當時人士所能夢想。而在今日。汽車工業上之一切發展。他且無論。僅就需用之人工而言。每日已達二直餘萬之衆。可想見其趨勢之孟晋。不僅爲致富之源·且亦足爲一般失業者之一種救濟也。

欲明英國汽車業進步之由來。宜先略述法德諸國先進之程序。蓋英國現在汽車之發達。雖佔第二席。然其創興實較他國爲遲。考法國當時有汽車二千八百九十七輛。其中之用於工業者僅五百四十三輛。一八九五年曾以汽車作初次競賽。自巴黎至波爾多往返共七百四十四英里。得勝之車。形式粗陋。並無頂頭汽門及化汽機之設備。所經盡崎嶇不平之劣路。平均每小時僅行十五英里。　一八九六年後。法國汽車工業進肯極速。而一切工商孱

業由是乃益臻發達。德國素以工業著稱。對於汽車之研究。尤注全神。因其與交通軍事師努力於運輸方法之工作。實無時不殫精竭智。以謀世界運輸業之革新。果也七年之内。成績大著。僅一九○七年度。一歲中已造成汽車二萬七千零二十六輛。足徵進展之速。至於美國當一九○○年。僅　有値價一百二十九萬元之汽車六百輛。爾時亨得福特猶是一少年工程師。日惟夢想自製其理想中之汽車。而其結果卒成爲世界最大之汽車製造家。地屈爾猶是他種工業繁榮之區。經三十年汽車専家之薈萃經營。竟成爲今日汽車工業之中心區。發展如此。更非他國所及三十年前。英國汽車界先進。鑒於法蘭西之以汽車而致百業繁盛。激發其競爭之觀念。始從事製造汽車。同時世界各國亦漸知汽車功用之宏。咸欲總握汽車工業之樞鈕。以爭先着。遂起劇烈之競鬥。因此對於各項製車原料。如油、橡皮、鋼鐵、及五金之類。亦聯帶發生國際之大角逐此實爲英國創造汽車之第一時期。由是遂日進無休矣。

復因一九○○年之初。各國爭以鉅資全力。用於汽車工業。冀得戰勝於國外市塲。乃制定汽車法規。以立國内市塲之保障。對於建樹速率、耐久、等新紀錄之専家不惜予以優厚之獎勵。所耗之資殆亦盈千累萬。其提倡鼓勵之策。有如此者自後汽車運輸之業乃日益發達。駸駸乎與輪舶鐵路相抗衡。國中荒僻之區。一變而爲繁盛之市。此皆三十年中發達之成績。在昔法規未頒佈前。汽車進展之趨勢。動多障碍。計自一八九六迄一九○四年間。所造之汽車。其重量與載重未有能逾三噸者。至於貨車。且在禁止之列。

當一九○○年之際。大不不列顚與愛爾蘭汽車總會即今之皇家汽車總會。曾有汽車里週游之組織。以獎掖有志之士。是爲汽車遠征之噧矢。當時參加者亦殊踴躍。歷游各大成鎭且及蘇格蘭之區域、轍所屆。備受當地之盛大歡迎。良以事屬創舉。聞者見莫不驚奇。咸欲爭飽眼福於是商停於市。農輟於野。相率來觀。即深在地層之煤礦職工。亦棄其開掘工作。來觀此利用汽油爲人控御之奇異機械。歡迎會舉行之際。銀行工廠且休業參加。足見此二十世紀之重要新機械。殆無地無人不深注意之。而汽車一物。爲陸路運輸之唯一利器。亦可由此種熱烈表現。予民衆以確切之信任。與深刻之印象也。

外胎内部。務宜清潔。週有他物。應即傾出之。胎骨布繩。應完健無斷裂或將斷裂處。將内胎先打氣少許。然後放入外胎内。蓋如此可免其絞扭或嵌入脚跟處。次即放上襯帶。襯帶之大小。應與車胎符合。放時宜小心。不應使之折起。放好後。再加少許氣。如是則極易上鋼圈。

宜將圓孔。靠緊内胎。撱圓孔在外面。與鋼圈相觸。倘非如此。則内胎易被鉗住。次察視車胎口邊。是否與鋼圈这捲邊接觸妥貼。

胎旣上好以後在未打满氣以前。應在胎之周圍。打繫數次。使胎易與鋼圈密合。氣打满後。裝上氣門蓋。即可備用。

（二）能負重量與膨脹力。用氣胎之目的。爲使全車舒軟。胎内有空氣。其舒軟之程度。全看空氣之壓力如何而定。此種壓力。因胎而異。與能負之重有關。不宜太强。亦不宜太弱。打氣時。應依製造家所規定之壓力表行之。因用此壓力。方得其壽命長久也。表内之數目。均是由經騐得來。非隨意亂定者。故宜注意遵守之。

（三）載量過重與膨脹不足氣胎受一定之膨脹後。僅能負載一定之最大重量。苟重量小於此最大數。膨脹之程度又正與負重相符合時。則胎命甚長。若重量大於該胎所能負者。則雖膨脹充足。其屈伸振幅。逐漸增朋。卒致過度。其結果將與膨脹不足之損害同。

The Use and Protection of Car Tires

By Yue Shan

The value of car tires is quite high. Smaller ones cost dozens of taels each, while larger ones cost hundreds of taels. Apart from fuel, car tires account for a significant portion of automobile expenses. The lifespan of a tire depends entirely on the usage conditions. At a time when gold is expensive and silver is cheap, and tire prices are soaring, you should pay attention to this to avoid unnecessary losses.

(1) Tire Installation: Tires are installed on steel rims. However, there are several procedures that should be observed during installation. The inside of the steel rim should not be painted because the paint often peels off after drying, causing rust to form immediately. This rust causes the rubber to adhere to the steel rim, making it difficult to remove. To avoid this problem, apply a small amount of graphite grease to the steel rim before installing the tire.

Before placing the inner tube inside the outer tire, make sure that the screw that fixes the valve is tightened. The internal parts of the valve should also be checked at the same time.

The inside of the outer tire must be clean. Any foreign objects should be removed. The fabric cords of the tire carcass should be intact and free of any breaks or impending breaks. Inflate the inner tube slightly before placing it inside the outer tire. This will prevent it from twisting or getting caught at the heel. Then, place the liner strip on top. The size of the liner strip should match the tire. Be careful when placing it to avoid folding it. After placing it properly, add a little more air. This will make it very easy to mount on the steel rim.

The round hole should be close to the inner tube, and the oval hole should be on the outside, touching the steel rim. If this is not done, the inner tube may be pinched. Then, check whether the tire bead is in full contact with the rolled edge of the steel rim.

After the tire is mounted, before fully inflating it, tie it around the circumference several times to help the tire seal tightly with the steel rim. After the air is fully inflated, install the valve cap, and it is ready for use.

(2) Load Capacity and Inflation Pressure: The purpose of using pneumatic tires is to make the entire car ride smoothly. The degree of smoothness depends entirely on the air pressure inside the tire. This pressure varies depending on the tire and is related to the load it can carry. It should not be too high or too low. When inflating the tire, follow the manufacturer's specified pressure gauge. Only by using this pressure can the tire's lifespan be extended. The numbers in the gauge are all derived from experience and are not randomly set, so pay attention to following them.

(3) Overloading and Underinflation: After a pneumatic tire reaches a certain inflation pressure, it can only carry a certain maximum weight. If the weight is less than this maximum number, and the degree of inflation matches the load, then the tire's lifespan will be very long. If the weight is greater than what the tire can carry, then even if it is fully inflated, its bending and vibration amplitude will gradually increase, eventually leading to excessive stress. The result will be the same as the damage caused by underinflation.

In both of these situations, the tire carcass is subjected to excessive work. The fabric cords gradually weaken and will soon be unusable. When the overload is more severe and the underinflation is more pronounced, the tire's lifespan will be even shorter.

The problem of overloading is that it makes the tire prone to punctures, cuts, and increased wear.

Inflating the tire beyond the required pressure often causes tire fatigue and is also not advisable.

If the load is too heavy, you must replace it with a larger tire. Never continue to use the original tire by simply inflating it more.

When underinflated, if you force the car to drive, the tire's edge and heel will definitely break. The less air, the more severe the damage will be. If you are driving a long distance, not only will the outer tire be useless, but the inner tube will also be damaged beyond repair.

The pressure inside the tire is related to the tire's lifespan, so it is important to maintain an appropriate and constant level and not allow it to change.

(4) Tire Damage: (A) Abrasion of the Tread: The biggest cause is improper usage and bad road conditions. Misalignment of the wheels also makes the tread wear out easily. At this time, the part of the wheel that touches the ground is not on the plane of rotation, which will inevitably force the tread to rub on the ground. This problem often appears on the front wheels.

Wheels that wobble during rotation, misaligned steel rim centers, and other similar causes can cause uneven and rapid tire wear.

Excessive use of the brakes can damage the tread. When the wheels slip, the rubber is scraped off, leaving debris on the ground. Sudden acceleration also often scrapes off the rubber.

Roads that are damaged, under construction, or frozen and uneven are all formidable enemies of car tires. On excessively bumpy roads, the weight of each axle, which was originally evenly distributed over a large area, is now concentrated on the smallest protruding part, so the rubber is easily scraped off, and the tire carcass cords are also easily broken.

(B) Tire Carcass Delamination: That is, the separation between the tread and the fabric layers or between the fabric layers. The cause of this type of damage is overloading or underinflation, which causes excessive stress on various parts of the tire carcass. When separation first occurs, friction is generated between the two separated surfaces, generating heat. Soon, the entire separation occurs.

A tire with internal delamination will burst easily when heated. The inner tube also heats up at the same time, sometimes even causing the outer tire to burst first. Any unpatched cracks are quite dangerous if mud and dust enter through them. This often causes the tire to burst.

(C) Tire Carcass Cord Breakage: The biggest cause is underinflation or overloading, which causes excessive bending. If the tire suddenly hits a curb or a pothole in the road when the air is insufficient, the tire carcass cords can also break.

When the tire carcass cords are slightly broken but not too badly, they should be repaired immediately. Otherwise, the damage will expand too much and cannot be repaired.

(D) Outer Tire Bead Damage: That is, the part that hooks onto the steel rim is damaged. This is mostly caused by excessive bending and poor steel rims.

If the rolled part of the steel rim is not correct, then the tire cannot be properly mounted on it and will be cut immediately. When the rolled edge of the steel rim is pressed down or deformed, it should be repaired. It is best to replace it with a new one because a repaired steel rim is never as good as the original. Do not mount the tire on a rusty steel rim until the rust has been completely removed.

(E) Sidewall Abrasion: The rubber on the tire sidewall is used to protect the tire carcass from water erosion and side abrasion. Cars that travel on streets are easily damaged because they often rub heavily against the street curbs. On muddy roads or poorly maintained roads, as well as on frozen ruts, the tire sidewalls are subjected to extremely severe abrasion. To avoid this, you must be extra careful when driving on the roads mentioned above.

If the abrasion is too severe and the tire carcass is exposed, you should apply rubber again as soon as possible. Otherwise, prolonged exposure will damage the tire carcass.

(F) Tread Punctures and Tears: Tread tears are common to all tires, but they are more dangerous for pneumatic tires. The tears often gradually enlarge, containing foreign objects. The two torn blocks also often peel off. This peeling area makes it very easy for water to enter the tire carcass, which, if not repaired quickly, will cause corrosion and make the tire unusable.

(G) Inner Tube Punctures: This type of puncture is not entirely caused by weak rubber or other defects. When its size does not match the outer tire, such as being too long or too wide, folding inside the outer tire, causing it to be occasionally pinched, or being too narrow, causing excessive elongation after inflation, are all causes of punctures.

Using cheap inner tubes, that is, tires made of inferior materials, is not economical, unless they are used on old outer tires. It is not appropriate to install old inner tubes inside new outer tires.

Inner tube bursts are often caused by being pinched under the heel of the outer tire during installation. When the tire is soft, the heel moves, causing a burst. The inner tube occasionally comes into contact with a rusty steel rim, causing a tear or complete cut. In thick-edged outer tires, if it is not properly placed and is folded, it will also often break.

Inner tubes, like outer tires, will be damaged and unusable if you drive with insufficient air. Driving too fast, carrying too much weight, or having insufficient pressure will cause the temperature to rise very quickly. This kind of heat is very detrimental to durability, so you must pay attention to it.

(5) Secrets of Use and Protection: The high value of car tires has already been mentioned, so you must pay special attention when using them. The various points mentioned above are summarized below for reference.

(1) Check the degree of inflation inside the tire.

(2) Stop immediately when there is no air. (3) Check the alignment of the wheels. (4) Remember that lubricating oil, sunlight, fire, and heat are all causes of tire damage. (5) Spare tires should be wrapped and stored properly. (6) It is best not to use rusty or damaged steel rims. (7) Repair any small sidewall tears immediately. (8) Check the brakes to ensure that the force on each wheel is very even. (9) Avoid severe impacts. (10) Never exceed the weight that the tire can carry.

(11) Accelerate gradually when starting, and do not be too sudden.
