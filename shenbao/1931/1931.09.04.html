Jiangbei Flood Devastation 1

Eighteen Counties Severely Affected

Guazhou East Riverbank Collapses Again

Nanjing Provincial Committee telegraphed after inspecting the Dongtai area, stating that although Dongtai was flooded and suffered population losses, the situation was not as severe as reported. However, villages along the edges of Gaoyou, Bao'ying, Hongze, and Dazong Lakes, with a population of over three million and tens of millions in property, have been completely washed away. The floodwaters are raging, and it is impossible to count the number of victims clinging to rooftops, trees, and gathering in slightly higher areas, making widespread relief efforts impossible. Only those visible are being provided with food first, but there is no general solution yet. (3rd Dispatch)

Zhenjiang: The east riverbank of Guazhou, across from Zhenjiang, has collapsed again, submerging 120 square li, affecting over 50,000 victims. (3rd Dispatch)

Zhenjiang: According to a telegram from Bao'ying County Magistrate Zhao to the Provincial Government, the water level has been rising sharply for days. Despite leading efforts to reinforce the dikes, the strong winds and surging water have caused almost all the dikes to collapse. The number of deaths is incalculable. Fortunately, the city remains intact. Please provide swift relief. (3rd Dispatch)

Zhenjiang: The Provincial Government has dispatched He Haiqiao and others, along with troops from the Pacification Commissioner's Office, to Dongtai to find a way to open a port and divert water into the river. (3rd Dispatch)

Zhenjiang: As of the evening of the 3rd, a total of 18 counties have been severely affected, with approximately 2.4 million victims. The hardest hit include Taizhou (400,000), Yangzhou, Gaoyou, Xinghua, Yancheng, and Dongtai (300,000 each). Moderately affected areas include Zhenjiang (150,000), Funing (100,000), Bin'ying, Jiangyin, Jurong, and Yizheng (50,000 each), Gaoxun and Lishui (30,000 each), Rugao, Jiangning, and Yangzhou (20,000 each).
