學校工廠合一

本年一月三十一日日本衆院決算委員會開會，討論學校動向，決定工業學校的設施，努力活用旣有的設備，或利用其附近工業學校所有的設備。本月十六日日本呆都帝大工業部，決定移至工廠授課，下月中旬，該校電氣工程科學生四十餘名，即先於加藤信義敎授指導下住宿於三菱電機公司某工廠，日間作兵器之寳地寳習，夜問則由各敎授分別講授課程。（見十七日本報）這種把學校工廠打成一片的學生生活，把敎，學，做，三件事合成一件的精神，日本當局「有議必決，有決必行，有行必速」的新氣象，眞是値得我們欽佩。

戰時需要增產，增產的意義是人力的增產和物力的增產。人力的增產比物力的增產重要，而技術人員的增產更比其他人才為重要。只有人力，方才能够充分利用已有的物力，補充不足的物力，創造新生的物力；而這些能夠想出法子，能夠指導工作的人，便是技術人員。技術人員在後方的責任，不亞於前綫的將士，假如後方的生產不能與前綫消耗的數量相符合，戰爭一定是衰而竭的。技術人員的重要如此，那末，充分利用已有的技術人員，和努力造就未來的技術人員，便是政府當局所應常注意的一件事，特別在戰爭時期，一步都鬆暇不得，一刻都浪費不得。

盟邦日本已經把這個問題解決了，解決的方法就是工廠學校合一。現在巳有的技術人員，無論是工廠技師，學校敎授，都負着二種責任，一方面努力使國家生產增進，一方面努力造就下一代的人才。同時，學生也負着二種責任，一方面努力求學使將來成功一個技術人才，一方而努力做工使現在國家增多生產。無論在那一方面的努力，敎也好，學也好，做也好，一切的一切，把戰爭做中心，這是戰時體制，這是戰鬥精神。

我們中國的工業原來落後，工廠的數量也不多，技術人員更缺乏，因此，僅就這些少數的人力物力，充分利用以謀將來發展的計劃，更是需要，更是急切。我們從前提出過「在業訓練」的辦法，又提出過利甩巳交還政府管理的敵產工廠，造就人才的意見。我們現在更希望全國的企業家，按照日本的方法，自動的請求大學校敎授和學生們的合作，依照其木身事業的性質，規定計劃，協助某二科某一部份技術人才的推進，這是對國家，對自己，對現在，對將來，都是有利的工作。同時政府方面應常竭力幫助企業的進行，予企業上的便利，獎勵這樣造就出來的人之。

新京二十四日中央社電　滿洲曾於去年網羅滿題科學界權威者組成資源調查隊後，即在蒙疆達布蘇等地實行大規模之探險工作。該隊於本月十九日在滿洲大陸科學院舉行研究報吿會，席間即由地質研究所之岡田重光氏。大陸科學院內之山淳氏。分別報吿探險結果。岡田氏報吿文題為一鹽湖成因論」，山淳氏報吿文題為「達布蘇湖水之成分」。按該資源調杳隊長為大陸科學院板野新夫農學博士，隊員爲由滿洲國方面及日本科學動員協會派遣之科學者一行，於去夏五月二十九日抵達現地，詳細調查歷三月餘，始判明湖水之實質，此外尙發現其他重要資源。關於地下養源僅曾試掘，今後已有充分之自信。又岡田氏之「鹽湖成因論一，判定達布蘇湖乃屬鹽素單一系之鹽湖，該地於二億年前乃為大澤，後因地殼變動，形成山地，流向低地之海水乾燥後，乃蒸發成鹽。岡田氏並蒐集魚介類之化石作為證明。關於達布蘇湖水之成分，內山氏巳報吿分析水質之一部，並確認該湖向有稀有之元素及其他重要資源。板野農學博士則謂蒙疆達布蘇湖乃蒙疆之一大鹽湖，其含鹽量之大，冠絕世界，該湖於表面上似結有厚約一米之鹽水，湖面全爲鹽之結晶、於其下則為大量之鹽水，鹽分之密度亦甚大。

美

美國的特色，在於用大型轟炸機的大編隊在良好天氣實行白畫轟炸。其轟炸機爲空中要塞「里倍當泰」，裝甲及戰鬥武器均此英機爲優，不多　燒夷彈，在白晝容易找尋目標，故光照準目標欲挪彈。在白書轟炸中，常被德國戰鬥機截擊，故美轟炸機具有强大的戰鬥力，並用大編隊與戰鬥機交戰。

在去年的許凡福爾特轟炸中，美國的犧牲極大，之後乃暫擇多雲的白晝，並以挪威及其他近距離地點爲目標。後來又用長距離戰鬥機在天氣晴朗的時候出擊。據最近發表，現已使用一種新的裝置，雖在多雲的天氣亦能透過雲層照準。但似不十分正確。

德

一九四一年的德國轟炸英國，其特色可得而奉者，不像今日的英國道樣持有區域轟炸的觀念，利用歐洲前進其地的電波正確飛行；重視行動範圍，不裝載炸彈至限度。但因對蘇戰中轟炸機的必要，英國的電波探知機，地上防空設備，戰鬥機邀擊方法的發達等，而不能不中止。當時的德國，不像今日的英國這樣以轟炸都市作爲戰略轟炸，而以爲有最大的重要性者，此亦爲其理由之一。然最近的連續轟炸倫敦，實爲壯舉。偷敦人士將重嘗昔日恐怖的日子。

震旦大學　新藏圖書珍籍

— 羅逸民

震旦大學最近得劉體智先生捐贈巨書四部，均為研究中國文獻及語言學之珍籍，巳於前日起陳列該校語學圖書館，以供觀覽。劉氏乃當代宿儒，家學淵源，其尊人秉章公係遜淸咸豐年間碩儒，任四川總督有年，歿後　賜「文莊」，所撰「劉文莊公奏議」八卷及「靜軒筆記」四卷，由體智先生付梓，即今珍籍之前二種也。另二種卷軼浩繁，係體智失生所撰著，為硏究中國靑銅銘刻之珍籍。一為「善齊吉金錄」，凡二十有八冊，內戰靑銅之圖畫及銘刻數千輻，均為劉氏昔年或現有藏物。一為「小校經閣」，凡十八冊，係六千五百餘件古銅銘刻之眞跡拓本，附以現代抄本，前人之註解，及劉氏本人之批訂。現代中國語言學者，凡關切中國書法史及用科學方法，解釋中國文字與碑銘者，對於劉氏斯著，决不可等閑視之。

關於古字之解釋，許愼著有「說文解學」，惟觀其書中所列各字體，可　其所知之古銅器及銘刻，為數有限。自澳以降，史書對於古銅之發現，時有道及。迨至宋代，始擴大蒐集，而决意對於銘刻，請求解釋。惟至晚淸，乃有長足之進步。關於銅器收藏之書籍，出版甚多，拓印銘刻，並作註解。然所刋拓本，往往為手抄本而非原本。刋物之增多，係因各著名廢墟銅器發掘日多所致，雖至今日，仍有此種趨勢。而在此類刋物之中，當以劉氏之畢生著作，為集其大成。

劉氏精通文史經典，於小學尤所精硏，對於古代銘刻若千章節，作精確之解釋，發前人所未發。氏不僅為中國文史之大家，其著作中，且表現現代科學之精神：即某種見解，無論其由來若何久遠，若與近年來經由縝密硏究與發掘所得之種種材料相抵觸，即毅然棄之，絕無猶豫。要之，劉氏實為東方文化之傳人。今者以上述四書，贈與震旦大學，使大多數人士，咸有觀摩之機會，誠足感謝也。

Unification of Schools and Factories

On January 31 of this year, the Japanese House of Representatives Budget Committee held a meeting to discuss the direction of schools, deciding on the facilities of industrial schools, striving to make full use of existing equipment, or utilizing the equipment of nearby industrial schools. On the 16th of this month, the Industrial Department of Tokyo Imperial University in Japan decided to move to factories for teaching. In mid-April, more than 40 students from the Department of Electrical Engineering of the university, under the guidance of Professor Nobuyoshi Kato, will stay in a certain factory of Mitsubishi Electric Corporation, engage in the practice of weapons production during the day, and attend lectures by various professors at night. (See the 17th edition of this newspaper). This kind of student life that merges schools and factories into one, and the spirit of combining teaching, learning, and doing into one, and the new atmosphere of the Japanese authorities who "must decide on what is discussed, must act on what is decided, and must act quickly on what is implemented," is truly worthy of our admiration.

Increased production is needed in wartime, and the meaning of increased production is increased manpower and increased material resources. The increase of manpower is more important than the increase of material resources, and the increase of technical personnel is even more important than other talents. Only with manpower can we fully utilize existing material resources, supplement insufficient material resources, and create new material resources; and those who can come up with ideas and guide the work are technical personnel. The responsibility of technical personnel in the rear is no less than that of the soldiers on the front line. If the production in the rear cannot meet the quantity consumed on the front line, the war will inevitably decline and be exhausted. The importance of technical personnel is such that making full use of existing technical personnel and striving to cultivate future technical personnel is something that the government authorities should always pay attention to, especially in wartime, when no step can be relaxed and no moment can be wasted.

Our allied nation Japan has already solved this problem, and the solution is the unification of factories and schools. Existing technical personnel, whether factory technicians or school professors, bear two responsibilities: on the one hand, striving to increase national production, and on the other hand, striving to cultivate the next generation of talents. At the same time, students also bear two responsibilities: on the one hand, striving to study hard to become a technical talent in the future, and on the other hand, striving to work hard to increase national production now. Regardless of the effort in any aspect, whether it is teaching, learning, or doing, everything is centered on the war. This is the wartime system, this is the fighting spirit.

China's industry was originally backward, the number of factories is not large, and technical personnel are even more lacking. Therefore, a plan to make full use of these few manpower and material resources to seek future development is even more necessary and urgent. We have previously proposed the method of "on-the-job training," and also proposed the idea of using enemy-owned factories that have been returned to government management to cultivate talents. We now hope that entrepreneurs across the country, following the Japanese method, will voluntarily request the cooperation of university professors and students, formulate plans according to the nature of their own businesses, and assist in the advancement of technical talents in certain departments or parts. This is a beneficial work for the country, for themselves, for the present, and for the future. At the same time, the government should always strive to help the progress of enterprises, provide conveniences for enterprises, and reward the talents cultivated in this way.

Aurora University New Collection of Rare Books

- Luo Yimin

Recently, Aurora University received a donation of four large books from Mr. Liu Tizhi, all of which are rare books for studying Chinese literature and linguistics. They have been on display in the university's linguistics library since the other day for viewing. Mr. Liu is a prominent scholar of the time, with a family history of scholarship. His father, Bingzhang, was a great Confucian scholar during the Xianfeng years of the late Qing Dynasty, who served as the Governor-General of Sichuan for many years. After his death, he was given the posthumous title "Wenzhuang." The "Collected Memorials of Liu Wenzhuang" in eight volumes and "Jingxuan Notes" in four volumes, written by him, were published by Mr. Tizhi and are the first two of today's rare books. The other two, which are voluminous, were written by Tizhi himself and are precious books for studying Chinese bronze inscriptions. One is "Shan Qi Ji Jin Lu" in twenty-eight volumes, containing thousands of pictures and inscriptions of bronzes, all of which were owned by Mr. Liu in the past or are now. The other is "Xiao Xiao Jing Ge" in eighteen volumes, which consists of rubbings of genuine works of more than six thousand five hundred ancient bronze inscriptions, with modern copies, annotations by predecessors, and annotations by Mr. Liu himself. Modern Chinese linguists, all those who are concerned about the history of Chinese calligraphy and explain Chinese characters and inscriptions using scientific methods, must not take Mr. Liu's works lightly.

Regarding the explanation of ancient characters, Xu Shen wrote "Shuowen Jiezi," but judging from the various font styles listed in the book, it can be seen that his knowledge of ancient bronzes and inscriptions was limited. Since Australia, historical books have mentioned the discovery of ancient bronzes from time to time. By the Song Dynasty, collection began to expand, and the decision was made to request explanations for inscriptions. However, it was not until the late Qing Dynasty that significant progress was made. Many books on the collection of bronzes have been published, with rubbings of inscriptions and annotations. However, the rubbings are often handwritten copies rather than the originals. The increase in publications is due to the increasing number of bronze discoveries in various famous ruins, and this trend continues to this day. Among these publications, Mr. Liu's lifelong works can be regarded as the culmination of them all.

Mr. Liu is proficient in literary and historical classics, and is especially versed in 小学. He provides accurate explanations for several chapters of ancient inscriptions, revealing what predecessors had not. He is not only a master of Chinese literature and history, but his works also express the spirit of modern science: that is, any view, no matter how old its origin, if it conflicts with the various materials obtained through meticulous research and excavation in recent years, he resolutely abandons it without hesitation. In short, Mr. Liu is truly an heir to Eastern culture. Now, he has donated the above-mentioned four books to Aurora University, giving most people the opportunity to observe them, which is truly commendable.
