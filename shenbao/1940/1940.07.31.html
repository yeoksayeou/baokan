英德戰爭的前瞻

英國與德義之間，至少在最近沒有媾和的希望；至戰爭開始的方式、大的還是由德義方面發動一個希特勒式的，暴風雨般的「閃電戰」；庶幾迅急地擊潰英倫三島的抵抗，而予以征服；以便在其他一切國際演變不及影響戰爭的過程以前，希特勒得先完成他統制全歐洲的計劃。

茲就本問題，分兩方面加以檢討：第一，對英國施行所謂「閃電戰」，其可能性如何？第二，德國對於整個歐戰的次一階段，究竟何所希圖？

所謂「閃電戰」也者，照希特勒自己所下的解釋，不過兩點；（一）所謂說戰就戰，令敵人措手不及；在敵人最懈怠，最不注意的時候，忽然像閃電般的，開始動作。（二）集中力量，首先打擊敵人最致命的弱點；且須對於這個弱點上，一而再再而三的打擊得最重。例如去年的波蘭戰役，甚至在開戰前一剎那間，波蘭當局還以爲德國絕對不敢挑起大規模的歐洲大戰；因之，在波蘭決心抵抗的前提之下，他們斷定希特勒絕對沒有進攻波蘭的勇氣；於是儘量在外交上玩着縱橫椑闔，用種種方法向德國嘲笑，而另一面却完全呈現着不嚴肅的懈怠狀態。希特勒針對這現象，就發動了對波蘭的十一路總攻擊，收穫了「閃電戰術」第一次凱旋。又例如最近的法國戰役，原來法國在這次大戰裏的最致命弱點，便是人的資源的絕對劣勢。希特勒抓住了法國這個弱點，集中陸空軍，急湧而前，逼着法國立刻決戰，使法國的這一劣勢立即暴露了出來。因之「閃電戰術」於是第二次收到了所期望的勝利。至於今後對英戰役，英國不會像波蘭似的措手不及的條件，但在陸軍數量，配備與訓練方面，英國還是絕對劣勢。假如德國可以壓迫英國陸軍即行決戰，則在最短期間，也許德國就可以制勝。然而在歐洲大陸與英倫三島之間，却隔着一道海洋；在德國渡海進攻的過程中，牠首先就得遭逢英國的海軍。試就海洋上的力量作一對比，却是德國的最大弱點所在；假使希特勒別無所謂秘密武器，則祗有一個空中力量，可作進攻英國的主力。空中力量對比的强弱，殆即可將這一次德國對英施用「閃電戰」的成敗，完全決定，空中力量對比之下，如果希特勒沒有絕對把握，則所謂對英「閃電戰」殆未必立即出現。

再觀測希特勒當前的希圖：自從去年三月，德國吞併了捷克，九月初，又完全開始了進攻波蘭；今年以來，他的武力更擴張到北歐一部及整個的西歐大陸；因之，他在目前實有其必須完成的繁重的整頓工作。在沒有整頓就緒以前，他是否集中所有力量，再到大陸對去冒險？這是一個很大的疑問！蓋向外侵略的最大目的，不外是增加自身的力量；則征服新領土後所必須倣的第一步工作，亦即在於盡量消化及吸收所吞併的國家，使自身力量漸漸膨大起來，然後再去從事新的冒險。德國目前是否已經消化而且吸收了所有被吞併國家的力量，任何人知其未然；因此之故，在牠進一步向外擴大以前，牠的當前最大工作，也就絕對不是進攻英倫三島！

我們總結上述的分析，最近的將來，除去英德間的空戰，或者會日見緊張外，所謂「閃電戰」也者，尙須有待吧！

說到了學徒的幸福，一定有許多人來非議我這句話，學徒有什麼幸福可言呢？天天幹着那些卑賤的工作，每月的收入還不够買日常生活必需品，還須受那老闆的脾氣，常常遭遇他的白眼，處在學徒的境地裏，眞是過着非人的生活，幸福在那裏呢？你說學徒的幸福，難道是瘋了嗎？不過我說學徒的幸福，是有些理由的。古語有云：歲寒然後知松柏之後凋，可見愈是惡劣的環境，奮鬬出來而成功的，愈配稱爲人世間之英雄好漢。現在的社會中，更不知有幾許成功的人，是由學徒升到老闆，其奮鬬之過程，實足欽佩！學徒的生活，誠然是非常痛苦，但假如學徒肯不自暴自棄，努力奮發，那麼成功楚在目前的。

怎樣拍攝電影？　（二）

— 德綱

怎樣開始攝片在拍片之前，當然先定劇本。劇本的種類很多，有喜劇，悲劇，社會性劇，歷史劇，宗敎性劇等等。導演影片的人選定了劇本後，便須將這蘊藏在理想中空洞的劇情，變成一有聲有色的實物。扭任導演是一件艱苦的工作，所以有人說一部名片的成功，並不是主演明星的功績，而應歸功於導演。導演的第一步工作，便是依據各演員的個性和才能，分派担任劇中不同的角色，而後定製佈景。用在攝影塲中的佈景，較普通舞台用的佈景考究得多，因爲開麥拉的鏡頭如同一隻精細的眼一樣，對於任何最小的一點，都不肯輕易忽略過。怖景的構造也山專家担任，他們能利用紙和木，製成各式各樣的東西，或者笨重的巨砲，威武的戰艦，巍峨的宮廷大厦等。佈景等都備妥後，便可開始攝片。在攝片時，導演先生便目不轉瞬指揮工作，校正演員的表情，姿勢，動作，對白等，尤其是動作，一般無經騐的演員，一舉一動總嫌太快，不能合於拍戲的要求。因爲太快的動作在影片中給觀衆看時，好像都是斷續的動作，不甚美妙。

在一張影片中，時常有很多節幕，都在同一地點，或同一佈景下舉行。爲節省人力和物力起見，各佈景地點相同，而次序不連的節幕，可在同時拍攝，而後把這些不連貫的節幕片子剪開，待攝畢其他各節幕時，再用膠水將各片挨次序先後相速接。在攝片時，儘管導演是何等的注意，演員等是何等的努力，但攝畢後總有許多節的片子不能使人滿意，而需剪去重拍。所以剪下重拍的片長，每較木片長好多倍。例如一張在電影放映時長爲一千八百或二千公尺的片子，此片在攝製時，起碼長一萬公尺，你想片子的損失，是何等的浩大！

Report from the Sichuan Education Commissioner: Progress in Preparing National Education (1) – Five-Year Plan Completed Three Years Ahead of Schedule – Funding, Teachers, and Methods All Stipulated

(Chengdu Airmail News) When the Sichuan Provincial National Education Committee held a meeting on July 11, Commissioner Guo Youshou reported during the meeting on the "Progress of Preparing National Education in Sichuan Province." He described the various plans in great detail. The report is recorded as follows: "Since Sichuan Province was ordered in September of the 18th year [of the Republic, i.e., 1929] to implement the new county system starting in March of this year, the implementation of national education has also been planned simultaneously. Formulation of Implementation Methods

Since receiving the order to implement the new county system, the Education Department immediately dispatched personnel in charge of national education and civil affairs personnel in charge of local autonomy to discuss implementation methods. After several exchanges of views, a draft plan for the implementation of national education was drawn up. In November of last year, this plan was taken to Chongqing to seek instructions from the Acting Chairman and the Ministry of Education. The Ministry stated that national education should be popularized nationwide in five years, but Sichuan Province, as the location of the wartime capital, has become the base for the War of Resistance and national reconstruction. It is proposed to complete it in three years to establish the foundation for a new national education system. After approval from the Acting Chairman and the Ministry of Education, and in accordance with the guidelines of the Acting Chairman, the Sichuan Provincial Implementation Measures for Establishing Township Central Schools and Bao National Schools were drafted and submitted to the 359th meeting of the Provincial Government Committee for approval. The Education Department, based on the resolutions of the central government and provincial meetings and the regulations of the National Education Implementation Guidelines, revised the previously established implementation measures and submitted them to the 380th meeting of the Provincial Government Committee for approval and implementation. The key points of the measures are: In the first year, each township will establish one central school, and at least one national school will be established for every three bao. In the second year, at least two national schools will be established for every three bao. In the third year, one national school will be established for each bao. At the same time, the Outline of the National Education Plan for Sichuan Province, the Distribution Table of Newly Added Classes in Each County and City in Sichuan Province for the Three-Year Implementation of National Education, and the Points to Note for the Planned Implementation of National Education in Each County and City in Sichuan Province were also drafted and promulgated separately. Currently, the laws and regulations related to national education have been compiled into the first volume of the Essentials of National Education Implementation Measures, and other relevant laws and regulations are still being compiled.

Approval of Funding

According to the regulations of the Outline of the National Education Plan for Sichuan Province, 20,000 primary school classes must be added each year for three years. The funding required for adding classes in the first year is 16 million yuan, 32 million yuan in the second year, and 48 million yuan in the third year. In the first year, 50% should be raised by each county locally, which should be 8 million yuan. 25% should be subsidized by the province and the central government respectively, which should be 4 million yuan. In the second year, 60% should be raised by each county locally, which should be 19.2 million yuan. 20% should be subsidized by the province and the central government respectively, which should be 6.4 million yuan. In the third year, 70% should be raised by each county locally, which should be 33 million yuan. 15% should be subsidized by the province and the central government respectively, which should be 7.2 million yuan. The funding for teacher training requires 5.4 million yuan each year, with half being raised by the central government and the province respectively, which is 2.7 million yuan. These figures have been submitted to the Ministry of Education and the provincial government for approval. The subsidy for national education in Sichuan Province for the second half of the 29th year [of the Republic, i.e., 1940] has been approved at 2.2 million yuan (of which 200,000 yuan will be used to run provincial normal schools). After the 30th year [of the Republic, i.e., 1941], it should be re-approved when preparing the annual budget. The Ministry of Education has approved the central government's subsidy for the first year: 3.5 million yuan for increasing schools and classes, and 900,000 yuan for subsidizing teacher training. — Cao Lingyou

Speaking of the happiness of apprentices, many people will surely criticize my statement. What happiness can apprentices have? They do lowly work every day, and their monthly income is not even enough to buy daily necessities. They also have to endure the boss's temper and often face his cold looks. Being an apprentice is truly living an inhuman life. Where is the happiness in that? Are you crazy to say apprentices are happy? However, I have some reasons for saying apprentices are happy. As the ancient saying goes, "Only when the year grows cold do we know that pine and cypress are the last to fade." It can be seen that the more adverse the environment, the more those who struggle and succeed deserve to be called heroes and heroines in the world. In today's society, I don't know how many successful people have risen from apprentice to boss. Their struggle is truly admirable! The life of an apprentice is indeed very painful, but if an apprentice is willing not to give up on himself, work hard, and strive forward, then success is within reach.

If you often browse biographies of famous people, then you will be even more convinced that apprentices are happy. Of all the successful people, both ancient and modern, I don't know how many came from apprentice backgrounds. During their apprenticeships, they forged strong wills and magnificent courage. When opportunity arrived, they showed their skills, raised their eyebrows, brought blessings to the masses, and became world-renowned figures. I can easily cite a few facts about famous people to prove my point:
