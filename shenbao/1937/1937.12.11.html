1937.12.11

General Offensive by Japanese Troops on the Capital

We Have Not Replied to the Japanese Demands; Fierce Resistance by Our Troops in Major Battle in the Suburbs of the Capital; Wuhu Reportedly Fallen, Dangtu in Peril

[Tokyo] Because Tang Shengzhi did not reply to Matsui's demand for peaceful surrender of the city, the Japanese army launched a general offensive around Nanjing at 1:30 PM today. Matsui had given Tang a deadline to reply by noon. (December 10th Reuters dispatch)

[Hankou] The war in the Nanjing area has rapidly developed since this morning, turning into the largest and fiercest battle since the start of the Sino-Japanese War. According to news received here, the Chinese army defending Nanjing is determined to hold on to the end and resist to the death. As of this morning, not a single Japanese soldier has entered the city. Although there was fierce fighting all night last night, it was limited to the southwestern suburbs of Nanjing, about twelve miles from the city. Neutral observers are paying close attention to all aspects of the Nanjing battle, believing that the fate of Nanjing is of great importance to China's entire future. (December 10th Kuomin News Agency dispatch)

[Tokyo] According to a telegram from Wuhu received here today, Nanjing and Dangtu near Wuhu were occupied by the Japanese army at 11:00 AM this morning. (December 10th Reuters dispatch)

[Nanjing] Seven thousand Japanese soldiers, including some cavalry, have advanced to Dasheng Pass, about eight miles upstream from Nanjing, to prevent the Chinese army defending the city from retreating towards Wuhu. (December 10th Kuominshe dispatch)

[Tokyo] The Minister of War reported to the cabinet today on the Nanjing war situation, stating that Nanjing and its surroundings are fortified with solid defensive works, but it is doubtful whether the Chinese army can offer any vigorous resistance. (December 10th Reuters dispatch)

[Tokyo] The General Headquarters announced today that the commander of the Japanese forces in the Nanjing area is Lieutenant General Asaka no Miya Yasuhiko, a former military councillor and son-in-law of the Meiji Emperor, currently fifty years old. (December 10th Reuters dispatch)

(Local News) According to inquiries from well-informed Westerners in this port, the fighting in Nanjing from yesterday morning to yesterday afternoon did not ease due to the note from Japanese Commander Matsui Iwane to General Tang Shengzhi, requesting peaceful entry into the city. On the contrary, the fighting around Guanghua Gate, Tongji Gate, and Zhongshan Gate became even more intense. From morning to noon, there was no moment of respite. The Chinese army was extremely determined to defend Nanjing to the death, inflicting heavy blows on the Japanese army. In the afternoon, when the Japanese army's demand for peaceful entry into the city was hopeless, it launched a general offensive, unfolding an even more intense and bloody battle. The Japanese army had apparently received reinforcements and supplies, mobilizing heavy artillery and tanks, and concentrating all its efforts and firepower on the southeastern Nanjing city walls, launching a massive bombardment and using large formations of aircraft to bomb the city walls, attempting to break through the city walls and enter. The Chinese army held fast along the city, using machine guns and mortars to bombard the Japanese soldiers approaching the base of the city walls, and also using heavy artillery to bombard the Japanese positions. The density of the fire from both sides was such that there was hardly a minute's pause. The locations where the fighting occurred were: ❶ Xiaolingwei, between Tangshan and Zhongshan Gate, where the struggle between the two sides was extremely fierce the night before. It is said that the Japanese army had approached Zhongshan Gate yesterday. ❷ Dajiaochang: the Japanese army divided its forces into two routes, attacking Tongji and Guanghua Gates yesterday morning. Several sections of the city wall were destroyed but were blocked and repelled by the Chinese army. ❸ Yuhuatai is still in the hands of the Chinese army. The Japanese army was blocked north of the fork in the road, and its attack on Yuhuatai was unsuccessful. However, Xishanqiao (a small station on the Jiangnan Railway), southwest of Yuhuatai, has been occupied by the Japanese army. ❹ Zijin Mountain: the Japanese army advanced towards Wulong Temple and Jiangwang Temple, attempting to attack Taiping Gate. The war is not far from Taiping Gate.

Japanese Army Attacks Two City Gates Unsuccessfully

[Nanjing] Yesterday, two thousand Japanese soldiers, carrying thirty tanks, launched a surprise attack on two gates of Nanjing. After the Japanese army occupied Dajiaochang Flying Field and the Air Defense School, they used them as bases for attacking Guanghua Gate and Tongji Gate. The Chinese army defending the gates used mortars, machine guns, and rifles to resist. General Tang Shengzhi urgently dispatched the Model Division to reinforce the defenses of Guanghua Gate. According to Chinese sources, after fierce fighting at the two gates, the Japanese army was pursued and driven away, suffering heavy casualties. The city gate of Guanghua Gate was pierced by artillery shells. Yesterday's Japanese surprise attack was stronger and more powerful, it had entered the city and captured Nanjing. The reason why the Japanese army was able to launch a surprise attack was that the Chinese army originally defending the gates attempted to withdraw from the positions at the city gates. The Chinese army was affected by the heavy bombing by Japanese aircraft, and its morale was affected, allowing the Japanese army to advance with almost no resistance. The sound of explosions was very loud last night, proving that the Chinese army was engaged in blowing up bridges and other important military locations. This morning, two Japanese balloons rose over Zijin Mountain to the south for reconnaissance. The Chinese army fired at them but missed. (December 10th, 10:00 AM Reuters dispatch)

Japanese Reportedly Occupy Wuhu

[Tokyo] According to reports received here, the Japanese army occupied Wuhu yesterday and advanced eastward from Wuhu this morning, occupying Dangtu. It is said that Tang Shengzhi, the Nanjing Garrison Commander, has ordered the construction of defensive works inside the city of Nanjing and advised all foreigners, including representatives of the diplomatic corps, to leave the capital. (December 10th Kuomin News Agency dispatch)

(Local News) In order to attack the rear of Nanjing, the Japanese army advanced on the Jiangnan Railway from Guangde and Lishui in two routes. After Ningguo was captured by the Japanese army on the 8th, they advanced north towards Wuhu. It is said that the Japanese army crossed the Qingshui River south of Wuhu yesterday and divided their forces into two routes to attack Yangcun, the southern suburb of Wuhu, and Qiaocun, the southwestern suburb of Wuhu.
