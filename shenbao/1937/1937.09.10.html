救濟難民問題

— 仁

從抗戰開始各處軍事地帶就發生了大批的難民。這些都是因戰事而失去了生計的。目前就以上海一地而論。最少也在十幾萬人左右。這十幾萬人。現在雖然已經從戰區中逃出性命來了。然而茫茫前途。又何處是他們的歸程呢。難民留在都市裏專靠收容救濟。自然不是一個辦法。就是像目前這種無計劃的遣散回鄕。又豈是一個善策。此種無計劃的遣散。結果不過是甲地少一批難民與乙地多一批難民而已。對於難民這個嚴重問題。根本就依然沒有解决。而且目前的抗戰。决不是一個局部局地的事。今日甲地可以成爲戰區。明日乙地又何嘗不能成爲戰區。然則又將由乙地遣散於丙地了。這樣轉輾遷移。結果祇見難民日益加多。後方愈益紛擾而已。

因此。根本的救濟難民。决不是專靠消極的收容與遣散所能奏效。而必須由積極的方面入手。纔可以徹底的解决。這所謂積極的辦法。可以分爲兩點來說。

第一。是使已經成爲難民的人。能够重獲生計之路。

第二。是使尙未成爲難民的人。不再變成難民。

關於第一點。最近行政院已通過了一個非常時期救濟難民辦法。主要的內容是。㊀在首都設救濟委員會總會。各地設分支會。辦理救濟事宜。㊁難民之無工作能力者。就地收容。或移鄰近地帶。㊂難民之有工作能力者。參酌人數工作環境妥爲分配。派往所屬鄕鎭協助生產或輸送事業。或於各地防衛組織及其他團體內。分配工作。㊃難民願服兵役者代爲照料其家屬。㊄難民失學兒童。給予以敎育㊅救濟總會費用係動用中央救災凖備金及指撥專款或募捐。分會費用係動用原有救濟機關與善團基金及地方救災基金竝得募捐。這一個辦法。自然是一個很積極的措置。假若果能有條不紊的按法而行。我們想成效一定是有的。不是。我們在這裏還不能不再提出一些補充的意見。㊀這項辦法中。還欠規定總分會間的關係（縣支會已規定由省分會統籌）正因爲這次的抗戰。是整個的全面的無論任何一切。都應該有一個統一的計劃。譬如說。那一些地方可以安揷無工作能力的難民。又譬如有工作能力的難民。應該到那一些地方去生產。生產些什麼。這種種都必須要全國統盤的籌劃。而由總會决定。發交分支會執行纔是。假若同現在這樣的不規定關係。而各省各自爲政的時候。恐怕將來結果會發生許多缺點的。㊁對於難民中之熟練的機械勞動者。應該有一種特殊的處置。這些人不但不應該讓他們分散或者讓他們從事其他的工作。而且應該迅速的命令集中後方。這種勞動者不唯他自身是戰時產業中的工作員。同時還可以由他們養成許多新的機械勞動者。加大戰時生產的力量。㊂我們以爲有工作能力的難民。在沒有分配到工作以前。應該由救濟會計劃指定適當的塲所。依職業分類集合。譬如依農民。紡織工人。火柴工人等而分類集合。其同職人數過少者。可將類似職業者集合一處。這樣。在需要工作人員的時候。就可以很迅速的召集到。

關於第二點如何使尙未成爲難民的人。不再變成難民。這個問題。政府尙無適宜的辦法頒布。但我們似乎也有幾點意見可以貢獻。㊀在最近之將來有成爲戰區可能的地方。應該預籌辦法。從事於人民的組織。譬如以農民來說應速使各村各鄕先專組織團結與籌劃未來發展的地方。這樣一旦到變故臨頭就可以很快集團的退向新目的地。再事生產。又譬如說工廠罷。那未可以先遷的就應該先遷。否則。應預先組織工人。計劃未來的去處。到敵人逼來前的五分鐘。用最迅速的手段。將全體人員機械原料撤退到新目的地。假若能够這樣未雨綢繆的做。决不至弄得像今日上海難民的那樣狼狽。㊁各地的工廠商店機關。在現在這個非常時期對於全體人員可以减薪或加工。但應嚴禁裁員。我們知道。多裁一員。卽事喪失一個人或一家的生計。亦卽多一個或一羣的難民。在目前這個時候。寧願每一個人少吃一碗飯。但有飯必須要大家吃。最近上海就有些商店機關。名義上薪水不折不扣。但有形無形的已裁去了許多人員。這種自欺欺人的製造難民的方策。無論如何希望能改進。退一步講卽令因萬不得已的原因。而必須裁員。那也應該裁高額收入者。這樣。雖然使一個人失業而成爲難民。但却已使數個或數十個少額收入者不成爲難民了。而且在事實上。高額收入者因爲平常收入豐富。較有積蓄所以卽令被裁。容或也還不致流爲難民哩。

— 江曉鳴

現在，我們迫切需要的工作，便是要建立起一座軍民交流的橋樑，意思就是說，我們淞滬沿線的救護工作，不能僅恃着上海方面民衆所辦的傷兵醫院就能夠盡量地收容，我們需要軍醫當局也要負起收容傷兵的責任。在事實上，民衆確已担任起救護的職責，而軍醫當局也曾已担任救護的職責，并且關于軍醫醫院和衛生兵站的分佈，早已有了極周詳配備，那末，我們要適應持久抗戰的準備，祇要做到能够盡量疏通上海方面的傷兵，更能完成我們最大的任務，便能保持上海傷兵醫院可以源源收容傷兵，不感恐慌。如今，這軍民交流的橋樑，差幸已經建起了，那末，有許多人躁急地焦慮到上海方面傷兵醫院，將不够收容，那是把上海租界過份的看作安全的後方，而忽略到軍備當局的能力和安全的配備，更不知內地正有許多民衆所辦的傷兵醫院的力量。雖然我們還希望上海方面有大量的傷兵醫院出現，但我們所應有的民軍交流的橋樑，不容我們不豐起，不容我們不顧慮未來的持久抗戰的局面，而虔誠的希望焦躁的朋友們多盡些財力和人力，多設法創辦幾座大規模的傷兵醫院。

我以爲傷兵的待遇，不可過豐，也不可以過薄，譬如說一個傷兵已經受了重傷，任憑你供給他珍饈美味，他也是無法享受，若是他們剛從難苦危險的環境中，忽而養尊處優起來，這一心理的改變，很可以引起人們貪逸茍安的惰性，見于一朝，這于抗戰前途祇有害而無益，最理想的希望，我們祇要能給他們些適口的菜飯，和比較能够滋養的食品，也就很可以了，我想有多餘消耗的錢，不如多擴充病床的單位，多收容些受傷的人，反而要實惠多。至于眞僞虐待傷兵的人，不僅應受良心的譴責，應該受四萬萬同胞共棄譴，我們祈禱中國不再看見這樣等于漢奸式的人。

Current Affairs Commentary

The Refugee Relief Problem

— Ren

Since the start of the war of resistance, large numbers of refugees have emerged from military zones everywhere. These are people who have lost their livelihoods due to the war. Currently, Shanghai alone has at least a hundred thousand people. These hundred thousand people have now escaped the war zone with their lives. However, where is their destination in the vast future? Keeping refugees in cities and relying solely on shelters and relief is naturally not a solution. Similarly, this kind of unplanned repatriation is hardly a good policy. This unplanned repatriation merely results in one less group of refugees in location A and one more group of refugees in location B. The serious problem of refugees remains fundamentally unresolved. Furthermore, the current war of resistance is by no means a local affair. If location A can become a war zone today, what's to say location B can't become a war zone tomorrow? Then, they will be repatriated from location B to location C. Such a repeated migration will only result in an increasing number of refugees and an increasingly chaotic rear.

Therefore, fundamental refugee relief cannot be effectively achieved by relying solely on passive shelters and repatriation. A thorough solution must be approached from a proactive perspective. This so-called proactive approach can be divided into two points.

First, is to enable those who have already become refugees to regain a path to livelihood.

Second, is to prevent those who have not yet become refugees from becoming refugees.

Regarding the first point, the Executive Yuan recently passed a set of measures for refugee relief during this extraordinary period. The main contents are: ㊀ Establish a general relief committee in the capital, with branches in various locations, to handle relief matters. ㊁ Refugees without working ability will be sheltered locally or moved to nearby areas. ㊂ Refugees with working ability will be properly allocated, taking into account the number of people and the working environment, and sent to their respective towns to assist in production or transportation. Alternatively, they will be assigned work within local defense organizations and other groups. ㊃ Those refugees who wish to serve in the military will have their families taken care of. ㊄ Provide education for refugee children who have dropped out of school. ㊅ The funds for the general relief committee will come from the central disaster relief reserve fund and designated special funds or donations. The funds for the branches will come from the original relief organizations and charitable groups' funds, local disaster relief funds, and donations. This approach is naturally a very proactive measure. If it can be implemented in an orderly manner according to the law, we believe there will definitely be results. If not, we cannot help but offer some supplementary opinions here. ㊀ This approach still lacks regulations regarding the relationship between the general and branch committees (the county branches are stipulated to be under the unified planning of the provincial branches). Precisely because this war of resistance is comprehensive, everything should have a unified plan. For example, which places can accommodate refugees without working ability? Also, for example, where should refugees with working ability go to produce? What should they produce? All of these must be planned comprehensively at the national level and decided by the general committee, then issued to the branch committees for execution. If the relationship is not specified and each province acts independently, we fear that many shortcomings will arise in the future. ㊁ A special arrangement should be made for skilled mechanical laborers among the refugees. These people should not only not be dispersed or allowed to engage in other work, but should also be quickly ordered to concentrate in the rear. Not only are these laborers workers in wartime industries themselves, but they can also train many new mechanical laborers, increasing the strength of wartime production. ㊂ We believe that refugees with working ability should be planned by the relief committee to designate appropriate venues, classified and gathered according to their profession before being assigned to work. For example, classified and gathered according to farmers, textile workers, match workers, etc. Those with too few people in the same profession can be grouped together with similar professions. In this way, when workers are needed, they can be quickly summoned.

Regarding the second point, how to prevent those who have not yet become refugees from becoming refugees. The government has not yet promulgated a suitable approach to this problem, but we seem to have several opinions that we can contribute. ㊀ In places that may become war zones in the near future, measures should be prepared in advance to engage in the organization of the people. For example, with farmers, villages and towns should be quickly organized to unite and plan for future development. In this way, once something happens, they can quickly retreat to a new destination as a group and engage in production again. Or, for example, factories. Those that can be relocated should be relocated first. Otherwise, workers should be organized in advance to plan for future destinations. Five minutes before the enemy approaches, the entire staff, machinery, and raw materials should be evacuated to the new destination as quickly as possible. If this can be done in a proactive manner, it will certainly not be as embarrassing as the refugees in Shanghai today. ㊁ Factories, shops, and institutions in various locations can reduce salaries or increase working hours for all employees during this extraordinary period, but layoffs should be strictly prohibited. We know that laying off one person means losing the livelihood of one person or one family, which also means one or more refugees. At this time, it is better for everyone to eat one less bowl of rice, but everyone must eat the rice. Recently, some shops and institutions in Shanghai have nominally kept salaries intact, but have laid off many employees in tangible and intangible ways. This self-deceiving strategy of creating refugees should be improved in any case. Taking a step back, even if layoffs are necessary due to unavoidable reasons, those with high incomes should be laid off. In this way, although one person becomes unemployed and becomes a refugee, it has prevented several or dozens of low-income earners from becoming refugees. Moreover, in fact, because high-income earners usually have rich incomes and more savings, they may not become refugees even if they are laid off.
