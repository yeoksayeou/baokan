⊙南京　一九三七年十月六日晚、美國務部對報界發表聲明如下、國聯諮詢委員會說明該委員會研究中國現在情勢及日本所負條約義務之報吿書、業經該委員會通過、其原文已經美國駐瑞士公使呈報美國國務部、據該公使稱、該員報吿書、已於本月六日經國聯大會採納、自此次達東方面糾紛肇始以來、美國政府卽曾敎勸中日兩方政府遏止敵對行動、並曾提議原助雙方尋覓遠東情勢上雙方所能接受之和平解决方法、七月十六日、八月二十三日、美國務卿曾先後公開闡明美國政府對於國際問題及國際關係所取之態度、此種態度、固適用於全世界、亦特别適用於目下中日兩國聞之不業的敵對行爲、美國政府認為在維持和平上所必具之處理國際關係之原則、厥爲一切國家避免以武力進行其國策、並避免干涉他國之內政、適用和平談判、及協商程序、調整國際問題互相尊重他國權利、並遵守已定義務、以及維持條約神聖之原則、十月五日、美總統在芝加哥曾再詳申此諸原則、力陳其重要、而於其討論國際情勢之際、更指明除在法律及吿週遵守之道義原則之下、不論國內異國際、均將無安定或和平之可言、國際間之混亂狀態、必將毀壤一切和平基礎此種局勢、且必致危及一切大小國家之目前安全或未來安全、是以美國人民對於恢復條約及國際道義之尊嚴一　、認爲有極端之重要與關懷、就遠東事態之發展而論、美國政府已迫於事實、不邊不邊日本在中國之行動、實與處理國際體係應具之原則相投幫、且與一九一三年二月六所訂關於處理中國事件應行遵守之原則與政策之九國公約，以及一九二八年八月二十七日所訂之非戰公約之規定相違背。本國政府關於前述事項所為之續論、大體上實與國聯大會之結論相符．（八日中央社電）

我們對於暴日的抵抗，不僅要靠武力，並且還要充分實施經濟抵制。所以這幾天來上海市商會等圑體，極力倡導對日經濟絕交，而同時全世界表同情於中國的人士，也都紛紛鼓吹排斥日貨，足見經濟抵制力量的偉大，實不下於武力，業已為全世界所公認了。

不過要全世界各國的人民，眞的能够帮着我們去抵制日貨，實際上怕也狠難。要曉得他們宣稱抵制日貸，原來祇是伸張公道，表示義憤；要眞的實行起來，他們有許多切身的利害關係，都不能不先打算。去年世界各國，不是為着阿比西尼亚而抵制意貨，對意經濟制裁嗎？效果又在那裹呢？要曉得在各國對日的國際關係根本未起變化以前，希望他們抵制日貨有效，實在狠難。因為在一般情形之下，公道的仗義，總敵不過自己的經濟利益打算。譬如日本重要的進口貨棉花，由美国和印度進口的，每年都在四萬萬元上下，試問她們都能犧牲偌大一筆出口利益，而去抵制日本的大宗生絲或棉布進口嗎？又日本的羊毛，差不多全部來自澳洲，試問澳洲能否犧牲她那兩萬萬元一年的羊毛生意而去抵制日本的棉布雑貨進口呢？我們祇看這幾天國際聯盟開會，一討論到對日經濟制裁，英帝國殖民地的代表，便有點躲躲閃閃不敢開罪的意思，也就可以想見國際帮助中國抵制日貨的前途了。

所以眞的要抵制日貨，只有我們自己切實去做；並且我們不僅抵制日貨，還要對日斷絕一切經濟關係，才能收到經濟抵制的效果。狠奇怪的，我們自七月七日蘆溝橋發動武力抗戰以來，已整整的三個月，為甚麼我們不同時實施經濟抗戰，對日斷絕一切經濟關係呢？現在發動對日經濟絕交，在我們長期的抗日戰事中，雖還不算狠遲；可是從現在起，必須要澈底實施，不要徒然當作一種具文，方才有狠大的效果。我們要曉得過去兩三個月內，我們不能做到對日經濟絕交，一方面固然由於在日本武力侵佔的區域，貨物進出口的大權，都操在他們手裹；他們儘可利用一般漢奸，來替他們推銷一切包攬一切，自然也就有一批批的奸商去通敵往來，因綠為利；同時也許有人藉口合同的約束，或者為着私交的感情；所以不能不暫時維持對日的經濟關係。可是到現在，這些事情都不應該讓他們再存在了。

我們現在最要緊的，勿論在戰區或非戰區，都要嚴厲警戒，用堅壁淸野的方法，密切注意漢奸的活動和一般人無意中資敵的舉動。凡是販賣敵貨或售貸於敵，或替敵人服役，或與敵人發生任何一切經濟關係的，都應當由政府以通敵論罪。便是我國在日本國內設立的那些銀行商店，也應當收束回國，斬斷經濟上的葛藤。日本倚賴我國商業經濟的利益最大，我們也祇有斷絕和她的商業經濟關係，纔足以給她以重大打擊。我們以為現在對日經濟絕交，最低限度需要做到下列幾點：

㊀要使海關冊中，今後沒有日貨的進口和對日本的出口數字。假使有的話，我們便應追查來源：誰是通敵，不難査到水落石出；便是洋行出面代辦，也無妨間接追查明白，以期澈底根絕來源。要像過去八月份的滬戰期中，江海關的進口日貨還有七百多萬金單位，那我們前綫的浴血戰爭，眞是白費力氣。

㊁要使內地已經進口的日貨，封存不得販賣。各地商會和同業公會應急起執行這事，不得允許奸商假借任何理由來出售他們所購存的日貨，尤應防止他們改頭換面的冒充別國貨物，甚或冒充國貨出售。我們知道滬戰未起的時候，上海奸商曾經以大批日貨報運長江下游各埠，以致出口輪船一次水脚有達十萬元以上者，運銷日貨本身的價額之巨，更可想而知。若果舊存日貨禁止發賣，便可阻絕奸商續進日貨的企圖，這也是正本淸源所必需的工作。

㊂要使所有在敵人公私機關團體工商行號中服務的人，都退出來：尤其在敵人商船兵艦中服務的，無論水手生火或領港，都應急速退出他們所直接加害於祖国的工作。我们的海員工會，更屬義不容辭的，聯絡世界各地海員，去負賣督促這個工作。現在各處戰區和非戰區中，都還有不少替敵人服役或保管一切產業器物的，甚或有拿敵人的通行證在敵人侵佔區域往來的，這種人如不退出，也應當作漢奸論罪。

㊃要使所有對日的公私債務，立卽停止或延期支付。前些日子，我們政府一面在對日抗戰激烈的時候，一面還照付日本的賠償債款。這在我們政府極力顧全信用方面説，自然不可厚非，但是這種“齊盜糧”的辦法，於抗戰的前途也太危險了。有人疑心我們若不付款，敵入就會掠奪侵佔區城的海關，那麼全部稅收都會損失。我希望這些推測之談不是車實眞相。因為我們旣然為着正義公道民族生存種種理由而抗戰，業已不恤犧牲一切，難道還想叨惠敵人佔領下面一點點稅款？我們只要認為停止支付敵人債款，是可加强我们抗戰力量的,我們便應毫不遲疑的去作。這也是政府和人民對日抗戰决心的具體表現呢。

總之，經濟絕交必須要澈底的實施，才有效果。這不必完全倚賴政府，最要還是希望各地商會和同業公會嚴厲執行實施。怕的有些資力雄厚的奸商，他們可以勾結敵人或暗託外商因緣為利，行使鬼蜮計劃，這就要政府和全國人民，隨時予以注意監察，作嚴厲的懲處了。我們希望這一次的偉大的對日經濟絕交，能和武力抗戰一樣，完成空前未有的最大效果！

（本報特寫）七月八日蘆溝橋事件發生以後，在上海的日商銀行朝鮮·台灣·正金·三菱·三井·住友·及各大辦業公司，各大紗廠的我國籍職員，都紛紛辭職，以示他們抗敵的决心·據八月四日的報章記載，“已有二百六十餘人，其中尤以幹部人才爲多·而淞滬抗戰發生之後，可以說得在所有日商銀行·公司·廠家的華籍職員，已經全部自動辭職了：這數目雖然沒有統計過，想起來决不在少數，那末，這些失業者現在如何生活着呢？這是我們值得注意的事。

關於各紗廠的失業工友，及各日商洋行的退職職員，都分別由各正式機關辦理登記手續。現在，日銀行的退職華員，也由“滬銀錢業聯誼會”在霞飛路五四二弄五號（巴黎大戲院東隔壁）該會辦理登記手續了。記者爲要知道一點較詳細的情形，昨天特地去訪問了一次。

戰时青年第八期

民衆應注意的四點

— 亞廬

我們惟有在抗戰中以求生存，這是誰都應知道的。上海之戰，敵軍不但在戰略上佔不到便宜，反遭我軍予以重大的創傷。我們由此更加堅信，惟有長期奮鬥，纔能博得最後勝利。所有以前的希冀，依賴，畏葸，恐日等不健全的心理，都應根本剷除。我們民衆雖還不需要到疆塲殺敵，但是際此非常時期，在後方的民衆，也應過着像軍隊裏有紀律的生活，就各自本位努力，爲國家𧗊一分力量。我認爲最低限度，要實行下列四端：

㊀節儉：在平常時候，大家祇知消耗，沒有新生產的智能，因此社會奢侈成爲萬方一概的風氣，言之實堪痛心。現在國家正在抗敵，國人當能有所驚覺了，况且軍需糧秣以及其他應用物質，在抗戰上都很重要，我們民衆應當節衣縮食，將其餘資援助國家，以增加殺敵的效果，這是民衆應有的救國精神。

㊁勤勞：古語云：“民生有勤，勤則不匱。”在這長期奮鬥之中，農工直接生產，固應加緊工作，使產量增加，必要的需求，源源不絕。就是其他間接生產的各種職業，也應奮發精神，勤勉服務。至於已經失業的人，速設法往內地找尋機緣，無論做工服農，都有發展的餘地，尤其希望團體機關給予他們種種便利和援助。如此則人人都能得勤勞機會，對於經濟機構，一定非常堅固。

㊂忍耐：要求民族生存，必須經過相當犧牲，現在衛國的兵士旣已舍身疆塲，那末我們民衆受些精神上的痛苦，物質上的損失，也應該忍耐。要知道物質的損失，將來還可以補償的，如果做了敵人的奴隸，那就永世不得翻身了。所以我們在戰時，都應咬緊牙關，極端忍耐，以博取民族的生存與未來的幸福。

㊃協助：語云：“衆擎易擧”，大的事非一人所能成功，必定要大家恊力同心而後有濟。前錢抗敵守士，固然是軍士的天職，但是後方接濟，那是我們民衆應𧗊的義務。因爲軍民能夠彼此合作，𦆵可以壯士氣而固軍心·至於救濟難民，開發農工等等工作，凡是有利於抗敵上的，都應羣起發揮恊助的精神。

上述四點，雖則是老生常談，但是我覺得在抗敵上却很重耍，因此不避卑陋，希望大家切實去做，給國家以無限的前程。啟事競明，永建，義祥諸位先生：請示通訊處，寄天津路一七八號上海巿童子軍戰事服務團宣傳處編輯股，以便通信。

Chiang Kai-shek Expresses Support for U.S. President's Stern Position

Our Soldiers Will Not Stop Until Justice is Established. Foreign Minister Wang Expresses Similar Sentiments and Thanks to Friendly Nations. He Hopes Powerful Nations Will Take Active Measures to Uphold Law and Morality.

[Nanjing] An Associated Press reporter yesterday visited Chairman Chiang Kai-shek, inquiring about his feelings regarding President Roosevelt's speech. Chairman Chiang issued the following statement: "The U.S. President has issued a famous statement advocating the respect for human rights and the sanctity of treaties. This not only moves the people of China, who have endured great hardship, but also excites those powerful nations who have always advocated for lasting peace built upon international morality. When the September 18th Incident occurred, the international community failed to cooperate in upholding treaty obligations, causing the world to suffer all sorts of serious consequences. Now, Japan once again disregards everything and repeats its aggression throughout China, directly believing that it is above any treaty or international law. Japan believes that all countries should obey its command regarding any issue in East Asia. For years, China has been moving towards unification, striving for peaceful construction, but Japan wants to destroy it with one cold blow. Japan does not allow any country to question or comment on its actions and plans, as if it considers itself the supreme legal authority in the world. Japan's aim is nothing more than to impoverish and weaken the Chinese people, eliminating their purchasing power completely, disregarding that China's purchasing power is an essential element in promoting international commerce. It not only wants to seize our nation's livelihood and monopolize the Chinese market but also wants to dominate the entire Pacific region. If this conquest of China is a delusion that China cannot overcome on its own, and other relevant countries cannot legally stop it, then China's great market will surely fall, and there will never be peace in the Pacific situation. Fortunately, today's news is that the U.S. State Department and the League of Nations General Assembly have both taken steps to condemn Japan as an aggressor, which deeply moves the Chinese people. We are now fighting to achieve the complete withdrawal of Japanese troops so that we can continue our peaceful construction plan. Our determination to defend ourselves is consistent from beginning to end. Even if it comes to the last soldier and the last inch of territory, we will not change our original intention. We will not stop resisting until justice is established and treaties regain their dignity. If countries that signed relevant treaties continue to allow international justice and law to be trampled upon, allowing Japan to continue its brutal aggression, it is tantamount to supporting its poisonous plot to defeat and destroy us. Now, the U.S. President has issued his great statement, clarifying human rights and peace, which makes us believe that those who uphold justice will surely get their wishes." (Central News Agency, 8th)

[Nanjing] Foreign Minister Wang Chonghui met with reporters on the 8th and issued the following statement: "China's current resistance is purely to maintain justice and humanity. In recent days, many facts have been gratifying, that all peace-loving countries in the world have risen up together and gathered under this clear banner with China. Civil society groups around the world have passed numerous resolutions expressing their deep sympathy to the Chinese people, and major politicians and leaders from all walks of life in various countries have condemned Japan's aggression against China, especially its killing of Chinese civilians, in Geneva and elsewhere. I, on behalf of the Chinese government and the Chinese people, express my sincere gratitude to these groups or individuals for all the moral and spiritual assistance they have given us during this national crisis. The Chinese government and the people have always firmly believed that truth and justice will ultimately prevail over might. Although the recent resolutions passed by the League of Nations General Assembly regarding the Sino-Japanese incident, and the first and second reports, are still somewhat lacking compared to the actions that China hoped the League of Nations could take in accordance with the Covenant, they are sufficient to clearly show that the entire civilized world is at least spiritually sympathetic and willing to support China in its resistance to aggression. This report has also established an important fact: that Japan's actions this time are a destruction of its treaty obligations, especially its obligations under the Nine-Power Treaty and the Kellogg-Briand Pact, and are absolutely indefensible. What is now hoped for is a swift and active measure to halt Japan's arbitrary destruction of international treaties and the fundamental principles of justice and humanity. Regarding the incident of Japan's aggression against China, the U.S. State Department has now reached the same conclusion as the League of Nations. The statement issued by the U.S. State Department on the 6th stated that Japan's actions are inconsistent with the principles of regulating international relations and violate the provisions of the Nine-Power Treaty and the Kellogg-Briand Pact. Moreover, President Roosevelt's speech in Chicago a few days ago has won the sincere support of all peace-loving countries. The principles stated by Roosevelt can be said to be timely and sufficient to serve as the basis for the United States and the League of Nations to jointly work to contain international aggression. Although Roosevelt's speech did not explicitly mention the Sino-Japanese conflict, there is no doubt that the peace crisis he pointed out already exists in the current Far Eastern situation. Roosevelt reiterated that no country should hope to escape the terrible consequences of international chaos through isolation or neutrality, which is an accurate and unchanging argument. Only when all peace-loving countries understand this principle and work together to reverse the trend can international morality be prevented from being completely destroyed. Now, the unanimous opinion of the world inside and outside the League of Nations has recognized that important peace treaties in the Far East have been destroyed. The current question facing the civilized world is how to restore the dignity of international treaties and save the world from returning to the great chaos of the past. From the Far Eastern Incident in 1931 to Japan's large-scale invasion of China, the facts have proven that merely stating morality and passing resolutions are not enough to restrain Japan, which is controlled by warlords and despises public opinion. If the Far East is to be restored to peace, peace-loving countries must take active measures to uphold the dignity of law and morality. President Roosevelt said, 'All nations who wish to live in peace under international moral standards can and should find a way to make their aspirations prevail over the evil forces of disorder and lawlessness.' I fully agree." (Central News Agency, 8th)

The Shanghai Municipal Consolation Committee has established a wounded soldier consolation group at Room 418, Zhonghui Building, Aidoa Road (telephone 85439) to facilitate the people's consolation of the wounded soldiers and to prevent unscrupulous people from taking advantage of the opportunity to inquire about military information. It has also consulted with various wounded soldier hospitals to hire enthusiastic ladies to serve as commissioners, stationed in various hospitals to receive condolences from all walks of life. The number of people who have come to offer condolences and apply for regular visits has been very enthusiastic. The groups that have already offered condolences include the Shanghai Islamic Association, the Drama Circle National Salvation Association, the 12th National Salvation Performing Troupe, and the 13th National Salvation Performing Troupe. The groups scheduled to visit various hospitals tomorrow, National Day, include the Shanghai Municipal Local Association, the Shanghai Municipal Vocational Circle National Salvation Association, the Foreign Firm Chinese Staff Association, the Shanghai YMCA, the Shanghai Branch of the Chinese Women's Consolation of Anti-Japanese and Self-Defense Soldiers Association, the Women's Movement Promotion Association, the Chinese Women's Society, the Shanghai Education Circle Wartime Service Corps, the Shanghai University Students' Friendship Association, and Ms. Liu Jun'e and Ms. Hu. This kind of enthusiasm for comforting the wounded soldiers is sufficient to demonstrate the value of the national war of resistance. If the wounded soldiers return to the front line and convey the feelings of the people in the rear, they will surely increase the morale of the front line.

Westerner Talks About Our Wounded Soldiers Yearning for War News

The North-China Daily News yesterday published a letter from a Westerner, Mr. Stirling, stating that after a foreigner visited one of the Chinese wounded soldier hospitals, he brought news that one of the things the wounded soldiers felt most lacking was news from the front. I believe that many Chinese young ladies in Shanghai, knowing that the wounded soldiers are eager to know the progress of the war on all fronts at any time, will be willing to come out to help. Now, if several copies of newspapers are sent to various hospitals twice a day for the wounded soldiers to read, this kind of sacrifice is not too great. I dare to say that they are still able to take on this task at this time. Translator's note: Although this is a small matter, it is enough to show Mr. Stirling's concern for the lives of our country's loyal and brave soldiers. I hope that the young ladies will immediately take action and not disappoint Mr. Stirling's good intentions in writing the letter.

Cotton Vests

People from All Regions Enthusiastically Donate

The Shanghai Municipal Consolation Committee recently received 390 cotton vests raised by the town head Huang Zhongqing from all walks of life in Yexie Town, Sixth District, Songjiang County, packed into six large packages, and sent to Shanghai by Jiang Shuisheng and Han Dashi. In addition, Chen Peide, Mrs. Mo Shangqing, and Xinyi Company each donated 100 pieces, and Zhu Gengxin donated 50 pieces. It is now heard that in order to make it easier to collect the quota of one million pieces, if commissioned to produce them in other places, each piece will be charged 6 jiao, 5 fen and 5 li in national currency. If commissioned to handle the donation of first aid kits for the soldiers, each kit will be charged 1 jiao in national currency. They can be sent to the Shanghai Municipal Chamber of Commerce on Yu Qiaqing Road in Shanghai, or to the Shanghai Municipal Local Association in the Zhonghui Building on Aidoa Road.

The faculty and staff of Zhengshi Middle School on Shanzhong Road in this city believe that since it is late autumn, the soldiers on the front line should be consoled because they are eating wind and dew and fighting bloody battles. Therefore, the dean of teaching, Yu Xiangsen, initiated the fundraising for cotton vests. With the enthusiastic donations of the faculty and staff, 400 pieces were raised in one day and will soon be entrusted to the local association to be sent to the front. It is heard that many foreign teachers in the school also gladly donated, showing that foreign friends also sympathize with our loyal and brave soldiers.

Since the Shanghai Municipal Consolation Committee launched the fundraising for cotton vests to keep the front-line soldiers warm, enthusiastic people from all walks of life have been sewing and donating them. The entire Chinese staff of the Jardine Matheson Tug & Lighter Company on the Bund in the French Concession specially donated 260 cotton vests, which were delivered to the local association yesterday for transfer. It is hoped that the Chinese staff of other foreign firms in this city will follow suit and donate.

The students of Shanghai Municipal He'an Elementary School initiated saving sugar expenses to save the country after the outbreak of the Sui War, and raised a total of 479 yuan, 7 jiao and 6 fen. When the Lugou Bridge Incident occurred, it was remitted to the front, but it was unexpectedly rejected and returned. Now, this money will be used to purchase cotton cloth and flowered clothes, which will be brought back by the students and asked their parents to sew 548 cotton vests to be delivered to the Shanghai Municipal Local Association to be transported to the front on Double Tenth to comfort the soldiers and express their respect.

Commercial Press Celebrates National Day by Comforting Soldiers

The Commercial Press will, as usual, be closed for one day on National Day. However, as this day falls on a Sunday, according to regulations, a compensatory day off should originally be given on the 11th. However, due to the current national resistance period, and the need to make extra efforts in culture, it has been decided that work will continue as usual on the 11th. All wages that should be compensated to employees for one day will be fully used to comfort the soldiers at the front. The sum of one thousand national currency has already been sent to the Shanghai Comfort Committee today. The balance will be settled when salaries are paid in the future, and any remaining amount will be sent as a supplement. The fact that the company is contributing money and the employees are contributing labor deeply aligns with the meaning of concerted national salvation. All sectors will surely be inspired upon hearing this.

Wartime Youth, Issue 8

Four Points That the Public Should Pay Attention To

— Ya Lu

We can only survive in the War of Resistance, which is something everyone should know. In the Battle of Shanghai, the enemy army not only failed to gain any advantage strategically, but also suffered significant damage from our army. This strengthens our conviction that only through long-term struggle can we achieve final victory. All previous unhealthy mentalities such as hope, reliance, timidity, and fear of Japan should be completely eradicated. Although our public does not yet need to go to the battlefield to kill enemies, in this extraordinary period, the public in the rear should also live a disciplined life like in the army, and work hard in their respective positions to contribute to the country. I believe that at a minimum, the following four points should be implemented:

㊀ Thrift: In normal times, everyone only knows how to consume and lacks the intelligence of new production. Therefore, social extravagance has become a universal trend, which is truly heartbreaking. Now that the country is resisting the enemy, the people should be awakened. Moreover, military supplies and other application materials are very important in the war of resistance. Our public should reduce clothing and food expenses and use the remaining funds to support the country in order to increase the effect of killing the enemy. This is the national salvation spirit that the public should have.

㊁ Diligence: An ancient saying goes, "The people's livelihood has diligence, and diligence will not be lacking." In this long-term struggle, farmers and workers are directly engaged in production, and they should intensify their work to increase production, so that necessary needs are continuously available. Even other indirect production occupations should also be spirited and diligently serve. As for those who have already lost their jobs, they should quickly find opportunities inland. Whether doing labor or farming, there is room for development, and it is especially hoped that groups and organizations will provide them with various conveniences and assistance. In this way, everyone can get the opportunity to be diligent, and the economic structure will definitely be very solid.

㊂ Patience: To demand national survival, considerable sacrifices must be made. Now that the soldiers defending the country have already given their lives on the battlefield, then we, the public, should also endure some mental suffering and material losses. We must know that material losses can be compensated for in the future, but if we become slaves of the enemy, then we will never be able to turn over for generations. Therefore, during wartime, we should all grit our teeth and be extremely patient in order to gain national survival and future happiness.

㊃ Assistance: As the saying goes, "Many hands make light work." A big thing cannot be accomplished by one person, and it must be done with everyone working together with one heart. It is certainly the soldiers' duty to resist the enemy at the front, but it is our public's obligation to provide support in the rear. Because the military and the people can cooperate with each other, we can strengthen morale and consolidate military hearts. As for relief to refugees, development of agriculture and industry, and other work that is beneficial to resisting the enemy, we should all rise up and give play to the spirit of assistance.

The above four points, although they are clichés, I think they are very important in resisting the enemy. Therefore, without avoiding my own insignificance, I hope that everyone will earnestly do them and give the country unlimited prospects.
