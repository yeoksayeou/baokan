在輪船上

記者此次因為急於歸國，臨時購買不到意郵及德郵的艙位，結果就坐了英國郵船，上了船以後，才發覺事情有些不妙，原來同船的旅客，幾乎全是在遠東殖民地服務過的英國人，雖說年來自己倒也過慣了英國的生活，除了愛好英國式的早點以外，多少還學到一點“凡事總得等着人家先開口”的紳士態度，但這一回的確感到有些窮於應付。普通英國人固然有一種不愛說話的脾氣，可是他們究竟是一個有禮貌的民族，最少决不至於使人當面難堪，譬如說，他們不大習慣於同人家“拉手”，然而“您好呀”“早安”“晚安”這類客套仍時常掛在口邊，在英國的社交界中，沉悶似乎是難免的事情，至於令人不安的一種現象，却不容易見到，到過殖民地的英國人顯然就不同，假如他們沒有全把“早安”“晚安”這類客套忘記的話，最少也因為不常用的緣故，生疏到懶於說出口來。

據船上一位中國工友的意見，輪船當局對於記者個人，已算特別優待，最好的證明，就是飯廳中我的坐位，同英國旅客在一起，其實，他們這種好意，却連累了我，在起初一二星期中，幾乎天天都得板起臉孔，同時還要聽一種大可以不必聽的話。

這批旅客對於遠東問題的常識，說起來還不及一位德國的出口商人。女太太們的問題更妙，例如“中國也有軍隊嗎？”於是乎我們來一個反問“假如中國沒有軍隊，怎麼會發生目前的中日戰爭？”她們聽了以後自然就不響。老爺們的觀察，中國最大的弱點還是在於內戰。告訴他們，此日中國人民覺悟了，“中國人不打中國人”就是此次全面抗戰的基礎。結果他們依舊半信半疑。

船上某高級職員每天把他所聽到的戰事消息報告我們，他是一位好人，不過帶來的消息，却總對於中國不利，有一天記者實在忍受不下，問他，就英國報紙也時常刋登有利於中國的新聞，為什麼他老是給我們這種不幸的消息，後來他就自動把這個義務差使取消掉。

一直到了檳榔嶼以後，這批旅客讀過南洋的英文報紙，態度才改變過來，他們覺得，在他們五六個月的假中，中國已經變成了另外一個世界，一位女太太對記者說，她眞的沒有想到中國的兵士是這樣的勇敢。

船長先生

船長先生雖說是一位中年的英國人，但大約因為過慣了海上生活的緣故，脾氣倒十分爽直，有一種愛說什麼話就說什麼話的氣概。他是一位反對報紙存在的論者，旅客中除了記者以外，還有一位英文馬來郵報的記者。有一天他就對這位英國記者說明，為什麼他要反對報紙，他說：“天下本無事，有了報紙，事情才愈鬧愈多，新聞記者就是惟恐天下不亂的人物……”

這位英國記者呆住了，他問，船長是不是不看報？

“看報”？船長大聲說：“不，我是從來不看報的，報紙是製造謠言的機關。”

這位道地紳士式的英國記者，也感到這個問題的不容易對付，他說：“難到報紙上的消息都不可靠？我們記者都在撒謊，無中生有？………恐怕先生是一位不大留心時事的人物……”

“不留心時事？”船長有點興奮了：“我天天在聽無綫電播音，國際間所發生的事情我全懂，實際上，報紙並沒有用處，不是嗎，報紙上所有的東西，無綫電播音上全有？”

船長這種論調，旣反對報紙上的新聞，而又贊成無綫電播音上的新聞，自然是一個矛盾，不過他說話時態度十分堅决，弄得這位英國記者反而顯出窘態，旁觀的我又因為坐得過遠，不便揷嘴。

就是這位船長，對於中日戰爭，也是另外一種看法。

他也同別的旅客一樣，輕視中國人民的抗戰力量，他有一次對記者預言，一二星期以內，中日戰事就要結束，他說，一二星期內，日本卽會估領長江以北，日本一達到這目的，全部問題立刻解决。

Wang A'gen, 35 years old, a resident of Luodian, taking advantage of the height of the battle of Luodian, along with his father and sister, Tao Wang (25 years old) sold all the cattle and miscellaneous items in their home, obtaining over 300 yuan in national currency, and escaped to Shanghai. They lodged with friends at the Jin Situ Temple on Jessfield Road in western Shanghai. To their dismay, at around 6 PM yesterday, three robbers, all armed with handguns, suddenly arrived and robbed the Wang family of the 300 yuan that was the lifeblood of their existence, taking it from under the pillow of Wang's elderly father. At that moment, A'gen and his sister, Tao Wang, in desperation, chased after the robbers and embraced two of them, shouting for help. One of the robbers, seeing his accomplice restrained, opened fire on the Wang siblings, shooting A'gen in the temple and his sister in the mouth, the bullet exiting the back of her head. Both died instantly. Their bodies were taken to the hospital by car and then transferred to the morgue for autopsy.

Swift Resolution of Robbery on Route A Lueger

French Police Detectives Arrest Six Male and Female Robbers

Chen Guangzu, a native of Jiaxing, residing at No. 35, Sanxin Lane, Route A Lueger, in the French Concession, was visited by three male and one female robbers at around 7 AM on the 26th (day before yesterday). Using the pretext of inquiring about a letter, they forced their way inside. They drew handguns, forbidding anyone from making a sound, and went upstairs into the rooms, ransacking chests and cabinets. They stole cash, clothing, and other items valued at over 500 yuan before fleeing with the stolen goods. The victim immediately reported the incident to the French police and requested an investigation. Around 5 PM that afternoon, Detective Inspectors Jiang Futian and Li Xianyang of the French Police received a tip that the robbers in the case were hiding at No. 76, Hongfu Lane, No. 66, Xin Zha Road, in the Public Concession. They immediately led detectives Yao Zhupo, Shen Jinshu, and officers Bu Zhenglong, Tao Ronglin, Dai Rongyan, Weng Huanzhang, Lu Dongliang, and others, along with Chinese and Western detectives from the Public Concession, to the address. They arrested the robbers Jin Mingquan (21 years old, from Songjiang), Xue Gengsheng (26 years old, from Changzhou), Xu A'er (32 years old, from Jiaxing), and female robbers Chen Sanmei (27 years old, from Zhenjiang) and Huang Li (54 years old, from Changzhou). In a rattan basket in Li's home, they found a Mauser pistol and stolen goods such as gold watches and silver ingots. They then proceeded to No. 520 Kangding Road, where they arrested another accomplice, Jiang Zhizhong (29 years old, from Changzhou), and brought them all back to the French Police Station. Yesterday, they were all transferred to the First Criminal Court of the Special No. 2 Court. Judge Liao presided over the hearing, and the Police Department's lawyers Yao Zhaodi and Chen Ming presented the case, requesting a postponement for further investigation. Detective Tao Ronglin then reported on the progress of the case, stating that Jin had been sentenced to one and a half years for theft, and Xue had been sentenced to eight months, also for theft. Due to the outbreak of war, they were released from Caohejing Prison and then formed a gang to commit robberies. According to their confessions, Chen Rongsheng initiated the plan. When Jin and Xue robbed the victim's home, they wore old copper washbasins as hats. Seeing that the victim's home had two new copper washbasin hats and a camel hair coat, they discarded their own old hats, each wearing one of the victim's new felt hats and putting on the camel hair coat. The two copper washbasin hats and camel hair coat, which were the subject of the case, were all the victim's stolen goods. A gold watch and silver ingots were found on the female robber Chen Sanmei, which were also stolen goods. According to her confession, she has intimate relations with Jin Mingquan, who asked her to go with him, saying he would recommend her to a factory for work, instructing her to go with him to commit the robbery. Jiang Zhizhong also admitted to participating in the robbery. Chen Rongsheng gave him the Mauser pistol, and after the robbery, he put it in the rattan basket and left it at Huang Li's home. The court verified the report, questioned the defendants, and then ordered a postponement for further hearing on December 8th at 9 AM. The defendants were remanded into custody.

Directors of Two Relief Centers Sentenced to Five Years for Embezzling Food

Li Rensheng, Director of the Xinhang Relief Center; Gao Shuqing, Director of the Hengfeng Relief Center

Li Rensheng, a Ningbo native who served as the director of the Xinhang Relief Center, under the jurisdiction of the Shanghai Charity Organizations United Relief Association, was previously reported for embezzling food intended for refugees, totaling fifty bags of white rice, and selling them to the Sanmao Rice Store at No. 359 Shanhai Pass Road for a price of 669 yuan. After being reported, Chief Detective Huang Fusen of the General Patrol Bureau arrested Li. Lawyers Zhang Shizhu and Jiang Baolian of the Police Department charged Li with embezzlement in the Special No. 1 Court. At the same time, Li Biao (also known as Li Junliang), the person in charge of the Sanmao Rice Store, was also prosecuted for receiving stolen goods. After the interrogation was concluded, Judge Meng Tingke held a court session yesterday morning and announced the judgment. The main text of the judgment stated that Li Rensheng, with the continuous intention of illegally possessing property, embezzled items held for public welfare, and was sentenced to five years of imprisonment, along with a fine of 1,000 yuan. If the fine is converted to labor service, it will be calculated at a rate of 1,000 yuan for six months of labor. Li Biao was found not guilty. Just as this case was coming to an end, the Putuo Road Police Station delivered a completely identical case yesterday morning. It involved Gao Shuqing, a Hangzhou native, 46 years old, who had previously served in the military as a deputy regimental commander. He had now retired. After the Shanghai war, Gao was appointed by the Shanghai Charity Organizations United Relief Association on August 26 as the director of the Hengfeng Relief Center at the intersection of Kangding Road and Rue Massenet in the French Concession. The center was very large, capable of accommodating more than 2,000 refugees, and the consumption of food was also enormous. Recently, due to the shortage of white rice, the General Association switched to issuing flour, which was made into steamed buns for distribution. On the 17th and 21st of this month, Gao requested the supply department to issue a large amount of flour. However, after receiving the flour, Gao embezzled a portion of it and entrusted Li Hongbin, a staff member of the Catholic Church at No. 370 Datong Road, to sell it on his behalf. On the 17th, there were 20 bags, and on the 21st, there were 50 bags, each sold for only 3.25 yuan. At the time, Gao told Li that the flour was entrusted by the factory for sale and did not say that it was obtained through embezzlement, so Li did not suspect anything. On the 24th, Gao obtained vouchers from the Relief Association and retrieved 200 bags of flour from the Yufo Temple warehouse on Penang Road, which were transported to the Hengfeng Relief Center by truck. While passing the Catholic Church, Gao suddenly unloaded 60 bags and entrusted Li to sell them on his behalf. Father Yuan of the church suspected something because the current market price was over 4.5 yuan per bag, but Gao's flour was particularly cheap. He began to investigate, and Yu Xiangqin, the director of the negotiation department of the Relief Association, also found out. Yesterday afternoon, when Gao went to the church to collect the money, it was reported to the Putuo Road Police Station. Detective Chen Xianrong arrested Gao. The remaining 45 bags of the 6,000 bags of flour had not yet been sold, and the proceeds from the 15 bags that had been sold, amounting to 50.22 yuan, were transferred to the police station along with the flour by the church. Yesterday morning, they were sent to the First Special Court. Lawyer Qian Xunjiu of the Police Department presented the case, prosecuting under Article 336 of the Criminal Code. He stated that Gao's behavior was extremely vile and completely inexcusable, requesting that he be severely punished according to the law to serve as a warning to others. When questioned, Gao completely denied the charges. After Judge Su Xiefen questioned him, Gao was sentenced to five years of imprisonment, along with a fine of 3,000 yuan and deprivation of civil rights for five years. The stolen goods and the proceeds from their sale were returned to the original owners.
