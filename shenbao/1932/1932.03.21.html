讀中國銀行二十年度報告（二）彬· 1

吾人於把握資本主義轉入第三時期。其恐慌與動搖較諸戰後爲異其性質而成爲無可挽救之痼疾一前提而後。則可知中行報告中「民國二十年度全世界·經濟狀况·去年衰敗之後·不獨毫無轉機抑且更趨惡化」之指陳自爲必然之現象。豈僅二十年度。或且永無繁榮復興之一日。　　資本主義一方面。竭力以在市塲尋覓利潤。另一方面又要求大衆之購買力。日益增高。然而事實上一般人民。盡困頓於極端貧困悲境之日亦即其購買力低减至最低限度之時。故雖資本主義之倉庫中滿儲一切工業上之產品。膨脹至不可耐。而飢寒之人民。仍無力一顧。故由於農業之凋敝農村之購買力。日益减低。都市中小有產者。日益加速其崩潰之過程都市亦日益衰敗於是而形成爲市塲萎縮。貿易激减。資本主義挾其豐盈之資本鉅量之產品捨「失業」而外。竟無處可以投擲。資本失業。生產過剩於是而减縮工資裁减工人。小企業倒閉。大企業緊縮。於是而失業之工人乃日益激增要求麵包之喊聲。日益響喨。而資本主義之命運。乃日即於黯淡

在一九三一年恐慌之黑潮中。資本主義之台柱如胡佛固亦曾竭力以求挽救其不可挽救之浩刦。其連環緩付戰債計劃之提出。即爲其掙扎撑持之一端。然而戰債緩付之結果。不僅未能挽回已逝之繁榮。反之。且不啻恰爲資本主義之一道催命符。在深刻化之恐慌狂潮中。英國瑞曲挪威相率而停止金本位。日本亦因而實行金再禁。抑且由於經濟上之恐慌。進而激成政治上之動盪。德國國粹黨之抬頭。英國混合内閣之組織。日本政友會之重新登台。皆爲十分顯明之事實。一方蔽之。窮促之環境。已迫使資本主義。陷於崩潰之途程上。而不得不發出無可奈何之呼聲。同時遂使中行報告書中不得不有「市面銷沉·生產過剩·失業激增·金融恐慌·如大火燃燒愈救愈烈」之結論。　　資本主義爲掙扎其最後之殘喘其唯一方法。即爲市塲再分割。與加重殖民地次殖民地之榨壓。當資本主義國家感覺其所處之天地過於狹隘之際。中國尙有未顯明瓜分之廣大市塲與處女地。自爲列强共同注目。中國即迅速成爲各種予盾之擁抱者。而同時由於中國民族資本主義經濟受桎梏於列强而不得發展。故亦無由形成一强有力之政治中樞。列强各以其利害開係。在中國尋覓其利用之工具。并相互明爭暗鬥。而同時中國之軍閥政客。亦各以其利害關係。而尋 覓依以生存靠山以互相爭逐。此爲中國之所以長此分崩離析。政治之所以長此混亂。工商業之所以長此凋敝。以至凡百事業之所以不振之根因。而此種因果關係。在今年則尤爲顯明。

Reading the China Bank's 20th Annual Report (Part 2) by Bin 1

After we grasp the premise that capitalism is entering its third phase, with its panic and turmoil differing from the post-war period in nature and becoming an incurable chronic disease, we can understand that the China Bank's report's assertion that "In the 20th year of the Republic, the global economic situation, after last year's decline, has not only shown no signs of recovery but has instead deteriorated further" is a natural phenomenon. It is not just the 20th year; perhaps there will never be a day of prosperity and revival.

On one hand, capitalism is striving to seek profits in the market; on the other hand, it demands that the purchasing power of the masses increase daily. However, the fact is that when the general population is trapped in extreme poverty and misery, their purchasing power decreases to the lowest limit. Therefore, although the warehouses of capitalism are filled with all kinds of industrial products, inflated to an unbearable extent, the starving and freezing people still have no ability to even take a look. Thus, due to the decline of agriculture, the purchasing power of rural areas is decreasing daily. Small property owners in cities are accelerating their collapse, and cities are also declining daily, thus forming a shrinking market and drastically reduced trade. Capitalism, with its abundant capital and massive products, has nowhere to throw them except "unemployment." Capital is unemployed, and production is surplus, leading to reduced wages and layoffs. Small businesses go bankrupt, and large enterprises shrink. Thus, the number of unemployed workers is increasing daily, and the cries for bread are growing louder. The fate of capitalism is approaching darkness.

In the black tide of the 1931 panic, pillars of capitalism like Hoover also tried their best to salvage the unsalvageable catastrophe. The proposal of his debt moratorium plan was one aspect of his struggle to hold on. However, the result of the debt moratorium not only failed to restore the lost prosperity but, on the contrary, was like a death warrant for capitalism. In the deepening panic, Britain, Sweden, and Norway successively suspended the gold standard, and Japan also implemented a gold embargo. Moreover, due to economic panic, it further triggered political unrest. The rise of the German National Socialist Party, the organization of the British coalition cabinet, and the re-emergence of the Japanese Seiyukai party are all very obvious facts. Wu Lien-teh Discusses Wartime Hygiene and Disease Prevention 39

During the fierce thirty-four-day battle in Shanghai between our country's 19th Route Army and the Japanese naval, land, and air forces, there were indeed areas for improvement in the hygiene of military police and troops, to prepare for future wartime use. We feel that there is unlimited awareness of this and that it is a matter of urgency. Wartime hygiene cannot be fully discussed in a short piece of writing. This is merely a brief description of the key points, based on the experience gained from this short, fierce battle. Looking at advanced countries, although military doctors are part of the military, their main work is parallel to that of the regular army. The duty of military medical officers is to protect the lives of the entire army, preventing disease before it occurs and providing emergency treatment. This can both increase the combat capability of the army and reduce the difficulty of transporting the sick and wounded to the rear. Because wartime hygiene is so important, the duties of military medical officers are not limited to treating the sick and wounded. The hygiene and disease prevention in the camps, and the daily life, diet, and drinking water of the soldiers, should all be fundamentally researched in advance to avoid digging a well only when thirsty. The prevention of various acute and chronic infectious diseases such as malaria, dysentery, and typhoid is also important. For the sake of brevity, only a few essential points are listed below for those concerned to consider: (1) Military Food and Clothing. According to years of research and experience, the main foods for the army should be rich in protein, fat, carbohydrates, and salts. The consideration of calories and vitamins should not be neglected. As for clothing, attention should be paid to wearing appropriate clothing. Boots and shoes should allow for free movement and prevent accidental injuries. Protection from rain, snow, and the hot sun is also essential. During the recent Shanghai battle, very few of our country's troops wore leather boots, most wore cloth shoes, and used Guangdong oil paper bamboo woven hats instead of raincoats. Fortunately, it was early spring, and the weather was dry and cold, so there was no major harm. However, many soldiers suffered foot injuries from wading through icy rivers, requiring amputation to save their lives. (2) Drinking Water Supply. In new battle strategies, many extended trenches are built to defend against enemy invasions. The trenches built in this Shanghai battle were about twenty miles long. It is difficult for troops living there for long periods to obtain pure drinking water. If necessary, wells should be drilled to a depth of one hundred to two hundred feet. However, this is often impossible due to the constant advance and retreat of the troops and unexpected circumstances. If the river water is too dirty and is not thoroughly boiled or chemically purified, drinking it will cause intestinal infections such as typhoid and dysentery, and even trigger epidemics of cholera and other diseases. In 1921, the 19th Route Army suffered many deaths from gastrointestinal infections, which is a pity. If preventive measures had been taken and scientific attention paid to drinking water to improve its quality, or if quinine had been taken internally as a preventive measure, this would not have happened. There are two simple ways to prevent gastrointestinal diseases: (A) All drinking water should be disinfected with bleaching powder or chloramine-T to kill water toxins. These powerful and inexpensive drugs can release free chlorine. One part of this gas per million parts of water can completely disinfect the water and make it safe to drink. However, it should be noted that the water quality in Jiangsu Province is too poor, so more chlorine may be needed. Generally, four watts of chloramine-T should be mixed with 100 cc of pure water as a disinfectant. Two drops of this solution should be used for every 1000 cc of dirty water, which is equivalent to four watts of powder being equal to one watt of chlorine gas, enough to disinfect dirty water. When disinfecting dirty water, it is best to have a fixed amount of water cans, calculate the amount of bleaching powder needed, add it, shake it, and transport it to the front line after two or three hours as safe drinking water. In addition to disinfecting drinking water, each soldier should be injected with a typhoid fever vaccine to prevent this disease. This prevention method has been proven to be an unparalleled method after years of battle research. Our country's army should immediately follow this example. Typhoid fever is the most easily transmitted and epidemic disease during wartime. Preventing it in advance can ensure safety. It only requires the officials in charge to understand its benefits and implement it, which is as easy as turning one's hand. Moreover, the Shanghai health authorities have widely used mixed vaccines against typhoid, paratyphoid, and cholera every summer in recent years, which can prevent three kinds of malignant infections at the same time. Although our country is accustomed to drinking boiled water or tea, it is difficult to obtain this convenience during marching, so scientific methods must be used instead. (3) Methods for Preventing Flies, Mosquitoes, and Insects. We all know that flies are vectors for dysentery, typhoid, cholera, and digestive diseases. To eliminate them, the first step is to find ways to destroy fly eggs and pupae to prevent their reproduction. Horse manure and other filth must be removed and burned. Other scattered and wet excrement should be placed in appropriate boxes and burned, or killed with lime or refined sodium and other insecticides. Anopheles mosquitoes, which belong to the genus Anopheles, are vectors of malaria and can transmit malaria to humans. In Jiangxi Province in 1926, countless soldiers were infected with malaria by this type of mosquito. Professional hygienists have good methods for killing mosquito eggs and pupae to stop their reproduction. When troops pass through areas where malaria is prevalent, it is best to give them 5 li of quinine internally every day as a preventive agent. Lice and head lice are often vectors for typhus, which should not be neglected, especially in winter. I once read stories of the suffering caused by this in Russia and Poland during the European war, which are chilling to hear. (4) Methods for Preventing External Injuries. External injuries are vital to the entire army during wartime. Therefore, armies around the world must carry small first-aid kits containing iodine and other emergency medicines in their backpacks. In addition, medicated bandages should be sewn into military uniforms to prevent unexpected needs. Even if an external injury occurs and help is not available, iodine can be applied to the wound, and then bandaged with medicated bandages. Iodine has the function of disinfecting and stopping bleeding, preventing subsequent blood infections. Once the wound is properly bandaged, a stretcher rescue team will come when the front line is relatively relaxed and transport it to the rear or field hospital for radical treatment. During the recent Shanghai battle, countless wounded soldiers were transported to the rear for treatment, and most of the hospitals were in the concessions. The first-aid methods on the front line were not fully implemented. The Red Cross, Red Swastika, and various charitable organizations were dedicated to rescuing wounded soldiers on the front line and transferring them to temporary hospitals for wounded soldiers in the rear. There were various degrees of injuries, but fortunately, there were many talented people and good equipment in the rear. Although there were occasional cases of gas gangrene, tetanus, and blood infections due to prolonged exposure in the fields, most of them were treated effectively. For example, there were cases of purulent infections after head injuries with convulsions, which were eventually completely cured by surgical procedures. It is rare for wounded soldiers to have such resistance to treatment. Fortunately, the Central Epidemic Prevention Department of Beiping had prepared 80 liters (1000 cc per liter) of tetanus antitoxin serum for treatment and prevention of the disease. St. John's University also prepared an immeasurable amount of Dakin's solution (a strong antiseptic solution containing chlorine and salts) to supply wound treatment. All wounded soldiers returning to the hospital are now supplied with a sufficient amount by this university.

Now the Shanghai battle has temporarily stopped, and the loyal and brave soldiers of the 19th Route Army have won praise from the world. Our government should adopt good methods and prepare in advance so that soldiers have such knowledge, and then going to the front line can reduce unexpected sacrifices.
