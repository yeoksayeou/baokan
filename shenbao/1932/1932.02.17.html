軍事戰與外交戰翰 1

兩旬以來。我將士與民衆莫不集中全副精力於自衛的抵抗。方有生新氣象。而外交上則仍立於孤懸地位。且有日趨於暗淡失敗之虞。此則不能不責備政府當局之太不注意也。

昔法國拿破侖失敗後。搭列蘭任戰敗國之代表出席維也納會議。周旋於俄奥普英之際。從未受戰勝國之壓迫而屈服。近大戰後德國受創最重。而史脫萊斯孟與各國周旋。訂羅加諾安全與仲裁條約。撤列强在萊因这駐防加盟日内瓦。一躍而爲常任委員。土耳其爲近東之病夫。大單後對土和約。束縛最苛。卒賴凱末爾在軍事上極力抵抗。伊斯美在外交上堅苦折衝。而獲得土耳其獨立之洛桑條約。凡此皆足爲弱國外交之模範。亦軍事與外交必須兼顧並重之鐵證。

反觀吾國歷年來之外交當局。好大喜功。不務實際。對内則籍種種誇大之宣傳。以欺騙國民。對外則調遣私人。扶植已勢。舉凡一切毫無黨派臭味。

經騐鍜練之人才皆拼棄不用。而與已派有關係者。則無論爲科學文學家軍事家。均可銜國家之使命。充折衝之重任。甚至年輕浮囂之留學生。亦可主掌使館。列席國際。醜態百出騰笑萬方。以如斯之人才。而欲責其在外交上拘救我民族國家之危亡。其可得乎。

且也。一年以來。駐外使領。大都出缺未補。每由普通館員。兼充代辦日本對我之武力侵略。自发生八之役以迄一二八之變瞬息己五閱月。僅令施顏兩代表。相繼在日内瓦支撑掙　。當然有孤掌難鳴之苦。據查自去年七月以來。駐外使館館員都因生活費無着。有斷炊之虞。甚至郵電諸費。亦無法籌措。外交情形。急如薪火。而使館與祖國。消息隔絶。致令館員與僑胞。終日旁皇莫知所措。在此種外交情勢下。而欲求外交之勝利。其可得乎

况乎今日在國際情勢萬分緊張之際。日本正努力於違反事實之宣傳政

府當局於此。一須從速遣派富有經騐之外交官。切實整頓駐外使館之行政

二須速籌經費。充作駐外使館溝通消息之資。並爲外交上一切正當之用務使駐外使館之神經。由麻木而漸進於靈敏。應付之手段。亦自緩怠而趨於活潑。外交之給養有着。折衝乃有所措手。此軍事戰與外交戰。必須互想呼應之說也。

Military Warfare and Diplomatic Warfare 1

For the past twenty days, our soldiers and civilians have been focusing all their energy on self-defense and resistance, showing a new spirit. However, diplomatically, we remain in an isolated position and face the risk of becoming increasingly bleak and failing. This cannot but be blamed on the government authorities for not paying enough attention.

In the past, after Napoleon's defeat, Talleyrand, as the representative of the defeated country, attended the Vienna Conference, negotiating with Russia, Austria, Prussia, and England, never succumbing to the pressure of the victorious countries. More recently, Germany suffered the most damage after the Great War, but Stresemann negotiated with various countries, signed the Locarno Treaties of Security and Arbitration, withdrew the stationed troops of the great powers in the Rhineland, and joined Geneva in one leap as a permanent member. Turkey was the sick man of the Near East, and the Treaty of Sèvres after the Great War imposed the most severe restrictions. In the end, relying on Kemal's vigorous resistance militarily and İsmet's arduous negotiations diplomatically, the Treaty of Lausanne was obtained, securing Turkish independence. All of these serve as models for the diplomacy of weak countries and ironclad proof that military and diplomatic affairs must be given equal importance.

Looking back at our country's diplomatic authorities over the years, they have been fond of grandstanding and neglecting practical matters. Internally, they use various exaggerated propaganda to deceive the people, and externally, they transfer personal followers to consolidate their own power. Everything is devoid of partisan character.

Talented people with experience and training are all discarded, while those related to their own faction, whether scientists, literary figures, or military personnel, can all hold the mission of the state and assume the heavy responsibility of negotiation. Even young and frivolous students studying abroad can preside over embassies and attend international conferences, making countless ugly scenes and laughingstocks everywhere. With such talent, how can one expect them to rescue our nation from danger and destruction diplomatically?

Moreover, for the past year, most of the ambassadors and consuls stationed abroad have been vacant and unreplaced, each being concurrently filled by ordinary staff members as acting representatives. Japan's military aggression against us has been ongoing for five months since the "August 13th Incident" and the "January 28th Incident." Only two representatives, Shi and Yan, have been sent to Geneva to hold the line, naturally suffering from the difficulty of one hand clapping. According to investigations, since July of last year, the staff members of foreign embassies have been facing the risk of starvation due to a lack of living expenses, and even postal and telegraphic expenses cannot be raised. The diplomatic situation is as urgent as a fire, but the embassies are cut off from communication with the motherland, causing the staff members and overseas compatriots to be at a loss all day long. Under this kind of diplomatic situation, how can one expect a diplomatic victory?

Moreover, today, at a time when the international situation is extremely tense, Japan is striving to conduct propaganda that violates the facts.

In this regard, the government authorities must first quickly dispatch experienced diplomats to thoroughly rectify the administration of foreign embassies.

Second, they must quickly raise funds to serve as resources for foreign embassies to communicate information and for all legitimate diplomatic purposes, so that the nerves of foreign embassies gradually progress from numbness to sensitivity. The means of response will also shift from slowness to agility. With adequate diplomatic support, negotiations can be handled effectively. This is the idea that military warfare and diplomatic warfare must echo each other.

Jiang, Wang, and Feng Meet in Xuzhou 117

Wang Expresses Great Optimism about Diplomacy

Xuzhou Correspondence: Due to the urgency of diplomacy and the unresolved Shanghai Incident, Executive Yuan Premier Wang Zhaoming telegraphed Military Affairs Committee Chairman Chiang Kai-shek to come to Xuzhou to discuss major diplomatic and military plans. After receiving the telegram in Kaifeng, Chiang, accompanied by his wife Song Meiling and a small number of staff and attendants, took a special train of seven cars east on the 11th at noon, passing Guide at 6:00 PM and arriving at the North Station of Xuzhou at 10:00 PM. Chiang rested on the train because it was late. Wang's special train of fourteen cars arrived from Pukou North at 9:40 PM on the 11th, with Feng Yuxiang, Li Jishen, Zhu Peide, Bao Wenwei (Zhang Xueliang's representative), Yang Aiyuan (Yan Xishan and Zhao Pilian's representative), Chen Bijun, and Zeng Zhongming. They arrived at East Station on the morning of the 12th. Military music was played loudly at the station, and those who came to greet him raised their hands in salute. Wang returned the salute and briefly told the reporters that after arriving in Pukou a few days ago, he felt that the diplomatic situation was better, so he crossed the river to Nanjing to discuss everything with Military and Political Minister He Yingqin, Foreign Minister Luo Wengan, and Finance Minister Song Ziwen. He expressed great optimism about diplomacy and said that although the Shanghai war had been fierce for days and the situation in Nanjing was serious, China had fully accepted the conditions proposed by various countries, and the wrong was on the Japanese side, while China was in the right. Wang then learned that Chiang was waiting for news at the North Station, so he ordered the train to start again. At 9:30 AM, he arrived at the North Station. Chiang saw Wang's train arriving and boarded Wang's train to meet with Wang, Feng, Li, Zhu, and others for about two hours. The content could not be ascertained. After the meeting, Chiang returned to his car for lunch, while Wang planned to send a telegram to the foreign affairs, military affairs, and finance departments in Nanjing, asking them to work hard. Chiang, Wang, and Li also sent a telegram in the name of the Military Affairs Committee to various dignitaries, inviting them to come to Xuzhou to attend the following meeting. Both telegrams were sent on the same day. The meeting place has been designated as the Garden Hotel. It is said that the meeting will be held after the dignitaries arrive in Xuzhou. (12th)
