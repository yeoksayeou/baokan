在野與在朝彬·

每一個人各有其政治主張。每一個政黨也各有其政治主張。我們不能使其苟同更無法使其强同。

然而每一個人毎一政黨的政治主張却絕對應該「一贯」因爲主張即是一種自信力。這種自信力即爲一個人或一個政黨的精與生命所寄託。一

個人或是一個政黨。旣經審愼的研究確定了他的政治主張之後。即應終始堅持。不爲富貴所淫不爲貧賤所移。不爲威武所屈這裏只有「能行」與「不能行」的問題絕不能因着「在朝」與「在野」的環境不同而變易其主張只有虚僞的否定自己的人或黨他的主張與態度才是否定的。游移的。隨着環境而轉移變換自相矛盾的。因爲他没有堅定的信仰。也没有固定的主張。有的只是一黨甚或一己本身的利益惟其一切都是從本身的利益而打算。所以前日在野的時候可以高唱着如彼的主張而今日在朝。却又可以做出如此的行爲。以「在野」「在朝」的環境之不同而變易其主張的即爲「二重人人

格」的表現在人即爲政客在黨即爲投機的政治集團

衣·食·住·行·爲人生的四大要素，尤其是住在上海謀生活的人們，住的問題，更爲重要，更爲痛苦，無論士農工商，每日辛苦勞力所得，至少大部分要報效房主，此種情形，不但此次次事變時如此·即戰前之滬上狀態，各業之種種利益·大半均爲房主所榨取無遺，此原爲滬上社會奇形發展的狀態也，溯自一二八滬變以還，閘北·吳淞·等處居民商店的重大犠牲，且莫論，即以英法兩租界而言，各業罷市，已逾兩月，連日各路商店，雖已陸續復業，然市面蕭條，已非昔比，故商界聯合會等多數建議，减免房租，維持現狀，最近市商會通告房主，自動减租，以蘇商困，此原爲目前滬上所最要而最急迫的問題，亦爲目前先應當討論而研究之問題，

有聲電影

— 乃徵

有聲電影自美國放映以後，過未多時，即風靡全球。我國上海南京蘇州等埠之各大戲院，亦已放映二年餘，又造成有聲電影迷不少。滬上明星公司首先同百代公司攝收有聲影片，但音語零收在蠟盤上。天一公司乃費極大之價，至美國購來全付收攝機械，並隨來攝影收音洗片師等。今已出二片，乃係片上發音者。後明星公司不願示弱，乃费鉅金往購可攝自然彩色之收音機器。至今日有聲電影有如此之發逹；一有佳片，我人無不踴躍往觀，但對於如何攝成，如何發音，則知者寥寥無幾，今余以淺普之理，及其自收攝至放映之步驟，簡單説明之。

有聲電影發音之方法，略言之，可分二種。一爲使用留聲機唱片相同之唱片發音，使其廻轉時與放映機相伴，此謂之維太風Ｖｉｔａｐｈｏｎｅｏ一爲在片邊上之光條所發音，謂之慕維通Ｗｏｖｉｅｔｉｏｎｅ　　維太風於一八九四年由美國怪傑愛迪生氏發明，其音收於零外之圓片上，在戲院放映時，用電留聲機發音，同時擴大其音，以電線通於銀幕後之喇叭，於是再由喇叭內放出音樂言語等聲。此法之麻煩處即收音與攝影之時間，須配之極準，即在戲院放映時，亦須預先試正。轉電留聲機之小馬逹，與轉片之馬逹，必須快慢適當，務使聲音與動作相吻合。倘若雙方不合，或片中斷去數尺；則我人見劇中人之唇已動，而音語未來；鋼琴蓋未開，而音調已鏘鏘出。以致弄得笑話百出；此爲維太風之弱點·其他如聲音之不淸楚，並有蠟片旋轉時之雜聲，或多映數次後音片上之細紋刻平而使音糊塗，皆令觀者不滿。滬上所映之歌女紅牡丹即此維太風發音。然萬事必有進步改良，今風行一時之慕維通即可免此弊矣。

慕維通爲美國人特福勒斯ＤｅＦｏｒｅａｔ博士所發明之方法製成，當是時，歐洲各國對於有聲電影之機械亦有種種之發明·但最須向之敬謝者，即發明將音波攝爲照片之波紋之路麥羅氏。今能使有聲電影更進步者，則爲光電池與三極眞空管。無論何種有聲電影皆須用此，以增其效用也。光電池可使音波變爲縐紋而攝於片邊上；三極眞空管即如吾人所見之無線電收音機中之眞空管，當收音與發音時皆需要之。以上二器，爲慕維通中最重要之機件。

收攝有聲片時，聲音與光線有同様之重要。演員之動作與言語亦須同時注意。一面攝影，一面送話器收音。送話器即如無線電播音用的相同，但較精細耳。音語一入此器，再由增幅器擴大之。（此器蠟片收音時亦用之；即放映時亦不可缺此；又無線電放送接收之機中亦用增幅器）。

音波經增幅器後所成之電流，即送入一能使光源生變化之電泡中，因此電泡中光度之變化强弱粗細，乃感影於影片之旁邊，成短而橫的條紋。其條紋甚細時，則音高而尖；甚粗時，則音低而鈍。放映時，此攝成之片須通過光電池之前面，由光電池所來之電流，通過增幅器而增大之；然後再通至總增幅器中，而送於銀幕後之喇叭中，傳入觀客之耳，觀客目中所見之表情，亦與音語符合，此即慕維通勝過維太風之要點也。

On Being Out of Office vs. Being in Office

Every individual has their own political views. Every political party also has its own political views. We cannot force them to agree, let alone compel them.

However, the political views of every individual and every political party should absolutely be "consistent" because an opinion is a form of self-confidence. This self-confidence is where the essence and life of an individual or a political party reside.

Once an individual or a political party has carefully researched and determined their political views, they should adhere to them from beginning to end. Not corrupted by wealth, not swayed by poverty, not subdued by force. The only question here is "can it be done" or "cannot it be done." Their views should never be changed based on whether they are "in office" or "out of office." Only those who falsely deny themselves, their views, and their attitudes are negative, wavering, changing with the environment, and self-contradictory, because they have no firm beliefs and no fixed opinions. All they have is the interests of their party or even themselves. Because everything is calculated from their own interests, they can sing lofty tunes when they are out of office, but when they are in office, they can do the opposite. Changing one's views based on the different circumstances of being "out of office" versus "in office" is a manifestation of a "dual personality." In an individual, it is a politician; in a party, it is a speculative political group.

We dare not say that the political systems of Western European countries are reasonable, but we cannot deny that the politicians and political parties of Western European countries can still maintain their open and upright, serious attitude. What are their views when they are out of office? Once in office, they boldly act according to their views. They absolutely trust themselves and are absolutely responsible. If it doesn't work, they can be impeached by others or other parties and step down. They are never willing to cater to the views of others or other parties to prolong their lives. This is the serious attitude that a politician should have, and it is also the serious attitude that a political party should have.

The Communist Party of the Soviet Union is in power indefinitely, like the people being sentenced to life imprisonment. The Italian Fascist Party is in power for a limited time, like the people being sentenced to a fixed term of imprisonment. Both are one-party dictatorships. They adhere to their own views from beginning to end, from being out of office to being in office, from secret struggles to public actions. From beginning to end, they strive to make their views a reality in a planned and step-by-step manner, to seek happiness for the masses and to demonstrate their achievements to the people, with absolute self-confidence and absolute responsibility. Therefore, the people change from resistance to obedience, just like under the pseudo-democratic political systems of England, America, France, and Japan. Ordinary politicians and political parties can still maintain this attitude. This attitude is precisely what our country's current politicians and ruling party lack.

Here, we are not saying that views cannot be changed at all. If the change of views is based on the interests of the majority of the people and meets the demands of the majority of the people, then this change is necessary. What we are saying is that when out of office, they advocate views that are suitable for the people's demands, but when in office, they do things that are contrary to the people's interests. We believe that this is not keeping one's word and not acting according to one's words. It is a deception of the people. No matter what your views are, no matter what your position is, we only ask for "truth" and "trust" because we can easily distinguish between right and wrong of true views and true actions, while negative views and negative actions are very likely to confuse our ears and eyes, making us unable to grasp them for a while. However, this is only "temporary." In fact, the fox's tail always shows. Similarly, the people's ears and eyes cannot be covered forever by one hand.

Therefore, we hope that our country's current politicians and ruling party, when in office, will look back at the "words" they said when they were out of office, and when they are out of office, they should also think about the "actions" they will take when they are in office.

Talking Film

— Nai Zheng

Since talking films were shown in the United States, it has not taken long to sweep the world. Major theaters in Shanghai, Nanjing, Suzhou, and other cities in China have also been showing them for more than two years, creating many talking film fans. Shanghai's Mingxing Film Company was the first to record talking films with Baidai (Pathé) Company, but the audio was recorded on wax records. Tianyi Film Company spent a huge price to purchase a full set of recording equipment from the United States, along with photography, sound recording, and film developing technicians. Two films have already been released, using sound-on-film. Later, Mingxing Film Company did not want to show weakness, so it spent a large sum to purchase sound recording equipment that can capture natural color. Today, talking films have become so advanced; whenever there is a good film, we all rush to see it. However, very few people know how it is made and how it works. Now, I will briefly explain the simple principles and the steps from recording to screening.

Briefly, there are two ways to produce sound in talking films. One is to use phonograph records similar to those used in gramophones, so that when they rotate, they are paired with the projector. This is called Vitaphone. The other is to use the sound waves from the light strip on the edge of the film, which is called Movietone. Vitaphone was invented by the American eccentric Thomas Edison in 1894. The sound is recorded on a round piece outside the film. When it is screened in a theater, a gramophone is used to produce the sound, and the sound is amplified at the same time. An electric wire connects it to the speaker behind the screen, and then the music and speech are emitted from the speaker. The trouble with this method is that the timing of the sound recording and the photography must be extremely accurate. Even when screening in the theater, it must be tested and corrected in advance. The small motor that turns the gramophone and the motor that turns the film must have appropriate speeds, so that the sound and movement are in sync. If the two sides do not match, or if a few feet are cut off in the film, then we will see the lips of the actors moving, but the sound has not yet come; the piano cover is not open, but the melody is already sounding. This leads to many jokes. This is the weakness of Vitaphone. Other problems, such as unclear sound, the noise of the wax record rotating, or the fine lines on the sound film becoming flat after being screened many times, causing the sound to be blurred, all make viewers dissatisfied. The Shanghai-screened "Singing Girl Red Peony" uses this Vitaphone sound. However, everything must improve. The Movietone, which is now popular, can avoid this problem.

Movietone was created by American Dr. De Forest. At that time, European countries also had various inventions for talking film equipment. But the one who should be most thanked is Mr. Rumer, who invented the method of photographing sound waves as wave patterns. Now, the ones that can make talking films even more advanced are the photoelectric cell and the triode vacuum tube. All talking films must use these to increase their effectiveness. The photoelectric cell can convert sound waves into corrugated lines and photograph them on the edge of the film; the triode vacuum tube is like the vacuum tube we see in radio receivers, which is needed when recording and playing sound. The above two devices are the most important components of Movietone.

When recording talking films, sound and light are equally important. The actors' actions and speech must also be noted at the same time. While filming, the microphone picks up the sound. The microphone is the same as the one used for radio broadcasting, but it is more refined. Once the sound enters this device, it is amplified by the amplifier. (This device is also used for wax record recording; it is also indispensable for screening; and amplifiers are also used in radio broadcasting and receiving machines).

The current formed by the sound wave after passing through the amplifier is then sent to a bulb that can change the light source. Therefore, the changes in the intensity, strength, thickness, and fineness of the light in the bulb are sensed on the side of the film, forming short, horizontal stripes. When the stripes are very thin, the sound is high and sharp; when they are very thick, the sound is low and dull. When screening, this photographed film must pass in front of the photoelectric cell. The current from the photoelectric cell passes through the amplifier and is amplified; then it is sent to the main amplifier and sent to the speakers behind the screen, transmitting it to the ears of the audience. The expressions seen by the audience's eyes also match the sound and speech. This is the key point of Movietone's superiority over Vitaphone.

The Cultivation of German Aviation Talents

— Qiu Xing

After the defeat in the war, the German people worked very hard for all kinds of revival undertakings. What we have seen is obviously a great progress: the most admirable thing is that they have plans, organizations, and research for all kinds of revival undertakings, which are thorough.

Today, the development of German aviation is truly astonishing. However, since the restriction of military preparations, military flights have been prohibited. Therefore, those experienced flying warriors who had fought hundreds of battles in the European War have also moved from military aviators to civilian aviation. Since civilian aviation received their help and contribution, it has progressed more powerfully than before. However, how can these advanced and excellent aviators exist forever? Sometimes, unexpected dangers are unavoidable, so new aviators have emerged. The people who guide the aviation industry in Germany use the most real and thorough methods to guide these new talents. Because of the development of the German aviation industry, there is a great need for these new talents, so they do not hesitate to invest huge sums of money as a cultivation fund.

Any young person who aspires to become an aviator must first pass the preparatory examination of a simple aviation school. After passing, they can enter the National Aviation School. The National Aviation School has subjects such as flight techniques, engines, aviation, radio, and international law. They must also learn two or more foreign languages. The school also has teachers to teach flight mechanics, and wooden and steel aircraft are studied separately. The examination is very difficult. The tuition fee is about 15,000 national currency.

However, the most noteworthy thing is that students only bear 3,500 yuan of this fee, and the rest is borne by the Han Cha Aviation Company and the government. Students who graduate from this school can work as second-class aviators at Han Cha Aviation Company, but they must fly at least 40,000 kilometers. Later, outstanding aviators will be successively promoted to formal aviators. The initial salary is about 1,200 yuan per year, plus 1.5 pounds for each kilometer flown.

Just by looking at the method of cultivating German aviation talents, we can see the goodness of their foundation. Therefore, from this good foundation of revival undertakings, we can infer the essence and progress of all German postwar revival efforts. (Reprinted)
