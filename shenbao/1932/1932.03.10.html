評二中全會（一）彬

國民黨二中全會已於日前閉幕。際此國難日函。國脈民命不如縷之艱局。國民黨未有以誠意毅然擔承禦禍重責之表示。故此二中全會之舉行。在我一般人民視之。都以爲無足重輕。伹國民黨旣有此會議。我人自不能不予以嚴正之批判。

在批判之先。吾人當先認取一大前提。即中國爲我全體人民所共有。而國民黨則爲國民中極少數黨人所组織。黨人近嘗昭示國人以『黨亡國亦亡。』救國先救黨。』但我人民所認識者則正反是。蓋黨可由黨人自亡。而國則絶不容亡於黨人之手。國民尤不容坐視國危急起挽救。自前黨人固不妨先救黨而我國民則决不能不先救國。脫先黨後國。是爲輕重倒置。極其所至。勢必以國殉黨。以黨亡國。吾人不得不認淸此一點。

今日二中全會宣言之要點凡七（一）曰『禦侮』此與去年十二月舉行一中全會『收回失地』之决議相仿彿。收回失地。已爲不兌現之具文。而所謂

『禦侮。』則自去年九一八以來。政府一再鄭重宣示全國。謂已有充分之準

備。最大之决心。政府不負人民。不負國家。迄今半載所謂『準備』與『决心』之欺騙性。於此次上海禍變中亦已揭露無遺。今後之所謂『禦侮。』得毌必待產生軍事委員會主席而後推進乎。（二）曰『綏靖剿匪。』言之固亦成理。但匪究何由而來。是否可以『剿』而滅。過去數年。政府旣日以内亂造成災荒積極『造匪。』而又日言『剿匪。』此不僅在事實上爲矛盾。且其結果。必至『匪』以剿而愈多。民以剿匪而愈困。（三）曰『外交軍事相輔而行。』所計固亦極得。但今旣無軍事。亦無外交。如曰有之。則不過淞滬苦戰之十九路軍。出席國聯之顏惠慶代表耳。今駐外使節旣多虚懸對外宣傳。更毫無準備。外交終始處於被動地位。着着失敗。更有何外交之可言。（四）曰『擬先澈澄肅淸貪汚。』廉潔政府。固早爲我人民所渴求但致此之道。裁員减政。特其最末之一端。而其

最重要者。則尤於人民有監督財政之權。收支必得人民之同意。今日從政者之不廉潔。公私不明。取捨任意。官肥民瘦。私囊裕而公帑竭。已爲不能否認之事實。空談澄淸。其有奚益。（五）曰『積極設置民意機關。培養民主政治之基礎。』自國民黨主政。早已無民意可言。今猶曰民意。當不能不認爲黨政府漸有覺悟之表示。獨是所謂民意機關。究何由而設置。乎國民會議。固亦曰民意團體也。國民會議果能代表眞正民意乎。國民黨如有接納民意實現民主政治之誠意。則民意固不必求之於指派之手續。而民主政治亦不必爲矯揉之培養。際此國難日亟。明知一黨之力不能打破難局。則亟應自動取銷一黨之『狄克推多。』公開政治。予全國人民以參政之機會。予人民以充分之自由。共赴國難。政治庶有新生氣象。脫徒藉所謂國民會議等。以敷衍人民。適見其心勞日拙耳。

Assessment of the Second Plenary Session of the Central Committee (1) Bin

The Second Plenary Session of the Kuomintang Central Committee concluded a few days ago. At this time of national crisis, with the nation’s fate and the people's lives hanging by a thread, the Kuomintang has not shown any sincere or resolute commitment to shouldering the heavy responsibility of defending against disaster. Therefore, the convening of this Second Plenary Session is regarded by the general public as insignificant. However, since the Kuomintang has held this meeting, we cannot but offer a serious critique.

Before critiquing, we must first acknowledge a major premise: that China belongs to all of its people, while the Kuomintang is an organization of a very small number of party members. Party members recently proclaimed to the nation that "If the Party dies, the nation dies too" and that "Saving the nation means saving the Party first." But what our people recognize is precisely the opposite. The Party may be destroyed by its own members, but the nation must never be allowed to perish at the hands of party members. The people, even more so, cannot stand idly by as the nation faces a crisis without taking action to save it. In the past, party members were certainly free to save the Party first, but our people absolutely cannot but save the nation first. Abandoning the nation for the Party is a reversal of priorities. If things go to the extreme, it will inevitably lead to sacrificing the nation for the Party, and the Party destroying the nation. We must recognize this clearly.

Today, the main points of the Second Plenary Session's declaration are sevenfold: (1) "Resisting Insults." This is similar to the resolution of the First Plenary Session held last December to "Recover Lost Territories." Recovering lost territories has already become a meaningless formality. As for the so-called "Resisting Insults," since September 18th of last year, the government has repeatedly and solemnly declared to the whole country that it has made full preparations and has the greatest determination. The government will not let down the people or the nation. Half a year has passed, and the deceptive nature of the so-called "preparations" and "determination" has been completely exposed in this Shanghai disaster. Will the so-called "Resisting Insults" have to wait for the establishment of a chairman of the Military Affairs Commission before it can be promoted? (2) "Pacifying and Suppressing Bandits." This is ostensibly reasonable. But how do bandits arise? Can they be eradicated by "suppression?" In the past few years, the government has actively "created bandits" by causing disaster and famine through internal strife, while simultaneously talking about "suppressing bandits." This is not only factually contradictory, but its result will inevitably be that "bandits" increase with suppression, and the people become more impoverished by bandit suppression. (3) "Diplomacy and Military Affairs Go Hand in Hand." This plan is indeed excellent. But today, there is neither military strength nor diplomacy. If there were any, it would only be the 19th Route Army in the bitter battle of Shanghai and the representative Yan Huiqing at the League of Nations. Today, the foreign envoys are mostly vacant positions and there is no preparation for external propaganda. Diplomacy is always in a passive position, failing at every turn. What diplomacy can be spoken of? (4) "Planning to Thoroughly Investigate and Eliminate Corruption." A clean government has long been desired by our people, but reducing staff and streamlining administration is only the last step. The most important thing is that the people have the right to supervise finances. Revenue and expenditure must have the consent of the people. Today, the lack of integrity among those in power, the blurring of public and private affairs, arbitrary decisions, officials getting rich while the people become poor, and private pockets overflowing while public funds are exhausted are undeniable facts. Empty talk of purification is of no benefit. (5) "Actively Establishing Popular Opinion Organs and Cultivating the Foundation of Democratic Politics." Since the Kuomintang came to power, there has been no popular opinion to speak of. Now, still mentioning popular opinion must be regarded as an indication that the Party government is gradually awakening. But how are these so-called popular opinion organs to be established? The National Assembly is also called a popular opinion body. Can the National Assembly truly represent the will of the people? If the Kuomintang has the sincerity to accept popular opinion and realize democratic politics, then popular opinion does not need to be sought through appointed procedures, and democratic politics does not need to be artificially cultivated. At this time of urgent national crisis, knowing that the power of one party cannot break the deadlock, it should immediately and voluntarily abolish the "dictatorship" of one party, make politics open, give the people of the whole country the opportunity to participate in politics, give the people full freedom, and jointly face the national crisis. Politics will then have a new atmosphere. Merely using the so-called National Assembly, etc., to perfunctorily deal with the people will only show that their efforts are increasingly clumsy.

Recently, Han, considering that the division had been stationed for a long time, should be transferred for training. On the 2nd, he summoned Sun Tongxuan to Jinan to discuss the transfer. Today (the 4th), Sun returned from Jinan to Zhoucun to arrange the departure of various units. After the division is transferred back, the 29th Division of Cao Fulin and the 74th Division of Qiao Lizhi will be dispatched to fill the positions. Currently, among the brigades under the 20th Division, except for the 59th Brigade of Zhao Xinde stationed in Zhanhua as a military reclamation force, the 58th Brigade of Zhang Shulin is stationed in Weixian, the 60th Brigade of Ma Jingong is stationed in Qingzhou, and the division headquarters and independent units are stationed in Zhoucun. They must be transported in batches. As for the division headquarters and independent units of the 29th Division, they are currently stationed in Jinan. Among the brigades under its command, except for the 87th Brigade of Rong Guangxing, which is stationed in Jiaozhou along the Jiaoji Railway, the 85th Brigade of Li Hanzhang is stationed in Dezhou, and the 86th Brigade of Chen Dexin is stationed in Dongchang. It is still uncertain whether the two brigades of Li and Chen will move east. Li Hanzhang came to Jinan yesterday to ask for instructions. Cao Fulin will lead the division headquarters to Zhoucun first. In addition, the 74th Division of Qiao Lizhi is currently stationed in Jinan. This morning, the 221st Brigade of Ma Guanyi of Qiao's division will first go to Weixian to replace Zhang Shulin's brigade, and the 222nd Brigade of Li Gaizhi will be stationed in the Qingzhou area.

Change of Head of the Finance Department of Hubei Province

He Chengjun temporarily concurrently serves as the head. All schools are suspended due to lack of funds. A dispatch from Hankou states that the finances of Hubei Province are becoming increasingly strained. Wu Guozhen, the head of the Finance Department, resigned because he could not cope with the situation. The successor is Yu Yuzhi. Yu is a member of the Provincial Party Committee and is temporarily acting on the order of He Chengjun. After taking office, he formulated five measures to reduce expenses. The original expenditure of 1,064,630 yuan for all political and educational departments was reduced to 816,540 yuan. Before a month had passed, Yu resigned again. As a result, no one dared to ask about the position of the head of the Finance Department. He was troubled by the search and telegraphed the Ministry of Finance to send someone to preside over the matter, but he did not receive a reply. He then temporarily took over the position and appointed He Hehua, the mayor of Hankou, to review official documents on his behalf. He arrived at the department on the 7th to take office, but has not taken any measures yet. However, teachers and staff of provincial schools went on strike that day due to the excessive arrears in salaries. At the same time, Huang Jianzhong, the head of the Education Department, also resolutely resigned. The entire education system in the province was plunged into a state of stagnation. According to He Chengjun, the province's revenue this month is only over 100,000 yuan, which is not enough to cover maintenance expenses. Since teachers and staff do not understand the government's difficulties and have gone on strike, he is unable to cope with the situation. Based on this, the future of Hubei's finances and education is not optimistic. (March 7th)
