丨紀念黃花崗死難烈士丨

今日爲黃花崗七十二烈士死難二十一度紀念日。同時又恰爲上海禍變爆發後兩週月又一日。在今日。我們試一閉目默念。則黃花崗一役七十二烈士與此次上海禍變中無數忠勇將士爲民族而犧牲之偉大精神。與其亘古不磨之斑爛碧血。當立時活躍於我們的眼前。爲悲壯爲嚴肅。爲偉大崇高。使人欽敬追慕而莫能自已。

我們認爲此一次悲壯的革命行動。在我國革命歷史上是具有偉大意義的一頁。它是斷送專制君主五千年來牢固寳座的一聲霹靂。是推進中國社會轉入一個新階段的一種動力。雖説它還只是以民族觀念爲立脚點而高呼「滅滿興漢」但實際則仍多由西歐資本主義先進國家侵略之刺激與民主思想之鼓盪。有此次悲壯的犧牲。以血與肉呌醒中華民族衰老的靈魂。而後有辛亥革命的爆發。而後幾千年來專制君主的命運。才最後宣告其死刑。所以黃花崗七十二烈士的死。可以謂爲當時時代精神的代表。換言之。他們是恰恰盡完了他們歷史使命。

黃花崗一役的革命運動。爲中國國民黨的先身同盟會所領導。此時國民黨還在艱難奮鬥的程途中。主義的偉力。激發而爲革命的狂潮。我們至今尙可以想見同盟會當日革命的精神。同時我們讀汪精衛題鄒海濱編黃花崗七十二烈士事略的序言。可知當時汪氏還正在北京牢獄現在國民黨已統一全國而爲主政之黨了。汪氏亦已由黨的先鋒。進而爲政治上的領袖了。然而今日的政治。較諸前日究何如。今日的國計民生。較諸前日又何如。外患日亟。山河破碎。撫今追昔。七十二烈士的犧牲。竟換得多少代價。汪氏在「七十二烈士事略」的序言中。曾有「非常傷感非常慚愧」的話。我們想在今日國民黨諸領袖以及汪氏。也許才眞的感到「非常傷感非常慚愧」罷。然而今日之危局。又豈僅「傷感」與「慚愧」所能了事嗎。

在今天我們紀念黃花崗七十二烈士。國民黨諸領袖當更慷慨流涙報告當日奮鬥的悲壯情况。然而我們認爲紀念七十二烈士。在於保持并繼續七十二烈士的革命精神。完成革命的偉業。目前我民族的生命。正爲强權所刦持。危急存亡。間不容髮。一二八禍變以來。我忠勇將士爲維護我民族之生存而犧牲的血跡。斑斑點點。顯示出深重的無限的悲憤。但就目前的情勢言。我們不能不承認一二八以來忠勇將士的血是白流的荒烟蔓草。戰壘宛在。不過又徒供後人之欷嘘憑吊。那麽我們今日正無暇紀念當日的烈事。眼前的事實。也就夠我們默念。更無暇爲七十二烈士哭。民族當前的大難。也就夠我們焦慮。我們今日紀念。正無異於爲我們自己致沉痛的哀忱。唱我們民族的輓歌。

光明在前。鞭兒在後。你古老的民族奮起罷。你呻吟宛轉的弱者奮起罷。不相信一二八的血便是白流的。我們當以更大的犧牲。發揚黃花崗七十二烈士與一二八忠勇將士的光榮。當以更大的努力。推動我民族與社會進入一新的階段。

Reflecting on the Present and Remembering the Past 1

丨Commemorating the Martyrs of Huanghuagang丨

Today is the twenty-first anniversary of the martyrdom of the seventy-two heroes of Huanghuagang. It also coincides with the two-month and one-day mark since the outbreak of the Shanghai Incident. On this day, let us try to close our eyes and silently remember. The great spirit of the seventy-two martyrs of Huanghuagang and the countless loyal and brave soldiers who sacrificed for the nation during the Shanghai Incident, and their eternally indelible and brilliant blood, will immediately come to life before our eyes. It evokes sorrow and solemnity, greatness and sublimity, compelling people to admire and yearn without restraint.

We believe that this tragic revolutionary action is a page of great significance in the revolutionary history of our country. It was a thunderbolt that shattered the five-thousand-year-old firm throne of autocratic monarchs and a driving force for China's society to enter a new stage. Although it only took the national concept as its foothold and shouted "Overthrow the Manchus and revive the Han," in reality, it was mostly stimulated by the invasion of advanced capitalist countries in Western Europe and the agitation of democratic ideas. This tragic sacrifice awakened the aging soul of the Chinese nation with blood and flesh, and then came the outbreak of the Xinhai Revolution. Only then was the fate of the autocratic monarchs of thousands of years finally declared its death. Therefore, the death of the seventy-two martyrs of Huanghuagang can be said to be the representative of the spirit of the times at that time. In other words, they just completed their historical mission.

The revolutionary movement of the Huanghuagang Uprising was led by the Tongmenghui, the predecessor of the Chinese Kuomintang. At this time, the Kuomintang was still struggling on its difficult journey. The great power of ideology was aroused into a revolutionary upsurge. We can still imagine the revolutionary spirit of the Tongmenghui at that time. At the same time, when we read Wang Jingwei's preface to Zou Haibin's "A Brief Biography of the Seventy-Two Martyrs of Huanghuagang," we know that Wang was still in prison in Beijing at that time. Now the Kuomintang has unified the country and become the ruling party. Wang has also progressed from the vanguard of the party to a political leader. However, how is today's politics compared to the past? How is today's national economy and people's livelihood compared to the past? Foreign troubles are becoming increasingly urgent, and the country is fragmented. Reflecting on the present and remembering the past, how much price has the sacrifice of the seventy-two martyrs been exchanged for? In the preface to "A Brief Biography of the Seventy-Two Martyrs," Wang once said, "Extremely sad and extremely ashamed." We think that today's leaders of the Kuomintang and Wang may truly feel "extremely sad and extremely ashamed." However, can today's critical situation be resolved merely by "sadness" and "shame"?

Today, as we commemorate the seventy-two martyrs of Huanghuagang, the leaders of the Kuomintang should be more generous in shedding tears and reporting the tragic situation of the struggle on that day. However, we believe that commemorating the seventy-two martyrs lies in maintaining and continuing their revolutionary spirit and completing the great cause of the revolution. At present, the life of our nation is being held hostage by powerful forces. The crisis of survival is imminent. Since the January 28th Incident, the bloodstains of our loyal and brave soldiers who sacrificed to safeguard the survival of our nation are speckled and show profound and infinite grief and indignation. However, judging from the current situation, we cannot but admit that the blood of the loyal and brave soldiers since January 28th has been shed in vain, with weeds growing rampant. The battlements are still there, but they only serve as a place for future generations to sigh and pay respects. Then we have no time to commemorate the martyrs of that day. The facts before our eyes are enough for us to remember silently, and we have no time to cry for the seventy-two martyrs. The great disaster facing the nation is enough for us to worry about. Our commemoration today is no different from offering our own condolences and singing the funeral song of our nation.

Light is ahead, and the whip is behind. Arise, you ancient nation. Arise, you weaklings who groan and twist. We do not believe that the blood of January 28th was shed in vain. We should make greater sacrifices to promote the glory of the seventy-two martyrs of Huanghuagang and the loyal and brave soldiers of January 28th. We should make greater efforts to push our nation and society into a new stage.
