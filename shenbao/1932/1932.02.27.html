毌忘甲午戰役的敎訓　彬

在距今二十八年前的清日甲午戰役中。我們曾蒙受了一次慘痛的敗衂。這一次敗衂。便定下了我們二十八年來長處於日本凌　轢宰制之下的命運。台灣朝鮮之被割以至去年九一八東北各省之被佔取。都是以此一戰爲其關鍵。

這一次的戰爭關係尤爲重大。爲我民族是否尙能維護其生存自由之最後試騐。如此戰而敗則我民族將從此陷入更慘酷之悲境。永永末由振拔。故我國人須一致認清此戰意義之重大。

甲午戰役之失敗。不是偶然的本來滿清的本身先已具有了種種必敗的條件所以才一敗而不可收拾。

第一。當甲午戰爭爆發之先日本早已有一戰的决心。當時駐日公使汪鳳藻駐韓公使袁世凱駐英法公使龔照瑗。都告日本有戰意。清政府　頇終始持國力未充不能與的主戰。張不早備戰。以致倉皇應戰。一敗再敗土氣旣墮。遂一蹶而不可復振。第二。外交情勢。日益緊急。而清政府內部。仍互相傾軋。互相牽制。李鴻藻翁同龢輩更乘機參奏李鴻章三大罪狀。李的增兵計劃。遂爲其所阻。日人陸奥光宗曾斷爲「清庭自殺其國家」。實爲的評第三。戰事旣已發動。自應全國總動員。共起作戰。但當時則惟有李鴻章懷抱决心。倉皇應戰各省者觀望不前。强敵迫於外。權臣牽於內。欲不敗而不。得戰後西批評謂「日人非與中國戰。乃與北京政府戰。非與北京政府戰。乃與李鴻章一人戰」由此我們可以想見當時國內的散漫如一盤散沙之情勢了第四。戰爭開始之前。日人在軍事上旣積極準備。在外交上亦充分佈置於各國情勢。都極明晰。所以能運用自由。而我方在外交上。則耳目手足呼應無效。運轉不靈。每事隔閡。在外交完全陷入孤立的窘境。第五戰幕旣已揭開即應極力堅持。準備長時期之苦鬥。但當時清政府還未到無法應戰的地步。即屈服乞和。所以喪權辱國造成二十八年來橫被宰制的惡果。「能戰而後能守能守而後能和」爭取勝利。在最後的五分鐘。這一點在目前我們尤其應該記牢。

上列的五點。都是甲午戰役致敗的原因。即是我們在今日所應重復回溫的課程。與所應鄭重領受的敎訓。

Do Not Forget the Lessons of the Sino-Japanese War of 1894-95 彬

In the Sino-Japanese War of 1894-95, which occurred twenty-eight years ago, we suffered a terrible defeat. This defeat determined our fate for the past twenty-eight years, a fate of being dominated and oppressed by Japan. The ceding of Taiwan and Korea, and the occupation of the Northeastern provinces last year on September 18th, all stemmed from this war.

The consequences of this war were particularly significant, serving as the final test of whether our nation could maintain its survival and freedom. If we were defeated in this war, our nation would fall into an even more tragic situation, from which it would never be able to recover. Therefore, our people must recognize the immense significance of this war.

The failure of the Sino-Japanese War was not accidental. The Qing Dynasty itself already possessed various conditions for inevitable defeat, which led to an irreversible collapse.

First, before the outbreak of the Sino-Japanese War, Japan had already made a firm decision to wage war. At that time, our ministers to Japan, Wang Fengzao, to Korea, Yuan Shikai, and to Britain and France, Gong Zhaoyuan, all reported Japan's intention to fight. The Qing government stubbornly maintained that its national strength was insufficient to engage in war, from beginning to end. It failed to prepare for war early, resulting in a hasty response and repeated defeats. Morale plummeted, leading to a downfall from which it could not recover. Second, the diplomatic situation was becoming increasingly urgent, but the Qing government remained internally divided and mutually obstructive. Li Hongzao, Weng Tonghe, and their ilk seized the opportunity to accuse Li Hongzhang of three major crimes, thwarting Li's plan to increase troop deployments. The Japanese, Mutsu Munemitsu, astutely observed that "the Qing court is committing national suicide," which was an accurate assessment. Third, once the war had begun, the entire nation should have been mobilized to fight together. However, only Li Hongzhang embraced a resolute determination to respond hastily to the war, while other provinces remained indifferent and unwilling to act. With a powerful enemy pressing from the outside and influential officials undermining from within, defeat was inevitable. Western critics after the war commented, "The Japanese were not fighting against China, but against the Beijing government. They were not fighting against the Beijing government, but against Li Hongzhang alone." From this, we can imagine the disunity and disorganization within the country at that time. Fourth, before the war began, Japan had actively prepared militarily and thoroughly arranged its diplomatic relations with various countries. It had a clear understanding of the situation, allowing it to act freely. On our side, diplomatic efforts were ineffective, uncoordinated, and sluggish. Every matter was hindered, and we were completely isolated diplomatically. Fifth, once the war had begun, we should have strived to persevere and prepare for a prolonged and arduous struggle. However, the Qing government, before it had reached a point where it could no longer fight, surrendered and sought peace, leading to the loss of sovereignty and humiliation, and resulting in twenty-eight years of oppressive control. "Only by being able to fight can we defend; only by being able to defend can we negotiate peace." Strive for victory in the last five minutes. This point is especially important for us to remember today.

The above five points are the reasons for the defeat in the Sino-Japanese War. They are lessons that we should revisit and seriously learn today.

For the next twenty years, the Open Door Policy became an informal mission of the nations. During the Washington Conference in 1921 and 1922, attended by the major nations with interests in the Pacific, this policy crystallized into the Nine-Power Treaty, which precisely defined and articulated the two major principles from which it originated. Article I of the treaty stipulates that the contracting states, other than China, agree to

(1) Respect the sovereignty, independence, and territorial and administrative integrity of China. (2) Provide China with the fullest and most unimpeded opportunity to develop and maintain an effective and stable government. (3) Use their influence to effectively establish and maintain the principle of equal opportunity for the commerce and industry of all nations throughout the territory of China. (4) Not to take advantage of conditions in China to seek special rights that would diminish the rights of the people of friendly nations, nor to engage in acts that would secretly endanger the security of friendly nations.

Therefore, this treaty represents a carefully considered and mature international policy. On the one hand, it guarantees the rights and interests of the contracting states in China. On the other hand, it guarantees the people of China the fullest opportunity to develop their sovereignty and independence, so that they can meet the new and enlightened standards maintained among all nations of the world today, without being hindered. At the time the treaty was signed, the world knew that China, after a new revolution, was attempting to gradually develop from an autocratic government into a free system of a self-governing republic.
