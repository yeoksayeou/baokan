國難會議之難產彬 1

國難會議前以粤方中委之倡議。繼以四届一中全會之决議。及國府之陸續多次聘請會員。久已甚囂塵上今會議且已决定於本月七日正式舉行矣。吾人於此一會議。嚮即不加重視。一月二十八日曾已爲評提出種種疑問就使會而能集集。而能議而謂其遂足以打破當前之國難。蓋亦等於痴人説夢。今請於會期迫近即將正式舉行之前。再予以嚴正之批判。

國難會議。旨在救國。在接納人民之公意。以定救國之根本方針。然而禦侮救災。早已爲全國人民一致之呼聲矣。澄淸政治。建設民主政治。以及種種應興應革之大政。全國人民亦早已一致明白要求矣。政府而眞誠意悔禍誠意接納人民之公意。則即應本此「民意。」與人民努力以共趨。更何必費多時之躊躇。舉行一不必有之會議。退一步言。即斯會而眞有舉行之必要。然而會員而以選聘聘。請之人。則又皆爲寛衣博袖西裝革履之所謂「名流」「聞人」或「領袖」「學者。」一次聘請之不足。繼之以再。繼之以三四。畢羅盡舉。舉凡曩日之所視爲軍閥官僚政客反動派爲無可容恕者今皆鉅細不遺。獨於佔全國人口最大多數。而又實際處於身受最大痛苦悲境之農工羣衆。竟未見徵聘一人。是則斯會而即眞能召集。亦不過爲「聞人」「名流」之會議耳。不過爲「聞人」「名流」之「公意」耳。

再退一步言。即會議之結果而誠足以代表人民之公意誠足以打破當前之艱難。拯救我民族於危亡。然而斯會之性質。旣非立法機關。復無拘束政府之權力。如一月間報載某中委談。「國難會不過爲討論出辦法來。供中央之參考。」則已明示决議之不必實行矣。設辦法議出。而「中央參考」之下。認爲不必採行。而長擱置於案頭。則斯會之集。不仍將徒爲兒戲毫無意義耶。

最近汪院長明白規定國難會議所討論之範圍爲救災禦侮綏靖。逾此即非所許。則斯會之集。徒以敷衍人民。更爲明顯。以言救災。自去年洪水驟發。政府雖亦曾有救濟水災委員會之組織。但除借美麥而外。所藉以杯水救車薪者。仍爲我人民一再捐輸之累積。所謂賑災公債三千萬。用於何處。吾人迄今未見政府之明告以言禦侮自九一八禍變突發。我人民即無日不敦促政府長期抵抗。收復失地。馬占山所部之苦戰龍江。與此次十九路軍之英勇抗暴。我人民之踴躍捐輸。熱烈愛護。助財助力。亦爲共見之事實。而政府則亘半年之久。對内高呼鎭靜。對外乞憐告哀。戰和不决。土不能守國。不能衛。則所謂「禦侮」一問題之討論。不又爲近於欺騙耶。再如綏靖。吾人認爲匪盜之作。都由於政治之黑暗。人民生計窮蹙。自不得不挺而走險。嚮使政治而長此黑暗。人民長困水火。則苛政之下。誰爲善良。誰爲匪盜。將從何而辨白。民不畏死。法將安施。年來政府籌巨欵。調大兵。剿匪之結果如何。則所謂綏靖問題之討論。彼手無寸鐵之「名流」「聞人。」又不過貢獻政府若干紙上談兵之辦法而已。

如止所陳。則國難會議之價値與其意義。吾人應已瞭然。一言以蔽之。不過爲敷衍人民之一種手段耳。吾人是否應重視斯會。被徵聘之諸君子是否甘爲傀儡。曷其三思。

鐵 127

— 徵

鐵的藴藏非常豐富，價値很低廉，牠的性質又優良，所以用途是極廣大，現在我請牠出來，介紹給諸位：據孟脱李斯氏（Ｍｏｎｔｅｔｉｕｓ）著的鐵器時代起始一書上面説，中國確是首先用鐵。崔豹古今注説：「黃帝與蚩尤戰於涿鹿之野蚩尤作大霧，士兵皆迷途，於是作指南車以示四方，遂擒蚩尤」造指南車必定是用磁針，製磁針則必須用鐵，可知我國在黃帝時已用鐵；推算其時，大約在西曆紀元前二七○○年；孟氏書上説埃及用鐵在紀元前一二○○年，爲西國最早用鐵了。倘若算起來，我中國用鐵，比他們早一五○○年。但是現在看看人家用鐵怎樣？自己怎樣？我們子孫不肖，祗有快些張氣。

講到鐵是化學元素中的一種，屬於金屬類的，符號是Ｆｅ，看上去是灰白色的。牠有極好的延長性；鐵絲的强靸及耐牽引性，在金屬中讓還牠最好了。我們要燒融牠，倒是非常吃力，因爲牠的融點是極高。牠的感磁性是很好的；所以鐵的東西，可以給磁石（俗名吸鐵石）吸引的。鐵的性質，要看牠所混含碳的量多少而分爲；熟鐵（WioughtLron），鋼（Sterl），生鐵（Cast　Lron）三種。

熟鐵是含量Ｏ·Ｓ％以下的鐵，牠性質與純鐵（Ｐｕｒｅ　Ｌｒｏｎ）相同。旣强韌，又富有延長性；拿牠放在火中燒紅，就能軟化，打成各種形狀極容易。牠還能夠感磁，但不易保存，做暫時電磁鐵就用牠的。

鋼是含碳量在１％左右的鐵，紅熱之，亦能軟化，同熟鐵一樣。融點較純鐵低，故鑄各種器具亦比較純鐵容易些。燒紅的鋼，急侵牠在冷水中，能夠增加牠的硬度。鋼非常容易感磁，又有保存的性；製各種永久磁石都用鋼的。

生鐵是含碳量在４％左右的鐵，融點在三種中算牠最低了；從鐵鑛中最初鍊得的鐵，就是生鐵；鍊出時急冷牠，則是含有鐵與碳的化合物，顏色白的，所以呌牠爲白生鐵（Ｗｈｉｔｅ　Ｃａｓｔ　Ｌｒｏｎ倘鍊出後，待牠漸漸冷却的，則牠會析出石墨（Ｇｒａｐｈｉｔｅ），（亦是一種碳質），變成灰色，呌灰生鐵（Ｇｒａｙ　Ｃａｓｔ　Ｌｒｏｎ）倘若鋼混合８％的錳，或１％的鉻，則牠的硬度更則利害。熟鐵含有３％的鎳，則變成强韌而有彈性。但是含有硫黃或磷的鐵，則脆弱而不適實用了。

鐵能夠同養氣化合，平常鐵器擱置得日子多了，便生一層黃的鐵銹；這就是因爲鐵與空氣中的養氣接觸，化合成一重氣化鐵。鐵還能與硫，氯等化合，成别種物質。

鐵因爲（一）强韌；（二）燒紅之能軟化；（三）從燒熱冷却的變化，能得到任意的硬度與彈性；（四）出產多而價廉，所以機械工業上都用牠。鋼可以製造鋒利的武器，綿延的鐵軌，航海巨艦，强硬的甲板，高立的橋樑，精密的機械，同其他一切建築的材料。生鐵製造鐵管，鐵柱，與各種器具。熟鐵因爲有一時的感磁性，我在上面略爲説過，可以做一種暫時的電磁鐵；電鈴，電報的機内都用着；有一種電磁起重機内的磁鐵，亦拿熟鐵做的。但是，純鐵的用途反而不靈，不過精製成的鐵粉，給貧血症的藥中用。

The Difficult Birth of the National Crisis Conference 1

The National Crisis Conference was initially proposed by the Guangdong faction's Central Committee members, followed by a resolution from the Fourth Plenary Session of the First Central Committee, and then by the National Government's successive invitations to members. The clamor surrounding it has been quite loud for some time, and now the conference is scheduled to officially begin on the 7th of this month. We have never placed much importance on this conference. On January 28th, we already published a commentary raising various questions. Even if the conference convenes and manages to reach a consensus, to think it will be sufficient to overcome the current national crisis is tantamount to a fool's dream. Now, as the conference date approaches and it is about to officially begin, we will offer another stern critique.

The purpose of the National Crisis Conference is to save the nation by accepting the public opinion of the people and determining a fundamental strategy for national salvation. However, resisting aggression and relieving disaster have long been the unanimous call of the people. Clarifying politics, building a democratic government, and various other major policies that should be promoted and reformed have also long been the unanimous and clear demands of the people. If the government sincerely intends to repent and genuinely accept the public opinion of the people, it should base its actions on this "popular will" and work with the people to achieve it together. Why bother to hesitate for so long and hold an unnecessary conference? Even assuming this conference is truly necessary, the members are selected and invited. Those invited are all so-called "celebrities," "famous figures," or "leaders" and "scholars" who wear loose robes and Western suits. One round of invitations is not enough, so it is followed by a second, then a third and fourth, including everyone exhaustively. Those who were previously regarded as warlords, bureaucrats, politicians, and reactionaries and considered unforgivable are now included without omission, regardless of their importance. However, the vast majority of the national population, who are actually suffering the greatest pain and misery – the peasants and workers – not a single one has been invited. Therefore, even if this conference can truly convene, it will only be a conference of "famous figures" and "celebrities." Shanghai Municipal Council's Method of Estimating Property Tax 31

The Shanghai Municipal Council's 1931 annual report states that at the beginning of the year, the Chinese Ratepayers' Association and other Chinese organizations raised objections to the Municipal Council's method of estimating property tax. In reality, the Municipal Council must use the estimated value as the basis for collecting taxes on properties for which the actual rental amount is unclear, which is permitted by Article 9 of the Land Regulations and consistent with municipal principles. This has been the practice for sixty years and was investigated and approved by the Consular Body in 1907. Generally, the Municipal Council relies on rent receipts, but difficulties sometimes arise, such as (1) properties occupied by the landlord themselves, which must be assessed by the Municipal Council; (2) properties occupied by friends or relatives of the landlord, where only nominal rent is paid, which cannot be used as a basis; (3) cases where a large key money or other conditions exist, so the rent is only nominal; (4) cases of forged rent receipts to deceive the Municipal Council, etc. To avoid misunderstandings and ensure consistent methods, the Municipal Council has appointed two valuers from the council and hired three external valuers to form a valuation committee, including one Chinese and one Japanese member. If taxpayers disagree with the valuation, they can submit an objection to the committee within 30 days. If they are still dissatisfied, they can appeal to the Council's "Finance, Taxation, and Appeals Committee" within another 30 days. The valuation becomes final after their assessment or if the taxpayer does not appeal within the deadline. It is noted that within this year, the valuation committee has reviewed the cases of 436 Zhongshe Road, 44 Henan Road, 446 Henan Road, China General Edison Company, Wing On Company, and the Catholic Church. Furthermore, Article 10 of the Shanghai Municipal Government's Regulations for the Collection of Property Tax, amended and promulgated on September 27, 1930, states: "The determination of rental value shall be based on the rent receipt or lease for the most recent two months. If there is a significant discrepancy between the house and the rental price, the department shall separately determine the rental price for the purpose of collecting property tax. For self-owned houses occupied by the owner, the Finance Bureau shall send personnel to determine the rental value. If there is a dispute over the estimated rental value, the property valuation committee shall assess it." Iron 127

Iron is very abundant and inexpensive, and its properties are excellent, so its uses are very extensive. Speaking of iron, it is a chemical element, belonging to the metal category, and its symbol is Fe. It looks grayish-white. It has excellent ductility; the strength and tensile resistance of iron wire are the best among metals. It is very difficult to melt it because its melting point is extremely high. Its magnetic susceptibility is very good; therefore, iron things can be attracted by magnets (commonly known as lodestones). The properties of iron are divided into wrought iron, steel, and cast iron, depending on the amount of carbon it contains.

Wrought iron is iron with a carbon content of less than 0.5%. Its properties are the same as pure iron. It is both strong and ductile; when placed in a fire and heated red, it softens and is very easy to forge into various shapes. It can also be magnetized, but it is not easy to preserve, so it is used to make temporary electromagnets.

Steel is iron with a carbon content of about 1%. When heated red, it can also soften, just like wrought iron. The melting point is lower than pure iron, so it is easier to cast various utensils than pure iron. Steel that is heated red and then rapidly quenched in cold water can increase its hardness. Steel is very easy to magnetize and also has the property of preserving magnetism; various permanent magnets are made of steel.

Cast iron is iron with a carbon content of about 4%. Its melting point is the lowest of the three. The iron initially smelted from iron ore is cast iron. If it is rapidly cooled during smelting, it contains a compound of iron and carbon and is white in color, so it is called white cast iron. If it is gradually cooled after smelting, it will precipitate graphite (also a type of carbon), turning gray and called gray cast iron. If steel is mixed with 8% manganese or 1% chromium, its hardness will be even greater. Wrought iron containing 3% nickel becomes strong and elastic. However, iron containing sulfur or phosphorus is brittle and unsuitable for practical use.

Iron can combine with oxygen. Ordinary ironware, when left for many days, will develop a layer of yellow rust; this is because iron comes into contact with oxygen in the air and combines into iron oxide. Iron can also combine with sulfur, chlorine, and other substances to form other materials.

Iron is used in the mechanical industry because of (1) its strength; (2) its ability to soften when heated red; (3) the ability to obtain arbitrary hardness and elasticity through changes in heating and cooling; (4) its abundant production and low price. Steel can be used to manufacture sharp weapons, long rails, giant ships, strong decks, tall bridges, precise machinery, and all other building materials. Cast iron is used to manufacture iron pipes, iron pillars, and various utensils. Wrought iron, because of its temporary magnetic properties, as I mentioned above, can be used to make a temporary electromagnet; it is used in electric bells and telegraph machines. A type of electromagnet in electromagnetic cranes is also made of wrought iron. However, pure iron is not very useful; however, refined iron powder is used in medicine for anemia.
