從言論自由說到思想自由彬

關於言論自由。吾人既曾於去年十一月間發表「言論自由之眞諦」一評。認爲此爲人民神聖不可侵犯之權利。政府絕不應加以若何之限制與干涉。今請更進而主張思想絕對自由。

此一主張之提出。或者有人視爲近有滑稽。同時或又有人視爲决洪水之防堤。危機至大。前者以爲吾人個人潛在之思想。固未嘗不自由。且亦無人可能予以限制。後者則以爲今日社會既已騷亂。若再取絕對之放任主義。對於思想不予以相當之防範。是無異於抱薪救火。將益以增長亂源。凡此兩說。自其表面視之。固皆似言之成理其實皆非。

吾人認思想自由之正確解釋。不爲個人思想「潛在」之自由。而應爲個人思想「公表」之自由。蓋人生皆有腦。即生皆有思。吾人靜思默念。即窮天極地。幻想萬象。固亦無人可能窺知。更無人可能加以束縛。然而此不過木偶之自由耳。吾人認爲行動固當受法律之拘束。而思想必予以公表之自由。使得與他人交通。以引起社會之評論與研究。公開討論。而後思想乃爲有意義有價値。吾人今日固皆有木偶式之思想潛在之自由矣。然而果有思想公表之自由乎。此吾人於主張思想自由之先。不能不正確辨認其眞諦之一解也。

次之。今日我國家我社會。固已陷入動盪不寗之境。靑年之思想。固極駁雜不純。然而此種動盪之現象。實爲由於政治之紊亂與社會經濟之破落。而非由於駁雜思想之鼓盪。抑且正以社會之動盪。民生日蹙。而政治上之權力者。乃更從而予以重重之桎梏。使其有思而不能思。有主張而不能公表。不能自由覓取出路。憤激鬱聚。適足以使其挺而走險。以求一快。是蓋有苦築堤防水。似可防患於一時。而洪濤激盪。堤終將一决而不可收拾。固莫若撤除堤防。多開水道。使水得其平。得暢其流。庶不至激爲拍天之巨浪。此吾人於主張思想自由之先。不能不明白辨認其利害之又一解也。

自國民黨主政以來。即厲行黨治。一切庶政。都以「黨化」爲前提。舉凡人民之思想與言論。概加以束缚。尤其自民十七國共分裂而後。對於思想言論。限制尤嚴。凡與國民黨主義與立場不同之他種學說。他種主義。不僅絕對不許其流傳。抑且絕對杜絕其研究。數年來靑年之斷送於此者。蓋已無可數計。吾人誠不知當局對於此種限制。亦嘗於法理上曾否加以深切之研究。更不知當局此種限制。是否能確信其遂足以遏抑人民不可遏抑之思潮。

如臯惡妻殺妾案。喧騰滬上。報紙且不惜以記載抗日血戰之寳貴篇幅刋登之。與香港馮德謙案。杭州劉夢瑩案同爲人所注意。近且剖腹取嬰。官廳力圖昭雪。茲將本案前後及社會所聞。略誌以告讀者。

本案非關妒愛。絕非三角關係所演成之血案。特不過一曲折之謀財害命耳。蓋案之起因。多分由於社會關係。徐成玉之娶妾。實系於無後爲大之「宗法觀念」。徐妻及徐姪之殺妾。乃由於「遺產承繼問題」。在農村社會之如臯鄉間。竟演出如許之鉅案者。無非錢之作祟。惟徐田不過百畝。在鄉雖曰小康。估計實不足萬金。此攘彼奪。又何苦來。

本案之暴發。由於鄰居不平之告發。此點在普通之刑案上不甚常見。但鄰里不平之原因。亦有原來。蓋如臯有一種特殊人物。藉訟爲活。鼎革後此風亦未稍殺。且多播揚風聲。以經手賄賂爲榮耀。此事發生後。尸父初曾告發。不久即行中止。蓋訟棍某某已經手了結。并對徐云。曾以千元了衙門。（即賄賂之意）。道路傳說。謠言以息。里之强項者乃奮爲舉發。並具結以堅信認。於是案遂大白。

From Freedom of Speech to Freedom of Thought

Regarding freedom of speech, we have already published a commentary titled "The True Meaning of Freedom of Speech" in November of last year. We believe that this is a sacred and inviolable right of the people, and the government should never impose any restrictions or interference. Now, we would like to go further and advocate for absolute freedom of thought.

The proposal of this argument may be seen by some as almost farcical, while others may see it as opening the floodgates, leading to a major crisis. The former believe that our individual, latent thoughts are inherently free and that no one can possibly restrict them. The latter, however, believe that society is already in turmoil, and to adopt an absolute laissez-faire attitude without proper precautions regarding thought is akin to adding fuel to the fire, which will only exacerbate the source of chaos. Both of these arguments, on the surface, may seem reasonable, but in reality, they are not.

We believe that the correct interpretation of freedom of thought is not the "latent" freedom of individual thought, but rather the freedom of "public expression" of individual thought. All people are born with brains, and where there is birth, there is thought. When we contemplate in silence, even to the ends of the earth, and fantasize about all things, no one can possibly know or restrain us. However, this is merely the freedom of a puppet. We believe that while actions should be bound by law, thought must be given the freedom of public expression, so that it can be communicated to others, stimulate social commentary and research, and be openly discussed. Only then does thought become meaningful and valuable. Today, we all possess this puppet-like, latent freedom of thought. But do we truly have the freedom of public expression of thought? This is one aspect of the true meaning that we must correctly identify before advocating for freedom of thought.

Secondly, our nation and our society are indeed mired in a state of turmoil and unrest. The thoughts of young people are indeed extremely complex and impure. However, this turbulent phenomenon is actually due to political chaos and socio-economic ruin, and not due to the agitation of complex thoughts. Moreover, it is precisely because of social unrest and the decline of people's livelihoods that those in power in politics are further imposing heavy shackles, preventing people from thinking when they have thoughts and from publicly expressing their views, unable to freely seek a way out. Resentment and frustration accumulate, which is enough to drive them to take risks in the pursuit of satisfaction. This is like building a dam to prevent flooding, which may seem to prevent disaster for a time, but when the torrents surge, the dam will eventually collapse and be beyond repair. It would be better to remove the dam and open up more waterways, allowing the water to find its level and flow freely, so as not to be stirred into a towering wave. This is another aspect of the advantages and disadvantages that we must clearly identify before advocating for freedom of thought.

Since the Kuomintang came to power, it has vigorously implemented party rule. All administrative affairs are premised on "party indoctrination," and the people's thoughts and speech are generally shackled. Especially since the split between the Kuomintang and the Communist Party in the 17th year of the Republic (1928), restrictions on thought and speech have become even stricter. Any other theories or ideologies that differ from the Kuomintang's ideology and position are not only absolutely forbidden from spreading, but also absolutely prevented from being studied. Over the years, countless young people have been ruined by this. We sincerely do not know whether the authorities have ever conducted in-depth research on this type of restriction in terms of legal principles, nor do we know whether the authorities can be sure that this type of restriction is sufficient to suppress the people's irrepressible tide of thought.

Bromine

— Hui

In 1826 of the Western calendar, Mr. Balard discovered bromine. Because it has a pungent and foul odor, its English name comes from the Greek word for stench. It is one of the eighty-five elements, with the chemical symbol Br2. At normal temperature, it appears as a dark brown liquid. Among the elements, only it and mercury are liquid; the other elements are all gases or solids.

When bromine is cooled to -7 degrees Celsius (7 degrees below zero), it solidifies into dark, needle-like crystals. When heated above 60 degrees Celsius, it boils and turns into reddish-brown vapor. However, it also volatilizes very easily at normal temperatures. If you smell it, the odor is unbearable.

Bromine vapor is very heavy. If you drop a few drops onto the bottom of a slightly heated flask, it will vaporize and turn into reddish-brown smoke, which stays at the bottom. The most concentrated bromine water contains up to 3% bromine.

We waited until dawn, and when the gunfire subsided slightly, we staggered out the door. But after walking only a few steps, a shell hit the wall next to us. A large chunk of the gray wall immediately collapsed. I was so scared that I stepped back a few steps, and the gray wall fell less than ten feet away from me. You see how dangerous it was."

He spoke with lingering fear, and a restless, faint light shone from his eyes. We both sighed in unison. He continued, "After escaping, I stayed at the home of a Mr. Lin. All the bedding and clothing were temporarily borrowed from him. I never dreamed it would come to such a messy state."

Two days ago, he came to my residence again, looking haggard and thin, still the same as before. After sitting down for a while, he searched in his pocket and pulled out a bunch of burnt iron tools. He earnestly said to me, "Everything I had is gone. This is the key to the front door of my house. A neighbor picked it up for me yesterday. More than twenty years of hard work, only this little memento remains. It's so heartbreaking."

He had collected no less than several hundred kinds of books and treasures related to Southeast Asia, but now they have all been burned to ashes. It's truly a pity. It's not a big deal that we lost tens of thousands of lives and hundreds of millions of properties. The most important thing is that our cultural institutions were destroyed. In our culturally backward China, this blow cannot be underestimated." He nodded and said in a drawn-out voice, "Yes."

Then we talked a lot more, and he finally said goodbye and left. As he walked down the stairs, I looked at his back, which seemed the same as what was commonly seen on Baoshan Road.

Animal Labor

— Lu Ling

Among animals, those that can use their intelligence to assist humans in their work, such as cattle, horses, and dogs, have been doing so for so long that it is no longer strange. In recent centuries, human affairs have become complex and there is not enough time for everything. Although there are machines that can assist, machines are machines, rigid and without spirituality. Certain tasks still require human labor. Human labor is limited, so we have to seek assistance from animals. Thus, the so-called animal labor has emerged. Using animals to assist people in their work requires a meticulous training process. In various European and American countries, there are those who practice animal training, mostly derived from the old methods used in circuses. In recent years, there has been visible progress, to the point where it seems that there are virtually no animals that cannot be trained to be useful. For example, elephants, known for their clumsiness, have recently had two trained to be useful in the Belgian Congo. They can carry heavy loads and travel long distances, and be directed by people, and during wartime, they can also be responsible for transporting ammunition and food, going in and out of the jungle, and doing their part for the country. Other animals, such as baboons in South Africa, have recently been taught to work on farms. It is said that their performance is very good, and their work efficiency is twice that of the natives. Also, monkeys in Sumatra are often raised by the natives to harvest coconuts. Their efficiency is like that of humans, and their diligence is also like that of humans. However, they are cunning in nature. They have fixed working hours every day. Whenever they see people working in the same place stop working and rest, they will also stop working at the same time, unwilling to do even a minute or a second more of work. This is also interesting.
