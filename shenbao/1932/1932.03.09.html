悼白里安 1

翰

素稱「世界和平天使」之白里安。已於前日逝世矣。噩耗傳來全球震動。而我酷愛和平之中華民族。適在强敵鐵蹄下圖掙扎。驟聞此哲人云亡。其悲感更何如耶。

白里安爲近世以來最奇特之政治家。前後組閣凡十一次。自一九二五年以迄最近。歷任外交部長。從無間斷。其人雄辩大略。更富理想。故不特爲法國國民所愛戴。抑且爲世界人士所推重。早年曾任新聞記者。與歐戰前主張非戰最烈之法社會黨領袖查萊士曾一度合作辦報。其後和平思想之發達。受查萊士之影響甚深。白氏生平有記錄之價值者。累累可成巨秩。祇就其關於世界和平事業之創造論。已足供吾人之無限推崇。羅加諾條約之簽訂德國之加入聯盟。莫非白氏一人斡旋之力。年來且進一步發表歐洲聯盟之計劃。欲使歐洲之和平。樹立永久磐石之基礎。繼囂俄之遺志。不僅無愧於「詩人外交家］之稱。

吾人對白氏之逝世。所以不勝其哀悼者。尤以白氏爲主張和平最堅之一人也。自九一八禍變以後。國聯行政會第一次接受中日事件。白氏以事不及參加。二三兩次皆由白氏主持。第三次在巴黎開會。白氏扶病出席。鞠躬盡瘁。其維持和平之苦心。亦可謂無以復加矣。乃竟無以挽回日方强硬之主張。轉移國際聯盟之難局。雖素稱國際上排難解紛之斵輪老手。至此亦徒喚奈何。最近賴伐爾閣潮。白氏乘機下野。從此國際舞臺上不見白氏之踪影。日内瓦議塲上。更不能領略白氏之言論丰采。最近白氏病重時。猶眷眷以東亞和平爲念及其死也。舉世各國無不表示哀悼。即德國之白魯寗。亦深致其惋惜之意舉世之所以崇仰白氏。非僅崇仰白氏之人格。更崇仰白氏之以身殉和平也。而今後。誰能繼起保障和平。爲國際聯盟之保姆耶。　　當一九二五年十月。布希糾紛時。白里安任國聯行政會主席。即曾發表堅决之主張。［一民族如感有外患之逼迫。苦訴國聯。行政會必挺身而出。大聲曰有吾在。排難解紛。何欋之有。］今竟以未能實践其志。而憂鬰以終。我人留此悲痛之印象眼前。更一聯想及於世界和平之危險。誠不能不悲從中來唏嘘不置也。

In Memory of Briand 1

Han

Briand, known as the "Angel of World Peace," passed away the day before yesterday. The sad news shook the world. And the Chinese nation, which deeply loves peace and is struggling under the iron heel of a strong enemy, felt even more grief upon hearing of the death of this philosopher.

Briand was the most extraordinary statesman of modern times. He formed cabinets eleven times. From 1925 until recently, he served as Foreign Minister without interruption. He was eloquent, strategic, and full of ideals. Therefore, he was not only loved by the French people but also respected by people around the world. In his early years, he was a journalist and once co-edited a newspaper with Jaurès, the leader of the French Socialist Party who most strongly advocated against war before the European War. Later, his peace ideals were deeply influenced by Jaurès. Briand's life has countless events worthy of recording, enough to fill volumes. Just his contributions to the cause of world peace are enough for us to admire him endlessly. The signing of the Locarno Treaties and Germany's entry into the League of Nations were all due to Briand's mediation. In recent years, he further proposed a plan for a European Union, seeking to establish a permanent foundation for peace in Europe. Continuing the legacy of Xiao E, he was not only worthy of the title "Poet Diplomat."

Our grief over Briand's death is especially profound because he was one of the strongest advocates for peace. After the September 18th Incident, the Council of the League of Nations first accepted the Sino-Japanese issue. Briand did not participate in the first meeting. Briand presided over the second and third meetings. He attended the third meeting in Paris despite being ill, dedicating himself to the task. His dedication to maintaining peace can be said to have been unparalleled. However, he was ultimately unable to reverse Japan's strong stance or shift the difficult situation of the League of Nations. Even as an experienced troubleshooter in international affairs, he could only lament his powerlessness. Recently, with the Laval cabinet's downfall, Briand seized the opportunity to step down. Since then, Briand's presence has been absent from the international stage. Briand's speeches and demeanor could no longer be appreciated at the Geneva conference. Even when Briand was seriously ill recently, he was still concerned about peace in East Asia. Upon his death, countries around the world expressed their condolences. Even Brüning of Germany deeply expressed his regret. The world's admiration for Briand is not only for his personality but also for his dedication to peace. In the future, who can step up to safeguard peace and be the guardian of the League of Nations? In October 1925, during the Buxi dispute, Briand, as Chairman of the Council of the League of Nations, made a firm statement: "If a nation feels threatened by external enemies and appeals to the League of Nations, the Council must step forward and loudly say, 'I am here. What difficulty is there that cannot be resolved?'" Now, he has ended his life in sorrow because he could not fulfill his aspirations. The painful impression remains in our minds, and when we think of the dangers to world peace, we cannot help but feel grief from the bottom of our hearts.

Attached: A Brief Biography of Briand 75

(Paris) The late French Premier Briand, whose full name was Aristide Briand, was born in Nantes Province in 1862. After graduating from the University of Saint-Nazaire and the Nantes Academy, he entered the world at the age of twenty. He initially practiced law, but soon switched to journalism, becoming an editor at the Nantes "Western Democracy" newspaper, using the pen name "Nichel." Every article he wrote was widely read. He later went to Paris, where his reputation grew. In 1902, he entered politics, being elected to the National Assembly from the Loire region. He then led the crowd with his eloquence and sharp insight. In 1906, he first served as Minister of Education in the Sarrien cabinet. Shortly after, the cabinet was reorganized, but Briand remained in the same position. In 1908, he became Minister of Justice. The following year, he became Premier and Minister of the Interior. During the railway workers' strike, Briand decisively responded by sending troops to manage the railways, causing a great uproar that eventually subsided. In 1912, Briand's first cabinet was dissolved, but he remained Minister of Justice in the subsequent cabinet. The following year, he formed a new cabinet, but it only lasted two months before collapsing. In 1914, when the European War broke out, Briand was Minister of Justice. In the early stages of the war, he strongly opposed the majority opinion in the National Assembly, firmly advocating resistance. Later, the National Assembly decided to order General Joffre to stop the retreating troops and fight the Germans on the Marne River, all thanks to Briand's efforts. In early 1915, he became Foreign Minister and led his comrades in forming the "Sacred Union," the cabinet vowing to make peace only after victory. The following year, as Foreign Minister, he issued the three-phrase armistice formula of "restoring to the original owner," "compensating for losses," and "providing adequate guarantees" in response to the German peace proposal. In 1917, the cabinet was dissolved, and Briand temporarily withdrew from politics. In 1921, he re-emerged to form a cabinet, still serving as Foreign Minister. During his tenure, there were two major events: the dispatch of troops to occupy the Ruhr industrial area of Germany and his personal attendance at the Washington Conference. The following year, the cabinet collapsed. Subsequently, in April 1925, he was reappointed Foreign Minister, and in November, he became Premier and Foreign Minister until March of the following year. At that time, the resolution of the war debt issue was largely due to Briand's negotiation efforts. However, Briand's greatest contributions to the country and the world were not only made during his time in power, but also during his attendance at the League of Nations. The Locarno Treaty, the Kellogg-Briand Pact, and the Young Plan for revising reparations, all prominent in world history, were largely due to Briand's efforts. In 1930, the outline of the European Federal Plan, with its high ambitions and far-reaching vision, was admired by the world. In that year, France withdrew its troops from the Rhineland ahead of schedule, largely due to Briand's efforts. When the Mukden Incident occurred last year, the League of Nations Council held a special meeting in Paris, chaired by Briand. Although the results were not obvious during the meeting, his mediation efforts were numerous. Three months ago, Briand resigned from the heavy post of Foreign Minister due to heart disease, resting at his villa, but he still did not forget politics and was still concerned about peace in the Far East. He planned to return to the political stage soon after recovering from his illness, but unexpectedly, his heart disease suddenly worsened, and he passed away, at the age of only seventy. All party and government organs, military and police schools, local public welfare organizations, nunneries, temples, ancestral halls, etc., are exempt from collection. 6. If the tenant colludes with the landlord to hide the true amount or report less rent, they will be fined one time the rent due, with the tenant and landlord each bearing half of the fine. 9. For self-owned houses, the rent should be estimated and an appropriate rent should be determined for collection, based on the size and comparison with neighboring houses. 10. Rent collection uses three-part forms, which are issued and published in the style prescribed by the Department. 11. Rent collection is supervised by the county government and the public security bureau, and the urban and rural public security sub-bureaus are responsible for the actual collection. 12. The public security bureau's handling fee for rent collection can be deducted from the rent income at a rate of 5%. The county's printing costs for receipts can be deducted at a rate of 3%. 13. The rent collection funds should be sent by the public security bureau to the county government on a monthly basis, together with the payment, and reported to the Department of Finance. Special funds are designated for special purposes and county governments are not allowed to retain them. 15. This constitution shall be promulgated and implemented by the Jiangsu Provincial Government.
