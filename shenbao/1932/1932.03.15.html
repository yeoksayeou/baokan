吾人所貢獻於國聯調查團者（一）彬

國聯調查團已於日昨到滬。我們對於此一行遠涉重洋。負有爲人類維護和平與正義之重責的來賓。謹特表示歡迎與欽敬的誠意。同時更敬致其誠摯的貢獻。

國聯調查團此來中國其目的爲調查東北。但今日船入吳淞。方踏入中國的土地。即可望見吳淞要塞上迎風飄揚着表示侵略與强横的太陽旗。慘淡而又嚴肅的陽光下。縱横狼藉着碎瓦與頽垣。戰壘宛在。彈痕猶新。由今日戰後的慘狀。即可推知一二八以後一月又二天砲火的荼毒。此種最先入目之印象。應爲調查團諸君始料所不及。

去年九一八日軍突佔東北此次又再犯上海。他們舉以宣告世界的理由。爲制止中國抗日運動。但抗日運動爲果。日本對我積極侵略激起我國民之憤恨爲因。此應爲世界人士所灼知。最近美國發生排斥日貨運動。又不是一二八日軍再犯上海後所激起的果嗎。日種惡因。乃收惡果。正如種瓜得瓜。種豆得豆。事所必然。此點在國聯所委託上海領事團調查眞相的報告書中。也曾指。出此種極顯明之因果關係。日人竟不能體認明白。恰如西哲耶穌所謂「只看見他人眼裏的刺。忘却了自已眼裏横着大樑木。」如日人能拔去自已眼中的大樑木。吾人拔去眼中的剌自然絲毫不成問題。所以日人所謂制止抗日運動。不過爲其一種飾辭。此爲我們誠摯貢獻於調查團諸君的第一點。國聯盟約第十條明白規定。有『聯合會盟員有尊重並保持所有聯合會各盟員領土之完全及現有政治上之獨立。以防禦外來侵略之義務』的條文。九國公約的原則。亦爲尊重中國領土完整。主權獨立。最近美史汀生致波拉函。也鄭重重申此義及門户開放的原則。日本今日竟悍然無所顧惜。撕碎了國聯盟約與九國公約。破壞了門户開放的原則。背棄國際信義。非特直接迫害中國。亦且是直接迫害世界。

日本對世界宣稱。謂「滿蒙成立新政權後。仍歡迎各國投資。仍保持門户開放的原則。」我們試思日本對於國際盟約旣絶不顧惜對世界輿論。亦毫無顧忌。决然破壞中國領土行政主權之完整。以求滿足其侵略的野心。以實現其所謂「國防計劃。」事實上東北已爲日本所獨占。而謂其仍將保持門户開放的原則。即三尺孩提。當亦知其不可能。所以日本一面破壞中國領土行政主權之完整。一面口頭欺騙各國。高唱門户開放。仍是違背國聯盟約與九國公約的精神。我們爲必保障中國領土行政主權之完整。而後可以言門户開放。此爲我們誠摯貢獻於調查團諸君的第二點。

What We Offer to the League of Nations Investigation Team (1) Bin

The League of Nations Investigation Team arrived in Shanghai yesterday. We extend our sincere welcome and respect to these guests who have traveled far across the oceans, bearing the heavy responsibility of upholding peace and justice for mankind. At the same time, we respectfully offer them our sincere contributions.

The purpose of the League of Nations Investigation Team's visit to China is to investigate the Northeast. However, today, as their ship entered Wusong and they set foot on Chinese soil, they could immediately see the sun flag, symbolizing aggression and tyranny, flying in the wind over the Wusong fortress. Under the bleak and solemn sunlight, shattered tiles and crumbling walls were scattered in disarray. The battlements remained, and the bullet marks were still fresh. From the tragic aftermath of today's battle, one can infer the devastation wrought by the month and two days of artillery fire after January 28th. This first impression should be something that the gentlemen of the investigation team did not expect.

Last year, on September 18th, the Japanese army suddenly occupied the Northeast, and now they have invaded Shanghai again. The reason they declared to the world was to stop the anti-Japanese movement in China. However, the anti-Japanese movement is the result, and Japan's active aggression against us, which aroused the resentment of our people, is the cause. This should be clearly understood by people around the world. Recently, the movement to boycott Japanese goods in the United States is also a result triggered by the Japanese army's invasion of Shanghai after January 28th. Planting evil causes yields evil results, just as planting melons yields melons and planting beans yields beans. This is inevitable. This point was also pointed out in the report of the Shanghai Consular Body, entrusted by the League of Nations to investigate the truth, which pointed out this very obvious cause-and-effect relationship. The Japanese were unable to understand it clearly, just like the Western philosopher Jesus said, "You see the speck in your neighbor's eye, but fail to notice the beam in your own eye." If the Japanese can remove the beam from their own eyes, then our removal of the speck from our eyes will naturally not be a problem at all. Therefore, what the Japanese call stopping the anti-Japanese movement is merely a kind of embellishment. This is the first point that we sincerely offer to the gentlemen of the investigation team. Article 10 of the League of Nations Covenant clearly stipulates that "Members of the League undertake to respect and preserve as against external aggression the territorial integrity and existing political independence of all Members of the League." The principle of the Nine-Power Treaty is also to respect China's territorial integrity and sovereign independence. Recently, U.S. Secretary of State Stimson's letter to Borah also solemnly reiterated this meaning and the principle of the Open Door Policy. Today, Japan brazenly disregards everything, tearing up the League of Nations Covenant and the Nine-Power Treaty, destroying the principle of the Open Door Policy, and betraying international trust. This is not only directly persecuting China, but also directly persecuting the world.

Japan declared to the world that "after the establishment of a new regime in Manchuria and Mongolia, it still welcomes foreign investment and maintains the principle of the Open Door Policy." Let us think about it. Since Japan absolutely disregards international covenants and has no scruples about world opinion, it resolutely destroys China's territorial and administrative sovereignty in order to satisfy its aggressive ambitions and realize its so-called "national defense plan." In fact, the Northeast has already been monopolized by Japan, and to say that it will still maintain the principle of the Open Door Policy is something that even a three-foot-tall child would know is impossible. Therefore, while destroying China's territorial and administrative sovereignty, Japan verbally deceives various countries and loudly proclaims the Open Door Policy, which still violates the spirit of the League of Nations Covenant and the Nine-Power Treaty. We believe that only by ensuring the integrity of China's territorial and administrative sovereignty can we talk about the Open Door Policy. This is the second point that we sincerely offer to the gentlemen of the investigation team.

League of Nations Investigation Team Arrived in Shanghai Last Night

The Shanghai Incident is Also Within the Scope of Investigation

A Stay of About One Week in Shanghai, or Possibly Longer

Reception for the Press at 6:00 PM Today

The League of Nations Investigation Team, including five commissioners from the United Kingdom, the United States, France, Italy, and Germany, as well as accompanying staff and experts, arrived in Shanghai on the 11th of this month aboard the President Adams of the Dollar Steamship Lines, from Japan. Originally scheduled to arrive at 4:00 PM yesterday, they were delayed by fog and wind, and finally arrived at Bailianjing, Pudong, at 7:30 PM last night. Government representatives Gu Weijun, Vice Foreign Minister Guo Taiqi, Mayor Wu Tiecheng, and Municipal Secretary-General Yu Hongjun boarded the ship to welcome them. They then accompanied the team members and others on a ferry prepared for them, arriving at the Customs Wharf at around 9:00 PM. After landing, they went directly to the Cathay Hotel on the Bund to rest. Details are recorded below:

Crowds of Welcoming Representatives

Yesterday, Chinese and foreign representatives who went to the Customs Wharf to welcome the team were extremely crowded. They arrived in succession from around 2:00 PM. Welcoming slogans were visible everywhere. Outside the iron gate of the wharf, there were hundreds of people standing and looking into the distance, all wanting to catch a glimpse of the members of the League of Nations Investigation Team, who bear the mission of peace. The reception prepared two ferries for the welcoming personnel to ride on. A portion waited at the Customs Wharf. Chinese and foreign welcoming representatives included, on the government side, representatives Gu Weijun, Secretary-General Wang Guangqi, Vice Foreign Minister Guo Taiqi, Shanghai Mayor Wu Tiecheng, Secretary-General Yu Hongjun, Zhang Xueliang's representative Zhou Tianfang, Ministry of Military Affairs representative Zhang Wen, Ministry of the Navy representative Zheng Liqing, League of Nations Chinese Secretary Wu Xiufeng, and group representatives, from the business community Yuan Ludeng, Bei Songsun, from the banking community Chen Zheqing, Xu Xinliu, from the press Dong Xianguang, from academia Li Zhaohuan, Wang Jingqi, from the Pacific Society Chen Liting, etc. On the foreign side, there were the investigation team's secretary Chapelet, advisor Dr. Young, U.S. Embassy Secretary Engert, U.S. Administrative Consul McHugh, Zhang Xueliang's advisor Dunne, Shanghai Volunteer Corps Commander Johnson, and on the Japanese side, representative to the League Aizawa Matuoka, Japanese Embassy Secretary Horiuchi.

Grand Arrival at the Port

The President Adams, the ship on which the investigation team members were traveling, entered the Wusongkou at 6:00 PM yesterday evening and arrived at Pudong Bailianjing Wharf at 7:30 PM. Representatives Gu, Vice Minister Guo, Mayor Wu, and other reception personnel, at 7:20 PM, took a special small ferry from the Customs Wharf across the river to Pudong to welcome them. Around 8:00 PM, they arrived at the Bailianjing Wharf. The Municipal Public Security Bureau, the Police Brigade, and a military band were lined up at the wharf, playing music to welcome them, with very thorough security measures. Soon, the welcoming personnel boarded the President Adams and met with the investigation commissioners in the lounge, shaking hands one by one and exchanging greetings. Dr. Gu Shaochuan introduced the welcoming personnel to the commissioners. About a quarter of an hour later, Mr. Gu led the welcoming personnel to guide the members of the team, their staff and experts ashore in succession. At that time, military music was played loudly on the pier and magnesium lights flashed as photographers competed to take pictures of the commissioners. They then boarded a small ferry across the river. The commissioners sat in the middle cabin with Gu Shaochuan, Guo Taiqi, Wu Tiecheng and others. At 9:00 PM, they arrived at the Customs Wharf. Representatives from various public groups and organizations had been waiting for a long time. When the members of the investigation team landed, they stepped forward to shake hands and greet them. Hundreds of representatives of civil groups gathered on the lawn of the Bund, all holding welcoming flags. As the team members approached, they applauded and shouted slogans. The team members stopped briefly to look at the words on the flags, and the reception staff explained them. Then they boarded cars and went to the Cathay Hotel on Nanjing Road to rest. Also, yesterday, all the shops along the roads posted slogans welcoming the members of the investigation team and respecting public law and treaties.

Japanese Representatives Arrived Together

This time, the League of Nations Investigation Team passed through Japan and stayed for a few days. The people of Japan, both in and out of the government, treated them with the utmost hospitality. Before their departure, the Japanese government specially sent 13 representatives, including the ambassador to Germany, Yoshida, to accompany the investigation team on their voyage to Shanghai. In addition to Yoshida, notable individuals included Kanzō Shiozaki, former director of the Treaty Bureau of the Japanese Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Colonel Wataru from the Army Ministry, Lieutenant Commander Sumida from the Navy Ministry, Setsuichi Aoki, Director of the Tokyo Branch of the International Association, and officials from the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Masaomi Yoshitomi and Kifune.

Sir Lytton Expresses

The chairman of the League of Nations Investigation Team, Sir Lytton of the United Kingdom, was wearing a blue coat. Although he was old, he was full of energy and amiable. A reporter from this newspaper, introduced by Dr. Gu Weijun, shook hands with the Sir on the ship and had a brief conversation. The reporter (asked): Your team passed through Japan this time and stayed for a short time. I don't know what your impression of Japan is? (Asked) How many days will you stay in Shanghai? (Answer) It is not yet decided. After consulting with all parties, the stay in Shanghai may be slightly extended, etc. At that time, there were many welcomers, and Mr. Lytton was busy. He said to the reporter, there is no time for a long conversation, I plan to meet with the press at the hotel at 6 pm tomorrow (today), and we will talk in detail at that time.

German Commissioner Speaks

The German representative, Dr. Schnee, is a well-known expert on colonial policy and has lived in the Far East for many years. A reporter met with Dr. Schnee at the Cathay Hotel last night. According to Dr. Schnee, the investigation team will stay in Shanghai for about a week, but if necessary, they may have to stay a few more days. This trip will also include the Shanghai case within the scope of the investigation. Dr. Schnee was in good spirits and had binoculars hanging on his chest, as in the photograph published in this newspaper yesterday. He asked the reporter about the year the Cathay Hotel was built and the origin of its name, which was quite interesting.
