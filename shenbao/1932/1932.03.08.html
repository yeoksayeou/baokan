The Importance of International Publicity 1

Commentators often say that today, resistance is the most effective form of external publicity. Its effect is far superior to postal and telegraphic publicity. Consider the recent Shanghai Incident. It was only after the Nineteenth Route Army fought valiantly that international public opinion suddenly changed. Isn't this clear evidence? However, during this ongoing conflict, the international situation changes rapidly. A single misstep can ruin the whole game. Publicity is absolutely indispensable today.

Political historian Sir Frederick Pollock once said, "The organization of telegraphs began after 1850, allowing governments to communicate news instantaneously at any distance and take appropriate measures. Its impact on diplomacy is enormous. Governments rely on its timely information for unified command. The task of diplomats is merely to convey information." This shows the importance modern diplomats place on publicity.

Japan, in the September 18th Incident, focused all its efforts on telegraphic publicity in diplomacy. On the one hand, it contacted European and American reporters to publish opinions unfavorable to us. On the other hand, it monopolized telecommunications, obliterating the true situation inside the Northeast. At the beginning of the incident, our representative, Mr. Shih, had not yet received detailed instructions from the government to prepare for a response. Meanwhile, the Japanese side had already used its own telecommunications to send all sorts of inaccurate facts to Geneva. As a result, the Japanese representative spoke eloquently, while our representative remained silent. During the January 28th Shanghai Incident, our representative, Mr. Yan, was initially desperate due to the lack of information, sending frequent urgent telegrams. However, the Japanese representative was again able to speak eloquently. Recently, both sides agreed to a four-hour ceasefire to allow charitable organizations to rescue refugees in the war zone. The Japanese took this opportunity to telegraph various countries, falsely accusing us of violating the ceasefire by opening fire. No rebuttal was heard from our side. Recently, the Japanese telegraphed the League of Nations, claiming that they had ceased hostilities, but in reality, they were still advancing. The League of Nations believed them, thinking that peace was in sight and immediately felt relieved. The atmosphere immediately eased. This shows the significant impact of international publicity and also shows that the Japanese publicity efforts are always ahead of us, while our publicity efforts are always lagging behind. When the International Investigation Team arrives in Tokyo, the Japanese government and various sectors will exhaust all their publicity efforts. When the Investigation Team comes to Shanghai in the future, how will various aspects of our country respond?

Central Government Determined to Resist for the Long Term 2

All armed comrades shall persevere and coordinate. The Military Affairs Commission will establish a special chairman.

Second Plenary Session of the Central Executive Committee Communiqué

Long-term resistance is the firm resolution; all hardships will be faced.

To all generals, including the heads of the Pacification Bureaus, commanders-in-chief, division and brigade commanders, and through them to all armed comrades: Japan is using its violence to invade and occupy the Northeast, extending to the Southeast. At this critical moment of survival, we have only one option besides surrendering to violence: active resistance. In the past, Yuan Shikai, due to surrendering to violence, signed the Twenty-One Demands, losing sovereignty and humiliating the nation, the consequences of which continue to this day. From this, it is clear that seeking peace through humiliation is counterproductive. We absolutely do not want to gamble the country's fate, but according to national history, a country that perishes through bloodshed can be revived, while a country that perishes without bloodshed will never be revived. Therefore, since September 18th of last year, we have repeatedly reminded our armed comrades throughout the country to take safeguarding against foreign aggression as their greatest duty. Recently, the Nineteenth Route Army in the Shanghai area, under orders to defend its territory, has fought with all its might, repeatedly repelling strong enemies. The Fifth Army, under orders to provide assistance, has fought alongside them. They have fought bitterly against the land, sea, and air forces of the brutal Japanese for over thirty days, inflicting excessive casualties. Although they have changed their positions due to strategic considerations, their elite troops have not been weakened. If all our armed comrades throughout the country can be as determined as the Nineteenth Route Army in defending their territory and as courageous as the Fifth Army in providing assistance, then the brutality of the Japanese, however rampant, will be contained. The central government is determined to resist for the long term and will not avoid any hardships. We hope that all our armed comrades throughout the country will heed this message. Treat the central government as hands and feet protect the head and eyes, and treat all localities as if their pain is our own. When the enemy's atmosphere arrives, immediately rise up to resist. When a call for troops is made, depart on schedule. Do not focus on defending territories or preserving strength. If all our armed comrades, numbering over a million, hold the common determination to live and die together and to persevere with violence, how can foreign aggression enter, and the nation will have a chance to revive. Let everyone strive for this. Seal of the Second Plenary Session of the Central Executive Committee

Reply to Kong Xiangxi's Telegram from Jiangxi

The government is determined to prepare for resistance against Japan.

The Military Affairs Commission will establish a chairman to oversee military orders.

Our army is changing strategies and temporarily withdrawing from Shanghai. Central Committee member Kong Xiangxi sent a telegram from Jiangxi to the Second Plenary Session of the Central Executive Committee, requesting that a comprehensive self-defense plan be quickly established to facilitate military operations. Yesterday, he received a reply from the plenary session, which stated, "To Mr. Kong Xiangxi, Honorable Sir: Confidential. We have received your telegram from Jiangxi, stating that since our army has retreated to the second line of defense, the people of Shanghai blame the government for lacking prior preparation and failing to provide timely reinforcements. Some say that during this military emergency, there should be a specialized commissioner responsible for leading the military affairs of the entire country. The people's激昂 sentiment is well understood, but all criticisms and the government's actual measures can be used for reference, but they often do not correspond to the facts. Since January, the government initially dispatched the Jiangsu and Zhejiang garrison troops to Shanghai, followed by the Henan and Jiangxi garrison troops, who proceeded day and night. However, the Japanese used maritime transport, while our army had to cross the river and travel on foot, so they could not be as fast. This is due to transportation issues and is extremely difficult to resolve. As for the preparation for resistance against Japan, the government is determined to resist for the long term. The frontline soldiers are also extremely brave. You, sir, are in Shanghai and can witness the facts, which can be explained. As for the fact that military orders are not centralized during this critical period, which is indeed inconvenient, the Second Plenary Session has decided to establish a chairman in the Military Affairs Commission to oversee military orders. We are informing you specifically and ask you to use various methods to explain this to all sectors to dispel misunderstandings.
