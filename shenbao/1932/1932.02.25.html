驕傲是［勝利］或［成功］的死敵在軍事上驕傲更其是一個可怕的名詞因爲［驕者」歷史上告訴我們是没有不敗的

當日軍於一月二十八晚决定向我開放第一顆進攻的槍彈之先在他們的心目中何嘗承認中國有半個敢於抵抗的男兒所以鹽澤敢於向駐滬各國領事發出［四小時内必可全部解决華軍］的大話而且似乎對於這種大話的實現毫無疑慮一種驕悍的形態盎然可掬果然在［驕者必敗］的鐵律之下戰幕揭開日軍便很輕易地獲得丁幾次的挫敗

日駐美大使出淵於訪晤史汀生之後曾對美聯社記者說［野村海軍中將現赴上海與美艦隊總司令台納少將作球戲兩領袖作球戲時兩軍亦必作球戲］最近鹽澤和野村失敗後日本軍閥還說［十九路軍因爲向來不知道大日本帝國皇軍的威嚴才敢抵抗的這種驕而且慢視兵兇戰危爲兒戲事的氣槪竟與左傳上所記楚子玉使門勃向晋侯請戰說［請與君之士戲君憑軾而觀之得臣與寓目焉］的故事逼肖日人狃於淸日日俄兩次戰爭的勝利趾高氣揚凌視世界遠航攻我志在必勝但終於］易司令屢戰屢北所謂［四小時必全部解决華軍的豪語竟成爲］句使世人齒冷的笑話這正是［驕者必敗］的一個近例一個眼前的實證

然而這事實對於我們却正是一種寳貴的教訓這教訓我們應該鄭重接受目前我們雖說獲得了幾次勝利但我們切忌因此而驕肆我們科學的軍備與國力都遠不如人所可恃的只有［公理］［壯氣］與［熱血］我們應當以臨深履薄而又沉着刻苦的態度準備長時間的更艱苦的血戰［持其志毋暴其氣］當［橫暴］已陳尸於［公理］的足前的時候我們才能破口唱出［凱旋之歌］

The Arrogant Will Surely Fail 1

Bin

Arrogance is the mortal enemy of [victory] or [success]. In military affairs, arrogance is an even more terrifying term, because history tells us that [the arrogant] never prevail.

When the Japanese army decided to launch the first offensive shot against us on the night of January 28th, did they ever acknowledge that China had even half a man who dared to resist? That is why Shiozawa dared to issue a boast to the consuls of various countries stationed in Shanghai, saying that "the Chinese army will surely be completely resolved within four hours." Moreover, he seemed to have no doubts about the realization of this boast, displaying a very arrogant demeanor. As expected, under the iron law of "the arrogant will surely fail," the curtain of war was raised, and the Japanese army was easily defeated several times.

After meeting with Stimson, the Japanese ambassador to the United States, Debuchi, told reporters from the Associated Press, "Vice Admiral Nomura is now going to Shanghai to play ball with Rear Admiral Tayner, Commander-in-Chief of the U.S. Fleet. When the two leaders play ball, the two armies will also play ball." Recently, after the failures of Shiozawa and Nomura, Japanese warlords even said, "The 19th Route Army dares to resist because they have never known the majesty of the Imperial Japanese Army." This kind of arrogance and disregard for the perilous nature of war as child's play is strikingly similar to the story recorded in the Zuo Zhuan, where Chu Ziyu sent Men Bo to the Marquis of Jin to request battle, saying, "Please play with your gentlemen, and let the Marquis observe them by leaning on the carriage bar. Let De Chen and I feast our eyes on them." The Japanese, emboldened by their victories in the Sino-Japanese and Russo-Japanese wars, held their heads high and looked down on the world, setting sail to attack us with the ambition of certain victory. But in the end,] Commander Yi was repeatedly defeated. The so-called "four hours to completely resolve the Chinese army" boast became a laughingstock that made the world sneer. This is a recent example, an immediate and practical demonstration, of "the arrogant will surely fail."

However, this fact is precisely a valuable lesson for us. We should solemnly accept this lesson. Although we have achieved several victories, we must avoid becoming arrogant and unrestrained because of them. Our scientific military equipment and national strength are far inferior to those of others. The only things we can rely on are [justice], [courage], and [ardor]. We should prepare for a long and arduous bloody battle with a cautious and painstaking attitude, [maintain our determination and not be impulsive]. Only when [tyranny] has been laid to rest at the feet of [justice] can we burst out singing the [song of triumph].

Our countrymen must remember that [the arrogant will surely fail]. The arduous struggle has only just begun. Now is not the time for us to sing the song of triumph.

Enemy Regimental Commander Killed 4

The Japanese army launched a general attack on Miaohang. Since the day before yesterday, our army has launched a fierce attack from Zhuqiaotou, and the Japanese army gradually retreated south to Tangjiazai, then to Jinmuzai Houjia Wooden Bridge. More than 2,000 remaining troops were surrounded there. At that time, most of the enemy troops had retreated far south of Houjia Wooden Bridge by ten miles. Upon hearing the alert, they gathered reinforcements and launched a counterattack with all their strength. Our army sent a detachment to meet them, separating the remaining enemy troops into two places. The intensity of the fierce battle was unprecedented in the Sino-Japanese Shanghai War. The Japanese army repeatedly attempted to launch a fierce charge, but was ultimately unable to succeed. From the night before last, they have been retreating step by step. The solution of the remaining troops is only a matter of time. Unexpectedly, 5,000 Japanese reinforcements who came to Shanghai had landed at Zhanghuabang and immediately separated to the front line and joined the battle. With this new force, the Japanese army immediately launched a counterattack with more than 40 tanks and divided into groups to harass our left and right wings. At this time, the number of front-line troops stationed by us was less than 5,000, facing more than 30,000 Japanese troops, and they had been fighting hard for dozens of hours, so they were contacted by the Japanese army, and more than 1,000 of the surrounded remnants broke out. At this time, although our soldiers were extremely tired, everyone was determined to die, so they still rushed to kill. One thousand encircled enemy troops were immediately annihilated, and the enemy troops were forced to retreat. They retreated more than ten miles south from Houjia Bridge. In this battle, our army fought more with less. Around Houjia Bridge, Japanese corpses piled up like mountains. Dozens of high-ranking enemy officers were also killed in battle. Yesterday, when our army was burying the remaining bones, they found that Baihai Shinano, the commander of the Second Regiment of the Ninth Division of the enemy, was also among the dead. This shows the intensity of the battle. Our army captured 24 enemy machine guns, more than 1,000 rifles, dozens of small steel cannons, and countless other spoils of war.

Enemy Retreats from Heijiazhai 5

After more than 5,000 enemy reinforcements landed at Zhanghuabang the night before last, in addition to sending a portion to help the remaining enemy in the Miaohang area, at about 5:00 yesterday morning, another thousand people were secretly dispatched from Zhanghuabang, hoping to reach Houjia Bridge from Heijiazhai, intending to harass our left wing and rescue the enemy. At that time, our army did not make a sound, allowing the enemy troops to go deep. However, when most of the enemy troops were about to arrive at Heijiazhai, our army jumped up and attacked their backs, inflicting a heavy blow on them. At that time, the garrison west of our Heijiazhai had also heard the alarm and rushed to participate in the battle, so the enemy was attacked from both sides. After a fierce battle that lasted for an hour and a half, more than half of the enemy troops were killed or injured, and they fled from Wen Dao.

105 Guns Obtained 6

At about 8:00 the night before last, more than 40 enemies, under the cover of intense artillery fire, attacked a certain village northeast of our Jiangwan Town. After our army fought bravely, more than 100 enemies were killed and more than 105 rifles were seized. The remaining enemies all collapsed and retreated.

Recovering Four Offices in the Fifth District 7

According to a report from the Municipal Public Security Bureau, our army had previously abandoned the area of the Fourth Branch of the Fifth District of the Yinxianggang Public Security Bureau due to strategic considerations, which had been occupied by the Japanese army for a long time. No Fierce Fighting Yesterday 8

After the enemy surrounded in Jinmuzai was completely resolved by our army at one o'clock yesterday morning, the enemy was exhausted, so there was no fierce fighting in Jiangwan yesterday afternoon. The enemy was based south of the Jiangwan Racecourse, and we were in the north. The enemy completely changed to a defensive position, and there were only skirmishes between sentries, with occasional intermittent gunshots and artillery fire. Also, the Da Mei Evening News reported that the Jiangwan front line was very quiet today (24th). Since the Japanese army's general offensive began on Saturday morning, the sound of artillery fire had been almost continuous for several days. Today, it was silent, and even the sound of machine guns and rifles was no longer heard. It is probably that both the Chinese and Japanese armies have been fighting fiercely for four days and need to rest. The commanders of both the Chinese and Japanese sides on the Jiangwan front admitted that there was no change today compared to yesterday.
