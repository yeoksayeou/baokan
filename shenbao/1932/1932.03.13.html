救濟上海敎育問題彬 1

一二八禍變爆發。上海頓淪爲砲火連天之戰塲。在慘酷之破壞與迫害之下。商離其肆工失其業。皆陷於停頓。而其最鉅大至於無可補償之損失。則尤推敎會

上海邇年學校林立。敎育頗爲發達。　且以商業中心而兼文化中心。此次禍變突發。而學校區域之吳淞江灣眞茹又適爲戰地。在日軍砲大轟炸之下。經多年慘淡經營之同濟大學。中國公學。復旦大學勞働大學曁南大學。持志學院中央大學醫學院。商學院上海法學院。水產學校商船學校。等或全部被燬。或菁華盡喪。即幸獲完整者。亦無法開學。其他中學小學。更復不少。直至目前。除曁南大學勞働大學中國公學持志學院上海法學院等三數校。巳遷地設法開學而外大多數學校。仍陷於停頓狀態。各地靑年因而失學者。爲數正不知若干。此種敎育上之損失實至足令吾人痛心。

吾人認爲此種現象。絶不容延長。此種損失絕不容增鉅。負有敎育之重責者。應亟以創造不精神從事恢復。如其不能則亦亟應另覓校舍。另遷善地。如各國立大學此時正宜遷往南京就各部院寛敝之房屋借用籌備開學俾靑年不至長時陷於失學之悲境。

至於各私立大學。吾人則尤望其能趁此時機。以合作之精神。集中力量。　辦一大規模之大學。蓋過去大學。雖林林總。總蔚爲大覩。但大都設備不同。規模狹隘。人力財力。兩感不敷。彼校旣附設一商科。而此處則又專設一商學院。彼校旣附設一法科而此處則又專設一法學院。性質旣同。而以力量分散。收效極微。何如破除門戶之見。通力合作。各盡其力。創辦一大規模之大學。此不僅爲嘉惠靑年而巳。其於敎育與文化前途裨益尤大

吾人認爲上海爲商業之中心。習旣奢靡。尤尙虚華此種環境。久不宜於心性未定之靑年。換言之。即上海根本不宜建設爲文化之中心。吾人切盼各學校趁此時機。遷往内地。不必麕集於此繁華奢靡之商埠。以養成多數　縱虚浮之靑年。

至我政府。在此時期吾人亦深盻其毌忘却此一嚴重問題。或託言非常時期。以財政困難。此問題無法談到。敎育爲立國之大本。敎育破產國家之前途。乃眞不堪設想。今日我國家整個敎育。以敎育方針之不確立。救育行政之紊亂敎育經費之扣發。實際巳瀕於破產。固不止上海一隅而巳。再不力謀挽救。我國今後。果尙有敎育之可言耶。

Rescuing the Shanghai Education Problem 1

The January 28th Incident erupted, and Shanghai was instantly plunged into a battlefield of relentless artillery fire. Amidst the brutal destruction and persecution, businesses were abandoned, and workers lost their jobs, everything grinding to a halt. However, the most immense and irreparable loss was undoubtedly suffered by education.

Shanghai had seen a proliferation of schools in recent years, and education had flourished. Moreover, as a commercial center, it was also a cultural hub. The sudden outbreak of this incident and the fact that the school districts of Wusong, Jiangwan, and Zhenru were precisely the battle zones meant that, under the massive bombardment by Japanese forces, Tongji University, China Public School, Fudan University, Labor University, Jinan University, Chi Chih College, Central University Medical College, Business College, Shanghai Law College, Fisheries School, Merchant Marine School, and others, which had been painstakingly developed over many years, were either completely destroyed or had their essence eradicated. Even those that luckily remained intact were unable to open. A considerable number of other middle and primary schools were also affected. As of now, except for a few schools like Jinan University, Labor University, China Public School, Chi Chih College, and Shanghai Law College, which have relocated and managed to resume classes, the majority of schools remain suspended. The number of young people who have lost access to education in various places is unknown. This kind of educational loss is truly heartbreaking.

We believe that this situation should not be prolonged, and this loss should not be amplified. Those who bear the heavy responsibility of education should urgently dedicate themselves to recovery with a spirit of creation. If they cannot, they should quickly find alternative school buildings and relocate to better locations. For example, national universities should seize this opportunity to move to Nanjing and borrow spacious buildings from various ministries to prepare for reopening, so that young people do not remain in the tragic state of being out of school for an extended period.

As for private universities, we especially hope that they can take advantage of this opportunity, with a spirit of cooperation, to concentrate their efforts and establish a large-scale university. Although there were numerous universities in the past, they could not be considered significant overall. Most had different facilities and were limited in scale, with both human and financial resources falling short. If one school already has a business department, another establishes a dedicated business college. If one school already has a law department, another establishes a dedicated law college. Since the nature is the same, yet the resources are scattered, the effect is minimal. Why not break down sectarian barriers, cooperate wholeheartedly, each doing their best, and establish a large-scale university?
