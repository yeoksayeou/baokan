告今日從事政治運動者㈠彬 1

處今日國難重重局面之下。國人本「天下興亡。匹夫有責」之義。於是努力從事於所謂政治運動者。乃應時而起。吾人就當前之事實而試予分析。則可知今日之從事政治運動者。雖其形式上皆以共赴國難爲出發點。但各有其立塲與本質。槪分述如次。

一曰舊軍閥官僚政客也。自國民黨底定全國以後。一部分投機之舊軍閥官僚政客。依然度其政治上優越之生涯。寖且儼然爲現政府之要角。惟尚有一部分迄今不得於現政府。失意枯坐。久已滿懷抑鬱。際此時會。乃視爲不可

復得之機。而羣起活動。昭示國民之主張。固無不日以國家與人民之利益爲前提。集其臭味相投之類。組織政黨。企圖捲土重來。向國民黨問鼎之輕重。以分得一杯政治之美羮。

吾人認爲國民有聞問政治之神聖權力。固不容少数人所把持壟斷。昔日之軍閥官僚政客。亦爲今日之國民。故從事於政治活動。吾人固不能謂其非是。然而今日民衆之所耍求。不在口頭上之主張。亦不在紙頭上之宣言。而在於救國之誠意。政治之臧否。視其合乎人民之利益興否而定論。民視最明。民聽最聰。能眞實代表人民利益者。乃眞能博得人民之擁護。否則甜言蜜語。雖能掩蔽人民之聰明於一時。終當爲其偉力所粉碎。過往史蹟歷歷具在。即如國民黨當北伐之始。以其眞能代表人民之利益。人民乃簞食壺漿以迎。備受熱烈之擁護。乃不久軍閥政治官僚政治復興。政治日趨於黑暗。自然漸失人民之信仰。吾人敢問今日之舊軍閥官僚政客。果有絕大之覺悟乎。昔日之頭腦果能接受時代思潮之洗禮乎。其活動之動機。果眞爲解除人民之困厄乎。其主張。其行動。果能以大衆之利益爲依歸乎。如其不能。則其結果其前途究如何。歷史未必許一再復演其舊劇。吾人敢正告今日從事政治活動之舊軍閥官僚政客者。愼勿以爲「混水中好摸魚。」人民長可欺罔。而日爲自己掘墳墓也。覆轍未遠。曷速回頭。

A Word to Today's Political Activists (I) Bin 1

Amidst the current situation of profound national crisis, our countrymen, adhering to the principle of "everyone is responsible for the fate of the nation," are striving to engage in what is called political movements. This has arisen in response to the times. If we analyze the current situation, we can see that although all those engaged in political movements today take jointly overcoming the national crisis as their starting point, they each have their own stance and nature. A general description is as follows:

First, there are the old warlords, bureaucrats, and politicians. After the Kuomintang established national stability, some opportunistic old warlords, bureaucrats, and politicians continued their privileged political lives, and even became important figures in the current government. However, there are still some who have not been successful under the current government, sitting idly in disappointment, filled with resentment for a long time. Seizing this opportunity, they regard it as an opportunity not to be missed and rise up to take action. The propositions they proclaim to the people invariably take the interests of the nation and the people as their premise. They gather those of like mind, organize political parties, and attempt to make a comeback, questioning the Kuomintang's weight, in order to obtain a share of the political pie.

We believe that the people have the sacred right to be involved in politics, which should not be monopolized by a few. The former warlords, bureaucrats, and politicians are also citizens today, so we cannot say that it is wrong for them to engage in political activities. However, what the people demand today is not verbal propositions or declarations on paper, but sincerity in saving the country. The merits of politics are determined by whether they conform to the interests of the people. The people's eyes are sharp, and their ears are keen. Those who can truly represent the interests of the people can truly win the support of the people. Otherwise, sweet words may be able to deceive the people's intelligence for a time, but will eventually be shattered by their great power. The historical records of the past are vividly present. Just as when the Kuomintang began the Northern Expedition, because it could truly represent the interests of the people, the people welcomed them with food and drink and gave them enthusiastic support. However, soon the warlord politics and bureaucratic politics revived, and politics became increasingly dark, naturally gradually losing the people's faith. We dare to ask, do today's old warlords, bureaucrats, and politicians have a great awakening? Have their past minds been able to accept the baptism of the trends of the times? Is the motivation for their activities truly to relieve the people's distress? Are their propositions and actions based on the interests of the masses? If not, what will be the result and the future? History may not allow the old drama to be repeated again. We dare to solemnly tell the old warlords, bureaucrats, and politicians engaged in political activities today: Be careful not to think that "it is good to fish in troubled waters." The people can be deceived forever, and they are digging their own graves day by day. The past is not far away, why not turn back quickly.
