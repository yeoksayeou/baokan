國民爲何不問政治彬

政治與人民之關聯。及人民必應過問政治之一問題。在二十世紀之今日。殆已毫無討論之餘地然而以我國文化推進之停滯。一般人民政治水平線之低下。此一不必討論之問題。今乃仍不得不提出而詳加闡述。并以喚起社會大衆之注意。此誠爲最足令人痛心之一現象。

今日我國家固昭然號稱「民主之國」也。「民主政治」「民主政府」之呼。聲固亦洋盈耳也然而按其實際。則大多數人民之思想與頭腦。仍爲「不識不知順帝之則」之觀念所支配對於政治。視爲與自身毫無關聯。置之不聞不問。年遭飢饉歳逢兵亂即顚沛流離。生無死所。亦不知其爲何而致此。而但謂爲「天意」與「命運」之必然。大多文化程度落後之工農羣衆。固無論矣。即一般知識份子。亦類都抱「不在其位不謀其政」之觀念對於政治取袖手旁觀之態度。其他如好爲立異鳴高者。則更視政治爲齷齪不可親近之途。更避之如恐不及有此不負責之國民而後政治乃能由少數之軍閥官僚政客所壟斷把持。寖且視爲大多數人民所不能不能問。軍閥政治與官僚政治形成。政治乃與大衆之利益日益背道而馳。故致使政治爲少數人所操縱把持。據爲私有。即由於吾人之不問政治。換言之。即由吾人之不負責任所造。成　　吾人常聞致力實業者。高呼「實業救國」矣。亦常聞致力敎育者。高呼「敎育救國」矣。但假如政治不上軌道。飢饉連年。民生凋敝苛捐雜稅。搜括無厭。官盡貪汚。吏皆狡詐。而又内亂頻仍。匪盗遍地。則所謂實業救國。果其有着手之餘地耶。所謂「敎育救國。」又果有着手之餘地耶。故捨政治而言救國。是誠有若緣木而求魚此爲最淺近最明顯易曉之理論。奈之何我國人。覚至今仍茫然罔覺耶

夫吾人一日有生。即一日不能離開政治。抑且吾人之行動。直接開接即皆足以影響政治。而同時亦受政治之影響。吾爲人類社會之一份子。食焉息焉與社會之關係。何等密切。我旣不能離社會而生存。即不能置社會之事於不聞不問。抑且我握有絕對可以聞問政治之神聖權力。任何人不能侵犯。正如我爲一公司之股東。握有絕對可以聞問公司一切事務之權力。此種譬喩。蓋至爲恰當。吾人於投有資本之公司中一切事務。則毎事必問。甚且不惜怒諸言。厲諸色。與人齗齗以爭。奈之何對於一國之事。爲吾人生所依賴者。竞漠然不肯一顧耶。

以我人之自棄其神聖之權力。致政治於不聞不問。坐令少數人壟斷把持。寖成今日之黑暗。外無以禦侮。内無以救災。國脈民命。不絕如縷。其咎豈僅在於少數之官僚政客與軍閥。

時危勢迫。國人今後亟應有政治之自覺。宜切實負起自身應負之責任。並運用其神聖之偉力。以裁抑軍閥政治與官僚政治。樹立眞正之民主政治。建設眞正之民主政府。如此而後中國乃能爭取光明之前途。

Why Don't Citizens Inquire About Politics?

The connection between politics and the people, and the issue of whether the people should necessarily be concerned with politics, is something that hardly needs discussion in this 20th century. However, given the stagnation of cultural advancement in our country and the generally low political awareness of the people, this issue, which should not need discussion, must still be raised and thoroughly explained, in order to awaken the attention of the public. This is truly a most distressing phenomenon.

Today, our country openly proclaims itself to be a "democratic nation." The cries of "democratic politics" and "democratic government" also fill our ears. However, in reality, the thoughts and minds of the majority of the people are still dominated by the concept of "ignorant and unknowing, following the laws of the Emperor." They regard politics as having nothing to do with themselves, ignoring it and not inquiring about it. When they encounter famine years or suffer the chaos of war, they are displaced and have nowhere to die, yet they do not know why they are in this state. They only attribute it to "Heaven's will" and the inevitability of "fate." This is certainly the case with the majority of the culturally backward masses of workers and peasants. Even the general intellectuals mostly hold the view of "not planning policies when not in office," adopting a hands-off attitude towards politics. Others, who like to be unconventional and declare themselves superior, view politics as a dirty and unapproachable path, avoiding it as if they fear it. It is only with such irresponsible citizens that politics can be monopolized and controlled by a small number of warlords, bureaucrats, and politicians, and even be regarded as something that the majority of the people cannot inquire about. The formation of warlord politics and bureaucratic politics causes politics to increasingly deviate from the interests of the masses. Therefore, politics is manipulated and controlled by a few people and treated as private property. This is due to our failure to inquire about politics. In other words, it is created by our irresponsibility. We often hear those who devote themselves to industry shout "Industry Saves the Nation!" We also often hear those who devote themselves to education shout "Education Saves the Nation!" But if politics is not on the right track, with years of famine, economic decline, excessive taxes and levies, insatiable exploitation, officials all corrupt, clerks all cunning, and with frequent internal strife and bandits everywhere, is there any room for so-called "Industry Saves the Nation" to take action? Is there any room for so-called "Education Saves the Nation" to take action? Therefore, to talk about saving the nation without politics is truly like climbing a tree to catch fish. This is the most basic, most obvious, and easily understood theory. Why is it that the people of our country still remain ignorant and unaware?

As long as we live, we cannot be separated from politics for a single day. Moreover, our actions directly or indirectly affect politics, and at the same time, we are also affected by politics. We are a part of human society, eating and breathing in it, and our relationship with society is extremely close. Since we cannot live apart from society, we cannot ignore the affairs of society. Moreover, we hold the sacred power to inquire about politics, which no one can violate. Just as we are shareholders of a company, holding the absolute power to inquire about all the company's affairs. This analogy is very appropriate. We must inquire about everything in a company in which we have invested capital, and even angrily argue and quarrel with others. Why is it that we are indifferent and unwilling to even glance at the affairs of a country, on which our lives depend?

By abandoning our sacred power and ignoring politics, allowing a small number of people to monopolize and control it, we have gradually created the darkness of today. We have no way to defend ourselves from foreign aggression, nor to save ourselves from internal disasters. The nation's pulse and the people's lives are hanging by a thread.
