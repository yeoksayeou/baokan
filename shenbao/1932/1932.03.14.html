敬告國聯調查團翰

國聯調查團。將於今日抵滬矣。團中人跋涉重洋。長途僕。僕蓋負國聯所付託之重要使命而來也。

自九一八禍變以來。日本侵略之範圍愈益擴大形勢亦愈嚴重。憶一月二十一日白里安曾通告行政院諸委員。謂「調查團開始工作時之狀態。巳與决議案日所期望者迴異。」詎料遷延至今。形勢更非昔比。日本侵佔東北未巳。更繼以東南禍變。今日調查團抵滬。目覩吳淞一帶。片片皆瓦爍塲。瘡痍滿目。試與歐戰中比知利時之悲哀相映照。不知調查團之感想何如。

國聯調查團之組織。依據去年十二月十日行政院之决議第五欵及第六欵就决議案條文略加研究。益以白里安之宣言可知調查團之任務至爲廣泛。當九一八事變之初。我施代表早有撤兵與調查兩提議薛西爾爵士亦嘗極力斡旋。催促芳澤容納而芳澤於撤兵。則佯爲允諾於調查則嚴詞拒絕洎乎第三次會議開會於巴黎。日本軍隊非但一兵未撤抑且變本加厲佔錦州逼熱河東北全部。均落日本之手斯時日本反提出調查團之建議其用心所在固不難預測也。

惟調查團之任務。爲國聯所付託。旣非代表某一國家更非代表某一方面。其立塲完全以國聯盟約爲基礎其行動惟國聯訓令是聽。盟約之根本精神。爲會員國間之平等待遇昔一九二五年布希糾紛。白里安曾堅决宣言。「國際聯盟之組織份子。爲强弱不一。大小不等之國家。但其待遇。一律平等。各享共同之正義而無分軒輊。」白氏斯言。對盟約之根本精神。解釋最爲透切依據斯旨。則日本屢謂我中國無組織不配受國聯待遇等之宣傳。巳不攻自破。

去年十二月二十五日。團員希德博士宣言「當悉心傾聽雙方陳述。以坦白態。度報告眞相。以平等公平之基礎。爲工作應當遵守之原則。」調查團在東京宣言。亦欲以「第三者之立塲。持公平之態度。」我人希望調查團。始終保持此公正的立塲。

其次國聯以會員國相互尊重「領土完整政治獨立」爲最高原則。最近西班牙代表蘇魯太在大會宣稱。「盟約第十條爲國聯之最高憲章」歷次行政院决議。亦曾再三致意二月十七日行政院致日申請書。對斯原則。尤明白表示。「凡不顧本條文而侵犯任何會員國之土地完整與變更其政治獨立者。其餘會員國皆不得認爲有效」蓋會員國若不能履行第十條之國際義務。則國際和平。即無從維持。日本始終揚言對我無土地與政治野心。而其破壞盟約之種種行爲。則早爲列國所公認。誠如瑞士代表摩太之言。「敵對行爲之發作。巳越過第十五條所載偶然事故之外。」惜乎盟約國對於解决國際糾紛之手段。非常緩慢。致成今日僵局。則今日調查團。宜如何於尊重最高憲章之精神下。迅行其調查工作。以完成此法律的原則也。

調查團之立塲與其工作之原則旣皆有法理之根據。其所調查之事實。自當與法理相符合苟事實與法律不相符合。則調查之記錄。將等於尋常之遊記。行政院决議明白規定。「就地研究任何情形。影響國際關係而有擾亂中日兩國和平者」則調查團所應搜集之事實。爲影響國際關係與擾亂中日和平之事實。亦即違反國聯盟約破壞國際和平之事實。惟此項事實。乃爲法律的事實。調查團若欲以第　者之立塲。持公平之態度。着手調查。則其所應注意者。亦惟斯法律的事實而巳。除此以外。調查團更有何求。

今調查團將往東北開始其工作矣吾氏族含苦忍痛以迄於今。自始即遵照盟約。信任國聯以愛好和平之切。希望國聯之殷。故責備國聯亦愈嚴。揆諸巳往。不得不深責國聯之缺乏果斷。坐視事態之日益擴大。調查團其將起而圖亡羊之補牢乎。我人姑拭目以視之。

1932.03.14

Announcement to the League of Nations Investigation Team

The League of Nations Investigation Team will arrive in Shanghai today. The members of the team have travelled across vast oceans, enduring a long and arduous journey. They come bearing the significant mission entrusted to them by the League of Nations.

Since the September 18th Incident, the scope of Japanese aggression has been expanding, and the situation has become increasingly serious. It is recalled that on January 21st, Aristide Briand informed the members of the Administrative Council that "the state in which the investigation team begins its work differs greatly from what was expected on the day of the resolution." Unexpectedly, after delays until today, the situation is even more different than before. Japan has not ceased its occupation of the Northeast; it has now continued with disasters in the Southeast. Today, as the investigation team arrives in Shanghai, they see in the Wusong area, pieces upon pieces of shattered tiles and debris, a scene full of devastation. Comparing this to the sorrow of Belgium during the European war, one wonders what the feelings of the investigation team will be.

The organization of the League of Nations Investigation Team is based on the fifth and sixth clauses of the resolution of the Administrative Council on December 10th of last year. After slightly studying the clauses of the resolution, supplemented by Briand's declaration, it is clear that the mission of the investigation team is extremely broad. At the beginning of the September 18th Incident, our representative Shi Zhao had already proposed both withdrawal of troops and investigation. Sir Eric Drummond also made great efforts to mediate, urging Yoshizawa Kenkichi to accept, but Yoshizawa pretended to agree to the troop withdrawal while sternly rejecting the investigation. When the third meeting was held in Paris, not only had the Japanese troops not withdrawn a single soldier, but they had intensified their actions, occupying Jinzhou and forcing Rehe, and the entire Northeast fell into Japanese hands. At that time, Japan turned around and proposed the establishment of an investigation team, and their intentions were not difficult to foresee.

However, the mission of the investigation team is entrusted by the League of Nations. It does not represent a certain country, nor does it represent a certain side. Its position is entirely based on the Covenant of the League of Nations, and its actions only follow the instructions of the League of Nations. The fundamental spirit of the Covenant is the equal treatment of member states. In the Bush-Greece dispute of 1925, Aristide Briand firmly declared, "The members of the League of Nations are countries of different strengths and sizes, but their treatment is uniformly equal. Each enjoys common justice without discrimination." Briand's words provide the most thorough explanation of the fundamental spirit of the Covenant. According to this principle, Japan's repeated propaganda that China is disorganized and not qualified to receive League of Nations treatment is self-defeating.

On December 25th of last year, team member Dr. Heinrich Schnee declared, "We will listen carefully to the statements of both sides, report the truth with an open attitude, and take equality and fairness as the principles to be followed in our work." The investigation team's declaration in Tokyo also stated that they wanted to "take the position of a third party and maintain a fair attitude." We hope that the investigation team will maintain this fair position throughout.

Secondly, the League of Nations takes "respect for the territorial integrity and political independence" of member states as its highest principle. Recently, Spanish representative Salvador de Madariaga declared at the General Assembly, "Article 10 of the Covenant is the highest charter of the League of Nations." Previous resolutions of the Administrative Council have also repeatedly emphasized this. In its application to Japan on February 17th, the Administrative Council clearly stated this principle: "If any member state disregards this article and infringes upon the territorial integrity of any member state or changes its political independence, the other member states shall not recognize it as valid." If member states cannot fulfill the international obligations of Article 10, then international peace cannot be maintained. Japan has consistently claimed that it has no territorial or political ambitions towards China, but its various actions that violate the Covenant have long been recognized by other nations. As Swiss representative Motta said, "The outbreak of hostile acts has gone beyond the accidental events described in Article 15." It is regrettable that the means by which the Covenant countries resolve international disputes are very slow, leading to the current stalemate. Therefore, today's investigation team should quickly carry out its investigation work under the spirit of respecting the highest charter to complete this legal principle.

Since the position of the investigation team and the principles of its work are all based on legal principles, the facts it investigates should naturally be consistent with legal principles.
