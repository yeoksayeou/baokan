誰負訓練靑年的責任彬

自日前「救亡與訓練靑年」一評發表後。記者曾數接靑年來函質疑。所質之點。大概　　「誰爲我們的模範。」與「誰配訓練我們兩問題。」記者於此。更廻憶去年此時旅行東北。在東北大學講演講畢。忽有學生發問曰。「中外歷史上之偉人。足爲我輩靑年模楷者固多。但今日在我國内。在我東北。政治上之領袖人物。究有誰人足以爲我輩之模楷。」記者沉思默念。竟久久無以對。檢視今日靑年質疑之函。廻憶在東北大學講演時之舊事。深感「誰爲我們的模範。」與「誰配訓練我們」兩問題。誠爲當前敎育上之絕大問題。吾人日責靑年好亂成性。華不務實。驕縱因循。而竟不一反省所責望於靑年者。自身是否曾已一一實踐。更深感敎育靑年。不在於死的書本。不在於死的模範。而在於活的現實。活的標本。舉凡政治上社會上之形形色色。以及政治領袖與社會領袖之行爲。皆爲靑年最有效之敎材。己正而後可以正人。己立而後可以立人。今日之政治如斯。社會如斯。耳濡目染。潛移默化。日爲靑年實施惡果之敎育。而乃向靑年責善。是眞有若綠木而求魚。

吾人今且暫置政治上社會上之一般人物不言。但就與靑年最相關切之敎育界人士而論。檢查最近數年來之敎育界。其中固不乏自重自好品學卓絕都足以爲學生之模楷之士。然而由於官僚主義之侵入敎育界。敎育亦成爲暗黑。一般敎師之自失其師資者。固亦滔滔皆是。茲請就敎育界之一般現。象略舉數　。

邇年以來國内學潮彼伏此興。不可遏抑。人皆曰由於學生之好亂。由於學風之替落。實則按其實際。察其背景。殆無一次學潮。不有敎師爲其主動。派别之角逐。位置之競爭。以及私人之嫌怨。皆利用學生爲工具。推波興瀾。不惜犧牲學生。以顧全一己之權利。於是而師道日替。於是而學風日壞。敎育日趨於破產。此其一也。

學海淵博。孰能盡善。故爲人師者尤當「學而不厭。誨人不倦。」庶能敎學相。長然而今日敎育界之人士。頗多學不求益。敎不求效。時代邁進。己日落後而不自覺。學生前步。則視爲乖戾反常。甚且任意告假缺課。以荒廢學生之時光。此其二也。

「正己正人。立己立人。」吾人旣於上文言之。今日敎育界之不能自重其師資者。如舞塲之留連。博奕之嗜好。以及不正當娛樂之涉染。復如奢靡無度。行止不檢。己不能正。究將何以爲人之則。令學生衷心欽式。更將何以培出佳善之靑年。此其三也。

師生之關係。不僅在於書本上之傳授。而尤在於人格之薰陶。我國往昔敎。育固多缺憾。但一日之師。終身服膺。師道之隆。蓋有自於智識以外之人格感化。即最近歐美最新敎育學説。亦極注重學生之課外生活。今我國在課堂上則爲「説書。」出門則成路人。即智識之傳授。尙恐未能盡責。更談不到人格之薰陶。此其四也。

Who is Responsible for Training Youth?

Since the publication of a commentary entitled "Saving the Nation and Training Youth" some days ago, this reporter has received numerous letters from young people raising questions. The points they question mainly concern "Who is our role model?" and "Who is qualified to train us?" At this point, this reporter recalls a trip to the Northeast last year. After finishing a lecture at Northeastern University, a student suddenly asked, "There are many great figures in Chinese and foreign history who can serve as role models for us young people. But today, within our country, in our Northeast, which political leaders are truly worthy of being our role models?" The reporter pondered in silence, unable to answer for a long time. Examining the letters of inquiry from today's youth and recalling the old incident during the lecture at Northeastern University, I deeply feel that the two questions, "Who is our role model?" and "Who is qualified to train us?" are indeed the greatest problems in current education. We constantly blame young people for being fond of creating chaos, lacking practicality, being arrogant and following old ways, yet we do not reflect on whether we ourselves have practiced what we expect from young people. I further feel that educating youth is not about dead books, not about dead role models, but about living reality and living examples. All kinds of things in politics and society, as well as the behavior of political and social leaders, are the most effective teaching materials for young people. Only by being upright oneself can one correct others. Only by establishing oneself can one establish others. Today's politics is like this, society is like this. What is seen and heard gradually influences and subtly changes, educating young people with evil consequences every day. Yet we demand good from the youth. Is this not truly like climbing a tree to catch fish?

Let us now temporarily put aside general figures in politics and society. Let us focus on those in the education sector, who are most closely related to young people. Examining the education sector in recent years, there is no lack of individuals who value themselves, are virtuous, and academically outstanding, all of whom are worthy of being role models for students. However, due to the encroachment of bureaucracy into the education sector, education has also become dark. Many general teachers have lost their qualifications as teachers. Let us briefly list a few of the general phenomena in the education sector.

In recent years, student movements have arisen and fallen one after another in the country, and cannot be suppressed. People all say it is due to students' fondness for creating chaos and the decline of the academic atmosphere. But in reality, observing the background, almost every student movement has teachers behind it, actively involved. Factional competition, the competition for positions, and personal grievances all use students as tools to stir up trouble, sacrificing students to protect one's own rights. Thus, the way of teachers declines day by day. Thus, the academic atmosphere deteriorates day by day. Education is increasingly going bankrupt. This is one aspect.

The sea of learning is vast, and who can be perfect? Therefore, teachers should especially "learn without being tired and teach without being weary" so that teaching and learning can complement each other. However, many people in today's education sector do not seek improvement in their studies and do not seek effectiveness in their teaching. As the times advance, they are lagging behind and do not realize it. If students take a step forward, they are regarded as rebellious and abnormal. They even arbitrarily take leave and miss classes, wasting students' time. This is another aspect.

"Correct yourself and correct others. Establish yourself and establish others." We have already said this in the previous text. Today's education sector cannot respect its teachers. They frequent dance halls, are fond of gambling, and are involved in improper entertainment. They are also extravagant and do not restrain their behavior. If one cannot be upright oneself, how can one be a role model for others, allowing students to sincerely admire and emulate them? And how can one cultivate outstanding youth? This is another aspect.

The relationship between teachers and students is not only about the transmission of knowledge in books but also about the cultivation of personality. Our country's past education had many shortcomings, but one day as a teacher meant lifelong respect. The prominence of the teacher's way stemmed from the influence of personality beyond knowledge. Even the latest European and American educational theories also pay great attention to students' extracurricular life. Today, in our country, in the classroom, it is just "storytelling." Once out of the door, they become strangers. Even the transmission of knowledge may not be done to the fullest extent, not to mention the cultivation of personality. This is another aspect.

The above four points are just one corner. Today, we only feel like weeping and lamenting the bankruptcy of education and the decline of the academic atmosphere. We only know that young people should be strictly trained. But have we ever reflected on the two major questions placed before us: "Who is the role model for youth?" "Who is qualified to train youth?" Why not think deeply and consider carefully?
