禦侮興國之先例㈡彬

昨評吾人已以新土耳其爲例。指陳我民族今後之出路矣。今請更以高唱大希臘主義大舉侵犯土耳其之希臘爲例。以測日本之將來。

希臘於一八三○年脫離土耳其而獨立。及國基漸固。即高唱大希臘主義。力圖擴張其國土。如地中海東巴爾幹及愛琴海對岸之小亞細亞各地。都爲希臘所埀涎。此與　本今日之高唱大陸政策。由呑併台灣朝鮮。進窺我東北。其貪婪驕恣。蓋如出一轍。

然而苦戰三年。希臘終於敗衂。夷考其失敗之因。其主要者。一爲經濟困難。二爲外交失敗。三爲政變影響。四爲軍心不固。凡此四端。居一必敗。希皆有。之故其一敗塗地。爲必然之結局。

希臘之覆轍。既已指陳如前。以希例日。則無一不酷肖。而日之貪橫。則固遠過於希也。希致敗之四因。以言經濟困難。則日本自滬變發生以來。敗政亦陷於大混亂之境。如東京時事新聞所載。股票。國債。生絲。棉絲市塲大暴落。匯兌行市。對美大激漲。產業界陷入萬分危險之境。海外貿易一落千丈。國家財政。已無辦法。滬變發生。復突增軍費四千萬元。益以總選舉費等。臨時之支出。深感羅掘俱窮。不得不在公債方面籌劃。但國民對於國債。前此已每人分担一百零六元之鉅數。萬萬無力負担。未來難關。當可想見。以言外交。日本之恣意橫行。目無世界。早爲列國所側目。自九一八以來。决然破壞遠東和平。予全人類以迫害。更昌言不諱。以美與蘇聯爲假想敵。與國既少。嫉視者多。此次國聯突然强硬勸告日本。注意其對於盟約應負之義務。及滬變後國際之一般輿論。即可知日本在外交上已陷入孤立之地位。以言政變。則一月五日若　被毆。一月九日晚井上被剌以及官僚派與軍人派之爭持亦足以證明其國内政爭之日益激烈。以言軍心。則自日軍來滬後。非戰空氣。彌漫全國。一月九日紀元節。明治大學學生與神田區羣衆之反對軍閥。反對進攻中國之示威運動。以及神户海員工人之罷工示威運動。益以連年歉收。供需缺乏。人心惶惶不定。軍心不固。亦正與希臘毫無二樣。不僅此也。日軍久疏戰陣。外强中乾。國内思想激化。階級對立。其本身已自百孔千瘡。難以收拾。况彼航海來攻。我坐抗禦。勞逸之勢。既相懸殊。而彼曲我直。則更爲兵家所最忌。故以希例日。就前者而觀。日固無勝利之理。就後者而觀。則日更不得不敗。事事歷。歷瞭若指掌。我國今日。但須一致團結。持久抗禦。希土苦戰三年。國人其努力爲之

A Precedent for Resisting Aggression and Revitalizing the Nation ㈡彬

Yesterday's commentary used the example of the new Turkey to point out the future path for our nation. Today, let us take the example of Greece, which loudly advocated the "Great Greece" ideology and launched a major invasion of Turkey, to predict Japan's future.

Greece broke away from Turkey and became independent in 1830. As its national foundation gradually solidified, it began to loudly advocate the "Great Greece" ideology, striving to expand its territory. Regions such as the eastern Mediterranean, the Balkans, and Asia Minor across the Aegean Sea were all coveted by Greece. This is exactly like Japan's current advocacy of its continental policy, from annexing Taiwan and Korea to coveting our Northeast. Its greed and arrogance are virtually identical.

However, after three years of bitter fighting, Greece ultimately failed. Examining the reasons for its failure, the main ones were: first, economic difficulties; second, diplomatic failure; third, the impact of political upheaval; and fourth, the instability of military morale. Any one of these four would lead to defeat, and Greece possessed them all. Therefore, its utter defeat was an inevitable outcome.

Greece's mistakes have already been pointed out. Comparing Japan to the example of Greece, there is nothing that is not remarkably similar. And Japan's greed and recklessness far exceed those of Greece. Regarding the four causes of Greece's defeat, in terms of economic difficulties, Japan's defeated policies, since the Shanghai Incident, have also fallen into a state of great chaos. As reported by the Tokyo Jiji Shinpo, stock prices, national bonds, raw silk, and cotton yarn markets have plummeted. Exchange rates have soared sharply against the US dollar. The industrial sector has fallen into an extremely dangerous situation. Overseas trade has fallen sharply. The national finances are already beyond repair. The Shanghai Incident has suddenly increased military spending by 40 million yuan. In addition, there are temporary expenditures such as the general election expenses, deeply feeling the exhaustion of resources and being forced to plan for public debt. However, the people have already shouldered a huge burden of 106 yuan per person in national debt, and they are completely unable to bear it. The difficulties of the future can be imagined. In terms of diplomacy, Japan's willful and reckless actions, disregarding the world, have long been watched with concern by other nations. Since the September 18th Incident, it has resolutely destroyed peace in the Far East and persecuted all of humanity. It even openly declares the United States and the Soviet Union as hypothetical enemies. It has few friends and many who are jealous. The League of Nations' sudden and strong recommendation to Japan to pay attention to its obligations under the Covenant, and the general international opinion after the Shanghai Incident, show that Japan has fallen into a state of diplomatic isolation. In terms of political upheaval, the assault on Wakatsuki on January 5th, the assassination attempt on Inoue on the evening of January 9th, and the contention between the bureaucratic and military factions are sufficient to prove the increasing intensity of domestic political strife. In terms of military morale, since the Japanese army came to Shanghai, an anti-war atmosphere has permeated the country. The anti-warlord and anti-invasion of China demonstrations by students of Meiji University and the masses in Kanda Ward on the Kigen-setsu (Foundation Day) on January 9th, as well as the strike demonstrations by Kobe maritime workers, coupled with years of poor harvests and lack of supply and demand, have caused widespread public unease. Instability of military morale is also exactly the same as in Greece. Not only that, but the Japanese army has long been out of practice and is outwardly strong but inwardly weak. Domestic ideological tensions and class antagonism have already left it riddled with holes and difficult to salvage. Moreover, they are attacking by sea, while we are defending from a fixed position. The disparity in effort and ease is already great. Furthermore, they are in the wrong, and we are in the right, which is most feared by military strategists. Therefore, comparing Japan to the example of Greece, from the former perspective, Japan has no reason to win. From the latter perspective, Japan is even more bound to fail. Everything is clear and apparent. Our country today only needs to unite as one and persist in resisting. Greece and Turkey fought bitterly for three years. The people of the nation should strive to do the same.
