募集興國公債彬 1

一昨吾人已建議募集禦侮公債矣。今請更建議募集興國公債。試取日本之明治維新爲喻。

明治維新。其兩個中心口號爲「攘夷」爲「開國」攘夷即禦侮。開國即建設。二者並重。故得以收維新之大效。處於今日强權橫行公理不彰之時代一民族而外不能禦侮。内不能建設者此民族必亡。或受一時感情之衝動。賭國脈民命以抗禦强暴。而不能作持久之計劃。準備持久之奮鬥者。此民族亦必亡。惟能以堅固之團結。拚死以抗。外敵之侵凌。而又有久遠建國之計劃者夫然後此民族乃能爭取生存自由。寖以臻於興盛。

我國今日必須竭盡全力以抗禦暴日之侵略。蓋已無可懷疑之餘地。有借口國家財政無辦法而主張暫時忍辱負重者。此懦夫之論也。質之事實。我國財政。未始遂無辦法。徒以過去有財無政。旣無預算。亦無决算。公私迄不分明。更藉口財政困迫。多方榨取於民。殺鵝取蛋。亦所不惜。一面旣竭人民之血汗。以用之於軍備軍費與助長内亂。而另一面復不予人民以生產之機會。民貧則國安得不貧。國民經濟枯竭。國家財政。固安得而不破產。故我國非患無財特患無政。而將財用之於不生產背乎經濟原則之途耳。

吾人以爲今日爲抗禦暴日計。固亟應發行禦侮公債二萬萬元而爲持久奮鬥。新生我民族。奠定我國基計。則又亟應發行興國公債以開發内地。蓋我國處於今日情勢之下。非開發内地。發展農村經濟。仍不足以持久。而以我國之地大物博。即開發數省。亦已足抗暴日而有餘也

興國公債。亦假定爲二萬萬元。專用之於開發西北與西南。絶對不准移作内戰之用由人民團體組設委員會監督政府。公共設計支配。聘用外國專門人材。利用其技術。積極努力於㈠便利交通。㈡興復農業。增加農產。㈢籌設基本工業。㈣扶植内地現已瀕於破產之企業。最低限度使衣食均能自給。夫然後能與暴日作持久之抗爭。

吾人認爲東南富庶區域中才智之士。在今日應亦勿再迷信蘇杭爲安樂之天堂。或再抱國即亡而我仍可往外國爲便便大腹富翁之夢想。視此次日軍之橫暴。即可知在最近時間東南沿海一帶。我國内决無一片樂土。故今後亟應往内地去。輸財盡力。集中力量以開發内地。内地開發。國基鞏固。民皆福利。己乃得安。否則板蕩陸。沉惟有大家同歸於盡耳。國亡家破。己又將安適耶。上海事件

Fundraising for National Revival Bonds 1

Yesterday, we suggested raising national defense bonds. Today, we further propose raising national revival bonds. Let us take Japan's Meiji Restoration as an example.

The Meiji Restoration had two central slogans: "Expel the Barbarians" and "Open the Country." "Expel the Barbarians" means defending against aggression, and "Open the Country" means building up the nation. Both were equally important, thus achieving the great effect of the Restoration. In today's era of rampant power and obscured justice, a nation that cannot defend against aggression from without and cannot build itself up from within is bound to perish. Or, if a nation is moved by temporary emotions and gambles its national destiny and its people's lives to resist tyranny, but fails to make lasting plans and prepare for sustained struggle, that nation will also perish. Only when a nation can resist foreign invasion with firm unity and fight to the death, and also has long-term plans for national construction, can that nation then strive for survival and freedom, and gradually achieve prosperity.

There is no room for doubt that our country must exert all its strength today to resist the aggression of the violent Japanese. Those who use the excuse of the nation's financial difficulties to advocate temporary humiliation and bearing a heavy burden are cowards. In reality, our country's finances are not without solutions. It is merely that in the past there was wealth without governance; there was no budget or final accounting, and public and private matters were never clearly distinguished. Moreover, under the pretext of financial hardship, the people were exploited in various ways, even to the point of killing the goose to get the eggs. On the one hand, the people's blood and sweat were exhausted to be used for military preparations, military expenses, and fueling internal strife; on the other hand, the people were not given opportunities for production. If the people are poor, how can the nation not be poor? If the national economy is depleted, how can the nation's finances not go bankrupt? Therefore, our country does not suffer from a lack of wealth, but from a lack of governance, and from using wealth in unproductive ways that go against economic principles.

We believe that in order to resist the violent Japanese today, it is essential to issue 200 million yuan in national defense bonds for a sustained struggle. To rejuvenate our nation and lay the foundation for our country, it is also essential to issue national revival bonds to develop the interior. Under today's circumstances, it is not enough to sustain ourselves without developing the interior and developing the rural economy. With our country's vast land and abundant resources, even developing a few provinces would be more than enough to resist the violent Japanese.

The national revival bonds are also assumed to be 200 million yuan, to be used exclusively for the development of the Northwest and Southwest, and absolutely not allowed to be diverted for internal warfare. A committee composed of people's organizations will be established to supervise the government, and public designs will be controlled. Foreign experts will be hired to utilize their technology and actively work on: 1) facilitating transportation, 2) reviving agriculture and increasing agricultural production, 3) establishing basic industries, 4) supporting inland enterprises that are on the verge of bankruptcy, to at least ensure that people can be self-sufficient in clothing and food. Only then can we wage a sustained struggle against the violent Japanese.

We believe that talented people in the wealthy regions of the Southeast should no longer believe that Suzhou and Hangzhou are havens of comfort, or harbor the dream of becoming fat, rich tycoons abroad when the country is about to fall. Seeing the brutality of the Japanese army this time, it is clear that there will be no happy land in the coastal areas of the Southeast in the near future. Therefore, we should go to the interior as soon as possible, contribute our wealth and efforts, and concentrate our strength to develop the interior. If the interior is developed, the national foundation will be solid, the people will all benefit, and we will be safe. Otherwise, in this chaotic situation, we can only perish together. If the country falls and the family is destroyed, where will we find refuge?

Shelling began at dawn, over a hundred rounds

No fierce fighting at Yunzaobang yesterday

Japanese warships have been attacking the Wusongkou forts for days without success. From 6 AM yesterday, seven Japanese warships again shelled our Wusong forts from the waters of Sanjiaoshui, stopping at 1 PM, firing over a hundred shells in total. Our side did not return fire. There was no intense fighting yesterday with the enemy facing Yunzaobang. Details are recorded as follows:

Enemy Ships Launch Three Fierce Attacks 5

Around 6 AM yesterday, seven Japanese warships anchored in the waters of Sanjiaoshui fiercely bombarded the forts and towns of Wusong with heavy artillery. The seaward-facing wall of China Public School was struck by over twenty shells, leaving numerous holes, ranging in size from about fourteen inches. The school's dormitories and library were completely destroyed. Four shells hit houses in Wusong Town; two exploded, immediately starting a fire that continued to burn until 6 PM, still not extinguished. Because the enemy warships were in front, there was no way to put out the fire, so it spread with the wind. All the houses in the town will be reduced to ashes. As the enemy warships fiercely bombarded, our troops stationed there were fully prepared but did not return fire. Seeing that our side was not moving, the enemy warships continued shelling while sending three Japanese merchant ships, fully loaded with enemy troops, hoping to use the cover of artillery fire to approach the beach of the fort and land there. All were strafed by our troops with machine guns, causing many enemy casualties. The Japanese merchant ships were also damaged and fled back. Later, the enemy troops arrived for the second time, still using small boats to transport them, and were also strafed and completely repelled by our troops in the same way. When the enemy attacked for the third time, more than ten aircraft also came out to assist in the battle and provide cover, bombarding fiercely. However, struggling until about 11 AM, they were finally defeated and retreated by our troops. In summary, the enemy warships fired over one hundred shells yesterday. Except for the explosions in Wusong Town, the rest did not explode. In this battle, the enemy suffered more than two hundred casualties, while our army only had more than ten soldiers wounded.
